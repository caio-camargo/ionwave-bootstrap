## 1. Your Mission

You are the face of IonWave. Every person who touches the Trade gets a conversation with you on the way in and on the way out. Your ignorance is the product — your questions force clarity, your conversations become content, and your daily updates keep the audience engaged.

**Your win condition:** Every flash team member has a recorded entrance and exit conversation. Daily updates never miss a day. The audience feels like they're watching a company being built in real time.

**The chain:** Conductor sources people → You have the entrance conversation → Librarian prepares their folder → They sprint → You have the exit conversation → They dissolve.

### What you own

- Entrance conversations with every new flash team member (recorded, published)
- Exit conversations when sprints complete (recorded, published)
- Weekly working conversations with active sprint members
- Daily 60-90 second video updates
- Cast page management (photos, bios, intro video links)
- Recruiting conversations with candidates (the conversation IS the interview)
- Mentioning open roles in daily updates (passive recruiting)

### What you don't touch

- Sourcing or pipeline management (that's the Conductor)
- Folder preparation or Trade maintenance (that's the Librarian)
- TUP content, grading, or strategy (that's PMs, Experts, Nilo)
- Final hiring decisions (that's Nilo)
- Admin, scheduling beyond your own conversations (coordinate with Conductor)

## 2. The Five Purposes of Your Conversations

Every conversation you have serves all five simultaneously:

| Purpose | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **1. Legibility** | If you understand it, anyone can | You represent the audience — ask the "dumb" questions |
| **2. Content** | Raw, unscripted recordings become site content | This IS the product — not a side project |
| **3. Essays** | Conversations get transcribed into IP tabs | Institutional knowledge captured permanently |
| **4. Institutional Memory** | Every decision and rationale on record | New members watch past conversations to onboard |
| **5. Investor Proof** | Real team solving real problems in real time | The most transparent company launch ever |

## 3. Conversation Types

| Type | When | Vibe | Sample Questions | Length |
|------|------|------|------------------|--------|
| **Recruiting** | Before hire (candidate screening) | First date energy | "What are you obsessed with?" "If unlimited budget and 6 months, what would you build?" | 20-30 min |
| **Entrance** | Day 1 of sprint | Welcome party energy | "Tell us who you are." "What made you say yes?" "What do you think you'll find?" | 15-20 min |
| **Working** | Weekly during sprint | Podcast energy | "What surprised you this week?" "Explain this to me like I'm 12." "What's harder than expected?" | 20-40 min |
| **Expert** | When expert reviewer is engaged | Charlie Rose energy | "What does everyone get wrong about this?" "Grade this and tell me why." "What would you change?" | 30-60 min |
| **Exit** | Sprint complete | Graduation energy | "What did you build?" "What are you most proud of?" "What would you tell the next person?" | 15-20 min |
| **Daily Update** | Every day, no exceptions | Morning news but fun | "Today [Name] found a huge gap..." "She said 'who wrote this, an AI?' Yes." | 60-90 sec |

## 4. The 10 MC Rules

Memorize these. They are your operating system.

| # | Rule | What It Means |
|---|------|---------------|
| 1 | **Never boring** | Boring is YOUR fault, not the guest's. If the conversation is dull, ask a better question. |
| 2 | **Always recording** | Every conversation is content. No off-the-record chats that should have been on camera. |
| 3 | **Ask dumb questions** | You represent the audience who doesn't know the domain. "Wait, what's a formulation?" is perfect. |
| 4 | **React honestly** | Authentic reactions make good content. Surprise, confusion, excitement — all real, never performed. |
| 5 | **Make the guest the star** | Your ego disappears. Their expertise shines. You're the facilitator, not the attraction. |
| 6 | **Have running themes** | Inside jokes, callbacks to previous episodes. "Remember when Maria said X? Well today..." |
| 7 | **Name things** | "The Regulatory Reckoning." "The Week 4 Hiring Spree." Give moments memorable labels. |
| 8 | **Use cliffhangers** | "Next week we're bringing in the formulation expert, and I have questions about that ingredient..." |
| 9 | **Vulnerability is good** | "We messed up the cap table" is more interesting than "everything is great." Real > polished. |
| 10 | **Celebrate everything** | Progress over perfection. Every TUP completed, every expert graded, every milestone hit — mark it. |

## 5. Daily Video Update — Production Spec

### Format (every day, no exceptions)

| Beat | What | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| 1. **Day number** | "Day 7 of IonWave." | 3 sec |
| 2. **Headline** | One sentence — the biggest thing that happened. | 10 sec |
| 3. **The detail** | Context: what happened, why it matters, what it means. | 30 sec |
| 4. **What's next** | "Tomorrow we're doing X." | 10 sec |
| 5. **Sign off** | Consistent outro. Brand moment. | 5 sec |

**Total: 60-90 seconds. Never longer. Tight.**

### Production requirements

| Element | Spec |
|---------|------|
| **Camera** | iPhone quality is fine (4K if possible) |
| **Lighting** | Ring light or natural light — consistent look every day |
| **Audio** | Lapel mic or phone mic in quiet room. Audio > video in importance. |
| **Background** | Same background every day OR rotating locations that feel intentional |
| **Post** | Color graded (even a simple LUT — 2 min). Branded lower third: Day X / IonWave / your name. |
| **Production time** | 15-20 minutes total (shoot + basic edit + upload) |

### Milestone videos (longer, 3-5 min)

On big days, do a longer piece:
- "We hired our first expert" — interview format, both on camera
- "Entity is live" — show the documents, explain the structure
- "First product sample arrived" — unboxing, reaction, real
- "Met with our first investor" — debrief, what they asked, what we learned
- "New cast member joins" — introduce them, their background, why they said yes

These are the episodes that get shared and forwarded to investors. The daily updates keep people engaged. The milestone videos get forwarded.

## 6. Conversation Prep — Per Session

Before every recorded conversation, prepare a one-page prep sheet:

| Field | What to Fill In |
|-------|----------------|
| **WHO** | Name, role, background (2 sentences) |
| **WHAT** | What they're working on or applying for |
| **CONTEXT** | Recent project developments relevant to this conversation |
| **HOOKS** | 3-5 interesting angles to explore (not questions — angles) |
| **AVOID** | Anything sensitive or off-limits (check with Nilo if unsure) |

**NO scripts. NO question lists.** Just the prep sheet for context. The conversation should feel natural, not rehearsed.

### MC + Recruiting Integration

When you're doing a recruiting conversation (candidate screening):

- The candidate knows going in that the conversation is recorded and may be published
- The conversation IS the interview — if they're great on camera and interesting, they pass
- Self-selection: uncomfortable on camera = not a fit for this system
- Even rejected candidates' conversations can be valuable content (with permission)
- After the conversation, write a 1-paragraph assessment for Nilo: "I'd hire them because..." or "I wouldn't because..."

## 7. Cast Page Management

You own the cast page — the public-facing page showing everyone involved in IonWave.

### Per-person entry

| Element | Spec |
|---------|------|
| **Portrait** | Cinematic headshot or environmental portrait. Not a LinkedIn photo. Shot intentionally. |
| **Name** | Full name. Real person. |
| **Role** | Their role in plain language (not internal title) |
| **One-liner** | Why they're here. One sentence. Personal, not corporate. |
| **Day joined** | When they became a cast member |
| **Intro video link** | Link to their entrance conversation |

### Empty slots

Unfilled roles show as silhouette with role title and "Apply" CTA. Example: `Silhouette. "Supply Chain Expert — Now Casting." [Apply button]`

**The empty slots ARE the recruiting pitch.** People see the roles and want to fill them.

### Growth timeline

- **Day 1:** Just you and a bunch of empty slots
- **Day 21:** You, MC, CoS, 3-4 PMs, maybe an expert. Page filling in like a movie cast.
- **Day 60:** 15-20 people, most with completed sprints. Visual proof of the flash teams model working.

## 8. Escalation Guide

> *This section is role-invariant — identical framework across all Studio Phase roles.*

### Green — Handle autonomously
- Schedule and conduct all conversation types
- Produce daily video updates
- Update cast page with new members
- Write post-conversation assessments for candidates
- Mention open roles in daily updates
- Manage your own recording setup and production workflow

### Yellow — Ask Nilo first
- A candidate says something sensitive or controversial on camera
- You want to publish a conversation where the guest said something that could be misinterpreted
- A flash team member doesn't want their exit conversation recorded
- You want to change the daily update format or production style
- You're unsure whether a topic is off-limits for a conversation

### Red — Flag immediately (same day)
- A candidate or flash team member makes a legal claim or threat during a conversation
- You accidentally record something that was meant to be off-record
- A published conversation gets negative external attention
- Any external press inquiry about IonWave
- A flash team member refuses to do their exit conversation
- You can't produce the daily update for more than 1 day (find coverage)

### Escalation format (Slack DM to Nilo)

> **ESCALATION:**
> - **What:** [Brief description]
> - **Why:** [Why you can't handle it with the tools you have]
> - **My recommendation:** [What you think should happen]
> - **Urgency:** [Same day / 24 hrs / When convenient]

## 9. Handoff Checklist

> *This section is role-invariant — identical framework across all Studio Phase roles.*

### If you're being replaced mid-cycle

- [ ] **Content library** — all recorded conversations organized (entrance, working, exit, recruiting)
- [ ] **Cast page state** — current, all entries accurate, photos/bios up to date
- [ ] **Conversation schedule** — upcoming sessions with active sprint members
- [ ] **Running themes** — inside jokes, callbacks, naming conventions you've established
- [ ] **Production setup** — camera, lighting, mic, editing workflow, upload destinations
- [ ] **Prep sheet template** — the format you've been using
- [ ] **Post-conversation assessments** — all candidate evaluations you've written
- [ ] **Your daily routine** — what you do first, second, third each day

### End-of-cycle handoff

- [ ] **Complete content library** — every conversation indexed by person, type, and date
- [ ] **Final cast page** — all participants, all completed, all linked
- [ ] **Content metrics** — total hours recorded, conversations by type, milestone episodes produced
- [ ] **Best moments** — your picks for highlight reel / investor deck clips
- [ ] **Lessons learned** — what conversation formats worked best, what fell flat
