# VA Kickoff Package

> **You were just hired. This is everything you need to execute Day 1.**
>
> Read this document top to bottom. By the end, you will know exactly what your job is, what you're screening for, what templates to use, how to track candidates, when to escalate, and what "done" looks like.

## 1. Your Mission

You are the first hire in the IonWave labor chain. Your job is to find and screen candidates for the **Chief of Staff (CoS)** role. The CoS will then take over and build the rest of the team.

**Your win condition:** CoS hired within 4 weeks.

**The chain:** You → CoS → Recruiter → Operator → Running Business

You are Depth 1 in the delegation chain. You don't make hiring decisions — you filter the pipeline so Nilo only sees qualified candidates. Your judgment is encoded in this document. Follow it.

### What you own
- Post the CoS job on platforms
- Screen all incoming applicants (first filter)
- Send email templates to candidates
- Update the pipeline tracker daily
- Schedule interview calls for Nilo
- Produce a 60-90 second daily video update

### What you don't touch
- Hiring decisions (that's Nilo)
- Compensation/equity discussions (escalate to Nilo)
- Changes to the job description (escalate to Nilo)
- Strategy, model content, or brand decisions

## 2. CoS Job Description

**Post this on:** LinkedIn, AngelList/Wellfound, and any relevant D2C/startup communities.

---

### Chief of Staff — IonWave (D2C Health Brand)

**About IonWave**
IonWave is a direct-to-consumer electrolyte brand being built using a venture studio model. The entire business — operations, marketing, supply chain, hiring, compliance — has been pre-engineered as a complete operating system. The CoS receives this system and manages the execution chain that turns it into a running business.

This is not a typical startup. You are not figuring things out from scratch. You are executing a verified plan with real resources.

**The Role**
The Chief of Staff manages the labor chain: hiring a recruiter, overseeing the operator search, and ensuring the team is built on schedule. You are the bridge between the studio (strategy/architecture) and the operator (day-to-day execution).

**What You'll Do**
- Hire and manage a recruiter to source operator candidates
- Oversee the operator search pipeline (10+ candidates screened, top 3-5 to interview)
- Set and enforce process timelines across the hiring chain
- Verify milestones against the execution tracker
- Provide daily async updates + weekly 30-min sync with the founder
- Reject below-threshold candidates autonomously

**What You Won't Do**
- Run the business day-to-day (that's the operator)
- Make final hiring decisions on the operator (that's the founder)
- Change equity terms, strategy, or brand positioning

**What You Receive**
- Complete operating model (634-tab ODD covering every process, metric, and playbook)
- Role-engineered job descriptions, assessment rubrics, and onboarding packets for every downstream hire
- Pre-built tools: pipeline tracker, email templates, evaluation matrices, escalation protocols

**Requirements**
- Experience managing hiring pipelines or recruiting operations
- Experience in D2C, e-commerce, or startup operations
- Can work autonomously with minimal supervision
- Strong written communication (daily updates are async)
- Available 15-20 hours/week for 8-12 weeks

**Compensation**
- $3,000-5,000/month
- 1-3% Studio equity (3-year vest)
- $1,000 bonus per successful placement
- $2,500 bonus when IonWave hits $100K MRR

**To Apply**
Send: (1) your LinkedIn or resume, (2) a brief paragraph on why this interests you, (3) one example of a hiring pipeline you've managed or process you've built.

## 3. Screening Checklist

Use this for every applicant. Score each dimension 1-5. **Advance candidates scoring 3+ on all dimensions. Reject anyone scoring 1-2 on any critical dimension.**

| # | Dimension | What You're Looking For | Score 1-5 | Critical? |
|---|-----------|------------------------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Hiring/ops experience** | Has managed a hiring pipeline, recruiting process, or operational workflow. Not just "helped with hiring" — owned the process. | ___ | YES |
| 2 | **D2C/startup familiarity** | Understands the D2C world, e-commerce, or startup operations. Doesn't need to be an expert — needs to not be lost. | ___ | YES |
| 3 | **Autonomy signals** | Evidence of working independently. Self-directed. Doesn't need hand-holding. Look for: freelance work, remote management, side projects. | ___ | YES |
| 4 | **Communication quality** | Application is well-written. Clear, concise, specific. No fluff. If their application is sloppy, their daily updates will be worse. | ___ | YES |
| 5 | **Motivation fit** | Why this? Not just "looking for a job." Look for: interest in the model, entrepreneurial curiosity, something specific about the opportunity. | ___ | No |
| 6 | **Availability** | Can commit 15-20 hrs/week for 8-12 weeks. No competing full-time commitments that would conflict. | ___ | YES |

### Instant rejections (don't waste time scoring)
- No relevant experience in hiring, ops, or D2C
- Application is a generic template (clearly sent to 50 jobs)
- Can't start within 1 week
- Asks about comp before demonstrating interest in the role

### Borderline candidates (score 3 on any critical dimension)
- Don't reject — escalate to Nilo with your notes
- Tag as "BORDERLINE" in the pipeline tracker

## 4. Test Task

Send this to candidates who pass the screening checklist (score 3+ on all dimensions). Give them **48 hours** to complete it.

---

**Subject: IonWave CoS — Next Step**

Hi [Name],

Thanks for your application. We'd like to move forward with a short task to see how you think about the role.

**The Task (30-45 minutes):**

You're the CoS of IonWave. It's Week 1. You need to hire a recruiter to source operator candidates. You have the job description and assessment rubric for the operator role.

Please provide:
1. **Recruiter sourcing plan** — Where would you find a recruiter for this type of search? List 3 channels and why.
2. **Timeline** — Draft a week-by-week timeline for the recruiter search (from posting to contracted).
3. **Red flags** — What are 3 red flags you'd watch for in a recruiter candidate?
4. **First-day email** — Write the email you'd send the recruiter on their first day, explaining what they need to do.

No right answer. We're evaluating how you think, how you organize, and how you communicate.

Please reply to this email with your response within 48 hours.

Best,
[Name]

---

### How to evaluate the test task

| Criteria | Strong | Weak |
|----------|--------|------|
| **Specificity** | Names actual platforms, has real timelines, gives concrete red flags | Vague ("I'd post on job boards"), generic answers |
| **Structure** | Organized, easy to follow, numbered or sectioned | Wall of text, hard to parse |
| **Communication** | The first-day email is clear, actionable, and professional | Email is vague, missing key info, or overly formal/stiff |
| **Judgment** | Red flags show real-world experience (e.g., "recruiter who can't explain their sourcing method") | Red flags are obvious/generic ("someone who's late") |
| **Speed** | Completed within 48 hours | Late or asked for extension |

**Advance:** Strong on 4+ criteria → Schedule Nilo interview
**Borderline:** Strong on 3 → Escalate to Nilo with notes
**Reject:** Strong on 2 or fewer → Send rejection email

When a candidate is ready for Nilo's interview, prepare this packet and send it to Nilo **at least 24 hours before the call.**

- [ ] **Candidate name and contact info**
- [ ] **Application materials** (resume/LinkedIn, their paragraph on why, their hiring example)
- [ ] **Screening scorecard** (your scores on all 6 dimensions with brief notes)

- [ ] **Any flags or concerns** (things Nilo should probe on — gaps in experience, unclear motivation, etc.)

### Post-Hire Handoff (after CoS is selected)

When the CoS is hired and contracted, prepare:

- [ ] **Full candidate file** (all materials above for the hired candidate)

- [ ] **Active pipeline** (any strong candidates who weren't selected — the CoS may want them for other roles)
- [ ] **Access handoff** (platform logins, email accounts, tracker access)

## 8. Escalation Guide

### When to handle it yourself (Green — autonomous)
- Reject obviously unqualified applicants
- Send any template email from Section 5
- Schedule calls using the calendar link
- Update the pipeline tracker
- Post the job on additional platforms using the same JD
- Send daily updates to Nilo

### When to ask Nilo first (Yellow — needs approval)
- Advance a borderline candidate (scored 3 on a critical dimension)
- Modify any email template beyond filling in names/dates
- A candidate asks about specifics you don't know (comp details, equity mechanics, start date flexibility)
- You want to try a new sourcing channel not in the original plan
- You're getting zero qualified applicants after 1 week

### When to flag immediately (Red — same-day escalation)
- Zero qualified candidates after 1 week of posting
- A candidate asks about compensation or equity details (don't answer — forward to Nilo)
- A candidate you advanced reveals a disqualifying issue
- You suspect a candidate is misrepresenting their experience
- You're unsure whether to advance or reject and can't decide using the checklist
- Any external press, legal, or sensitive inquiry about IonWave

### Escalation format (Slack DM to Nilo)
> **ESCALATION:**
> - **What:** [Brief description]
> - **Why:** [Why you can't handle it with the tools you have]
> - **My recommendation:** [What you think should happen]
> - **Urgency:** [Same day / 24 hrs / When convenient]

## 9. Your Daily Video Update

In addition to the hiring pipeline work, you produce a daily 60-90 second video update for the IonWave site. This is part of your daily workflow, not a separate project.

### Format (every day)
1. **Day number** — "Day 7 of IonWave."
2. **Headline** — One sentence on the biggest thing that happened. "Today we screened 8 applicants and advanced 2 to the test task."
3. **The detail** — 30 seconds of context. What happened, why it matters.
4. **What's next** — "Tomorrow we're doing X."
5. **Sign off** — Consistent outro.

### Production requirements
- Phone camera (iPhone quality is fine)
- Good lighting (ring light or natural light, consistent look)
- Lapel mic or phone mic in quiet room (audio > video)
- Same background every day OR rotating locations that feel intentional
- Total daily production time: 15-20 minutes (shoot + basic edit + upload)

### Milestone videos (longer, 3-5 min)
On big days, do a longer piece:
- "We got our first 50 applications"
- "CoS finalist interviews start today"
- "We hired our CoS" — introduce them on camera
