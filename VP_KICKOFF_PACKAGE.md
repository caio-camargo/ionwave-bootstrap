## 1. Your Two Missions

You run two parallel tracks from Day 1. Neither waits for the other.

### Track A: Capital Formation (Fundraising)

**Win condition:** $30-50K raised on a SAFE, with 2-4 investors committed.

- Build an investor target list (50+ names)
- Run outreach — warm intros and cold approaches
- Pitch IonWave using the Trade Pitch One-Pager and full Trade
- Handle due diligence conversations
- Close commitments — paper SAFEs, funds wired
- Send monthly investor updates to all engaged investors

### Track B: Operator Recruitment

**Win condition:** Operator hired, contracted, and beginning the 3-week M1 certification.

- Source operator candidates from D2C communities, platforms, and referrals
- Screen against the Operator evaluation framework (Section 5)
- Run deep evaluations (Trade comprehension, operational planning, metrics fluency)
- Present shortlist of 2-3 candidates with evidence
- Facilitate final selection and contract signing

### What you don't do

- Run the business day-to-day (that's the Operator)
- Build the product, website, or ads (that's the Operator)
- Manage the VA or MBA intern (their jobs are done — they found you)
- Redesign the Trade (the system is the system — you pitch it, you don't reinvent it)
- Report to the founder daily (the compliance agent handles monitoring)

## 2. Your First Two Weeks: The Integration Gate

Before you start Capital Formation or Operator recruiting, you must complete the **Integration Work Artifact**. This is a quality gate — you cannot proceed until it passes.

### The Assignment

Write a condensed Trade narrative (2,000-3,000 words) covering three models in your own words:

**Model 1: The Business Model**
- What is IonWave's value proposition and who is the target customer?
- What are the unit economics — COGS, price point, margin, and why do they work?
- What differentiates IonWave from competitors (LMNT, Seaonic, others)?
- What are the kill criteria — when does the business stop vs. continue?
- What is the 90-day validation plan?

**Model 2: The Execution Model**
- How is the Trade structured? (41 TUPs — what does this mean practically?)
- How does the Operator work with Claude (AI co-pilot)?
- What does the Operator do in the first 90 days?
- What is the compensation structure and why is it designed this way?
- What are the Operator kill criteria?

**Model 3: The Capital Model**
- How much capital does IonWave need and what is it used for?
- What type of investors should be targeted and why?
- What is the fundraising instrument (SAFE, etc.) and terms?
- What's the investor pitch — why should someone write a check?
- What risks does an investor face and how are they mitigated?

### Evaluation

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Pass** | Sufficient comprehension to proceed |
| **Pass with notes** | Mostly good, specific gaps to address |
| **Revise** | Significant gaps — rewrite with feedback (1 week) |
| **Fail** | Fundamental lack of comprehension — reconsider placement |

**Why this matters:** You'll be pitching investors and evaluating operators. If you don't understand the Trade deeply enough, every downstream conversation is weaker. This is the strongest anti-degradation mechanism in the chain.

### Source Documents for Integration

| Priority | Document | What It Covers |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Must read** | M0: Trade Thesis | Strategic foundation, market, competitive positioning |
| **Must read** | M4: Fundraising | Capital Formation playbook, investor targeting, pitch |
| **Must read** | M1: Operator System | Operator role, compensation, onboarding, certification |
| **Must read** | Trade Pitch One-Pager | Condensed overview |
| **Should read** | M35: Execution Plans | Phase-gated timeline, milestones |
| **Should read** | M2: Product Science | Formulation, substantiation, regulatory |
| **Recommended** | M5: Brand Identity | Positioning, messaging |

Claude can guide you through the TUP landscape and help prioritize based on your questions.

## 3. Capital Formation Playbook

### Target Raise

| Metric | Range |
|--------|-------|
| **Amount** | $30-50K |
| **Instrument** | SAFE ($250-500K valuation cap) |
| **Use of funds** | Product/inventory (30-40%), Marketing (30-40%), Operations (15-20%), Reserve (5-10%) |

### Investor Targeting (Priority Order)

**Tier 1: Angel investors with D2C experience**
- Check sizes: $5-100K. They understand the market and can evaluate the Trade's quality.
- Where: AngelList, D2C founder communities, supplement industry networks.

**Tier 2: Health/wellness-focused angels**
- Check sizes: $5-50K. Thesis alignment — believe in the market.
- Where: Health/wellness investor groups, biohacking communities, LinkedIn.

**Tier 3: Micro-VCs (consumer/CPG focus)**
- Check sizes: $25-250K. Small funds ($5-50M AUM) investing in consumer brands at seed.
- Where: Crunchbase, VC databases, warm intros through angels.

**Not now:** Institutional VCs. Too early. Revisit after validation sprint.

### Pipeline Math

```
Research & target list:  50+ investors identified
Outreach:                30-40 warm/cold approaches
Meetings:                10-15 conversations
Interested:              5-8 who want to go deeper
Committed:               2-4 who commit capital
Closed:                  1-3 checks totaling $30-50K
```

### The Core Pitch (adapt to your voice)

> IonWave is a premium marine plasma electrolyte brand. The electrolyte market is validated — LMNT does $66M+ in annual revenue. The gap is at the premium end: ocean-sourced minerals, where the #1 complaint is taste.
>
> What makes IonWave different isn't the product — it's the system. The founder built a complete operating manual: 41 structured business models covering every function. Each one is quality-scored and designed for execution by someone who hasn't met the founder. The entire system is AI-augmented.
>
> The venture has built-in kill criteria. If COGS exceeds $0.60, if CAC exceeds $75 for 14 days, if conversion is below 2% — the business pauses or kills. No sunk-cost trap.
>
> We're raising $30-50K on a SAFE to fund the first 90 days of execution.

### Investor Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|---------|
| "It's pre-revenue" | "Yes, and the terms are favorable. The validation sprint is 7 weeks. If it doesn't work, capital loss is bounded by kill criteria." |
| "Where's the founder?" | "The founder designed the system. The model is founder-independent by design — execution is by an operator with a complete playbook and AI co-pilot." |
| "41 TUPs sounds over-engineered" | "It's comprehensive, not complicated. Each TUP is a standalone module. The operator doesn't have to figure things out from scratch." |
| "What if the operator doesn't work out?" | "Kill criteria at Week 6 and Week 12. Root-cause attribution before any termination. Replacement protocol is defined." |
| "What's my exit?" | "Standard SAFE conversion at Series A or qualified financing. Bootstrap liquidity path documented." |

## 4. Operator Recruitment Playbook

### The Operator Role

**Mission:** Execute the Trade. Take IonWave from $0 to $100K MRR in 12 months.

**What the Operator does:**
- Entity formation, supplier onboarding, storefront build (Weeks 1-4)
- First ad campaigns, first orders, first data (Weeks 5-8)
- Iterate on ads, optimize conversion, scale winners (Weeks 9-12)
- Ongoing operations: customer service, inventory, marketing, financial management

**Daily co-pilot:** Claude AI has the entire Trade in context. The Operator asks Claude about any operational question and gets answers backed by 41 TUPs.

### Operator Compensation (share transparently during recruitment)

| Component | Amount | Trigger |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Base cash** | $4,000/mo → $6K → $8K | Start → Winner Found → $100K MRR |
| **Equity** | 15% (4yr vest, 1yr cliff) | On contract signing |
| **Bonus: First Blood** | $5,000 | 100 customers, CAC < 2x target |
| **Bonus: Winner Found** | $10,000 | CAC at target, 3+ winning ads, positive unit economics |
| **Bonus: $100K MRR** | $25,000 | Sustainable profitable revenue |
| **Health stipend** | $500/mo | At Winner Found |

**Equity value scenarios** (share with candidates):

| Scenario | Annual Revenue | Valuation | 15% Equity Value |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Conservative | $500K ARR | $1M | $150K |
| Base case | $1.2M ARR | $3.6M | $540K |
| Optimistic | $3M ARR | $12M | $1.8M |

### Operator Profile

**Must-have:**

| Characteristic | Why | Evidence |
|---------------|-----|----------|
| **Execution discipline** | Running a business from a playbook. Must execute consistently. | Previous operational role with measurable output. |
| **D2C / ecommerce familiarity** | Core business is online D2C. | Has run a Shopify store, managed ad campaigns, or equivalent. |
| **Self-directed** | No manager. Claude assists, but the Operator makes daily decisions. | Entrepreneurial experience or autonomous roles. |
| **Data comfort** | Must read dashboards, interpret CAC/LTV/ROAS. | Can explain metrics from previous work. |
| **Communication clarity** | Daily standups, weekly reports, investor updates. | Writing sample quality. |

**Red flags:**
- "I have ideas about how to change the strategy" — wants to redesign, not execute
- Cannot explain metrics from previous work — data illiteracy
- Focused entirely on equity value without understanding the work
- No previous autonomous work experience

### Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Who You'll Find | Approach |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **D2C founder communities** | Former brand operators | Twitter/X, EcomCrew, D2C Slack/Discord |
| **Upwork** | Experienced ecommerce operators | Search "D2C operator", "Shopify brand manager" |
| **Referrals** | Warm candidates | Ask 5 D2C people: "Who's the best operator you've worked with?" |
| **AngelList/Wellfound** | Startup operators seeking equity roles | Post role or search profiles |
| **LinkedIn** | Broader pool | Targeted search with personalized outreach |

### Evaluation Framework

**Stage 1: Initial Screen (30 min)**
1. "Walk me through the last business or project you operated. What were you responsible for?"
2. "What metrics did you track daily?"
3. "If I gave you a Shopify store, a product, and $5K in ad budget, what would you do in the first week?"
4. "What's your understanding of IonWave?"

**Pass to Stage 2 if:** Concrete operational experience, data fluency, self-directed, genuine interest.

**Stage 2: Deep Evaluation (60 min + Trade review)**
After giving them 1 week with Trade materials (M0, M1, M35):
1. "Explain IonWave's go-to-market strategy in your own words."
2. "Walk me through your first 30 days as IonWave Operator."
3. "COGS comes in at $0.65 instead of $0.60. What do you do?" (Kill criteria scenario)
4. "What CAC would make you worried at Week 4? At Week 8?"
5. "Write a mock daily standup report for a day where one thing went wrong."

**Stage 3: Reference Check (2 references)**
1. "How did they handle unexpected problems?"
2. "Were they self-directed or did they need frequent direction?"
3. "How was their communication?"
4. "Would you work with them again?"

### M1 Certification (after Operator is selected)

The Operator completes a 3-week reading plan before receiving live system access:
- **Week 1:** Business Context (M0, M2, M5)
- **Week 2:** Operational Systems (M9, M24, M30)
- **Week 3:** Execution Plans (M35, M36)

6-item certification checklist — full detail in M1 operator_system.md.

## 6. Escalation Guide

### Green — Handle autonomously
- All investor outreach and meetings
- All operator sourcing and screening
- Pipeline tracker updates
- Monthly investor updates
- Scheduling, follow-ups, reference checks

### Yellow — Check with Nilo first
- Investor asks about specific equity mechanics beyond the SAFE structure
- Operator candidate wants to negotiate terms outside the documented compensation framework
- You want to change the fundraising instrument (e.g., from SAFE to convertible note)
- Zero qualified operator candidates after 6 weeks
- A candidate wants to talk directly to the founder

### Red — Flag immediately (same day)
- An investor commits — paper the SAFE promptly
- An investor or candidate raises a legal or regulatory concern
- You discover material information that changes the Trade's viability
- Zero investor meetings after 4 weeks of outreach
- An operator candidate you've shortlisted reveals a disqualifying issue
- Any external press or sensitive inquiry about IonWave

### Escalation format

> **ESCALATION:**
> - **What:** [Brief description]
> - **Why:** [Why you can't handle it]
> - **My recommendation:** [What you think should happen]
> - **Urgency:** [Same day / 48 hrs / When convenient]
