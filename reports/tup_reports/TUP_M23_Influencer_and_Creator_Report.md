- Weekly scan of tagged/hashtagged posts mentioning IonWave
- Tools: Brand24 or Mention.com ($49-99/mo) OR manual Instagram/TikTok search (free)

2. **Assess**: Is it a minor wording issue or a material health claim?
3. **Contact**: DM/email within 4 hours requesting edit or takedown
4. **Escalate**: If no response in 24hrs, invoke contract termination clause
5. **Document**: Log every incident for legal record
6. **Learn**: Update talking points card if wording was ambiguous

**This package is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Do not seed product, sign contracts, or engage partners until all 4 components exist.** The cost of one FTC enforcement action ($10K+ minimum, plus brand damage) far exceeds the cost of building this package.

## 1. The UGC Creator Model

UGC Creators = people who create content for you to run as ads. They are NOT posting to their audience. You are BUYING their content to use in YOUR ads.

**Why UGC works for IonWave:**
- Authentic feel (not polished studio ads — supplement buyers are skeptical of overproduced content)
- Diverse faces and styles (test which demographics resonate)
- Scalable content production (3-5 creators = 10-20 videos/month)
- Lower cost than traditional production ($50-300/video vs $2,000+ for studio)
- Pairs with M14's testing framework — each UGC video is a creative test

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tab 502; industry UGC adoption rates for DTC supplements | Date: 2026-02-08]

## 3. Creator Brief Essentials

**Every UGC brief MUST include these 7 elements:**

### 1. Product Information
- What is IonWave? Marine plasma mineral supplement
- Key benefits: 78 bioavailable minerals from deep-ocean water, not lab-synthesized electrolytes
- Differentiator: Natural ocean-sourced vs. manufactured (LMNT, Liquid IV)
- [VOID — requires: Approved product claim language from M7 Regulatory. DO NOT let creators make health claims without legal review.]

### 2. Target Audience
- Who should this speak to? Specify per video:
  - Health-conscious 28-45, active lifestyle
  - Keto/carnivore community (electrolyte-aware)
  - Athletes/CrossFitters (performance angle)
  - Biohackers (mineral optimization angle)

### 3. Video Specifications
- **Length**: 30-60 seconds (15s for hooks-only cuts)
- **Format**: Vertical 9:16
- **Resolution**: 1080x1920 minimum
- **Delivery**: Raw .mp4 files (no editing — we edit for different cuts)

### 4. Hook Options
- Give creators 2-3 hooks to try per video
- Cross-reference M14 (Testing) hook library when available
- Example hooks for IonWave:
  - Problem: "I used to feel exhausted by 2pm every day..."
  - Curiosity: "I just discovered what's actually in my electrolyte mix..."
  - Social proof: "My nutritionist told me about this..."
  - Contrast: "I switched from [generic electrolyte] and here's what happened..."

### 5. Key Talking Points
- Must mention: [VOID — requires brand-approved talking points from M8]
- Natural ocean-sourced minerals (78 trace minerals)
- Not lab-synthesized
- How to use (mixing, routine)

### 6. Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Be authentic and natural (not scripted-sounding)
- Show the actual product (unboxing, mixing, drinking)
- Share genuine reactions
- Film in natural lighting
- Include captions/text overlay if comfortable

**DON'T:**
- Make specific health claims ("cures", "treats", "prevents" anything)
- Compare to competitors by name (legal risk)
- Use before/after claims without disclaimer
- Film in a bathroom or messy setting
- Over-produce — authenticity > production value

[Confidence: A | Source: FTC guidelines for supplement endorsements; brand safety best practices | Date: 2026-02-08]

### 7. Reference Videos
- Include 2-3 examples of what good UGC looks like
- [VOID — requires: Build a reference library from first 5 approved UGC videos. Until then, use competitor UGC examples found on TikTok/Instagram for LMNT, AG1.]

## 4. Content Repurposing System

**One piece of UGC content should become MANY assets.**

### From a Single 60-Second UGC Video:

| Derivative | Format | Use Case |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Full 60s video | Video ad | Facebook/Instagram/TikTok ads |
| 30s cut | Video ad | Instagram Reels, TikTok |
| 15s hook-only cut | Video ad | Story ads, pre-roll |
| 3-5 different hooks on same body | Video ad variations | A/B testing hooks (M14 framework) |
| Static screenshots | Image ad | Facebook/Instagram feed |
| Testimonial quotes | Text + image | Website, email social proof |
| Email content | Embedded GIF/image | Post-purchase sequence (M17), welcome series |
| Website content | Embedded video | Product page, testimonials page |
| Retargeting creative | Video ad | Cart abandonment retargeting |

**Multiplier effect (U6 — Dialogue Upgrade, revised)**:
- **Primary derivatives** (reliable quality): 4-6 assets — full video, 30s cut, 15s hook, 2-3 hook swaps on same body. All video, all usable.
- **Secondary derivatives** (quality varies): 2-4 assets — static screenshots, quote pulls, email embeds, website embeds. May or may not be usable depending on source video quality.
- **Realistic total**: 1 UGC video → 6-10 usable assets. At $100-150/video, effective cost per asset = $10-25.

[Confidence: B | Source: Industry repurposing framework, revised downward per persona dialogue Round 6 | Date: 2026-02-08]

### Repurposing Checklist (Per Content Piece)

**Immediate (within 24hrs of approval):**
- [ ] **Compliance check**: Does this content contain any claims NOT in the approved talking points? If yes, flag and do not deploy. (U6 — Dialogue Upgrade)
- [ ] Create 60s, 30s, 15s cuts
- [ ] Test 3-5 different hooks on same body
- [ ] Extract 2-3 stills for static ads
- [ ] Pull quotes for social proof library
- [ ] **Compliance check on derivatives**: Does any derivative (quote, still, cut) contain unapproved claims when taken out of context? If yes, do not use that derivative.

**Within 1 week:**
- [ ] Add to testimonial library (website)
- [ ] Consider for email content (M17 flows)
- [ ] Set up retargeting variant

**For Winners (>2x ROAS target):**
- [ ] Brief 2 new creators with same hook/angle but different person
- [ ] Create variations (different person, same script)
- [ ] Test on additional platforms
- [ ] Add to "winning hooks" reference library for future briefs
- [ ] Increase ad spend on this creative
- [ ] Document what worked (hook type, angle, creator style) in creative log

## Round Log

### Round 1: Attribution Gap
- **Challenge (Skeptical Investor)**: Break-even math on hybrid deals requires 13 sales per influencer. Show the math works for micro-influencers with 10-50K followers.
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Single-post ROI is poor. The real value is the 90-day affiliate code window and compounding reach across Stories, Reels, link-in-bio.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Both miss that affiliate attribution is fragile — many customers Google the brand instead of using codes. Need to track both direct (code) and indirect (branded search lift) attribution.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U1)** — Added attribution framework to pipeline_operations.md: direct (code) + indirect (search lift, direct traffic) measurement. Decision rule for borderline ROAS partnerships.

### Round 2: FTC Compliance Package
- **Challenge (Compliance Auditor)**: Contract templates say [VOID — requires M7] but you're about to put product in 30-50 people's hands who may make prohibited health claims.
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: For gifted product, FTC requirements are lighter but still present. Need pre-seeding compliance package.
- **Synthesis (Skeptical Investor)**: Cost of one regulatory action >> cost of compliance. Need 4 components: talking points card, prohibited claims list, monitoring plan, violation response protocol. GATING requirement.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U2)** — Added Pre-Launch Compliance Package to contract_templates.md. 4-component package. NON-NEGOTIABLE gating requirement before any seeding.

### Round 3: Phase 0 vs Phase 1 Confusion
- **Challenge (Growth Engineer)**: Strategy says "Phase 1: no influencer spend" but map says "Phase 0: seed 20-30 nanos." Contradiction?
- **Response (Skeptical Investor)**: Product seeding ≠ influencer spend. Seeding = product COGS only. Phase 1 = paid UGC creators (which IS the ad creative budget). Phase 2 = paid influencer deals (incremental).
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Product seeding still creates FTC material connection. Compliance package must ship with every seeded product.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U3)** — Clarified Phase 0/1/2 distinction in influencer_creator_strategy.md. Added Unit Economics Validation Gate for Phase 2 entry (UGC ads >1.5x ROAS).

### Round 4: Perpetual UGC Rights Risk
- **Challenge (Skeptical Investor)**: Full perpetual rights for UGC — what about brand evolution, problematic creators, outdated content?
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Perpetual is industry standard for work-for-hire UGC. Risk of NOT having perpetual rights (losing a $50K/mo winning ad) > risk of perpetuity.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Keep perpetual rights but add voluntary content retirement policy: semi-annual audit, retire problematic content, archive inactive.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U4)** — Added content retirement policy to pipeline_operations.md: semi-annual UGC audit in monthly review table.

### Round 5: Tier 2 Target Validation
- **Challenge (Compliance Auditor)**: Named Tier 2 targets (DeLauer, Berry) might have current competitor deals. List could waste months of outreach.
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Add pre-outreach competitive screening: check recent sponsorships, competitor affiliate pages, exclusivity status.
- **Synthesis (Skeptical Investor)**: 15-minute screen per target before outreach. Check #ad tags, competitor affiliate pages, ambassador bios. Don't waste outreach on locked targets.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U5)** — Added pre-outreach competitive screening step to influencer_map.md with 4-point checklist.

### Round 6: Repurposing Multiplier Overstatement
- **Challenge (Growth Engineer)**: 1 video → 8-12 assets claimed, but static screenshots from video look terrible vs purpose-shot images. Multiplier overstated.
- **Response (Skeptical Investor)**: More honest: 4-6 primary derivatives (video cuts + hook swaps), 2-4 secondary (mixed quality). Still good economics.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Additionally, every derivative needs compliance check — quotes pulled from video might contain unapproved claims.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U6)** — Revised multiplier to 6-10 (from 8-12) in creator_ugc_playbook.md. Added compliance checkpoint to repurposing checklist.

## 1. The Core Distinction

Two different models. Same person can play both roles, but the **deal structure, purpose, and economics are different**.

| Dimension | Influencer | Creator (UGC) |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Primary purpose** | Posts to THEIR audience — renting reach | Creates content for YOU to run as ads — buying assets |
| **Where content lives** | Their social channels | Your ad accounts, website, emails |
| **Who sees it** | Their followers | Your targeting (paid media) |
| **Main value** | Audience trust + credibility | Authentic-looking content at scale |
| **Payment model** | Flat fee + affiliate % (hybrid recommended) | Per video/asset fee |
| **Typical cost** | $50-$10K+ per post (tier-dependent) | $50-$300 per video |
| **Usage rights** | Usually organic only (paid amplification costs extra) | Full ad rights (in perpetuity recommended) |
| **Scalability** | Limited by their audience size | Unlimited with ad spend |
| **Control** | Low — their creative style | High — follows your brief |
| **Best for** | Awareness, credibility, niche targeting | Conversion, ad testing, creative volume |

## 2. When to Use Each

### Use Influencers When:
- Building brand awareness in a specific niche community
- Need credibility/social proof from a trusted voice
- Want to reach a specific audience you can't target with ads alone
- Launching a product and want authentic endorsement
- Have budget for organic reach (minimum viable: $500-2,000/month)

### Use Creators (UGC) When:
- Need high volume of ad creative for testing hooks, angles, formats
- Want authentic-looking ads (not polished studio production)
- Optimizing for conversion (ROAS-driven)
- Need diverse faces/demographics in ads
- Budget is limited — cheaper per asset than influencer posts
- You control the message exactly via briefs

### Combined Strategy (Recommended for IonWave)
Best approach: **Use BOTH strategically**, phased by maturity:

**Phase 1 (Month 1-3): Creator-first**
- 3-5 UGC creators making 10-20 videos/month [Confidence: C | Source: Danilo tab 508 recommendation, scaled down for Founder Mode | Date: 2026-02-08]
- Test hooks, angles, formats via paid ads
- Scale winners with ad spend
- Budget: ~$1,500-3,000/month for content + ad spend separate

**Phase 2 (Month 3-6): Add Influencers**
- Start with product seeding to 20-30 nano/micro-influencers (gifted only)
- Sign 2-3 micro-influencers on hybrid deals ($100-250 flat + 15% affiliate)
- Repurpose influencer content as social proof on site and in emails
- Budget: ~$500-1,500/month for influencer deals + product cost

**Phase 3 (Month 6+): Scale Both**
- 5-10 creators on ongoing contracts
- 5-10 active influencer partnerships
- Move successful nanos/micros to paid hybrid deals
- Test mid-tier influencers ($1K-5K) if proven ROI from micro tier
- Consider influencer-to-creator hybrid: pay for content + usage rights

[Confidence: C | Source: Danilo's combined strategy recommendation (tab 508), adapted for IonWave's Founder Mode phasing | Date: 2026-02-08]

## 4. Deal Structures

### 1. Gifted Only (Product Seeding)
- **What**: Free product, no cash payment, no guaranteed post
- **Cost**: Product COGS only (~$18.85-$37.70 at 40% gross margin on $47.14 unit)
- **Best for**: Nano influencers, initial seeding, building relationships
- **Expected response rate**: ~5-15% will post organically [Confidence: D | Source: Industry benchmark, highly variable | Date: 2026-02-08]
- **IonWave recommendation**: Seed 30-50 nanos in Phase 2. Cost: ~$600-$1,900 in product. Even 5 organic posts = $120-$380 per authentic post.

### 2. Flat Fee
- **What**: Fixed payment per post/deliverable
- **Cost**: Tier-dependent ($50-$5,000+)
- **Best for**: One-off campaigns, guaranteed deliverables
- **Risk**: No performance alignment — they get paid regardless of results

### 3. Affiliate Only
- **What**: Commission on sales (15-30%), no upfront payment
- **Cost**: Variable — only pay on results
- **Best for**: Performance-focused, budget-friendly
- **Risk**: Good influencers won't work on pure affiliate (they know their value)
- **IonWave affiliate rate**: 15-20% recommended. At $47.14 unit price, that's $7.07-$9.43 per sale. With ~40% gross margin ($18.86 gross profit), affiliate commission is 37-50% of gross profit — manageable if CAC < $18 target. [Confidence: B | Source: Cross-referenced with M25 Financial Operations margin data | Date: 2026-02-08]

### 4. Hybrid (RECOMMENDED for IonWave)
- **What**: Smaller flat fee + affiliate percentage
- **Cost**: $100-250 flat + 15% commission (for micro tier)
- **Best for**: Ongoing partnerships, aligns incentives
- **Why it's best**: Influencer gets baseline payment (respects their work), plus upside if it converts. Brand gets performance alignment.
- **IonWave example deal**: $150 flat + 15% affiliate + free product. Break-even at 2 sales through their code. Anything beyond = profit.
  - Math: $150 flat + $18.85 product cost = $168.85 total. Each sale through affiliate: $47.14 revenue - $28.28 COGS - $7.07 affiliate = $11.79 net. Break-even: $150 / $11.79 = ~13 sales. [Confidence: B | Source: Calculated from M25 financial model unit economics | Date: 2026-02-08]

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tabs 501, 509; unit economics from M25 | Date: 2026-02-08]

| **Phase 2** (Month 3-6) | Growth | Add paid influencer deals | $2,000-4,000 | Cash (incremental) | 2-3 hybrid deals ($150 flat + 15%). **Gate: only start after UGC ads achieve >1.5x ROAS consistently.** |

**Key distinction (U3 — Dialogue Upgrade):**
- **Phase 0 is NOT "influencer spend"** — it's product seeding (COGS only). No cash changes hands.

- **Phase 2 influencer spend IS incremental** — only start after proving unit economics through UGC ads. **Unit Economics Validation Gate**: UGC ads must achieve >1.5x ROAS for at least 2 consecutive weeks before investing cash in influencer deals.

**Phase 0 compliance note**: Product seeding creates a material connection under FTC guidelines even without cash payment. The Pre-Launch Compliance Package (see contract_templates.md) MUST ship with every seeded product.

## Component 1: Instruction Document

### How to Build an Influencer & Creator Program (14 Steps)

**Phase 0: Foundation (Before Any Outreach)**
1. **Define the distinction**: Influencers post to their audience (awareness). Creators make content for your ads (conversion). Same person can do both, but the deal structure differs.
2. **Build compliance package**: Before touching anyone — approved talking points, prohibited claims, monitoring plan, violation response protocol. NON-NEGOTIABLE for regulated categories (supplements, health, finance).
3. **Set up tracking**: Unique affiliate/promo codes per partner. Attribution tracking for both direct (code use) and indirect (branded search lift). Set up Google Search Console branded query baseline.

**Phase 1: Creator Program (Month 1-3)**
4. **Source 3-5 UGC creators**: Start with platforms (Billo, Insense) or DIY sourcing (Instagram DMs to micro-creators).
5. **Brief creators**: 7-element brief — product info, target audience, video specs, hook options, talking points, do's/don'ts, reference videos.
6. **Manage production cycle**: Brief → Delivery (7 days) → Review (48hrs) → Revisions (max 2) → Approve → Deploy to ads.
7. **Repurpose every piece**: Each video → 4-6 primary derivatives (cuts + hook swaps) + 2-4 secondary (stills, quotes). Compliance check every derivative.

**Phase 2: Influencer Seeding (Month 2-4)**
8. **Build target list**: Map your influencer landscape in 3 tiers — Dream (aspirational), Achievable (Month 6+), Micro (start here).
9. **Vet candidates**: Weighted scorecard (7 criteria, minimum 3.5/5.0). Check red flags. Pre-outreach competitive screen for mid-tier+ targets.
10. **Seed product**: Send to 30-50 nano/micro-influencers. Include compliance package. No cash payment. Track who posts organically.

**Phase 3: Paid Partnerships (Month 3-6)**
11. **Sign hybrid deals**: Small flat fee + affiliate commission. Only after UGC ads prove unit economics (>1.5x ROAS gate).
12. **Execute contracts**: Use contract templates. Legal review required. 90-day content live minimum. FTC disclosure mandatory.

**Phase 4: Scale & Optimize (Month 6+)**
13. **Monthly review**: Pipeline conversion, top/under performers, budget vs results, creative fatigue, competitive moves, content retirement audit.
14. **Scale triggers**: >10 partnerships → consider content coordinator. >$50K/mo revenue → consider influencer platform (Grin, AspireIQ). Bridge top performers to ambassador program.

### Attribution Framework (U1 — Dialogue Upgrade)

**Critical**: Influencer ROAS based solely on affiliate code redemptions **systematically undercounts** influencer value. Many customers see the influencer post, then Google the brand directly instead of using the code.

**Track both attribution types:**

| Attribution Type | How to Measure | Weight in ROI Calc |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Direct (code/link)** | Unique promo code redemptions, UTM-tagged link clicks | Primary — directly attributable |
| **Indirect (influence)** | Branded search lift (GSC), direct traffic spike (GA4), social mentions spike within 7 days of campaign | Supplementary — correlated, not proven |

**Decision rule**: If code-tracked ROAS is 1.0-1.5x BUT branded search lifted >15% during campaign period, the partnership is likely profitable. Don't kill it based on code ROAS alone. Run for 2 more campaigns before final judgment.

## 1. Weighted Vetting Scorecard

Score every potential influencer partner before outreach. Minimum score of **3.5/5.0** to proceed with outreach. Score of **4.0+** qualifies for paid partnership.

| # | Criteria | Weight | Score (1-5) | Scoring Guide |
