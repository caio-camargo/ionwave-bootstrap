Unlike synthetic electrolytes, ocean-sourced minerals carry inherent risks: heavy metal contamination (mercury, lead, arsenic, cadmium), microbiological contamination, and environmental pollutants. Third-party testing is not optional - it is a regulatory and brand trust requirement. Every batch must be tested. COA (Certificate of Analysis) publication builds trust and differentiates from competitors who make purity claims without evidence.
  - **Testing Requirements:**
    - 
        **Test:** Heavy metals panel
      - **What It Covers:** Lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium
      - **Frequency:** Every batch
      - **Lab Type:** ISO 17025 accredited
      - **Estimated Cost:** $150-300 per panel
      - **Turnaround:** 5-7 business days
    - 
        **Test:** Microbiological testing
      - **What It Covers:** Total plate count, yeast, mold, E.coli, Salmonella, S.aureus, coliforms
      - **Frequency:** Every batch
      - **Lab Type:** ISO 17025 accredited
      - **Estimated Cost:** $200-400 per panel
      - **Turnaround:** 7-10 business days
    - 
        **Test:** Full mineral analysis
      - **What It Covers:** Complete mineral profile verification (all 78+ minerals claimed)
      - **Frequency:** Every batch
      - **Lab Type:** ICP-MS capable lab
      - **Estimated Cost:** $300-500 per analysis
      - **Turnaround:** 7-14 business days
    - 
        **Test:** Pesticide screen
      - **What It Covers:** Common pesticide residues
      - **Frequency:** Quarterly or per new supplier lot
      - **Lab Type:** ISO 17025 accredited
      - **Estimated Cost:** $200-350 per screen

    - 
        **Test:** Allergen testing
      - **What It Covers:** Shellfish, fish allergen cross-contamination (critical for ocean-sourced)
