## 1. Amazon: The Strategic Decision

### Opportunity vs Risk

| Opportunity | Risk |
|-------------|------|
| 300M+ active customers | Lower margins (30-40% fees) |
| Customers already searching for electrolytes | Don't own the customer relationship |
| Prime shipping = conversion boost | Race to bottom on price |
| Capture comparison shoppers ("LMNT alternative") | Amazon can copy successful products (Basics line) |
| — | Reviews can make or break you |

### Core Principle

> **Amazon should be INCREMENTAL, not primary.**
> Don't launch on Amazon until D2C works. Amazon is a volume play with lower margin.
> Use it for customers who would never buy D2C, not to replace D2C.

### Price Parity & Anti-Cannibalization Strategy *(Dialogue Upgrade U1)*

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Amazon price ≥ D2C price | Never undercut your own website on Amazon |
| D2C subscription discount > Amazon S&S | D2C subscription = 20% off MSRP ($39.20). Amazon S&S = 15% off ($41.65). D2C always wins |
| D2C-exclusive bundles | Offer bundles on D2C that are NOT available on Amazon |
| Amazon-to-D2C bridge | Insert card in Amazon shipments: "Get 20% off your next order at ionwave.com" |
| Cannibalization monitoring | Track D2C subscriber churn rate monthly post-Amazon launch. If D2C churn spikes >3 percentage points within 60 days of Amazon launch, investigate immediately |

**Pricing Hierarchy (Must Maintain)**:
1. D2C Subscription = BEST deal ($39.20 = 20% off)
2. D2C One-time = MSRP ($49.00)
3. Amazon S&S = 15% off ($41.65)
4. Amazon One-time = MSRP ($49.00)

### When to Launch on Amazon

All must be TRUE before proceeding:

- [ ] D2C profitable and growing (at least $50K MRR)
- [ ] Strong review base on own site (can seed Amazon reviews from existing customers)
- [ ] Unit economics work at Amazon margins (~30-40% fees)
- [ ] Inventory/supply chain can handle two channels simultaneously
- [ ] Brand Registry completed (requires active trademark)
- [ ] NOT because D2C is struggling

## 4. Listing Optimization Playbook

### Process

1. Identify top 3 competitors with best conversion on Amazon
2. Screenshot and document their entire listing
3. Extract: headlines, bullet points, descriptions, image strategy
4. Rewrite each element with IonWave product/brand positioning
5. A/B test variations (Amazon Experiments tool)
6. Iterate based on performance data

### Elements to Optimize

| Element | Best Practice | Who to Study | IonWave Angle |
|---------|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| Hero image | Clean, product-focused, lifestyle context | Seed, AG1 | Marine/ocean blue aesthetic, clean pack shot |
| Headline | Benefit-first, keyword-rich, <200 chars | LMNT | "78 Ocean Minerals • Complete Hydration • Zero Sugar" |
| Bullet points | Benefits > features, social proof, scannable | Ritual | Lead with mineral count, ocean source story |
| Description | Story + benefits + trust signals | Athletic Greens | Founder story, science backing, comparison to 3-mineral products |
| Social proof | Reviews, press mentions, certifications | Seed | Subscriber count, D2C review highlights |
| Price display | Subscription savings prominent | LMNT | Subscribe & Save discount visible |
| A+ Content | Brand story + comparison charts + FAQ | Liquid IV | 78 vs 3 mineral comparison, ocean sourcing infographic |

### Title Formula

```
[Brand] [Product Type] [Key Benefit] - [Key Differentiator] - [Format] - [Count]

Example:
IonWave Marine Electrolytes for Complete Hydration - 78 Ocean Minerals - Powder Stick Packs - 30 Count
```

### Bullet Point Framework (5 bullets)

1. **Primary benefit**: What it does for the customer
2. **Key differentiator**: 78 minerals vs 3 (LMNT, Liquid IV)
3. **Ingredient story**: Ocean-sourced, no artificial anything
4. **Usage**: How/when to use, serving suggestion
5. **Trust signal**: Satisfaction guarantee, subscriber count, tested/certified

## 5. Review Generation Protocol

### Target: 50 Reviews in 60 Days

Why: Social proof unlocks conversion. Below 50 reviews = low trust on Amazon. First 50 reviews are the hardest and most important.

### Compliant Methods (Amazon TOS Safe)

| Method | Timing | Expected Response Rate | Notes |
|--------|--------|----------------------|-------|
| Post-purchase email | Day 14 after delivery | 10-15% | "If you love IonWave, we'd appreciate a review" |
| SMS request | Day 21 after delivery | 5-10% | Quick text, link directly to review page |
| Package insert card | In every box | 2-5% | Physical card asking for honest review |
| Follow-up sequence | Day 30 (non-responders) | +3-5% | Second gentle ask |
| Amazon "Request a Review" button | Day 7-21 | 1-3% | Built-in Amazon tool, one-click |

### What NOT to Do (Amazon TOS Violations)

| ❌ Violation | Consequence |
|-------------|-------------|
| Offer incentives for reviews (discount, free product) | Account suspension |
| Buy fake reviews | Account termination, legal action |
| Ask for 5-star reviews specifically | TOS violation |
| Review gating (only sending happy customers to review) | TOS violation |
| Friends/family reviews | Detected and removed by Amazon |

### The Math

| Metric | Number |
|--------|--------|
| Reviews needed | 50 |
| Assume 10% review rate | — |
| Customers needed | 500 |
| Timeframe | 60 days |
| Orders per day | ~8 |
| At $49 AOV | $400/day revenue on Amazon |

**Implication**: To hit 50 reviews in 60 days, Amazon needs to be doing ~$12K/mo in revenue. This is achievable if D2C is driving awareness and Amazon captures overflow/comparison shoppers.

### Amazon Cold Start Strategy *(Dialogue Upgrade U6)*

The #1 challenge for a new Amazon listing: zero reviews, zero sales history, zero organic rank. PPC alone won't solve it.

| Phase | Timing | Strategy | Goal |
|-------|--------|----------|------|
| Phase 1: Seed from D2C | Week 1-2 | Email D2C customer list → "We're on Amazon! Leave a review" | 20-30 sales + 5-10 reviews |
| Phase 2: Long-tail PPC | Week 2-4 | Target keywords competitors ignore: "ocean mineral electrolyte", "78 mineral hydration" (NOT "electrolyte powder") | First page rank on low-competition terms |
| Phase 3: Vine Program | Week 4-8 | Enroll in Amazon Vine (official review program, ~$200/product) | 15-30 verified reviews |
| Phase 4: Broaden PPC | Week 8+ | Scale PPC to broader keywords as organic rank improves | Sustainable organic + paid traffic mix |

**Critical rule**: DO NOT run broad PPC on "electrolyte powder" until you have 50+ reviews and 3.5+ average stars. You'll burn money competing against LMNT's 50K+ reviews.

### B2B Reorder Automation *(Dialogue Upgrade U3)*

Gyms and small businesses are terrible at reordering — the owner forgets, runs out, switches to whatever rep shows up. Without automated reorder, B2B accounts churn at 60-70% within 6 months.

| Program Element | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| Auto-reorder default | Set standing monthly order with auto-ship. Account only needs to cancel/modify (default = continue) |
| Minimum viable version | Automated email reminder at Day 25 of 30-day cycle with one-click reorder link |
| Auto-reorder discount | 5% off for auto-reorder accounts vs ad-hoc ordering |
| Churn monitoring | Track B2B churn monthly. Target: <20% quarterly churn rate |
| Win-back trigger | If no order in 45 days (for monthly accounts): automated outreach + re-engagement offer |

### B2B Unit Economics *(Dialogue Upgrade U14)*

| Metric | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Acquisition cost** | $200 | 3 hrs sales time × $50/hr + $50 sample cost |
| **Monthly revenue per account** | $588 | 24 units × $24.50 wholesale |
| **Monthly COGS** | $288 | 24 units × $12 (est.) |
| **Monthly gross margin** | $300 | $588 - $288 |
| **Monthly service cost** | $25 | 30 min/month × $50/hr imputed |
| **Monthly net margin** | $275 | $300 - $25 |
| **Payback period** | 0.7 months (~3 weeks) | $200 / $275 |
| **Annual LTV (80% retention)** | $2,640 | $275 × 12 × 0.8 |
| **B2B LTV:CAC** | 13.2x | $2,640 / $200 |

**Verdict**: B2B economics are excellent if retention holds. The key lever is retention — auto-reorder program (above) is critical to maintaining the 80% annual retention assumption.

| Signal | Action | Condition |

## 4. Multi-Touch Attribution Model *(Dialogue Upgrade U2)*

### The Problem
When running Meta + TikTok + Influencer + Google simultaneously, how do you know which channel drives the sale? Supplement purchase cycles are 2-4 weeks — the touch that gets the click isn't always the touch that caused the decision.

### Day 1 Attribution Stack (Simple, Sufficient)

| Method | What It Captures | Trust Level |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Post-purchase survey: "How did you hear about us?" | Self-reported first touch | HIGH for strategic decisions |
| UTM tagging on all links | Last-click digital attribution | HIGH for tactical optimization |
| Unique discount codes per channel | Channel-specific conversion | HIGH for influencer/podcast |
| Branded search volume trend | Brand awareness proxy | MEDIUM — directional only |

### Decision Rules

| Scenario | Which to Trust | Why |
|----------|---------------|-----|
| Self-reported and UTM agree | Both — high confidence | Signals align |
| Self-reported says "podcast" but UTM says "Google" | Trust self-reported for STRATEGY | They heard about you on podcast, then Googled you to buy. Podcast gets strategic credit |
| UTM says "Meta" but no self-reported signal | Trust UTM for TACTICS | Use for ad optimization, bid adjustments |

### When to Upgrade Attribution
Do NOT build sophisticated attribution (MTA, MMM, Rockerbox, Northbeam) until >$50K/mo total ad spend. Below that threshold, simple methods above are sufficient and more reliable for small sample sizes.

### Recommended Sequence

```
STEP 1: AMAZON ←←← First expansion after D2C PMF
  Why first: Large audience, manageable complexity, manageable margin hit
  Key rule: Don't cannibalize D2C — use for INCREMENTAL reach
  Gate: D2C profitable at $50K+ MRR, unit economics work at Amazon margins

STEP 2: WHOLESALE / B2B ←←← Parallel with Amazon
  Why now: Low complexity, reasonable margins, recurring revenue
  Targets: Gyms, CrossFit boxes, wellness offices, clinics
  Gate: Brand has credibility (reviews, social proof), production capacity

STEP 3: LINE EXTENSIONS ←←← 6-12 months post-launch
  Why now: Increase LTV from existing customers

  Gate: Hero SKU LTV:CAC >3x for 60+ days, 5/7 readiness triggers met

STEP 4: RETAIL ←←← Only if brand is strong
  Why last: Margin hit (30-50%), complexity, slotting fees, slow payment terms
  Start with: Natural/specialty (Whole Foods, Sprouts), NOT big box
  Gate: $100K+ MRR D2C, retail-ready packaging, cash reserves for slotting

STEP 5: INTERNATIONAL ←←← Only if US is humming
  Start with: UK, Canada, Australia (English-speaking, similar regulatory)
  Gate: $100K+ MRR US, regulatory homework complete, logistics partner identified
```

### Expansion Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | What to Do Instead |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Expand to "save" a failing D2C | New channels don't fix product-market fit | Fix D2C first, then expand |
| Launch Amazon before D2C PMF | Split focus, dilute resources, wrong learnings | Prove the model D2C first |
| Big box retail before small retail | Slotting fees crush you, velocity requirements unrealistic | Start local/regional natural |
| International before US proven | Regulatory complexity + logistics cost, without proven playbook | Dominate US first |
| Multiple expansions simultaneously | Operator bandwidth collapses | One expansion per quarter max |

### White Label Guardrails *(Dialogue Upgrade U11)*

White label is NOT "any time, low complexity." White labeling creates a competitor using your own formula. Without guardrails, a gym chain that sees your margins will go direct to your supplier.

| Guardrail | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Never white-label hero SKU** | Only derivative or simplified formulations |
| **Minimum volume** | 1,000 units/order minimum (not worth the risk for small orders) |
| **Exclusive territory** | White label partner cannot sell within your primary sales channels (online D2C, Amazon) |
| **Annual review clause** | Termination right if partner develops competing brand |
| **Pricing** | Cost-plus only (COGS + 20-30%), never wholesale pricing |
| **Gate** | Only after hero brand established ($100K+ MRR). Before that, focus on building your own brand |
| **Complexity reclassification** | Medium (not Low) — legal, quality control, brand risk require management |

## 1. Taste Profile Documentation

### Why Taste Matters

Taste is the **#1 reason for churn in supplements**. The IonWave challenge: ocean mineral concentrate has an inherently mineral/salty taste. This is a feature, not a bug — but only if expectations are set correctly.

**The Rule**: Set ACCURATE expectations, then exceed them. Never oversell taste.

### Taste Profile by Flavor

| Flavor | Primary Taste | Secondary Notes | Aftertaste | Best Comparison |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|------------|-----------------|
| Citrus Lime | Tart citrus | Mild saltiness | Clean, slight mineral | Like unsweetened lime sparkling water |
| Unflavored | Salty | Mineral/ocean | Mineral | Like mild ocean water |
| [Future flavor] | TBD | TBD | TBD | TBD |

### Taste Expectation Copy — What to Say

| ✅ GOOD Copy | ❌ BAD Copy | Why |
|-------------|------------|-----|
| "Refreshing citrus taste with a clean mineral finish" | "Tastes just like regular lemonade!" | Sets wrong expectation, guarantees disappointment |
| "Subtly salty — you'll know it's working" | "You won't even taste the salt!" | Lie — they will taste salt, and then they'll feel lied to |
| "Not overly sweet like sports drinks" | "Delicious!" | Vague, sets high bar, different for everyone |
| "It tastes functional. You'll taste the minerals. That's how you know it's real." | "Best-tasting electrolyte on the market!" | Claim you can't back up, invites comparison |

**The Golden Frame**: "It tastes functional. You'll taste the minerals. That's how you know it's real."

This reframes the taste as a signal of quality rather than a flaw. Turn the weakness into the proof point.
