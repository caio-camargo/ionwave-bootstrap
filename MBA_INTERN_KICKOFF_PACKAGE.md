## 1. Your Mission

You have one primary job: **find a VP-level deals/fundraising person** for the IonWave venture.

This is not a general internship. It's a focused, deliverable-driven engagement where you source, pitch, evaluate, and recommend a VP candidate who will lead Capital Formation (fundraising) and recruit the Operator.

**Your win condition:** VP candidate identified, evaluated, and recommended — scoring 3.5+ on the evaluation rubric, ready to sign the bilateral contract.

### What you own
- Understand the Trade well enough to pitch it credibly
- Research and build a VP target list (50+ names)
- Run personalized outreach — warm intros and cold approaches
- Screen VP candidates against the evaluation rubric
- Manage the candidate pipeline and submit biweekly reports
- Present a shortlist of 2-3 evaluated candidates
- Deliver a final recommendation with evidence

### What you don't touch
- Running the business (that's the Operator's job)
- Fundraising (that's the VP's job)
- Product, supply chain, marketing decisions (documented in the Trade)
- Managing the VA (their job is done — they found you)
- Finding the Operator (the VP will do that)

## 2. What You're Pitching

IonWave is a premium marine plasma electrolyte brand — a health supplement business designed for direct-to-consumer online sales.

What makes this different from a typical startup:

1. **Complete operating manual.** 41 structured business models (TUPs) covering every operational domain — from product formulation to paid advertising to financial operations. Each is quality-scored and confidence-graded.
2. **AI-augmented execution.** The entire system is designed for AI legibility — the operator works alongside Claude daily.
3. **De-risked validation.** Kill criteria are defined upfront. The business has clear go/no-go gates.
4. **Founder-independent.** The system is designed to work without the founder in the execution chain.

You have the **Trade Pitch One-Pager** for quick conversations and access to the **full Trade** (41 TUPs) for deep context. Read both.

## 4. VP Candidate Profile

### Who you're looking for

Think: **someone who makes deals happen**. They've either raised money, closed partnerships, recruited executives, or sold high-value professional services. They're comfortable in rooms with senior people. They can pitch, negotiate, and follow through.

**They are NOT:**
- A passive advisor who gives opinions but doesn't execute
- A consultant who writes reports but doesn't close deals
- A first-time professional who's "excited about fundraising"

### Must-have characteristics

| Characteristic | Why It Matters | Observable Evidence |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Credibility with senior people** | They'll pitch investors and recruit an operator | Track record of working with or selling to executives |
| **Deal-closing ability** | Capital Formation requires getting people from "interested" to "committed" | Can point to deals they personally closed — revenue, funds raised, partnerships landed |
| **Business model comprehension** | Must understand the Trade well enough to pitch it | Can read the Trade and articulate value proposition, unit economics, risk framework in own words |
| **Network or network-building ability** | Must reach investors and operator candidates | Previous roles requiring outbound relationship building |
| **Execution discipline** | Deliverables and deadlines — not just "networking" | Track record of managing pipelines, reporting progress |

### Nice-to-have

- D2C / CPG / supplement industry experience
- Startup fundraising experience (seed rounds)
- Comfort with AI-augmented workflows
- Entrepreneurial experience

### Disqualifiers

| Trait | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Cannot name a single deal they personally closed** | The role is fundamentally about closing |
| **Wants a passive/advisory role** | Active execution role with deliverables |
| **Demands large cash compensation pre-revenue** | Equity role — some retainer may be negotiable, but cash-dependent = misaligned |
| **Cannot commit 10+ hours/week** | Capital Formation and operator recruiting require sustained effort |
| **Cannot comprehend the Trade after adequate review time** | If they can't understand the business, they can't pitch it |

### VP Compensation (what you're offering them)

| Component | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| **Equity** | 5-10% [DEC-CHAIN-002 — exact amount TBD] |
| **Retainer** | Possible, amount TBD |
| **Upside** | If IonWave hits targets ($2M EBITDA at 24 months), 5-10% is substantial |

**How to pitch the economics:** The Trade documents 60% gross margins, a $30K MRR Year 1 target, and $2M EBITDA 24-month potential. This is not a moonshot — it's a de-risked bet with a complete playbook.

## 5. VP Screening Rubric

### How to use this

You're evaluating someone who is likely more experienced than you. This rubric gives you **structured, observable criteria** so you don't rely on impression. Apply it honestly. Document evidence. If uncertain, say so.

### The 6 Dimensions

#### Dimension 1: Deal-Closing Track Record (Weight: 25%)

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Multiple concrete deals they personally closed. Names amounts, counterparties, timelines. |
| **4** | 1-2 significant deals they led. Clear on their specific contribution. |
| **3** | Has deal experience but in supporting role. |
| **2** | Claims deal experience but vague on specifics. |
| **1** | No deal-closing evidence. |

**How to test:** "Walk me through two or three deals you personally closed. What was the context, your role, and the outcome?"

#### Dimension 2: Trade Comprehension (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | After reviewing Trade materials, articulates value prop, unit economics, risk framework, competitive positioning. Asks nuanced questions. |
| **4** | Good comprehension. Can pitch credibly. May miss some nuance. |
| **3** | Understands basics but oversimplifies. Couldn't defend under investor scrutiny. |
| **2** | Surface-level. Reads One-Pager but can't go deeper. |
| **1** | Cannot comprehend the business model even with full materials. |

**How to test:** Give them the One-Pager and 2-3 key TUPs. After a week: "Pitch me IonWave as if I'm an angel investor. Then tell me the three biggest risks."

#### Dimension 3: Network & Reach (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Active, relevant network. Can name 10+ people they'd reach out to. |
| **4** | Some relevant connections. Can identify 5-10 specific people. |
| **3** | Limited network but demonstrated ability to build from scratch. |
| **2** | No relevant network and vague about building one. |
| **1** | No network and no relationship-building evidence. |

**How to test:** "If you started next week, who are the first 10 people you'd reach out to for Capital Formation? Why?"

#### Dimension 4: Execution Discipline (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Track record of managing pipelines with structured output. Personal system for tracking. |
| **4** | Generally disciplined. Has managed projects with deliverables. |
| **3** | Some structure but inconsistent. |
| **2** | Little evidence of structured execution. |
| **1** | No evidence. |

**How to test:** "In your most recent deal-focused role, how did you track your pipeline? What was your weekly rhythm?"

#### Dimension 5: Capital Formation Strategy (Weight: 10%)

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Specific, actionable ideas for IonWave fundraising. Understands SAFEs, angel dynamics. |
| **4** | General fundraising knowledge. Reasonable approach. |
| **3** | Conceptual understanding. Basic plan. |
| **2** | Vague. "I'd reach out to investors" without strategy. |
| **1** | No understanding of fundraising mechanics. |

**How to test:** "What's your approach to raising $30-50K on a SAFE for a pre-revenue D2C brand?"

#### Dimension 6: Motivation & Commitment (Weight: 10%)

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Clearly excited about IonWave specifically. Asks deep questions. Commits 10+ hrs/week. |
| **4** | Interested and engaged. Realistic expectations. |
| **3** | Interested but noncommittal. Weighing many options. |
| **2** | Lukewarm. Motivated by equity upside without genuine interest. |
| **1** | Not genuinely committed. |

**How to test:** "What concerns do you have about this role?" (Genuine candidates have real concerns.)

## 6. Sourcing Methodology

### The Funnel

```
Research & targeting:  50+ potential candidates identified
Outreach:              30-40 personalized messages sent
Responses:             8-12 engaged (20-30% response rate)
Screening calls:       5-8 initial conversations
Deep evaluation:       3-4 rubric-scored evaluations
Shortlist:             2-3 candidates presented
Recommendation:        1 top candidate
```

### Where to Research

| Source | Who You'll Find |
|--------|----------------|
| **Angel investor networks** | People who invest in D2C/CPG and might want an operating role |
| **D2C founder communities** | Former founders who've raised money |
| **Management consulting alumni** | Post-consulting professionals looking for operating partner roles |
| **Corporate development professionals** | M&A/partnership people at CPG companies |
| **VC associates/principals** | VC-adjacent people looking for operating roles |
| **Executive recruiting firms** | Recruiters who place VP/C-level in D2C/CPG |

### Outreach Principles

1. **Personalize or don't bother.** VP-level people delete generic messages.
2. **Lead with the Trade's quality.** "41 business models, quality-scored" is differentiated.
3. **Be direct about what you're asking.** Don't be cagey.
4. **Respect their time.** Ask for 15 minutes, not an hour.
5. **Follow up once.** If no response after 5-7 days, one follow-up. Then move on.

### Example LinkedIn Message

> Hi [Name] — your work at [Company] building [specific thing] caught my attention. I'm sourcing for a VP-level role in a premium D2C health venture.
>
> What's unusual: the venture has a complete operating manual — 41 structured business models, quality-scored, with AI-augmented execution. It's not a pitch deck; it's a full system designed for execution.
>
> We're looking for someone who can lead Capital Formation (seed fundraising) and recruit an operating partner. Compensation is meaningful equity (5-10%).
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call to see if there's a fit?

### Example Warm Intro Email

> Subject: Introduction — VP role in D2C health venture
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out. I'm helping build the leadership team for IonWave — a premium marine plasma electrolyte brand with a uniquely comprehensive foundation.
>
> The VP role involves two parallel tracks: Capital Formation (seed fundraising, advisor network) and Operator recruitment. Compensation is significant equity.
>
> [Mutual connection] thought your background in [specific experience] might make this interesting. Would you have 15 minutes this week?

### Screening Process

**Initial Conversation (15-20 min):**
1. Brief intro — who you are, your role in the cascade (be transparent)
2. Quick pitch — One-Pager version of IonWave (2-3 minutes)
3. Their reaction — let them ask questions. Their questions tell you a lot.
4. Their background — "Tell me about your most relevant deal-closing experience"
5. Logistics — availability, compensation expectations, timeline fit

**Deep Evaluation (30-60 min + Trade review):**
After they've had 1 week with Trade materials:
1. Trade Comprehension Test — can they pitch IonWave back to you?
2. Deal History Deep-Dive — walk through 2-3 specific deals
3. Capital Formation Strategy — "How would you approach raising for IonWave?"
4. Full rubric scoring (Section 5)

### Volume Targets

| Period | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Weeks 2-3 | 15+ new outreach messages |
| Weeks 4-5 | 10+ new outreach + follow-ups. First screening conversations. |
| Weeks 6-8 | 5+ new outreach. Focus shifts to deep evaluation. |
| Weeks 9-12 | Shortlist refinement. Focused conversations. Negotiation. |

## 7. Pitch Materials Guide

### Staged Disclosure

Not every candidate sees everything at once. Match material to their engagement level.

**Stage 1 — First Contact:** Trade Pitch One-Pager only.
VP-level people don't read lengthy packages before a first conversation.

**Stage 2 — After Initial Conversation:** Selected TUPs.
Share M0 (Trade Thesis), M4 (Fundraising), M1 (Operator System), Chain Specification. Give them 1 week to review.

**Stage 3 — Serious Evaluation:** Full Trade access + VP Brief.
They're seriously considering the role. Full access lets them do their own due diligence.

### Handling Common VP Questions

| Question | How to Answer |
|----------|--------------|
| "Where's the founder?" | "The founder designed the system. The model is founder-independent by design." |
| "Why equity and not cash?" | "Pre-revenue. Cash goes to execution. The equity offer is significant because the founding team is small and the playbook is complete." |
| "What if the business doesn't work?" | "Kill criteria are built in. The first 8 weeks validate or kill. Your equity risk is bounded." |
| "Can I talk to the founder?" | "The founder is intentionally out of the execution chain. [Check with Nilo on whether/when that's available.] I can share the full Trade for direct evaluation." |
| "How much equity exactly?" | "5-10% range. Exact terms are part of negotiation. I can share the bilateral contract framework." |

### Pitch Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Lead with the Trade's quality — 41 TUPs is genuinely unusual
- Be honest about what's unproven — pre-revenue, first-edition system
- Emphasize the de-risking — kill criteria, phase gates, bounded commitment
- Let the materials do the heavy lifting — the Trade itself is the best pitch

**Don't:**
- Oversell — don't promise $2M EBITDA as certainty
- Hide the cascade model — be transparent that a VA found you and you're finding the VP
- Pitch without understanding the Trade — if you can't answer questions, credibility drops
- Rush — VP-level candidates take time to evaluate

## 9. Escalation Guide

### Green — Handle autonomously
- All VP candidate outreach and follow-ups
- Screening conversations (initial and deep evaluation)
- Pipeline tracker updates
- Biweekly reports
- Sharing Trade materials per the staged disclosure guide
- Asking Claude for help with any of the above

### Yellow — Check with Nilo first
- A VP candidate asks about specific equity mechanics or vesting terms you can't answer
- A VP candidate wants to talk directly to the founder
- You want to try an outreach channel not in the methodology
- Zero responses after 3 weeks of outreach
- A candidate asks you to modify the VP role scope or compensation structure

### Red — Flag immediately (same day)
- Zero qualified VP candidates after 8 weeks
- A shortlisted candidate reveals a disqualifying issue
- A candidate you've been working with drops out after deep evaluation
- Any external press, legal, or sensitive inquiry about IonWave
- You suspect a candidate is misrepresenting their experience or network
- All candidates refuse equity compensation (may indicate equity offer needs adjustment)

### Escalation format

> **ESCALATION:**
> - **What:** [Brief description]
> - **Why:** [Why you can't handle it with the tools you have]
> - **My recommendation:** [What you think should happen]
> - **Urgency:** [Same day / 48 hrs / When convenient]

## 13. A Note on Evaluating "Up"

You're evaluating someone who is likely more experienced and senior than you. This is the nature of the cascade — and it's the hardest structural challenge in the model.

**What helps:**
- The rubric gives you objective anchors. You don't need to be a VP to check whether someone can name specific deals they've closed.
- The Trade Comprehension test is concrete. Either they can articulate the business model or they can't.
- Claude has the full Trade context and can help you assess whether a candidate's statements are accurate.
- If you're genuinely uncertain about a dimension, **say so** in your report. Honest uncertainty is more valuable than false confidence.

**What doesn't help:**
- Don't be intimidated into inflating scores because someone is senior.
- Don't skip the rubric because "they're clearly good." Apply it consistently.
- Don't ignore red flags because everything else is strong. Document them.
