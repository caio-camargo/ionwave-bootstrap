Not suitable for retained executive search.

## 2. Compliance Manager (CM) Engagement

### 2.1 Role Summary

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Position | Compliance Manager |
| Type | Part-time, ongoing contract |
| Reports to | Studio 4 (Nilo Vicioso) |
| Function | Daily standards enforcement, accountability, escalation |

The CM assesses and grades. The CM does NOT have authority to intervene — that belongs to the Trustee (§3). The CM is the eyes and ears; the Trustee is the hands.

> **CRITICAL (U18):** CM must report DIRECTLY to Trustee or Studio (Nilo), never through the CoS. The CoS has equity incentives ($1-3% Studio equity + $2.5K bonus at $100K MRR) that compromise CM independence if CM reports through them. The CM's value depends entirely on uncompromised assessment.

### 2.2 Compensation

| Component | Amount | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Base retainer | $500/month per operator monitored | Scales with operator count |
| Example | 3 operators = $1,500/month | — |
| Payment | Monthly, first of month | — |
| Equity | **None** | Maintains independence — CM must not have financial upside in venture success |

**Market Context (2026):** $500/month per operator is viable only as a tech-enabled light-touch model (SaaS dashboard monitoring + 2-3 hours/month human review). Genuine portfolio monitoring with coaching requires $2-5K/month. The CM role as defined here is a metrics reviewer + escalation trigger, not a hands-on coach. This is appropriate — coaching is the Studio's job, monitoring is the CM's job.

### CM Capacity Model (U7)

| Operators Monitored | Quality Level | Time per Operator | Notes |
|---------------------|-------------|------------------|-------|
| 1-3 | Excellent | 45-60 min/day total | Deep daily review, proactive pattern detection |
| 4-8 | Good | 30-45 min/day total | Standard daily review, escalation-focused |
| 9-12 | Adequate | 20-30 min/day total | Dashboard-only review, reactive monitoring |
| 13+ | Degraded | <15 min/day total | Quality collapses — hire second CM |

**Maximum recommended**: 8-10 operators per CM at $500/month. Beyond 10, hire a second CM or increase per-operator fee to $750-1,000.

### CM Grading Rubric (U8)

| Grade | Definition | Criteria | Action |
|-------|-----------|---------|--------|
| **AAA** | Exceptional | All KPIs exceeded. Milestones ahead of schedule. Innovation beyond ODD. | Celebrate. Consider for multi-brand operator role. |
| **AA** | Strong | All KPIs met. On-track for milestones. Consistent execution. | Standard monitoring. |
| **A** | Good | Most KPIs met. Minor misses within tolerance. Responsive to feedback. | Standard monitoring. |
| **BBB** | Acceptable | KPIs mostly met but trending negative. Some missed deadlines. | Increased monitoring cadence. Weekly check-in. |
| **BB** | Below Average | Multiple KPIs missed. Behind on milestones. Slow response to feedback. | Performance improvement plan (2-week). Daily check-in. |
| **B** | Concerning | Significant KPI misses. Behind schedule. Resistance to feedback. | Escalate to Studio. Formal review meeting. |
| **CCC** | Poor | Most KPIs missed. Milestones at risk. Communication breakdown. | **Escalate to Trustee immediately.** Remediation or termination discussion. |
| **CC** | Very Poor | All KPIs failing. No visible effort to correct. Trust eroded. | Termination recommendation to Studio. |
| **C** | Critical | Abandonment, dishonesty, or incompetence. Brand at risk. | Immediate termination process. |
| **D** | Terminated | Grade sustained at C/CC for 2+ months, or single egregious event. | Exit protocol triggered. |

Grading cadence: **Monthly** formal grade. **Weekly** informal assessment. Daily dashboard review.

## 3. Trustee Framework

### 3.1 What Is a Trustee?

A Trustee is a fiduciary who protects the interests of beneficiaries (investors/LPs) by overseeing how assets are managed. In the Trade/TUP context:

- Trustee represents investor interests
- Ensures capital is deployed as promised
- Monitors Operator/Studio performance
- Has authority to intervene if things go wrong
- Reports to beneficiaries on status

> **Trustee ≠ Compliance Manager.** CM assesses and grades. Trustee has AUTHORITY to act.

### 3.2 Trustee Responsibilities

| Responsibility | What It Means | How Exercised | Frequency |
|---------------|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Duty of Care | Act with reasonable diligence | Review reports, ask questions | Ongoing |
| Duty of Loyalty | Put beneficiary interests first | No conflicts, full disclosure | Ongoing |
| Capital Oversight | Ensure proper deployment | Approve major expenditures | Per tranche |
| Performance Monitoring | Track against commitments | Review CM grades, KPIs | Monthly |
| Risk Management | Identify and address risks | Review risk register | Monthly |
| Reporting | Keep beneficiaries informed | Quarterly reports, annual review | Quarterly |
| Intervention | Act when thresholds breached | Pause operations, replace Operator | As needed |

### 3.3 Trustee Authority Matrix

| Action | Trustee Authority | Trigger | Process |
|--------|------------------|---------|---------|
| Approve capital tranche | Required | Milestone achieved | Review + sign-off |
| Pause ad spend | Can order | CAC > 2x threshold | Immediate effect |
| Pause all operations | Can order | Grade = CCC or fraud suspected | 24hr notice |
| Replace Operator | Can initiate | Grade = D or abandonment | With Studio consent |
| Wind down venture | Can initiate | Kill criteria met | Board vote if applicable |
| Remove Studio | Can initiate | Gross negligence/fraud | Per LP agreement |
| Amend strategy | Advisory only | Market conditions change | Recommend to Studio |

### 3.4 Trustee vs Other Roles

| Role | Represents | Authority | Accountability |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Trustee** | Investors/LPs | Fiduciary, can intervene | To beneficiaries |
| **CM** | Assessment function | Grades, no authority | To Trustee/Studio |
| **Studio** | Operating partner | Strategic + operational | To Trustee/investors |
| **Operator** | Execution | Day-to-day only | To Studio |
| **Board** | Governance | Policy, major decisions | To shareholders |

### 3.5 Legal Authority Basis (U15 — CRITICAL)

> **The Trustee's authority MUST derive from the operating agreement or LP agreement, not just from TUP documentation.** Internal documents (this file) describe the framework, but the legal power to pause operations, replace operators, or wind down ventures must be formalized in the entity's governing documents. Budget $5-10K for attorney to draft these provisions.

### 3.6 Trustee Operating Procedure (U13)

| Activity | Cadence | Source | Format |
|----------|---------|--------|--------|
| Review CM weekly summary | Weekly | CM submits by Monday 9am | Structured template: KPIs, grade, escalations, notes |
| Review CM monthly grade | Monthly | CM submits by 5th of month | Full grading rubric with commentary |
| Review financial report | Monthly | Studio/bookkeeper | P&L, cash position, burn rate, runway |
| Capital tranche review | Per milestone | Operator submits milestone evidence | Milestone checklist + KPI proof |
| Quarterly report to LPs | Quarterly | Trustee prepares | Narrative + financials + grade history + risk register |
| Annual comprehensive review | Annually | All parties | Full performance review, strategy assessment, governance check |

**CM-to-Trustee Handoff**: CM sends weekly summary by Monday 9am. Trustee reviews by Tuesday end of day. If grade is BBB or below, Trustee schedules call with Studio within 48 hours. If grade is CCC or below, Trustee triggers formal escalation per Authority Matrix (§3.3).

### 3.7 Trustee Compensation (U14)

| Model | Fee Structure | Appropriate When |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Professional trustee** | $1,000-3,000/month (retainer) | LP fund with multiple ventures. Trustee has fiduciary training. |
| **Investor-as-trustee** | No fee (oversight is part of investment management) | Lead investor fulfills trustee role. Common in small funds. |
| **Advisory board chair** | 0.25-0.5% equity + $500/month retainer | Trusted advisor with governance experience. Hybrid model. |

**Key**: Trustee must NOT have equity in the specific venture they oversee (conflicts with independence). Studio equity is acceptable if Trustee oversees portfolio, not individual TUP.

### 3.8 Trustee Selection Criteria

**Must Have:**
- Independence from Studio and Operators
- Financial literacy (can read P&L, understand unit economics)
- Fiduciary experience or training
- Availability for oversight duties
- Willingness to make hard decisions

**Nice to Have:**
- Legal or accounting background
- Investment experience
- Industry knowledge

**Disqualifying:**
- Financial interest in venture success (beyond fee)
- Relationship with Operator that compromises independence
- History of fiduciary breaches

## 4. Contract Templates Library

**DISCLAIMER: These are structural guides, not legal documents. Consult an attorney before using any contract template.**

### 4.1 Contract Index

| Contract | Parties | Key Terms | When Needed |
|----------|---------|----------|-------------|
| Operator Agreement | Studio ↔ Operator | Equity, vesting, responsibilities, termination | Before operator starts |
| SAFE | Company ↔ Investor | Valuation cap, discount, pro-rata | Fundraising |
| Convertible Note | Company ↔ Investor | Principal, interest, conversion | Fundraising |
| Advisor Agreement | Company ↔ Advisor | 0.25-1% equity, 24mo vest, no cliff | Adding advisors |
| Supplier Agreement | Company ↔ Supplier | Pricing, MOQ, lead time, quality, COA | Before first order |
| Contractor Agreement | Company ↔ Contractor | Scope, IP assignment, payment, term | Hiring contractors |
| Influencer Agreement | Company ↔ Influencer | Deliverables, payment, usage rights, FTC | Influencer partnerships |
| 3PL Agreement | Company ↔ 3PL | Pricing, SLAs, liability | Before shipping (use 3PL's standard, negotiate) |
| Terms of Service | Company ↔ Customers | Purchase terms, returns, liability | Before launch |
| Privacy Policy | Company ↔ Customers | Data collection, use, rights | Before launch (use generator tool) |

### 4.2 SAFE Key Terms

Use YC SAFE template (standard, investor-familiar). Link: ycombinator.com/documents

**IMPORTANT (U17):** YC's current default is the **post-money SAFE**, which is dramatically different from the pre-money SAFE. In a post-money SAFE, the valuation cap includes the SAFE itself in the cap calculation, meaning significantly more dilution to founders than a pre-money SAFE at the same cap. Many founders do not understand this distinction. Consult attorney and model dilution scenarios before choosing.

Key fields to specify:
- Purchase Amount: $______
- Valuation Cap: $______
- Discount Rate: ____% (typically 20%)
- Pro-Rata Rights: Yes/No
- MFN (Most Favored Nation): Yes/No

Conversion triggers: Equity financing (priced round), liquidity event (acquisition), dissolution.

### 4.3 Advisor Agreement Key Terms

- Equity: 0.25-1% (typical)
- Vesting: 24 months, monthly, no cliff
- Cash: $0 (standard for equity advisors)
- Duration: 12-24 months
- Termination: Either party with 30 days notice
- IP: Advisor assigns any IP created
- Confidentiality: Standard NDA provisions

### 4.4 Supplier Agreement Key Terms

- Product specification: attach spec sheet
- Quality standards: GMP, COA required for each batch
- Unit price: $____ per ____
- MOQ: ____ units
- Volume discounts: if applicable
- Lead time: ____ days/weeks
- Payment terms: Net ____
- Shipping: FOB/DDP/etc.
- Right to audit
- Defect remedy process
- Initial term: ____ months
- Termination: ____ days notice

### 4.5 Contractor Agreement Key Terms

- Scope of work: specific deliverables and timeline
- Rate: $____/hour or $____ fixed
- Payment schedule: milestone-based preferred
- Relationship: Independent contractor, NOT employee
- Contractor responsible for own taxes
- No benefits
- IP Assignment (U30): **Explicit assignment clause** — "Contractor hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in work product." Do NOT rely on work-for-hire doctrine for contractors (only applies to employees or specific enumerated categories under copyright law)
- Confidentiality: NDA provisions included
- Return of materials on termination
