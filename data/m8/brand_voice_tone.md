## Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** = What we always sound like (personality, consistent across all channels)
**Tone** = How we adjust for context (urgent vs. calm, technical vs. simple)

---

## Brand Voice Principles

### 1. Informed, Not Preachy

**What this means**: We share knowledge generously, but we don't lecture. We invite curiosity.

**Do**:
- "Most electrolytes have 3 minerals. Ocean water has 78. Here's why that matters."
- "Your body is 60% water. That water needs minerals to do anything. Let's talk about which ones."

**Don't**:
- "You're doing hydration wrong. Here's what you need to know."
- "Everyone should be supplementing with marine plasma. It's science."

[Confidence: B | Source: Brand personality workshop (Informed Guide archetype), competitive tone analysis (AG1, Momentous, LMNT) | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

### 2. Science-Backed, Not Academic

**What this means**: We use real science, but we translate it. No jargon without explanation.

**Do**:
- "Marine plasma is bioidentical to blood plasma — same minerals, same ratios. Your cells recognize it instantly."
- "Quinton's protocol (1897) showed that seawater could replace blood plasma in dogs. 125 years later, we're still proving him right."

**Don't**:
- "Ionic minerals exhibit superior bioavailability due to their reduced molecular size and charge facilitation across cellular membranes."
- "The homeostatic mineral equilibrium achieved via isotonic marine plasma approximates extracellular fluid composition."

[Confidence: B | Source: D2C wellness brand voice research 2026, Momentous/AG1 science communication patterns | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

### 3. Transparent, Not Salesy

**What this means**: We show our work. Comparisons are honest. We admit what we don't know.

**Do**:
- "Quinton charges $69/month. We charge $59. Same source, simpler packaging, direct-to-you."
- "We can't prove that you'll 'feel a difference in 3 days.' Some people do. Some take 2 weeks. Mineral deficiency reversal isn't instant."

**Don't**:
- "The #1 marine plasma brand in America!" (unverified claim)
- "Revolutionary new formula!" (it's seawater — nothing revolutionary, just good)

[Confidence: B | Source: Brand strategy positioning (transparency as differentiation), consumer trust research 2026 D2C trends | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

### 4. Calm, Not Urgent

**What this means**: Minerals are foundational, not a quick fix. We don't create false urgency.

**Do**:
- "Mineral deficiency builds slowly. Replenishment does too. This is a daily habit, not a magic bullet."
- "We're here when you're ready."

**Don't**:
- "Last chance! Sale ends tonight!"
- "Don't let mineral deficiency ruin your health!"
- "Limited stock — order now!"

[Confidence: B | Source: Brand personality (grounded, steady), contrast to aggressive DTC tactics | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

### 5. Ocean-Rooted, Not Mystical

**What this means**: We honor the ocean as our source, but we don't make it woo-woo.

**Do**:
- "The ocean is where life began. Your blood plasma still carries that signature — 78 minerals in the same ratios."
- "We harvest from 30 meters below the surface, where sunlight barely reaches. Cold, clean, mineral-rich."

**Don't**:
- "Harness the ancient power of the sea!"
- "Ocean energy flows through every cell!"
- "Unlock your primal hydration!"

[Confidence: B | Source: Marine plasma category research (Quinton positioning), avoiding wellness woo-woo trap | Date: 2026-02-10]

## Tone Variations by Context

### Website / Landing Pages

**Context**: First impression, building trust, converting cold traffic
**Tone**: Confident, clear, benefit-forward

**Example**:
> **Headline**: 78 Minerals. 30 Seconds. Zero BS.
>
> **Subhead**: Your supplements are synthetic. IonWave isn't. Pure marine plasma from the Bay of Biscay — the same 78 minerals your body evolved to use.
>
> **CTA**: Try Risk-Free

[Confidence: B | Source: Bootstrap 09 ad copy bank + landing page best practices 2026 | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

### Email / Retention

**Context**: Talking to existing customers, nurturing relationship
**Tone**: Warm, educational, grateful

**Example**:

>

> Hi [Name],
>
> You've been taking IonWave for [X] days now. Here's what you're actually putting in your body:

>
> Not synthetic. Not isolated. Not engineered. Just ocean water, exactly as your cells recognize it.
>
> Questions? Hit reply. We read every one.
>
> — The IonWave Team

[Confidence: B | Source: Email best practices, retention tone research (AG1, Momentous) | Date: 2026-02-10]

### Customer Support

**Context**: Problem-solving, empathy, clarity
**Tone**: Helpful, patient, human

**Example**:
> **Customer**: "I don't feel any different after 3 days. Is this working?"
>
> **Response**:
> Great question. Mineral replenishment isn't instant — your body has been running low for months or years, so it takes time to rebuild.
>
> Some people notice a difference in 3-5 days (better energy, fewer cramps). For others, it's 2-3 weeks. If you're severely deficient, it could be longer.
>
> The science is clear: 78 ionic minerals at the right ratios will support your cells. But "feeling it" varies by person.
>
> Stick with it for 30 days. If you still don't notice anything, we'll refund you. No questions asked.

[Confidence: B | Source: Customer support best practices, D2C retention strategies | Date: 2026-02-10]

## Writing Guidelines

### Sentence Structure

**Short sentences > long sentences.**

Clarity > cleverness. If a sentence runs past 25 words, break it up.

**Do**:
- "Marine plasma is bioidentical to blood plasma. Your cells recognize it instantly."

**Don't**:
- "Marine plasma, being bioidentical to the composition of human blood plasma in terms of mineral content and ratio, is recognized by your cells with a high degree of efficiency and immediacy."

---

### Active Voice > Passive Voice

**Do**:
- "We harvest seawater from 30 meters below the surface."

**Don't**:
- "Seawater is harvested from a depth of 30 meters."

---

### Specific > Vague

Numbers, sources, and proof points beat generalities.

**Do**:
- "78 minerals, sourced from the Bay of Biscay, cold-filtered to preserve ratios."

**Don't**:
- "Lots of minerals from a clean ocean source."

---

### Benefit > Feature (But Include Both)

Lead with what it does for them, back it up with how.

**Do**:
- "Energy that lasts all day — because your cells finally have the 78 minerals they need to make ATP."

**Don't**:
- "Contains 78 ionic minerals including magnesium, potassium, and trace elements."

## Words We Use

| **Use This** | **Not This** | **Why** |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| Marine plasma | Seawater | "Seawater" sounds unprocessed/unsafe. "Marine plasma" = scientifically precise. |
| Ionic minerals | Electrolytes | "Electrolytes" = reductive (only 3-5 minerals). "Ionic minerals" = complete spectrum. |
| Bioidentical | Natural | "Natural" is vague/overused. "Bioidentical" = science-backed precision. |
| Sachets | Packets / Sticks | "Sachets" = premium, intentional. "Packets" = generic. |
| 78 minerals | Complete mineral profile | "78 minerals" = specific, verifiable. "Complete" = vague claim. |
| Bay of Biscay | Atlantic Ocean | "Bay of Biscay" = specific sourcing, European credibility. "Atlantic" = too broad. |
| Quinton protocol | Marine therapy | "Quinton protocol" = 125 years clinical heritage. "Marine therapy" = vague. |

[Confidence: B | Source: Terminology research (marine plasma industry), differentiation strategy | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

## Words We Avoid

| **Avoid** | **Why** | **Use Instead** |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| Revolutionary | Overused, triggers skepticism | "Proven for 125 years" |
| Miracle / Magic | Sounds like snake oil | "Effective" / "Researched" |
| Detox | Pseudoscience baggage | "Supports cellular function" |
| Cleanse | Wellness woo-woo | "Mineral replenishment" |
| Boost / Hack | Implies shortcuts | "Support" / "Optimize" |
| Toxins | Vague fear-mongering | Specific claims or avoid |
| Ancient wisdom | Sounds unscientific | "Clinical heritage since 1897" |

[Confidence: B | Source: Wellness industry credibility research, FDA supplement claim guidelines | Date: 2026-02-10]

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any content, ask:

- [ ] **Is this educational without being preachy?** (Informed Guide)
- [ ] **Is the science explained, not just cited?** (Accessible, not academic)
- [ ] **Are we honest about limitations?** (Transparent, not salesy)
- [ ] **Does this feel calm and grounded?** (Not urgent or hype-driven)
- [ ] **Is the ocean connection clear but not mystical?** (Rooted, not woo-woo)
- [ ] **Are we using specific numbers and sources?** (Credible, verifiable)
- [ ] **Would a skeptic find this convincing?** (Trust-building)
