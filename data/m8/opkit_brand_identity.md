## Overview

This OpKit guides any D2C supplement brand through building a complete brand identity system: positioning, voice, packaging, naming, messaging, and visual identity.

**Output**: 7 integrated deliverables covering brand strategy, voice guidelines, packaging system, domain/trademark strategy, storytelling, visual identity, and industry perspectives.

**Time**: 16 content tabs, estimated 2-3 hours actual work with research + persona dialogue.

**When to use**: Any supplement brand (marine plasma, greens, electrolytes, adaptogens, etc.) from pre-launch through Phase 2 brand maturation.

---

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Define Positioning (Brand Strategy File)

**Deliverable**: `brand_strategy.md`

1. **Positioning Canvas** — Answer 6 positioning questions:
   - Who is it for? (ICP from M27)
   - What is it? (Product category + differentiation)
   - What's the gap? (Existing solution weakness)
   - What's the promise? (Core benefit)
   - What's the proof? (Why believe you?)
   - What's the personality? (Brand archetype)

2. **Competitive Audit** — Map 4-6 competitors on 2 axes:
   - Y-axis: Clinical/Scientific → Accessible/Lifestyle
   - X-axis: Premium Pricing → Affordable
   - Identify your white space

3. **Brand Evolution Model** — Define 3 phases:
   - Phase 1 (Year 1): Challenger positioning (differentiation focus)
   - Phase 2 (Year 2-3): Category ownership (education focus)
   - Phase 3 (Year 3+): Market leader (ecosystem focus)

4. **Visual Principles** — Set 4-5 non-negotiable design principles
   - Example: "Clinical credibility without sterile coldness"
   - Example: "Approachable premium (not luxury, not budget)"

**Template**: See `brand_strategy.md` scaffold (IonWave example)

---

### Step 2: Build Voice Principles (Brand Voice & Tone File)

**Deliverable**: `brand_voice_tone.md`

1. **Core Voice Principles** (5 principles):
   - Define your brand archetype (e.g., Informed Guide, Expert Friend, Challenger)
   - List 5 voice principles with explanations
   - Example: "Science-backed, not academic" (we cite studies but avoid jargon)

2. **Tone Variations by Context** (6 contexts):
   - Ads (social proof, urgency)
   - Landing pages (education, trust)
   - Email (personalization, relationship)
   - Customer service (empathy, solutions)
   - Social media (conversational, responsive)
   - Packaging (clarity, compliance)

3. **Writing Guidelines** — Provide do/don't examples:
   - ✅ DO: "Marine plasma delivers 78 minerals in the same ratio as your blood."
   - ❌ DON'T: "Marine plasma is isotonic to blood plasma and contains bioidentical mineral ratios."
   - Explanation: First version explains, second gatekeeps.

4. **Word Banks**:
   - Words we use (15-20): bioavailable, complete, ocean, balance, etc.
   - Words we avoid (10-15): detox, cleanse, miracle, pharmaceutical, etc.

**Template**: See `brand_voice_tone.md` scaffold (IonWave example)

---

### Step 3: Design Packaging System (Packaging Design File)

**Deliverable**: `packaging_design.md`

1. **Package Architecture**:
   - Primary package (sachet, capsule bottle, powder tub)
   - Secondary package (box, if applicable)
   - Tertiary package (mailer)
   - Dimensions, materials, sustainability notes

2. **Visual Design**:
   - Front panel: brand, product name, hero claim, image
   - Back panel: Supplement Facts Panel (CRITICAL — requires lab CoA), instructions, claims, warnings
   - FDA compliance checklist (structure/function claims only, no disease claims)
   - Prop 65 warning (if applicable — requires heavy metal testing)

3. **Sustainability Strategy**:
   - Material choices (foil vs compostable, trade-offs documented)
   - Honest messaging ("Foil packaging not recyclable — we're working on better options")

4. **Unboxing Flow**:
   - Box → sachet/product → insert card → referral card
   - Optional: progress tracker (inside lid checklist for 30-day protocol)

**Template**: See `packaging_design.md` scaffold (IonWave example)

**CRITICAL BLOCKER**: Lab-verified Supplement Facts Panel required. Cannot print packaging without supplier CoA + third-party ICP-MS analysis (FDA requirement).

---

### Step 4: Domain & Trademark Strategy (Brand Naming File)

**Deliverable**: `brand_naming_domain.md`

1. **Name Evaluation** (if name already chosen):
   - Pronunciation test (can you say it on a podcast without spelling it?)
   - Search volume check (is it searchable? Or lost in generic results?)
   - Trademark clearance (USPTO Class 005 — dietary supplements)
   - Domain availability (.com preferred, fallback strategy documented)

2. **If Name Not Yet Chosen** — Naming Framework:
   - Category signal (does it hint at what you sell?)
   - Memorability (1-2 syllables ideal, 3 max)
   - Spelling simplicity (avoid creative spelling)
   - Cultural sensitivity check (translate to top 5 languages)

3. **Social Handle Strategy**:
   - Acquire @brandname on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Twitter/X
   - If unavailable, document fallback (@brandname_official, @getbrandname, etc.)

**Template**: See `brand_naming_domain.md` scaffold (IonWave example)

**IMMEDIATE ACTION**: Verify domain availability and USPTO trademark clearance BEFORE printing packaging.

---

### Step 5: Craft Messaging & Storytelling (Messaging File)

**Deliverable**: `brand_messaging_storytelling.md`

1. **Brand Story** (4 parts):
   - Problem (status quo weakness)
   - Science (what exists but is inaccessible)
   - Gap (why no one has made it accessible)
   - Solution (your product bridges the gap)

2. **Founder Story** (if applicable):
   - Personal connection to the problem
   - Discovery moment (how you found the solution)
   - Mission (why you're building this)
   - **VOID if pre-launch** — requires founder interview

3. **Messaging Ladder** (awareness hierarchy):
   - Unaware: "Feeling low energy, brain fog, muscle cramps?"
   - Problem-aware: "You're probably mineral deficient."
   - Solution-aware: "Ocean minerals are more bioavailable than synthetic."
   - Product-aware: "IonWave is accessible marine plasma."

4. **Tagline Options** (3-5 variants):
   - Test in ads (Phase 1)
   - Use FDA-safe version on packaging (conservative)
   - Example: "78 Minerals. 30 Seconds. Zero BS." (ads) vs. "Complete Mineralization, Simplified." (packaging)

5. **Social Content Pillars** (4-5 themes):
   - Education (mineral deficiency signs)
   - Science (bioavailability research)
   - Lifestyle (how to use)
   - Community (customer stories)
   - Behind-the-scenes (sourcing, production)

**Template**: See `brand_messaging_storytelling.md` scaffold (IonWave example)

---

### Step 6: Visual Identity System (Remaining Brand Systems File)

**Deliverable**: `remaining_brand_systems.md`

1. **Logo Specifications** (commission designer):
   - Wordmark or icon+wordmark?
   - Ocean wave element? Mineral visual?
   - Scalable to 16px favicon?
   - Color vs. black & white versions

2. **Typography**:
   - Primary font (headings): sans-serif recommended for supplements
   - Secondary font (body): high-legibility sans-serif
   - Web-safe and licensed for packaging

3. **Color Palette**:
   - Primary color (brand color, appears on 80% of touchpoints)
   - Secondary colors (2-3 supporting colors)
   - Neutral colors (background, text)
   - Accessibility: all text meets WCAG AA contrast ratios

4. **Photography Brief**:
   - Lifestyle shots (people using product)
   - Product shots (packaging, sachet, pouring)
   - Ingredient shots (ocean, minerals)
   - Budget: $3-5K for brand photoshoot

5. **Iconography** (Phase 2+):
   - Custom icon library (15 icons: minerals, benefits, science)
   - Consistent style (line weight, corner radius)

**Template**: See `remaining_brand_systems.md` scaffold (IonWave example)

**CRITICAL BLOCKER**: Logo design and photography are launch blockers. Cannot build website, ads, or packaging without visual assets.

### Template 3: Packaging Compliance Checklist

```markdown
# Supplement Packaging Compliance Checklist

**Brand**: [Your Brand]
**Product**: [Product Name]
**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD]

## FDA Requirements (DSHEA)

- [ ] Supplement Facts Panel present
- [ ] "Supplement Facts" (not "Nutrition Facts") header
- [ ] Serving size and servings per container
- [ ] List of all dietary ingredients
- [ ] Amount per serving (mg, mcg, IU, etc.)
- [ ] % Daily Value (if established)
- [ ] "**" footnote for ingredients without DV
- [ ] Manufacturer name and address
- [ ] Net quantity of contents
- [ ] Disclaimer: "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

## Claims Compliance

- [ ] All claims are structure/function (not disease claims)
- [ ] No implied disease treatment ("boosts immune system" OK, "cures colds" NOT OK)
- [ ] Substantiation for all claims (studies cited or removed)
- [ ] No unqualified absorption percentages (98% → "highly bioavailable")

## Prop 65 (California)

- [ ] Heavy metal testing completed (lead, cadmium, arsenic, mercury)
- [ ] If thresholds exceeded, Prop 65 warning present
- [ ] If below thresholds, no warning needed (document test results)

## Trademark & Legal

- [ ] Brand name trademark cleared (USPTO Class 005)
- [ ] No trademarked terms used without permission
- [ ] Legal review of all copy (recommended: $2-5K attorney consult)

## Sustainability Claims

- [ ] Material claims accurate ("recyclable" only if municipally accepted)
- [ ] No greenwashing ("eco-friendly" requires substantiation)
- [ ] Honest trade-offs documented ("foil not recyclable, working on alternatives")
```

## Adaptation Guide (Different Product Categories)

### Adapting for Other Supplement Types

**Greens Powder**:
- Positioning: Taste + convenience challenge (existing greens taste terrible)
- Packaging: Tub + scoop (not sachet), resealability critical
- Claims: Focus on vegetable servings equivalent, not disease claims
- Photography: Green color hero shot, smoothie lifestyle shots

**Protein Powder**:
- Positioning: Cleaner ingredient profile or specific diet (vegan, keto, etc.)
- Packaging: Larger tub (30 servings), flavor variety emphasis
- Claims: Protein content + amino acid profile (verified by lab)
- Photography: Fitness lifestyle, post-workout context

**Capsule Supplements** (vitamins, adaptogens):
- Positioning: Bioavailability or specific outcome (sleep, focus, immunity)
- Packaging: Bottle + label (not box), child-resistant cap required
- Claims: Dosage substantiation critical (mg per serving)
- Photography: Bottle hero shot, daily ritual context

**Budget Variations**:
- **Low budget (<$5K)**: Skip photoshoot, use stock photos + product mockups. Commission logo only ($500-1K). Defer video content.
- **Medium budget ($5-15K)**: Half-day photoshoot ($2-3K), logo + basic brand guidelines ($1-2K), legal review ($2-5K).
- **High budget (>$15K)**: Full-day photoshoot + video ($5-8K), comprehensive brand guidelines + iconography ($3-5K), legal + trademark + testing ($5-10K).

## Universal Principles (Apply to Any Brand)

1. **Positioning First, Aesthetics Second** — Logo/colors don't matter if positioning is confused. Nail the positioning canvas before designing anything.

2. **Voice Consistency Requires Examples** — Principles alone aren't enough. Copywriters need 50+ graded examples to execute consistently.

3. **FDA Compliance is Non-Negotiable** — Aggressive claims ("cures," "treats," disease names) trigger FTC enforcement. Structure/function claims only.

4. **Packaging Locks In Brand** — Sachets/bottles print in bulk (MOQ 10K-50K units). Positioning must be evergreen on the package, flexible on the box.

5. **Trademark Before Printing** — USPTO clearance takes 6-12 months. Check availability BEFORE printing packaging, or risk rebrand.

6. **Founder Story Beats Generic Story** — Personal connection to the problem is 10x more compelling than "we saw a gap in the market."

7. **Test Taglines in Ads, Not on Packaging** — Packaging requires FDA-safe, conservative language. Ads can be punchier.

8. **Phase Your Evolution** — Phase 1 = Challenger positioning (differentiation). Phase 2 = Category ownership (education). Phase 3 = Market leader (ecosystem).
