## Package Architecture

### Primary Package: Sachet (Single-Serve)

**Format**: Foil laminate stick pack
**Dimensions**: 25mm W × 120mm H (standard stick pack size)
**Fill Weight**: 5g (marine plasma powder concentrate)
**Material**: 3-layer foil laminate (PET/Aluminum/PE) — moisture barrier critical
**Print**: 4-color process, matte finish exterior
**Tear**: Notch at top for easy opening
**Sealing**: Heat-sealed on 3 sides

[Confidence: A | Source: Standard stick pack specifications, Bootstrap 05A Packaging Analysis, marine plasma industry standards | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Why Sachets Over Tubs**:
- **Portability**: Travel-friendly, fits in pocket/purse
- **Freshness**: Individual seal = no oxidation after opening
- **Dosing**: Pre-measured, no scooping errors
- **Premium Perception**: Sachets = intentional, tubs = commodity
- **Shipping**: Lighter, smaller, cheaper to ship

[Confidence: B | Source: Bootstrap 05A competitive packaging research, D2C supplement packaging trends 2026 | Date: 2026-02-10]

---

### Secondary Package: Box (30-Count)

**Format**: Hinged-lid box (magnetic closure for premium)
**Dimensions**: 6" W × 4" H × 2" D (holds 30 sachets in 3×10 arrangement)
**Material**: 18pt coated cardboard, matte soft-touch finish, FSC-certified (sustainable)
**Print**: Full-color offset, spot UV on logo (premium tactile experience)
**Interior**: Sachets stand upright in 3 rows of 10, easy to pull
**Closure**: Magnetic flap (vs. tuck flap) — premium, reusable

[Confidence: B | Source: Bootstrap 05A Packaging Brief, 2026 premium supplement packaging research (AG1, Momentous) | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Box Functions**:
1. **Protection**: Keeps sachets dry, organized, undamaged during shipping
2. **Branding**: Primary brand interaction (sits on counter, visible daily)
3. **Information**: FDA-required labeling, usage instructions, sourcing story
4. **Retention**: QR codes to content, refill reminders, brand touchpoints

---

### Tertiary Package: Shipping Mailer

**Format**: Custom-printed corrugate mailer or poly mailer (sustainable option)
**Dimensions**: 7" × 5" × 2.5" (fits 30-count box + insert card)
**Material**: Recyclable kraft corrugate (preferred) or compostable poly mailer
**Print**: 1-color logo, "Thank you" message, recycling instructions
**Inserts**: Welcome card, referral card (both recyclable)

[Confidence: B | Source: D2C subscription packaging best practices, sustainability trends 2026 | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Why Kraft > Glossy White**:
- Sustainable perception (eco-conscious audience)
- Lower cost
- Aligns with "ocean purity" brand positioning (not wasteful)

## Sustainability Strategy

### Material Choices

| Component | Material | Sustainability | Trade-Off |
|-----------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Sachet** | Foil laminate (PET/Al/PE) | ❌ Not recyclable (most programs) | ✅ Best moisture barrier, extends shelf life |
| **Box** | FSC-certified cardboard | ✅ Recyclable, renewable | ✅ No trade-off (standard practice) |
| **Mailer** | Kraft corrugate or compostable poly | ✅ Recyclable or compostable | ✅ Slight cost increase vs. virgin poly |

[Confidence: B | Source: 2026 sustainable packaging research, FDA supplement packaging requirements (moisture barrier critical for marine plasma) | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Sachet Dilemma**:
- **Problem**: Foil sachets are not recyclable in most municipal programs (mixed materials)
- **Alternative 1**: Compostable sachets (PLA/cellulose) — BUT moisture barrier is weaker, shelf life reduced
- **Alternative 2**: TerraCycle partnership — customers mail back empty sachets for recycling
- **Decision**: Foil sachets (product integrity > recyclability for now), with disposal instructions + future TerraCycle exploration

[Confidence: C | Source: Sustainable packaging research, marine plasma stability requirements (speculation on TerraCycle feasibility) | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Intelligence Gap**: Shelf life testing needed to validate if compostable sachets maintain product quality for 12+ months.

**Upgrade Path**: Commission stability testing (compostable vs. foil sachets), explore TerraCycle partnership, add "how to dispose" instructions on packaging.

## Form Factor Analysis

### Why Sachets Win (vs. Tubs, Bottles, RTD)

| Format | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| **Sachets** | Portable, fresh per dose, premium, lightweight shipping | Not recyclable (foil), higher per-unit cost | **D2C subscription** (IonWave) |
| **Tub (plastic)** | Low cost, strong retail shelf presence | Bulky shipping, wasteful, not sustainable, oxidation after opening | Value/retail buyers (not us) |
| **Tub (recyclable)** | Sustainable, retail-ready | Higher cost, still bulky | Eco-conscious retail (Phase 3+) |
| **Pouch (stand-up)** | Compact, moderate cost | Moderate shelf presence, dosing imprecise | Mixed D2C/retail |
| **RTD (glass bottle)** | Premium, no mixing | Heavy, expensive, fragile, shelf-stable challenges | Premium retail (not Phase 1) |
| **RTD (aluminum can)** | Recyclable, retail-strong | Heavy, high MOQs, co-packer required | Mass retail (not Phase 1) |

[Confidence: B | Source: Bootstrap 05A Packaging Analysis, D2C supplement packaging trends 2026 | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Decision**: Sachets for Phase 1-2 (D2C subscription). Explore tub (recyclable) or pouch for Phase 3 retail expansion.

## Prop 65 Considerations (California)

**What is Prop 65?**: California law requiring warnings if product contains chemicals known to cause cancer or reproductive harm.

**Marine Plasma Risk**: Seawater may contain trace heavy metals (lead, cadmium, arsenic) depending on sourcing location.

**Mitigation**:
1. **Source Verification**: Supplier CoA must show heavy metals below Prop 65 thresholds
2. **Third-Party Testing**: Independent lab analysis (ICP-MS) for heavy metals
3. **Warning Label** (if needed): "⚠ WARNING: This product contains chemicals known to the State of California to cause [cancer/reproductive harm]."

[Confidence: B | Source: Prop 65 supplement compliance research, marine product testing requirements | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Intelligence Gap**: No CoA from supplier yet. Heavy metal levels unknown.

**Upgrade Path**: Require supplier CoA, commission third-party heavy metal testing, determine if Prop 65 warning is required.

## Serving Size Engineering

### Why 5g Per Sachet?

**Rationale**:
- **Marine Plasma Concentration**: 5g powder ≈ 10ml Quinton Hypertonic liquid equivalent (standard therapeutic dose)
- **Water Dilution**: Mix with 8-12oz water = isotonic or near-isotonic solution (matches blood plasma osmolarity)
- **Portability**: 5g fits standard 25mm stick pack, lightweight
- **Cost**: 5g powder = $1.97 COGS (estimated), supports $59/30-day pricing

[Confidence: C | Source: Quinton dosing protocols, marine plasma concentration calculations (estimated, not verified) | Date: 2026-02-10]

**Intelligence Gap**: Exact powder concentration (% marine plasma solids) and mineral density not confirmed. 5g serving size is assumption pending supplier specs.

**Upgrade Path**: Supplier spec sheet, lab testing to confirm 5g delivers equivalent mineral load to 10ml Quinton.
