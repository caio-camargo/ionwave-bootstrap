## Messaging Hierarchy (By Audience Sophistication)

### Tier 1: Unaware (Cold Traffic)

**Message**: "Your electrolytes are incomplete. Here's what you're missing."

**Angle**: Problem-aware (mineral deficiency, synthetic supplements)
**Tone**: Curiosity-driven, educational

**Message**: "Marine plasma is the complete mineral solution you've been looking for."

**Tone**: Comparison, value

## Messaging Do's & Don'ts

### Do:

- Lead with specificity: "78 minerals" not "lots of minerals"
- Use Quinton heritage for credibility: "125 years of clinical use"
- Compare honestly: "Same source as Quinton, 33% less"
- Acknowledge what we don't know: "Some feel a difference in 3 days. For others, it's 2 weeks."
- Use ocean imagery without mysticism: "Bay of Biscay, 30 meters depth" not "ancient ocean power"

### Don't:

- Make medical claims: "Cures fatigue" → "Supports energy levels"
- Exaggerate absorption: "98% bioavailable" (unverified) → "Highly bioavailable ionic form"
- Create false urgency: "Limited stock!" (we're D2C, no scarcity)
- Use wellness woo-woo: "Detox," "cleanse," "unlock primal energy"
- Overpromise speed: "Feel results in 24 hours" → "Most notice changes within 2-3 weeks"

[Confidence: B | Source: FDA supplement regulations, brand voice guidelines, consumer trust research | Date: 2026-02-10]
