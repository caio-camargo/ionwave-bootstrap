## Overview

**Critical Path Priority**: This is the single most important validation step for IonWave.

Marine plasma supplements have a narrow supplier landscape (3-5 known B2B suppliers globally). Unlike commodity supplements (vitamins, proteins), marine plasma requires:
- Ocean water harvesting infrastructure
- GMP pharmaceutical production for sterility
- Cold micro-filtration technology (preserves minerals vs heat sterilization)
- FDA registration for US market
- Proven supply chain for liquid format (sachets/ampoules)

**Timeline Implication**: Supplier research gates formulation (M5), COGS validation (M3), and launch feasibility. Must be completed within 30 days of Trade #84 kickoff.

## Supplier Evaluation Criteria

When evaluating quotes from suppliers, score each on 7 criteria (1-5 scale, 5=best):

| Criterion | Weight | What to Check | IonWave Minimum |
|-----------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Quality/Certifications** | 30% | GMP certified? FDA registered? COA provided per batch? Third-party contaminant testing? | 4+ required |
| **MOQ Flexibility** | 20% | Can they start at <5,000 units? Or do we need $50K+ upfront order? | 3+ preferred |
| **Lead Time** | 15% | <60 days from PO to delivered? Industry standard is 14-18 weeks first production, 8-10 weeks reorders [Confidence: B | Source: matsunnutrition.com/choosing-contract-manufacturer-with-fast-lead-times | Date: 2026-02-10] | 3+ required |
| **Unit Economics** | 15% | COGS per 10ml sachet (target: <$0.30-0.50 ingredient cost to hit 45-67% gross margin at $49 retail) | 4+ required |
| **Sourcing Transparency** | 10% | Will they disclose exact ocean location, depth, extraction method? | 4+ preferred (brand differentiation) |
| **Packaging Capability** | 5% | Can they fill sachets? Or do we need separate copacker? | 3+ preferred (simplifies) |
| **Exclusivity Risk** | 5% | Do they supply major competitors (LMNT, Seaonic, etc.)? If yes, any exclusivity clauses? | No blockers |

**Scoring System**:
- **28-35 points** (80%+) → Primary supplier candidate
- **21-27 points** (60-79%) → Backup supplier

## Supplier Qualification Checklist

Before signing a contract or placing a PO >$10K, validate these items:

### Regulatory & Compliance
- [ ] FDA registration number verified (search FDA database)
- [ ] GMP certificate current (request copy, verify issuing body)
- [ ] COA provided for most recent batch (check heavy metals, microbial, salinity)
- [ ] Insurance: Product liability coverage ≥$2M (request COI)
- [ ] NSF or third-party certification (preferred, not required)

### Operational Capability
- [ ] Production capacity confirmed (can they scale to 10K units/month within 6 months?)
- [ ] Lead time verified (documented in contract, not just verbal)
- [ ] MOQ acceptable (≤$25K first order OR ≤5,000 units)
- [ ] Payment terms workable (Net 30 preferred; 50% deposit acceptable; 100% upfront = red flag unless established brand)
- [ ] Packaging capability (sachets, labeling, or specify copacker needed)

### Quality & Transparency
- [ ] Facility visit offered (or virtual tour if international)
- [ ] Exact ocean sourcing location disclosed (GPS coordinates or region + depth)
- [ ] Processing method documented (cold micro-filtration vs heat sterilization — prefer cold)
- [ ] Sample product received and tested (taste, appearance, salinity check)
- [ ] References provided (2+ existing B2B customers, ideally in US market)

### Business Terms
- [ ] No exclusivity clauses that block IonWave from using backup suppliers
- [ ] Price lock for first 12 months (or at least first 2 orders)
- [ ] Reorder terms specified (MOQ for reorders — ideally lower than initial)
- [ ] Termination clause fair (90-day notice, prorated refunds if quality issues)

**Pass/Fail Gate**: Must check ≥18 of 22 items before signing contract. If <18, escalate decision to Nilo.

## Supplier Audit Checklist (Post-Contract, Pre-Launch)

Once a supplier is selected and contracted, conduct a pre-launch audit (ideally in-person or via video call):

### Documentation Audit
- [ ] Batch records for last 3 production runs (verify consistency in COA results)
- [ ] Supplier's supplier audit (if they source ocean water from a third party)
- [ ] SOP for handling complaints / quality issues
- [ ] Recall plan documentation (required by FDA cGMP)

### Facility Audit (Virtual or In-Person)
- [ ] Clean room or controlled environment for sterile filtration?
- [ ] Temperature-controlled storage for bulk marine plasma?
- [ ] Pest control program in place?
- [ ] Equipment calibration logs current?
- [ ] Staff training records (GMP training within last 12 months)?

### Supply Chain Verification
- [ ] Confirmed ocean water source (photos, GPS, documentation)
- [ ] Backup sourcing plan if primary ocean location becomes unavailable (weather, regulation, pollution)
- [ ] Inventory on-hand (how much bulk plasma do they stock? Can they fulfill emergency reorders?)

### Quality Control
- [ ] Third-party lab COA (not just in-house testing)
- [ ] Heavy metals test results <FDA limits (lead, mercury, cadmium, arsenic)
- [ ] Microbial test results (TPC, yeast/mold, coliforms, salmonella — all pass)
- [ ] Salinity verification (hypertonic should be ~3.3%, isotonic ~0.9%)

**Audit Outcome**:
- **Pass** → Proceed to first production run
- **Minor Issues** → Document corrective actions, re-audit in 30 days
- **Major Issues** → Halt production, escalate to Nilo, activate Backup Supplier Plan

**Timing**: Complete audit before placing first PO >$25K or >5,000 units.
