## Why Sourcing Matters

**Not all ocean water is equal.**

Marine plasma's efficacy claim rests on its mineral composition, which varies dramatically by:
- **Depth**: Surface water (<100m) has pollution, microplastics, lower mineral density; Deep water (600m+) is pristine, mineral-rich, ancient
- **Location**: Proximity to coastlines = industrial runoff; Mid-ocean currents = cleaner
- **Temperature**: Cold water = higher mineral saturation
- **Plankton density**: Phytoplankton blooms concentrate nutrients

**This is a KEY DIFFERENTIATOR** for IonWave and a **potential vulnerability**:
- **If we can substantiate sourcing** (specific location, depth, testing) → strong brand positioning vs competitors who are vague
- **If we can't substantiate** → competitors can attack credibility, FTC could flag unsubstantiated "pure ocean" claims

1. **Plankton bloom location** (nutrient-rich currents)
2. **Distance from pollution** (mid-ocean or protected zones)
3. **Cold sterile filtration** (preserves minerals, removes contaminants)

## Questions for Suppliers (Sourcing Deep Dive)

When vetting Biocean, Actimar, Quinton, or backup suppliers, ask these questions to substantiate sourcing claims:

### Location & Extraction
- [ ] **Exact GPS coordinates** of extraction point? (Or at minimum: region + distance from coastline)
  - *Why it matters*: Validates "pristine ocean" claim; FTC substantiation
- [ ] **What depth** is water extracted from?
  - *Expected answer*: 20-30m for plankton blooms (marine plasma strategy) OR 600m+ for deep ocean water
  - *Red flag*: Vague answer ("deep ocean" without specifics) or <10m (too close to surface pollution)
- [ ] **Extraction method**? (Pump, pipeline, manual collection?)
  - *Why it matters*: Pipeline = industrial scale, consistent quality; Manual = small batch, higher cost
- [ ] **How often** do you extract? (Continuous, seasonal, batch-based?)
  - *Why it matters*: Seasonal = potential stockout risk if demand spikes

### Transportation & Processing
- [ ] **How is ocean water transported** from extraction point to processing facility?
  - *Expected answer*: Food-grade tanks, temperature-controlled
  - *Red flag*: Non-food-grade containers, long transport times (degradation risk)
- [ ] **Storage conditions** before processing? (Temperature, light exposure, duration)
  - *Why it matters*: Marine plasma degrades if exposed to heat or light for >48-72 hours pre-sterilization
- [ ] **Processing method**? (Cold micro-filtration, UV sterilization, heat pasteurization, etc.)
  - *Preferred*: Cold micro-filtration (preserves minerals, removes contaminants)
  - *Avoid*: Heat pasteurization (can alter mineral bioavailability)
- [ ] **Filtration pore size**? (e.g., 0.22 micron for sterile filtration)
  - *Why it matters*: Validates sterility (bacteria/virus removal) per FDA requirements

### Quality & Purity
- [ ] **Heavy metals testing** — COA for last 3 batches? (Lead, mercury, cadmium, arsenic)
  - *Requirement*: All below FDA limits for dietary supplements
- [ ] **Microplastics testing**? (Relatively new standard, not all suppliers test)
  - *Differentiator*: If supplier tests for microplastics and shares results → strong marketing claim
- [ ] **Salinity verification**? (Hypertonic should be ~3.3%, isotonic ~0.9%)
  - *Why it matters*: Validates product consistency batch-to-batch
- [ ] **Full mineral analysis** (78+ trace elements)?
  - *Why it matters*: Core of marine plasma efficacy claim; want consistency across batches

### Transparency & Verification
- [ ] **Can we visit the facility**? (In-person or virtual tour)
  - *Red flag*: Refusal to show facility = potential quality/transparency issue
- [ ] **Photos or video** of ocean extraction site?
  - *Why it matters*: Marketing content for IonWave brand story ("See where our marine plasma comes from")
- [ ] **Certifications**? (GMP, FDA registration, NSF, NATURA 2000, organic, etc.)
  - *Minimum*: GMP + FDA registration for US market
  - *Nice-to-have*: NATURA 2000 (EU protected ecosystem), NSF (third-party quality)
- [ ] **Other brands you supply**? (For reference, not exclusivity concern)
  - *Why it matters*: If they supply reputable brands (e.g., Dr. Tom Cowan, Water & Wellness) → trust signal

### Supply Chain Resilience
- [ ] **Backup sourcing plan** if primary ocean location becomes unavailable? (Weather, regulation, pollution)
  - *Expected answer*: Secondary extraction site OR inventory buffer (6+ months of bulk plasma)
  - *Red flag*: "We've never had an issue" (not a plan)
- [ ] **Seasonal variability** in availability or quality?
  - *Why it matters*: Plankton blooms vary by season; could affect supply consistency
- [ ] **Inventory on-hand** (how much bulk plasma do they stock)?
  - *Why it matters*: If they stock <3 months, stockout risk for IonWave reorders

## Red Flags in Sourcing Claims

Be skeptical if a supplier exhibits any of these red flags:

1. **Won't disclose exact source location** ("proprietary" or "trade secret")
   - *Why it's a problem*: Impossible to verify purity claims; potential FTC issue if we make location-based marketing claims without substantiation
   - *Action*: Press for at least region + depth; if still refuse, disqualify

2. **Can't provide third-party test results** (only in-house COA)
   - *Why it's a problem*: Self-reported quality; no independent validation
   - *Action*: Require third-party lab COA (e.g., Eurofins, SGS, NSF) before contract

3. **Vague about depth** ("deep ocean" without specifics, or "varies")
   - *Why it's a problem*: Depth is a key quality signal; vagueness suggests surface water (cheaper to harvest)
   - *Action*: Ask 3 times in different ways; if still vague, disqualify

4. **No facility visit option**
   - *Why it's a problem*: Transparency red flag; could indicate quality issues, non-existent facility (middleman), or non-GMP facility
   - *Action*: Request virtual tour at minimum; if refuse, escalate to Nilo before proceeding

5. **Price seems too good** (<50% of competitors)
   - *Why it's a problem*: Likely diluted, surface water, or non-pharmaceutical-grade processing
   - *Action*: Calculate expected COGS based on depth, filtration, GMP overhead; if price is <70% of expected, investigate deeply before proceeding

6. **No batch-to-batch consistency data**
   - *Why it's a problem*: Marine plasma quality varies naturally (seasonal plankton blooms, currents); reputable suppliers blend batches to maintain consistency
   - *Action*: Request COAs for last 5-10 batches; check salinity and mineral profile variance (should be <5% variance)

**Decision Protocol**:
- **1-2 red flags** = Proceed with caution, document in contract negotiation
- **3+ red flags** = Disqualify, move to backup supplier
