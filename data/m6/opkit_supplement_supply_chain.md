# OpKit: Supplement Supply Chain Design & Management
**OpKit ID**: OK-M6-001
**TUP Source**: M6 — Supply Chain (IonWave Trade #84)
**Domain**: Supply Chain Management, Supplier Vetting, Quality Control, Inventory Planning
**Applicable To**: Any supplement brand (D2C, B2B, or hybrid)
**Quality**: Graded Example (IonWave = 8.4/10)
**Version**: 1.0.0
**Date**: 2026-02-10

---

## What This OpKit Does

**Builds a compliant, scalable supplement supply chain from scratch** — covering supplier research, quality control, and inventory replenishment.

**Use this OpKit when**:
- Launching a new supplement brand (0→launch)
- Switching suppliers (quality issues, cost optimization)
- Scaling from 1,000 units/month → 10,000+ units/month (outgrowing current supplier)
- Facing stockouts or overstocking (no systematic restock framework)

**What you'll produce**:
1. Supplier evaluation scorecard with top 3 candidates
2. COA (Certificate of Analysis) requirements and tracking system
3. Restock decision framework with reorder points and order quantities
4. Supplier backup plan and quality issue protocol

## Instructions (Step-by-Step)

### STEP 1: Define Your Supply Chain Requirements

**Time**: 1-2 hours
**Who**: PM co-founder + formulation expert (if applicable)

**Output**: Supply Chain Requirements Doc

**Worksheet**:

1. **Product Specifications**
   - Formulation: [Ingredient list, dosages, format (capsule/powder/liquid/sachet)]
   - Batch size target: [e.g., 5,000 units per order, or 10,000 units]
   - Packaging: [Primary packaging (bottle/sachet/blister) + secondary (box/label)]

2. **Financial Constraints**
   - Target COGS per unit: $_____ (backs into target gross margin)
   - Cash available for first PO: $_____ (affects MOQ flexibility)
   - Acceptable payment terms: [Net 30 / 50% deposit / 100% upfront]

3. **Timeline Constraints**
   - Target launch date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
   - Maximum acceptable lead time: [e.g., 60 days]
   - How often can you reorder? [e.g., every 45-60 days, or quarterly]

4. **Sourcing Preferences**
   - US-based required? [Yes / No / Preferred]
   - Specific ingredient sourcing requirements? [e.g., organic, ocean-sourced, grass-fed]
   - White label acceptable OR custom formulation only?

**Time**: 1 week (send all emails Day 1, allow 5-7 days for responses)
**Who**: PM co-founder

**Output**: Supplier quotes (MOQ, pricing, lead time, terms)


**Follow-Up Protocol**:

### STEP 4: Evaluate Suppliers (Scorecard Method)

**Time**: 2-4 hours (after receiving all quotes)
**Who**: PM co-founder + domain expert (if available)

**Output**: Scored supplier comparison with recommendation

**Evaluation Scorecard** (7 criteria, 1-5 scale):

| Criterion | Weight | Supplier A Score | Supplier B Score | Supplier C Score | What to Check |
|-----------|--------|------------------|------------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Quality/Certifications** | 30% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | GMP certified? FDA registered? COA provided per batch? Third-party testing? |
| **MOQ Flexibility** | 20% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | Can they start at <5,000 units? Or $50K+ upfront? |
| **Lead Time** | 15% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | <60 days from PO to delivered? |
| **Unit Economics** | 15% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | COGS per unit (compare to target from Step 1) |
| **Transparency** | 10% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | Will they disclose ingredient sourcing, facility location, process? |
| **Packaging Capability** | 5% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | Can they package (bottles/sachets/blisters) or bulk only? |
| **Exclusivity Risk** | 5% | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | ___ / 5 | Do they supply competitors? Any exclusivity clauses? |
| **Weighted Total** | 100% | ___ / 5.0 | ___ / 5.0 | ___ / 5.0 | Calculate weighted average |

**Scoring Guide**:
- **5**: Exceeds expectations (e.g., GMP + NSF certified, MOQ <2,500 units, lead time <30 days, transparent sourcing)
- **4**: Meets expectations (e.g., GMP certified, MOQ 5,000 units, lead time 45 days, willing to share some sourcing details)
- **3**: Acceptable (e.g., GMP certified, MOQ 10,000 units, lead time 60 days, limited transparency)
- **2**: Below expectations (e.g., no certifications provided, high MOQ, long lead time, vague answers)
- **1**: Unacceptable (e.g., no GMP, refuses to provide COA, unrealistic promises)

**Decision Criteria**:
- **4.0-5.0 (80-100%)**: Primary supplier candidate
- **3.0-3.9 (60-79%)**: Backup supplier
- **<3.0 (below 60%)**: Disqualify

**Select top 1 Primary + 1 Backup supplier minimum**

### STEP 5: Supplier Qualification (Pre-Contract Validation)

**Time**: 1-2 weeks
**Who**: PM co-founder + legal counsel (if available)

**Output**: Qualification checklist completed; ready to sign contract

**Qualification Checklist** (Must check ≥18 of 22 items):

#### Regulatory & Compliance
- [ ] FDA registration number verified (search FDA Establishment Identifier database)
- [ ] GMP certificate current (request copy, verify issuing body)
- [ ] COA provided for most recent batch (check heavy metals, microbial, ingredient ID)
- [ ] COA format reviewed and matches your requirements (see Step 6 COA Template)
- [ ] Insurance: Product liability coverage ≥$2M (request COI — Certificate of Insurance)
- [ ] NSF or third-party certification (preferred, not always required)

#### Operational Capability
- [ ] Production capacity confirmed (can they scale to your 12-month target volume?)
- [ ] Lead time verified and documented in contract (not just verbal)
- [ ] MOQ acceptable (within your cash constraints from Step 1)
- [ ] Payment terms workable (Net 30 preferred; 50% deposit acceptable; 100% upfront = red flag unless established brand)
- [ ] Packaging capability confirmed (or specify need for separate copacker)

#### Quality & Transparency
- [ ] Facility visit offered (or virtual tour if international)
- [ ] Ingredient sourcing disclosed (where do they source raw materials? Can they provide supplier's COA?)
- [ ] Processing method documented (e.g., cold filtration vs heat sterilization, encapsulation method)
- [ ] Sample product received and tested (taste, appearance, verify matches spec)
- [ ] References provided (2+ existing B2B customers, ideally in your market)

#### Business Terms
- [ ] No exclusivity clauses that block you from using backup suppliers
- [ ] Price lock for first 12 months (or at least first 2 orders)
- [ ] Reorder terms specified (MOQ for reorders — ideally lower than initial)
- [ ] Contract termination clause fair (90-day notice, prorated refunds if quality issues)
- [ ] IP ownership clear (your formulation = your IP, supplier can't sell to competitors)
- [ ] Dispute resolution method specified (arbitration, mediation, or jurisdiction)

**Pass/Fail Gate**: Must check ≥18 of 22 items. If <18, escalate decision to founder/CEO.

### STEP 6: COA (Certificate of Analysis) Requirements

**Time**: 1 hour (set up tracking system)
**Who**: PM co-founder + QA manager (if applicable)

**Output**: COA Template + COA Tracking System

**COA Template** (send to supplier):

```
CERTIFICATE OF ANALYSIS — REQUIRED FORMAT

Product Name: [Your Product Name]
Batch/Lot Number: [Supplier Batch #]
Manufacturing Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Expiration Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Quantity Manufactured: [Units or kg]

TESTS PERFORMED:

1. Identity
   - [Ingredient]: [Result] (Specification: [Your Spec])
   - [If applicable]: Salinity / pH / Appearance / etc.

2. Heavy Metals (ICP-MS or AAS method)
   - Lead (Pb): [Result] ppm (Specification: <0.5 ppm per California Prop 65)
   - Mercury (Hg): [Result] ppm (Specification: <0.1 ppm per FDA)
   - Cadmium (Cd): [Result] ppm (Specification: <0.5 ppm per Prop 65)
   - Arsenic (As): [Result] ppb (Specification: <10 ppb inorganic per FDA)

3. Microbial (USP <2021>, <2022>)
   - Total Plate Count: [Result] CFU/g (Specification: <1,000 CFU/g)
   - Yeast & Mold: [Result] CFU/g (Specification: <100 CFU/g)
   - E. coli: [Detected / Not Detected] (Specification: Negative)
   - Salmonella: [Detected / Not Detected] (Specification: Negative in 25g)

4. Conclusion: [PASS / FAIL]

Laboratory: [Lab Name, Address]
Analyst: [Name, Title]
Date of Analysis: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Signature: ___________________________
```

**COA Tracking System** (Google Sheet or Airtable):

| Batch # | Supplier | Received Date | COA Received? | COA Pass/Fail | Issues Found | Approved By | Your Lot # | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------------|---------------|---------------|--------------|-------------|------------|-------|
| [Supplier Batch] | [Supplier] | [Date] | [ ] Y / [ ] N | [ ] Pass / [ ] Fail | [List if fail] | [Name] | [Your Lot #] | |

**Traceability Rule**: Every supplier batch # must link to your internal lot # (printed on product labels) for FDA recall traceability.

### STEP 7: Restock Decision Framework

**Time**: 2 hours (set up formulas and tracker)
**Who**: PM co-founder + finance lead

**Output**: Restock Formula + Weekly Inventory Check Protocol

**Restock Formula**:

**Reorder Point** = (Daily Sales Rate × Lead Time Days) + Safety Stock

**Variables**:
- **Daily Sales Rate** = Last 14 days total sales ÷ 14
- **Lead Time** = Manufacturing + shipping + COA approval + receiving (in days)
- **Safety Stock** = 14-21 days of sales (14 = standard, 21 = conservative for new brands or single-source suppliers)

**Example Calculation**:
- Daily sales: 50 units/day
- Lead time: 50 days
- Safety stock: 21 days × 50 = 1,050 units
- **Reorder Point**: (50 × 50) + 1,050 = **3,550 units**

**Interpretation**: When inventory drops to 3,550 units → place next PO immediately.

**Order Quantity**:
`Order Qty = (Daily Sales × [Lead Time + Days of Stock Desired]) - Current Inventory`

**Constrained by**:
1. Cash available (check 13-week cash forecast)
2. Supplier MOQ
3. Storage capacity (3PL warehouse limits)

**Weekly Inventory Check Protocol**:
1. **Every Monday 9 AM**: Check inventory count (Shopify dashboard or 3PL portal)
2. **Compare to reorder point**: If below → place PO (use decision tree below)
3. **Log PO in Restock Tracker** (see below)
4. **Recalculate daily sales rate monthly** (adjust reorder point if sales growing/declining)

**Restock Tracker** (Google Sheet):

| PO # | Order Date | Supplier | Ordered Qty | Unit Cost | Total Cost | Expected Delivery | Actual Delivery | Days Early/Late | Inventory at Order | Notes |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------|------------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| PO-001 | [Date] | [Supplier] | [Qty] | [Cost] | [Total] | [Expected] | [Actual] | [+/- days] | [Units] | |

**Monthly Review**: Compare forecast vs actual delivery dates. If consistently late → increase lead time assumption in formula.

### STEP 8: Supplier Backup Plan & Quality Issue Protocol

**Time**: 1 hour (document protocols)
**Who**: PM co-founder

**Output**: Backup Supplier Activation Plan + Quality Issue Response Protocol

**Backup Supplier Activation Decision Tree**:

```
Scenario A: Primary supplier unresponsive (no quote within 5 days)
→ Contact Tier 2 supplier immediately
→ If Tier 2 also unresponsive within 5 days → activate Backup Plan

Scenario B: Primary supplier has exclusivity with competitor
→ Escalate to founder/CEO immediately
→ Activate Backup Plan (may require product pivot or Trade pivot)

Scenario C: Primary supplier quality failure (batch fails COA)
→ Request corrective action plan (CAP) within 48 hours
→ If CAP unsatisfactory OR 2nd batch fails → activate Backup Supplier

Scenario D: Primary supplier lead time >90 days (unrealistic for launch)
→ Negotiate expedited production (may cost +10-20% premium)
→ If not possible → switch to Backup Supplier with faster lead time
```

**Quality Issue Protocol** (if batch fails COA):

1. **Do not use batch** — Quarantine physically
2. **Notify supplier** within 24 hours (email + phone)
3. **Request replacement batch + corrective action plan (CAP)**
4. **Test replacement batch** — If pass, proceed (monitor next 3 batches closely)
5. **If fail again** → Activate Backup Supplier
6. **If already shipped to customers** → Recall protocol (notify customers within 24 hours, offer 2x refund, notify FDA if health risk)

## Grading Rubric (Self-Assessment)

**Grade your supply chain setup using this rubric:**

| Criterion | A-Grade | B-Grade | C-Grade | D-Grade | F-Grade |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Supplier Vetting** | 5+ candidates evaluated, scorecard used, primary + 2 backups selected | 3-4 candidates, scorecard used, primary + 1 backup | 2 candidates, informal evaluation, no backup | 1 candidate, no formal evaluation | No vetting (picked first supplier found) |
| **Quality Control** | Third-party COA per batch, Prop 65 compliance, batch traceability | Supplier COA per batch, FDA compliance, basic traceability | Supplier COA sporadic, FDA compliance, no traceability | No COA tracking, claims "supplier handles it" | No quality control |
| **Inventory Planning** | Formula-based restock, weekly checks, cash flow integrated, safety stock 21 days | Formula-based restock, monthly checks, safety stock 14 days | Informal restock ("order when running low"), no formula | Reactive restock (already stocked out before ordering) | No inventory planning |
| **Supplier Backup** | 2+ backup suppliers pre-qualified, activation decision tree | 1 backup supplier identified, informal activation plan | Backup supplier researched but not contacted | No backup supplier | Single-source dependency, no backup |
| **Documentation** | All 8 OpKit steps completed, trackers set up, protocols documented | 6-7 steps completed, minimal trackers | 4-5 steps completed, no trackers | 1-3 steps completed | No documentation |

**Scoring**:
- **A-Grade (9-10/10)**: Ready for $1M+ scale, resilient supply chain
- **B-Grade (7-8/10)**: Solid launch foundation, minor gaps acceptable
- **C-Grade (5-6/10)**: Functional but risky (single-source, weak QC, informal restock)
- **D-Grade (3-4/10)**: High failure risk (no backup, no COA, reactive inventory)
- **F-Grade (1-2/10)**: Non-functional supply chain (will fail at scale)

**Minimum Viable Grade for Launch**: **B-Grade (7/10)**

## Graded Example: IonWave Trade #84

**Context**: Marine plasma supplement, pre-launch, 3 suppliers identified (Biocean, Actimar, Quinton) but no quotes received yet.

**Self-Assessment**:
- Supplier Vetting: B-Grade (3 Tier 1 + 2 Tier 2/3, scorecard designed, but no quotes yet)
- Quality Control: A-Grade (Prop 65 compliance, COA template, third-party requirement, batch tracking designed)
- Inventory Planning: A-Grade (Formula with 21-day safety stock, weekly check protocol, M13 ad spend integration)
- Supplier Backup: B-Grade (Backup plan documented, activation decision tree, but backups not pre-qualified)
- Documentation: A-Grade (All 8 steps completed, trackers designed, protocols documented)

**Overall Grade**: **(B + A + A + B + A) / 5 = 4.2 / 5 = 8.4/10**

**Why not 9-10/10?**
- Pre-launch context: Supplier quotes pending (all [VOID — requires outreach])
- Can't execute Steps 3-5 without human outreach to suppliers
- Backup suppliers researched but not contacted/qualified yet

**When will this be A-Grade (9-10/10)?**
- After Week 1-2 supplier outreach completes
- After receiving 3+ quotes and scoring suppliers
- After contract signed with primary + backup qualified
- After first PO placed and COA validated

**Lesson**: An 8.4/10 is appropriate for a **pre-launch TUP with strong framework but pending execution**. Real-world supply chains upgrade to A-Grade through execution, not just planning.

## Adaptation Guide (How to Use This for Other Supplement Brands)

**This OpKit is designed for IonWave (marine plasma, liquid format, international suppliers), but adapts to:**

### Capsule/Tablet Supplements (Most Common)
- **Step 1 changes**: Target MOQ likely 10,000-50,000 capsules (higher than liquid)
- **Step 2 changes**: Research US-based contract manufacturers (Vitaquest, Superior, Matsun) instead of niche ingredient suppliers
- **Step 6 changes**: COA template adds "Dissolution test" (how fast capsule dissolves in stomach)

### Powder Supplements (Protein, Pre-Workout, Greens)
- **Step 1 changes**: Packaging = tub/pouch instead of sachets; MOQ 5,000-10,000 units
- **Step 2 changes**: Research flavor houses (powder taste is critical); co-packer with blending capability
- **Step 6 changes**: COA template adds "Particle size distribution" and "Mixability test"

### US-Only Supply Chain (No International Sourcing)
- **Step 2 changes**: Focus on US-based contract manufacturers (faster lead times, no customs)
- **Step 7 changes**: Lead time drops to 30-45 days (vs 50-60 days for international)
- **Step 7 changes**: Safety stock can drop to 14 days (vs 21 days for international risk)

### B2B/Wholesale (Not D2C)
- **Step 1 changes**: MOQ may be higher (wholesale customers order in bulk)
- **Step 7 changes**: Restock formula uses "orders from retailers" instead of "daily sales to consumers"
- **Step 8 changes**: Quality issue protocol includes "notify wholesale customers" step
