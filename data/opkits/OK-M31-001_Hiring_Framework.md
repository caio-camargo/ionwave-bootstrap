## OpKit Purpose

Complete hiring system for bootstrapped/lean D2C brands from zero to $100K+ MRR:
- **When to hire** (solo → team transition triggers)
- **Who to hire** (operator vs employee vs contractor, phase-gated roles)
- **How to hire** (sourcing, interviews, evaluation, onboarding)
- **How to compensate** (equity vs cash, market data, negotiation frameworks)
- **How to fire/offboard** (legally, humanely, cleanly)

**Target Users**: D2C founders, operators, Studio 4 ventures
**Applicable Businesses**: D2C consumables (supplements, food, beverage), D2C physical products, subscription brands
**Not Applicable**: B2B SaaS, service businesses, deep tech (different talent models)

## How to Use This OpKit (14 Steps)

### PHASE 1: Load Context (Steps 1-3)

**Step 1: Read the Graded Example (IonWave)**
Read all 5 IonWave files in `data/m31/` to understand structure and quality expectations.
- Start with `hiring_strategy.md` (team evolution)
- Then `job_specifications.md` (understand all role types)
- Then `hiring_processes.md` (sourcing → hire workflow)

**Time**: 1-2 hours

---

**Step 2: Adapt Team Evolution to Your MRR Trajectory**
Copy IonWave hiring roadmap from `hiring_strategy.md`, adjust revenue triggers:
- Phase 1 ($0-$25K MRR): Operator only
- Phase 2 ($25-$50K MRR): + Creative PM
- Phase 3 ($50-$100K MRR): + Ops PM
- Phase 4 ($100K+ MRR): + Growth PM or Head of Ops

If your business model differs (e.g., B2B D2C, high-AOV 1-time purchase), adjust:
- B2B D2C: May need sales PM before creative PM
- High-AOV ($200+ AOV): May hire ops PM earlier (fulfillment complexity)

**Time**: 30 min

---

**Step 3: Assess Your Current Phase**
- Pre-launch or $0-$15K MRR → Operator hiring imminent
- $15-$30K MRR → Creative PM within 1-2 months
- $30-$60K MRR → Ops PM within 1-2 months
- $60K+ MRR → Evaluate Growth PM or Head of Ops

**Decision**: What role are you hiring next?

**Customize**:

- Adjust "Must Have" requirements if needed (e.g., supplement-specific → your category)

**Time**: 15-30 min

**Step 5: Customize PM Competence Assessment (if hiring Operator)**
Copy PM Competence Framework from `hiring_strategy.md`.

**Adapt 18 questions**:

Example adaptation for skincare brand:

- "What's LMNT's weakness?" → "What's Drunk Elephant's weakness?"

**Time**: 30 min

---

### PHASE 3: Source Candidates (Steps 6-7)

**Step 6: Pipeline-Building (Start 4-6 Weeks Early)**
Don't wait until you need to hire. Start sourcing 4-6 weeks early.

**Sourcing Channels** (from `hiring_processes.md`):
1. **Referrals** (ask 5 D2C founders: "Who would you hire if you could clone someone?")
2. **Upwork** (post role, search "D2C ecommerce Shopify operator")
3. **Twitter/X** (D2C community, search "looking for opportunity")
4. **Communities** (Trends.vc, D2C Slack/Discord — lurk 2-4 weeks → contribute → DM)

**Target**: 10-15 applicants → 3-5 screening interviews → 1-2 focused interviews → 1 hire

**Time**: 4-6 weeks (passive pipeline-building), 2-4 weeks (active interviewing)

---

**Step 7: Screen Applicants**
Use Candidate Evaluation Matrix from `hiring_processes.md`:
- D2C Experience (25%)
- Media Buying (20%)
- Ownership Mindset (20%)
- Learning Velocity (15%)
- Culture Fit (10%)
- Communication (10%)

**Pass Criteria**: Score >75/100 to advance to screening interview.

**Time**: 15-30 min per applicant

---

### PHASE 4: Interview (Steps 8-9)

**Step 8: 3-Stage Interview Process**
Follow process from `hiring_processes.md`:

**Stage 1: Screening (30 min)**
- Background, Results, Process, Learning, Motivation
- Pass if: Specific D2C results with numbers, ownership mindset, learning velocity

**Stage 2: Focused (60-90 min)**
- Domain deep-dive (media buying, creative, ops, analytics)
- Weave in PM Competence questions conversationally (if operator)
- Pass if: 12+ of 18 PM Competence questions answered competently (score >60)

**Stage 3: Reference (15-20 min per reference, 2-3 references)**
- Working style, strengths, struggles, "would you work with them again?", handle pressure

**Time**: 2-3 weeks total (calendar time, not active time)

---

**Step 9: Score and Decide**
Use weighted scorecard:
- 80-100: Strong hire (make offer)
- 65-79: Conditional hire (address gaps in onboarding)
- 50-64: Pass (keep in pipeline for future)
- <50: No hire

**Time**: 1 hour (compile scores, deliberate)

---

### PHASE 5: Negotiate Compensation (Step 10)

**Step 10: Use Compensation Framework**
From `compensation_framework.md`:

**For Operator**:
- Start at 15% equity (midpoint of 10-25%)
- Adjust: +5% if scaled brand to $100K+ MRR, +3% if invests cash, -3% if junior, -5% if wants above-market cash
- Cash vs equity trade-off: $10K cash ≈ 1.5-2% equity reduction
- Vesting: 4-year with 1-year cliff (industry standard)

**For Contractor PMs**:
- Creative PM: $25-40/hr or $50-150 per ad (project-based)
- Ops PM: $10-20/hr (VA tier) or $25-40/hr (experienced)
- Growth PM: $25-40/hr + 5-10% of new channel Month 1-3 revenue

**Dilution Check**:
After operator grant, calculate remaining equity:
- Studio/Founder: 60-70%
- Operator: 10-25%
- Remaining: 5-30% (for employee pool, advisors, reserve)

Don't over-grant operator equity without checking downstream constraints.

**Time**: 1-2 hours (negotiation + documentation)

---

### PHASE 6: Onboard (Step 11)

**Step 11: Execute Onboarding Checklist**
From `hiring_processes.md`:

**Day 1** (8-11 hours total):
- Access to all tools (Shopify, Meta, Klaviyo, etc.)
- Read ops model (all M31 content + M0 thesis + relevant TUPs)
- Meet with CM for orientation
- Set up screen recording (Loom)

**Week 1**:
- Shadow existing processes (if applicable)
- Complete first daily report
- Run first ad review (or strategy doc if pre-launch)

**Month 1**:
- Fully autonomous on daily operations
- First weekly report delivered
- First process improvement suggested

**PM Competence Gate**: Reassess PM Competence Score at Week 2-3. If <60, fire within 2-4 weeks (don't wait 90 days).

**Time**: 8-11 hours Day 1 setup, ongoing mentorship Week 1-4

---

### PHASE 7: Manage Performance (Step 12)

**Step 12: Track PM Competence, Fire Fast if Needed**
From `hiring_processes.md` + `hiring_strategy.md`:

**PM Competence Tracking**:
- Week 2-3: Reassess 18-question scorecard
- If score <60: Document gaps, give 1-2 week improvement plan
- If no improvement or score <45: Fire within 2-4 weeks

**Firing Process** (if needed):
1. **Before Firing Checklist**: Decision final? Performance documented? Legal review? Severance? Final paycheck? Access revocation plan?
2. **The Conversation**: Be direct, brief (5-10 min), compassionate but firm, have witness
3. **Timing**: Monday-Thursday (not Friday, not holiday weeks)
4. **Immediate Offboarding**: Revoke all access same day

**Time**: 1-2 hours (firing conversation + documentation)

---

### PHASE 8: Scale Team (Steps 13-14)

**Step 13: Hire Next Role When Triggered**
Revisit Step 2 (team evolution). When revenue hits next threshold or founder time exceeds capacity, start sourcing next role 4-6 weeks early.

**Constraint**: 3-4 PMs maximum. If you need more than 4 people, system is too complex. Simplify before scaling headcount.

---

**Step 14: Update Compensation Dilution Waterfall**
After each hire, update cap table:
- Recalculate remaining equity available
- Ensure employee pool isn't exhausted
- Reserve 2-3% for advisors, 2% buffer for unexpected hires

**Time**: 30 min per hire

## Grading Rubric (A-F Scale)

### A-Grade Hiring (9-10/10):
- PM Competence Score >75 before ad spend
- All hires from pipeline-building (not scrambling)
- Operator equity negotiation documented, dilution math validated
- Onboarding completed in <8 hours, operator autonomous by Week 2
- Zero bad hires (all hires score >75 post-Month 3 performance review)

### B-Grade Hiring (7-8/10):
- PM Competence Score 60-75 before ad spend
- Most hires from pipeline, occasional scramble
- Operator equity negotiated, rough dilution awareness
- Onboarding 8-11 hours, operator autonomous by Month 1
- 1 bad hire out of 3-4 (fired within Month 1-2)

### C-Grade Hiring (5-6/10):
- PM Competence Score 45-60, some ad spend waste
- Reactive hiring (post role when desperate)
- Operator equity arbitrary (no framework)
- Onboarding 12-15 hours, operator struggles Month 1-2
- 2 bad hires out of 3-4

### D/F-Grade Hiring (<5/10):
- PM Competence Score <45, burning cash on ads
- Scrambling, no pipeline
- Operator equity over-granted or under-granted (regret)
- No onboarding, operator floundering
- Majority bad hires, high churn

## Adaptation Guide

### Different Business Models

**B2B D2C** (selling to gyms, offices, etc.):
- Hire Sales PM before Creative PM (B2B requires relationship sales, not just ads)
- Operator needs B2B experience (deal-making, not just media buying)

**High-AOV D2C** ($200+ AOV, 1-time purchase):
- Hire Ops PM earlier (fulfillment complexity, higher chargeback risk)
- Creative PM focus on conversion (LP design) not volume (ad testing)

**Low-AOV Subscription** (<$30/month):
- Retention PM more critical than Growth PM
- Ops PM = customer success (churn prevention), not just fulfillment

### Different Revenue Stages

**Pre-revenue** (Month 0):
- Only hire operator if founder can't operate full-time
- Don't hire anyone else

**$0-$15K MRR**:
- Operator solo, building PM Competence

**$15-$30K MRR**:
- Creative PM if ad volume >40 hrs/month

**$30-$60K MRR**:
- Ops PM if CS >15 hrs/week

**$60K-$100K MRR**:
- Growth PM or Head of Ops (depends on constraint)

**$100K+ MRR**:
- Consider CEO if complexity requires strategic leadership
- Fractional CFO if fundraising or audit needs

### Different Geographies

**Non-US Markets**:
- Adjust compensation (US Upwork rate $39/hr, adjust -20-30% for LatAm, Eastern Europe)
- Tax/legal differs (consult local attorney for contractor vs employee classification)
- Remote-first still applies (global talent pool)

## Known Limitations

1. **Pre-revenue assumptions** — IonWave example is C/D-grade on timing (pre-launch). Adjust based on your actual MRR.

2. **Supplement-specific content** — PM Competence questions reference "ocean-sourced electrolytes." Adapt to your product category.

3. **Studio 4 model** — Equity splits assume Studio + Operator model (60-65% / 10-25%). If pure founder-led, adjust to founder equity retention.

4. **US-centric** — Compensation data, legal guidance (at-will employment, firing protocols) are US-focused. Adapt for international.

5. **No technical roles** — This OpKit covers D2C operational roles (operator, PMs), not engineering/design. For tech hiring, see different frameworks.
