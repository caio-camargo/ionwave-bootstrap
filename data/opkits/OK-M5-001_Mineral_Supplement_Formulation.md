# OK-M5-001: Mineral-Based Supplement Formulation Framework

**OpKit ID:** OK-M5-001
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Created:** 2026-02-10
**Category:** Product Development / Supplement Formulation
**Scope:** Mineral-based supplements (electrolytes, trace minerals, alkalizing blends)
**NOT Applicable To:** Vitamins, probiotics, botanicals, or herbs (different bioavailability, stability, and regulatory considerations)

---

## Purpose

This OpKit provides a systematic framework for formulating ANY mineral-based supplement, from concept through validated product. It extracts the generalizable method from IonWave's electrolyte formulation (M5) and presents it as a reusable process for:
- Electrolyte supplements (different ratios for keto, endurance, general wellness)
- Trace mineral blends (zinc, selenium, chromium, etc.)
- Alkalizing formulas (calcium/magnesium/potassium citrates)
- Marine-derived mineral products (ocean water, spirulina, sea vegetables)

**Use this OpKit when:** You need to formulate a mineral supplement with scientific rigor, regulatory compliance, and honest confidence grading.

---

## Framework Overview

**6-Phase Process:**
1. **Research & Benchmarking** — Understand mineral science, competitor formulas, regulatory landscape
2. **Specification Development** — Define mineral profile, forms, amounts, confidence grades
3. **Scientific Substantiation** — Map evidence to claims, identify gaps, establish safety boundaries
4. **Product Development** — Taste, stability, manufacturing, packaging optimization
5. **Validation & Testing** — Analytical testing, beta users, clinical studies (if needed)
6. **Registration & Launch** — Quality control, regulatory compliance, post-market surveillance

### 1.2 Bioavailability by Mineral Form

**Research Question:** Which chemical form of the mineral absorbs best?

| **Mineral** | **Common Forms** | **Absorption Ranking** | **Notes** |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Magnesium** | Oxide, chloride, citrate, glycinate, threonate | Glycinate (40-50%) > Citrate (30%) > Chloride (12%) > Oxide (4-5%) | Oxide = cheap but poor absorption, glycinate = premium |
| **Calcium** | Carbonate, citrate, phosphate | Citrate (soluble, doesn't require stomach acid) > Carbonate (cheaper, requires acid) | Carbonate best taken with food |
| **Iron** | Ferrous sulfate, ferrous bisglycinate, ferrous fumarate | Bisglycinate (high, gentle on GI) > Sulfate (high, but GI upset) > Fumarate (moderate) | Heme iron (from meat) > all supplements |
| **Zinc** | Picolinate, citrate, gluconate, oxide | Picolinate (high) > Citrate (moderate) > Gluconate (moderate) > Oxide (low) | Absorption affected by phytates, calcium |
| **Potassium** | Chloride, citrate, gluconate | All ~90% absorbed (form matters less for K) | FDA limits K supplements to 99mg due to GI irritation (not toxicity) |

**IonWave Application:** Ocean minerals = chloride/sulfate forms (lower bioavailability than citrate/glycinate, but natural ratios of 78 minerals = trade-off accepted).

### 1.4 Regulatory Landscape

**Understand FDA/FTC Rules for Mineral Supplements:**

| **Regulatory Area** | **Key Rules** | **Implications** |
|--------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Structure/Function Claims** | Can claim support for normal function (e.g., "supports bone health") WITHOUT FDA approval | Must have substantiation, cannot claim treatment/cure/prevention of disease |
| **Disease Claims** | Cannot claim to treat/cure/prevent disease (e.g., "cures osteoporosis") | Turns supplement into unapproved drug → FDA enforcement |
| **Supplement Facts Panel** | Must list all minerals with amounts & % DV | If mineral <2% DV, can list as "‡" (not established) |
| **Upper Limit Warnings** | If exceeds UL, must warn "Consult physician" | Example: K >99mg requires disclosure (regulatory precedent, not toxicology) |
| **Drug Interactions** | Must warn if mineral interacts with common medications | Example: Mg/Ca reduce quinolone antibiotic absorption |
| **GMP Compliance** | Manufacturer must follow 21 CFR Part 111 (cGMP for dietary supplements) | Ensures purity, potency, labeling accuracy |

**IonWave Application:** Post-M7 audit ($420K-$2M FTC exposure) → adopted conservative structure/function claims only, no disease claims, thorough drug interaction warnings.

**Confidence Grading System:**
- **A (90% confident):** Strong evidence, established RDA/UL, proven physiological role
- **B (70% confident):** Moderate evidence, some uncertainty (e.g., optimal form, exact dosing)
- **C (50% confident):** Weak evidence, plausible but unproven (e.g., bioavailability advantage)
- **D (30% confident):** Very weak/no evidence, speculation or supplier-dependent
- **VOID:** Data required before launch (supplier CoA, testing, clinical study)

**IonWave Application:** Core electrolytes (Na/K/Mg) = A-grade. Trace minerals = D-grade (supplier CoA required). Bioavailability advantage = C-grade (pending RCT).

## PHASE 3: Scientific Substantiation

### 3.1 Evidence Hierarchy

**Map Research to Claims:**

| **Claim** | **Evidence Type** | **Strength** | **Example** |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|------------|
| "Provides X mg of Mineral Y" | Analytical testing (CoA) | **STRONG (A)** | Factual, verifiable |
| "Supports normal muscle function" | Established physiological role (textbooks, NIH) | **STRONG (A)** | Structure/function claim, well-documented |
| "Faster absorption than Form X" | Comparative bioavailability RCT | **STRONG (A, if RCT exists)** | Requires head-to-head human study |
| "Superior to competitors" | Head-to-head RCT (your product vs. competitor) | **STRONG (A, if RCT exists)** | Expensive ($50K-$150K), rarely done pre-launch |
| "Based on marine plasma research" | Historical studies (Quinton, 1900s) | **WEAK (D)** | Lacks modern RCTs, FDA-approved uses |
| "78 minerals better than 3" | RCT showing health benefit from ultra-trace elements | **WEAK (D)** | No such RCT exists |

**IonWave Application:** Strong claims (A-grade) = "provides 1,000mg sodium," "supports electrolyte balance." Weak claims (D-grade) = "78 minerals superior to synthetic 3" (factual that 78 exist, but benefit unproven).

**Deliverable:** Claims hierarchy table with evidence type, strength, and substantiation source.

### 3.2 Safety Boundaries

**Identify Contraindications & Drug Interactions:**

| **Safety Issue** | **Mechanism** | **Population Affected** | **Recommendation** |
|-----------------|--------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Hyperkalemia** | Excess potassium (>5.5 mEq/L) → cardiac arrhythmia | Kidney disease, K-sparing diuretics, ACE inhibitors | **DO NOT USE** without physician supervision |
| **Hypermagnesemia** | Excess magnesium (>2.5 mg/dL) → diarrhea, cardiac issues | Kidney disease, >500mg supplement dose | Limit to <500mg/day, warn kidney patients |
| **Hypertension** | High sodium intake → elevated blood pressure | Uncontrolled hypertension, heart failure | Consult physician if >2,300mg total Na daily |
| **Drug Interactions** | Mg/Ca bind to antibiotics → reduced drug absorption | Quinolone/tetracycline users | Separate by ≥2 hours |

**Deliverable:** Safety contraindications table with mechanisms, affected populations, and label warnings.

### 3.3 Claims Substantiation Checklist

**Pre-Launch Audit:**

- ✅ **All claims mapped to evidence** (no unsubstantiated statements)
- ✅ **Structure/function only** (no disease claims like "cures," "treats," "prevents")
- ✅ **Comparative claims have RCT** (if claiming "better than X," must have head-to-head data)
- ✅ **% DV accurate** (based on FDA Daily Values, not RDAs)
- ✅ **Drug interactions documented** (common meds that interact with minerals)
- ✅ **Contraindications specified** (kidney disease, pregnancy, children, etc.)
- ✅ **Upper Limit warnings** (if exceeds UL, "Consult physician" label)

**IonWave Application:** Post-M7 audit, adopted conservative claims-only approach → no disease claims, no unsubstantiated bioavailability superiority (pending RCT).

## PHASE 4: Product Development

### 4.1 Taste Engineering (For Powders/Liquids)

**Mineral Taste Challenges:**

| **Mineral** | **Taste Profile** | **Masking Strategies** |
|-------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Sodium** | Salty (1,000mg = 0.5 tsp table salt) | Citrus flavors, tartness (citric acid), dilution (20+ oz water) |
| **Magnesium (sulfate)** | Bitter, astringent (Epsom salt notes) | Berry flavors, mint (cooling effect), sweetener (stevia, monk fruit) |
| **Calcium (carbonate)** | Chalky, gritty mouthfeel | Citrus, creamy flavors (coconut), effervescence (tablets) |
| **Iron** | Metallic | Strong fruit flavors (grape, cherry), coating (enteric-coated tablets) |
| **Trace minerals** | Metallic, ocean/seawater notes | Embrace it (ocean theme) or mask with bold flavors |

**Taste Testing Protocol:**
1. **Internal iteration:** 5-10 flavor variants, team taste test, narrow to top 2
2. **Beta user testing:** 50-100 users (demographic diversity), blind vs. competitors, Likert scale rating
3. **Success criteria:** ≥70% rate 7/10+, ≥60% prefer your product, <15% deal-breakers
4. **Iteration loop:** Adjust flavor/sweetener ±20%, re-test subset, finalize

**IonWave Application:** Citrus-forward (lemon-lime), stevia sweetener, citric acid tartness to cut saltiness. Target: "Not as salty as LMNT, not as sweet as Liquid IV, distinct mineral character."

**Deliverable:** Flavor strategy with taste testing protocol and success criteria.

### 4.2 Stability & Shelf Life Testing

**Accelerated Stability Protocol (FDA/ICH Guidelines):**

| **Condition** | **Duration** | **Timepoints** | **Testing** |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Accelerated** (40°C/75% RH) | 6 months | 0, 1, 3, 6 months | Mineral content (HPLC/ICP-MS), microbiological (TPC, yeast/mold, pathogens), sensory (taste panel), physical (clumping, color) |
| **Real-time** (25°C/60% RH) | 24 months | 0, 3, 6, 12, 18, 24 months | Same as accelerated (confirmatory) |

**Pass/Fail Criteria:**
- Mineral content ≥95% of label claim at 6 months (accelerated)
- No microbial growth beyond limits (TPC <1,000 CFU/g, yeast/mold <100 CFU/g)
- Sensory score ≥6/10 (acceptable quality)
- No visible clumping, moisture damage, or color change

**Shelf Life Assignment:**
- 6-month accelerated pass → 24-month expiry (tentative)
- Confirm with 12-month real-time data (adjust if degradation faster than predicted)

**IonWave Application:** Target 24 months with foil-laminate stick packs, nitrogen flushing, desiccant sachets for bulk containers.

**Deliverable:** Stability testing protocol with conditions, timepoints, pass/fail criteria, and shelf life rationale.

### 4.4 Packaging Optimization

**Format Selection:**

| **Format** | **Pros** | **Cons** | **Best For** |
|-----------|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Stick packs** | Portion control, portability, shelf stability (foil laminate) | Not recyclable (mixed materials), higher cost per serving | Single-serve convenience, travel, on-the-go users |

## PHASE 5: Validation & Testing

### 5.1 Analytical Testing (Supplier & 3rd Party)

**Required Tests:**

| **Test** | **Purpose** | **Frequency** | **Pass Criteria** | **Cost** |
|---------|-----------|--------------|------------------|---------|
| **Mineral content** (ICP-MS or HPLC) | Verify label claims | Every batch (in-house or supplier CoA) | ±10% of label claim | $500-$2,000/batch |
| **Heavy metals** (Pb, Hg, Cd, As) | Safety (ocean/soil-sourced minerals at risk) | Every batch (critical for ocean minerals) | <FDA limits (Pb <0.5 ppm, Hg <0.1 ppm) | $500-$1,000/batch |
| **Microbiological** (TPC, yeast/mold, pathogens) | Safety (prevent contamination) | Every batch or random (1 in 5) | TPC <1,000 CFU/g, E.coli/Salmonella absent | $200-$500/batch |
| **Moisture content** | Stability (high moisture = clumping, degradation) | Every batch | <5% (for powders) | $100-$200/batch |
| **Dissolution** (for tablets) | Bioavailability (tablet must disintegrate) | Random (1 in 10 batches) | ≥80% dissolved in 30 min (USP standard) | $300-$600/test |

**IonWave Application:** Every batch tested for heavy metals (ocean-sourced = higher risk), random microbiological testing (1 in 5 batches).

### 5.3 Clinical Studies (Optional, Post-Revenue)

**When to Invest in RCT:**
- **Comparative claim needed:** "Superior bioavailability vs. Competitor X" requires head-to-head RCT
- **Medical professional recommendation:** Doctors/RDs require peer-reviewed data
- **Premium justification:** $50K-$150K RCT unlocks higher pricing if proven benefit

**RCT Design Example (Bioavailability Study):**
- **Design:** Randomized, crossover, 60 participants
- **Intervention:** Your product vs. competitor (e.g., IonWave vs. LMNT)
- **Measurements:** Serum Na/K/Mg at 0, 1, 2, 4, 6 hours; urine excretion 24h
- **Outcome:** AUC (area under curve) for blood mineral levels → proves faster/higher absorption
- **Cost:** $50K-$150K (depending on lab costs, participant payments, analysis)
- **Timeline:** 6-9 months (IRB approval, recruitment, data collection, statistical analysis)

**IonWave Application:** Planned Q3 2026 RCT to validate ocean mineral bioavailability vs. synthetic (unlocks "clinically proven" claim).

## Decision Frameworks

### Framework 1: Mineral Form Selection

**When to Choose:**

| **Goal** | **Recommended Form** | **Trade-off** |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Maximum bioavailability** | Glycinate, citrate, picolinate (chelated forms) | Higher cost (+$0.10-$0.30/serving) |
| **Cost-effective** | Oxide, chloride, carbonate (simple salts) | Lower absorption (2-5x less than chelated) |
| **Natural/ocean-sourced** | Ocean minerals (chloride/sulfate blends) | Variable bioavailability, requires supplier verification |
| **Specific benefit** (e.g., Mg for sleep) | Glycinate (calming), threonate (brain health) | Premium pricing, niche appeal |

---

### Framework 2: Dosing Determination

**How to Set Serving Size:**

1. **Start with RDA:** If mineral is universally deficient (e.g., Mg), aim for 25-50% RDA per serving (allows stacking with diet)
2. **Adjust for use case:** If targeting specific population (keto = high Na need), increase to meet that need (1,000mg Na for keto vs. 500mg for general)
3. **Respect UL:** Never exceed UL in recommended daily dose (e.g., Mg supplement UL = 350mg → max 3 servings if 120mg/serving)
4. **Test flexibility:** Allow users to adjust (1-3 servings daily based on individual needs)

---

### Framework 3: Claims Hierarchy (Conservative → Aggressive)

| **Claim Level** | **Example** | **Substantiation Required** | **Risk** |
|----------------|-----------|---------------------------|---------|
| **Factual** | "Provides 1,000mg sodium per serving" | Analytical testing (CoA) | **LOW** (verifiable fact) |
| **Structure/Function** | "Supports electrolyte balance" | Established physiological role (textbooks, NIH) | **LOW** (allowed by FDA) |
| **Comparative (descriptive)** | "Ocean-sourced, unlike synthetic competitors" | Factual sourcing difference | **MEDIUM** (if implies superiority without proof) |
| **Comparative (outcome)** | "Better bioavailability than Brand X" | Head-to-head RCT | **HIGH** (FTC substantiation requirement) |
| **Disease claim** | "Prevents dehydration" or "Treats cramps" | RCT + FDA drug approval | **VERY HIGH** (illegal without approval) |

**Recommendation:** Stay in Factual + Structure/Function tiers for launch. Invest in RCTs post-revenue to unlock Comparative (outcome) claims.

| **Mineral** | **Amount** | **Form** | **Confidence Grade** | **Rationale** |

## Adaptation Guide

### For Different Mineral Types

**Electrolytes (Na, K, Mg, Ca) → Trace Minerals (Zn, Se, Fe, Cu):**
- **Dosing:** Trace minerals need much lower amounts (RDA for Zn = 8-11mg vs. Mg = 310-420mg)
- **Toxicity:** Trace minerals have lower ULs (e.g., Se UL = 400µg, very easy to exceed)
- **Interactions:** Trace minerals compete for absorption (Zn blocks Cu, Ca blocks Fe) → separate dosing or balance ratios carefully

**Alkalizing Blends (Citrate Forms) → Electrolyte Blends (Chloride Forms):**
- **pH effect:** Citrate = alkalizing (raises urine pH), chloride = neutral
- **Use case:** Alkalizing for kidney stone prevention, muscle recovery; chloride for hydration/performance

### For Different Formats

**Powder → Tablets/Capsules:**
- **Bioavailability:** Tablets require disintegration time (test with USP dissolution method)
- **Excipients:** Need binders (cellulose), lubricants (magnesium stearate), disintegrants (croscarmellose)
- **Stability:** Less moisture-sensitive than powder (hermetically sealed bottles)

**Powder → Liquid Concentrate:**
- **Preservation:** Need preservatives (potassium sorbate, sodium benzoate) or refrigeration
- **Stability:** pH control critical (minerals can precipitate if pH shifts)
- **Dosing:** Dropper allows precise micro-dosing (good for trace minerals)

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

| **Pitfall** | **Consequence** | **Prevention** |
|------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Over-claiming bioavailability** | FTC enforcement, litigation | Only claim "superior absorption" if you have head-to-head RCT |
| **Ignoring drug interactions** | Customer adverse events, lawsuits | Research all minerals → common medications (antibiotics, BP meds, diuretics) → label warnings |
| **Supplier dependency (single source)** | Supply chain failure, reformulation if supplier changes | Dual-source strategy, ±10% trace mineral variation acceptable across batches |
| **Under-testing heavy metals** | Contamination (especially ocean/soil-sourced minerals) → FDA warning letter, recall | Test EVERY batch for heavy metals (Pb, Hg, Cd, As), not random sampling |
| **Ignoring taste (assuming "health > taste")** | Poor retention, negative reviews | Invest in taste testing (50-100 users minimum), iterate until ≥70% acceptance |
| **Overpromising timeline** | Missed launch date, rushed formulation, quality issues | Build in 20-30% buffer (e.g., NSF takes 8-12 weeks → apply Month 3, not Month 4) |
