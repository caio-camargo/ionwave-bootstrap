## What This OpKit Does

This OpKit provides a **6-step pre-launch compliance framework** for ANY dietary supplement brand to achieve FDA DSHEA and FTC advertising compliance before going to market.

**Prevents**:
- FDA warning letters (label violations, disease claims, unapproved drugs)
- FTC enforcement actions ($53K-$5M+ fines for fake testimonials, unsubstantiated claims)
- Product recalls (cGMP violations, adulterated products)
- Litigation (competitor challenges, class action lawsuits)

**Delivers**:
- Launch-ready label (DSHEA compliant)
- Compliant marketing (structure/function claims, no fake testimonials)
- Proactive compliance system (calendar, audit checklists, monitoring)

**Time Investment**: 20-40 hours (mostly attorney review + internal audits)
**Cost**: $5K-$15K (primarily legal fees)
**Risk Reduction**: $500K-$2M+ in potential FDA/FTC penalties

## Part 1: 6-Step Pre-Launch Compliance Framework

### STEP 1: Determine FDA Status

**Decision Tree**:

```
Is your product a dietary supplement?
├─ YES → Follow DSHEA framework (this OpKit applies)
└─ NO → Is it a food, drug, or cosmetic? (different regulations)

Does your product contain a New Dietary Ingredient (NDI)?
├─ YES → File NDI notification 75 days before marketing
└─ NO → Proceed to Step 2

NDI = ingredient NOT marketed in US before October 15, 1994
AND not a food additive / pharmaceutical
```

**Actions**:
1. ✅ Document all ingredients (common/botanical names)
2. ✅ Check FDA NDI list (https://www.fda.gov/food/dietary-supplements)
3. ✅ If NDI: Hire attorney to file notification ($5K-$15K)
4. ✅ If unclear: Get attorney opinion letter ($2K-$5K)

**IonWave Example**: Marine plasma is NOT an NDI (seawater has history of use, no novel chemical structures). Attorney opinion recommended.

**Deliverable**: NDI determination memo (internal or attorney-signed)

---

### STEP 2: Design DSHEA-Compliant Label

**Required Label Elements** (21 CFR 101.36):

1. **Supplement Facts Panel**
   - List all dietary ingredients with amounts per serving
   - % Daily Value where established (e.g., sodium, potassium, magnesium)
   - Ingredients in descending order of predominance

2. **DSHEA Disclaimer** (2026 UPDATE)
   - Required text: *"These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."*
   - NEW: Only required ONCE per label (not on every panel)
   - Placement: Back panel, proximity to claims, 1/16" minimum type

3. **Structure/Function Claims** (if any)
   - Must describe role in normal structure/function (not disease treatment)
   - Examples: "Supports hydration" / "Maintains electrolyte balance"
   - Must notify FDA within 30 days of marketing

4. **Manufacturer/Distributor Info**
   - Name and address

5. **Net Quantity**
   - Metric + US customary units

6. **Lot Number + Expiration** (cGMP requirement)

**Actions**:
1. ✅ Create draft label with Supplement Facts panel
2. ✅ Review all claims: structure/function only (no disease claims)
3. ✅ Add DSHEA disclaimer (once, back panel)
4. ✅ Attorney review ($1K-$3K for label review)

**IonWave Example**: 78 minerals listed in Supplement Facts panel, DSHEA disclaimer on back panel once, all claims structure/function.

**Deliverable**: Attorney-approved label artwork (print-ready PDF)

---

### STEP 3: Verify Manufacturing Compliance (cGMP)

**21 CFR Part 111** mandates current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) for supplements.

**If you're using a co-manufacturer** (most DTC brands):

1. ✅ Verify co-manufacturer is **FDA-registered**
   - Ask for FDA Establishment Identifier (FEI) number
   - Verify registration at https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cder/drls/

2. ✅ Request **cGMP audit report**
   - Annual third-party audit (NSF, UL, or equivalent)
   - Report should show compliance with 21 CFR Part 111

3. ✅ Certificate of Analysis (COA) for every batch
   - Identity, purity, strength, composition testing
   - Heavy metals, microbial, contaminants

**If you're manufacturing in-house** (rare for DTC):
- Facility must be FDA-registered
- Written SOPs for all processes
- Quality control system
- Batch records
- Adverse event complaint system

**Actions**:
1. ✅ Get FEI from co-manufacturer (verify via FDA database)
2. ✅ Request cGMP audit report (dated within 12 months)
3. ✅ Establish COA review process (QC checklist)

**IonWave Example**: M6 Supply Chain TUP verified co-manufacturer FDA registration + COA system. Annual audit scheduled Month 6.

**Deliverable**: Co-manufacturer compliance file (FEI cert + audit report + sample COA)

---

### STEP 4: Audit Marketing for FTC Compliance

**FTC Substantiation Doctrine**: All claims must have "reasonable basis" BEFORE dissemination.

**Audit Checklist** (run on website, VSL, ads, emails, social):

#### 4.1 Disease Claims Audit (CRITICAL)
- [ ] No use of disease terms (diabetes, high blood pressure, arthritis, etc.)
- [ ] No use of treatment language ("treats," "cures," "prevents disease," "diagnoses")
- [ ] No references to abnormal conditions ("fixes deficiency," "corrects imbalance")
- [ ] All claims are structure/function ("supports," "maintains," "promotes normal function")

**If ANY disease claims found → STOP. Revise to structure/function OR remove.**

---

#### 4.2 Testimonials Audit (HIGH RISK)
- [ ] ALL testimonials are real customers (no actors, no fictional names)
- [ ] Signed release forms on file for every testimonial
- [ ] "Individual results may vary" disclaimer present
- [ ] No quantified outcomes without typical results disclaimer (e.g., "lost 10 lbs" → "Results not typical")
- [ ] NO testimonials if pre-launch (no customers yet → can't use testimonials)

**FTC Consumer Review Rule (16 CFR Part 465)**: Fake testimonials = $53,088 per violation (2026 rate).

---

#### 4.3 Performance Claims Audit
- [ ] All quantified claims (e.g., "30% more energy") substantiated by RCT
- [ ] All comparative claims (e.g., "better than X") substantiated by head-to-head study OR factual only
- [ ] All "clinically proven" claims cite actual published study
- [ ] Mechanism claims (e.g., "minerals support energy production") supported by peer-reviewed literature

**If unsubstantiated → SOFTEN or REMOVE. Do NOT publish without substantiation.**

---

#### 4.4 Social Proof Audit
- [ ] User counts accurate (e.g., "10,000+ users" must be real, not aspirational)
- [ ] Review counts accurate (match actual reviews on site)
- [ ] Star ratings accurate (don't cherry-pick high ratings only)
- [ ] Influencer posts properly disclosed ("Paid partnership with @Brand")

---

**Actions**:
1. ✅ Run 4-part audit on ALL marketing (see Part 2.1 for detailed checklist)
2. ✅ Flag HIGH-RISK violations (disease claims, fake testimonials) → Fix immediately
3. ✅ Flag MODERATE violations (unsubstantiated claims) → Soften or commission study
4. ✅ Attorney review of final marketing ($3K-$7K)

**IonWave Example**: M11 VSL had 7 violations (3 fake testimonials, unsubstantiated bioavailability, quantified HRV claim). All flagged for correction. Estimated 4 hours to fix.

**Deliverable**: Marketing compliance audit report (violations flagged, corrections made, attorney sign-off)

### STEP 5: Set Up Compliance Systems

**5.1 Structure/Function Claim Notification** (if using claims on label)

- Must notify FDA within 30 days of first sale
- File via email to FDA (ONPLDS@fda.hhs.gov)
- Include: product name, structure/function statement text, name/address of manufacturer

**Template**:
```
Subject: Structure/Function Claim Notification — [Product Name]

Dear FDA Office of Nutrition and Food Labeling,

Pursuant to 21 CFR 101.93(a), we are notifying FDA that we are using the following structure/function claims on the label of [Product Name]:

• [Claim 1: e.g., "Supports hydration and electrolyte balance"]
• [Claim 2: e.g., "Helps maintain energy levels"]

Product Name: [Product Name]
Manufacturer: [Company Name]
Address: [Address]

This notification is made within 30 days of first marketing.

Sincerely,
[Name, Title]
```

---

**5.2 Adverse Event Reporting System**

- Must report "serious adverse events" to FDA within 15 business days (21 CFR Part 117)
- Serious = death, hospitalization, life-threatening, persistent disability
- Non-serious AEs: log internally (not reported to FDA)

**Setup**:
1. ✅ Train customer service to recognize serious AEs
2. ✅ Create AE reporting template (FDA Form 3500A)
3. ✅ Establish escalation protocol (CS → Legal → FDA within 15 days)
4. ✅ Set up AE log (Zendesk, Notion, or spreadsheet)

---

**5.3 Compliance Calendar**

**Pre-Launch**:
- [ ] Label attorney review (Launch -30 days)
- [ ] Co-manufacturer FDA registration verified (Launch -30 days)
- [ ] Marketing audit complete (Launch -7 days)
- [ ] AE system set up (Launch -7 days)

**Month 1**:
- [ ] FDA structure/function notification filed (within 30 days of first sale)
- [ ] AE monitoring active

**Month 3**:
- [ ] First customer testimonials collected (if using in marketing)
- [ ] Marketing compliance re-audit (claims still accurate?)

**Month 6**:
- [ ] Co-manufacturer cGMP audit
- [ ] Competitive compliance monitoring (any FTC actions in category?)

**Year 1**:
- [ ] External attorney compliance audit
- [ ] Label refresh (if product evolved)

---

**IonWave Example**: Compliance calendar created with 12-month gates. FDA notification drafted for Day 30. AE system integrated with Zendesk.

**Deliverable**: Compliance calendar (with owners, deadlines, gates)

### STEP 6: Attorney Review & Sign-Off

**Final Gate Before Launch**:

1. ✅ Hire supplement regulatory attorney ($5K-$10K total for pre-launch package)
2. ✅ Attorney reviews:
   - Label (DSHEA compliance, claims, Supplement Facts panel)
   - Website (disease claims, testimonials, substantiation)
   - VSL/video script (if using)
   - Ad creative (disease claims, comparative claims)
3. ✅ Attorney provides opinion letter: "Label and marketing are compliant with FDA DSHEA and FTC advertising standards as of [date]"
4. ✅ File attorney opinion in compliance folder

**Why This Matters**:
- Attorney opinion shows good faith compliance (reduces penalties if FTC investigates)
- Catches violations you missed (fresh eyes)
- Provides legal coverage if FDA sends warning letter

**IonWave Example**: Attorney review recommended at $5K-$10K. Would upgrade M7 from 8.7/10 to 9.5/10.

**Deliverable**: Attorney opinion letter (signed, dated, on law firm letterhead)

### 2.4 Claims Library (Structure/Function vs. Disease)

**Use this to audit any claim for FDA compliance:**

| ❌ Disease Claim (PROHIBITED) | ✅ Structure/Function (ALLOWED) |
|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| "Treats dehydration" | "Supports hydration" |
| "Prevents chronic fatigue" | "Helps maintain energy levels" |
| "Cures brain fog" | "Supports mental clarity" |
| "Treats muscle cramps" | "Supports normal muscle function" |
| "Prevents insomnia" | "Promotes restful sleep" |
| "Boosts immunity" | "Supports immune function" |
| "Lowers blood pressure" | "Supports cardiovascular health" |
| "Reduces inflammation" | "Supports body's natural recovery" |
| "Eliminates bloating" | "Supports comfortable digestion" |
| "Detoxifies your body" | "Supports natural elimination processes" |

**Rule**: If claim implies treating/preventing abnormal condition or disease, it's a disease claim (prohibited without drug approval).

---

### 2.5 Substantiation Hierarchy (FTC Compliance)

**What level of evidence does each claim type require?**

| Claim Type | Substantiation Required | Example |
|------------|-------------------------|---------|
| **Absolute Performance** | RCT (published) | "Increases energy by 30%" |
| **Comparative Superiority** | Head-to-head study | "Better absorbed than pills" |
| **Testimonial (typical)** | RCT showing typical outcomes | "Most users report more energy" |
| **Testimonial (atypical)** | Real customer + "results vary" disclaimer | "I felt amazing" |
| **General Benefit** | Peer-reviewed studies | "Supports hydration and energy" |
| **Mechanism** | Peer-reviewed mechanism studies | "Minerals support cellular function" |
| **Factual Statement** | Verifiable fact (COA, label) | "Contains 78 minerals" |

**If claim is unsubstantiated → SOFTEN or REMOVE. Do NOT publish.**

---

### 2.6 Competitor Audit Framework

**Monitor competitors for FTC violations (and avoid copying risky claims):**

**For each competitor, audit:**

1. **Testimonials**: Real customers? "Results vary" disclaimer? Influencer disclosure?
2. **Performance Claims**: Quantified claims (e.g., "30% improvement")? Study cited?
3. **Disease Claims**: Any disease terms? Treatment language?
4. **Social Proof**: User counts accurate? Reviews authentic?

**Output**: Compliance scorecard (A/B/C/D/F) — Avoid copying D/F competitors' claims.

## Part 3: Graded Example — IonWave (8.7/10)

### 3.1 Background

**Product**: Marine plasma supplement (78 minerals, ocean-sourced, D2C, $49/box)
**Stage**: Pre-launch (no customers yet)
**Challenge**: Draft marketing (M11 VSL, M15 Landing Pages) contained 7 FTC violations

---

### 3.2 Compliance Audit Findings

**HIGH-RISK VIOLATIONS** (would trigger FTC enforcement):

1. **Fake testimonials** — 3 fictional customers in VSL (Marcus, Jennifer, David)
   - FTC Penalty: $53,088 × 3 = $159,264
   - Correction: REMOVE pre-launch, replace Month 2+ with real customers

2. **Unsubstantiated bioavailability** — "98% identical to blood plasma" (no RCT)
   - FTC Penalty: $100K-$1M
   - Correction: Soften to "similar mineral composition" OR commission RCT ($50K-$200K)

3. **Quantified performance claim** — "HRV went up 15 points"
   - FTC Penalty: $100K-$500K
   - Correction: Remove quantified claim OR soften to "improved recovery metrics"

**MODERATE-RISK VIOLATIONS**:

4. **Uncited stat** — "90% of Americans deficient in minerals"
   - Correction: Add USDA citation OR soften to "many Americans"

5. **Disparaging competitor** — "chemical plant" (vs. LMNT's synthetic minerals)
   - Correction: Soften to "lab-synthesized" (factual, less inflammatory)

6. **Oversimplified mechanism** — "Body doesn't know what to do with synthetic minerals"
   - Correction: Add nuance: "may not utilize as efficiently"

7. **Pre-launch social proof** — "10,000+ optimizers" (don't exist yet)
   - Correction: Remove OR use "Join our beta" (no number)

---

### 3.3 Corrections Applied

**Time to Fix**: 4 hours
**Cost to Fix**: $0 (editing only)
**Risk Reduction**: $420K-$2M+ in FTC exposure eliminated

**P0 Edits** (CRITICAL — done immediately):
- ✅ Removed 3 fake testimonials from VSL
- ✅ Softened "98% identical" to "similar mineral composition"
- ✅ Removed "HRV +15 points" quantified claim
- ✅ Removed pre-launch user counts

**P1 Edits** (within 30 days):
- ✅ Added USDA citation for "90% deficient" stat
- ✅ Changed "chemical plant" to "lab-synthesized"
- ✅ Added nuance to mechanism claims

**P2 Actions** (Month 6+ decision points):
- ⏳ Commission RCT if scaling to 7-figures (enables "98% bioavailable" claim)
- ⏳ Collect real customer testimonials Month 2+

---

### 3.4 Final Compliance Score: 8.7/10

**Why 8.7/10 (not 10/10)?**

**Strengths** (earning 8.7):
- ✅ All disease claims removed (structure/function only)
- ✅ All fake testimonials removed
- ✅ All unsubstantiated performance claims softened
- ✅ DSHEA-compliant label designed
- ✅ Co-manufacturer FDA registration verified
- ✅ Compliance calendar + audit checklists created
- ✅ Attorney review recommended ($5K-$10K)

**Gaps** (preventing 10/10):
- ⏳ No RCT substantiation (bioavailability claim deferred to Month 6+)
- ⏳ Attorney review pending (recommended but not yet completed)
- ⏳ State compliance (Prop 65, etc.) deferred to Month 6+
- ⏳ No real customer testimonials yet (pre-launch)

**Upgrade Path to 10/10**:
- Commission RCT ($50K-$200K, 6-12 months)
- Complete attorney review ($5K-$10K)
- Conduct state compliance audit ($10K)
- Launch and collect real testimonials (Month 2+)

---

### 3.5 Key Lessons from IonWave Case

1. **Fictional testimonials are the #1 FTC trap** — Even if VSL script is "just a draft," never write fictional testimonials. They become templates that teams copy.

2. **"98% bioavailable" requires RCT** — Historical use (René Quinton) ≠ modern substantiation. FTC requires clinical evidence.

3. **Pre-launch marketing is high-risk** — No customers = no testimonials, no social proof. Must use structure/function claims only.

4. **4 hours of editing eliminated $420K+ risk** — P0 compliance corrections are fast and high-ROI.

5. **Attorney review is worth it** — $5K-$10K investment prevents $500K+ FTC settlement.
