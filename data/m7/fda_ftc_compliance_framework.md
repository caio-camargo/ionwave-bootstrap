## Part 1: FDA DSHEA Framework

### 1.1 Pre-Market Notification Requirements

**New Dietary Ingredient (NDI) Determination**

Marine plasma is NOT considered an NDI if:
- It contains only minerals and trace elements naturally present in seawater
- Processing does not create novel chemical structures
- It has a history of use (e.g., Quinton Plasma use in France 1900s-present)

**IonWave Status**: Marine plasma (ocean-harvested minerals) does NOT require NDI notification IF the product is simply concentrated/filtered seawater with no synthetic additions.

[Confidence: B | Source: 21 CFR 190.6, FDA NDI Draft Guidance 2016 | Note: **Attorney review required** before final determination]

**Action Required**: Document sourcing process (depth, filtration, no chemical modification) to support non-NDI position if challenged.

---

### 1.2 Structure/Function Claims vs. Disease Claims

**The Critical Distinction**

| Claim Type | Definition | FDA Approval Required? | Examples |
|------------|------------|----------------------|----------|
| **Structure/Function** | Describes role in maintaining normal structure/function of body | NO (just notify FDA within 30 days of marketing) | "Supports hydration" / "Maintains electrolyte balance" / "Promotes cellular function" |
| **Disease Claim** | Implies product treats, prevents, mitigates, or cures a disease | YES (requires drug approval — $billions, 10+ years) | "Treats dehydration" / "Prevents kidney stones" / "Cures chronic fatigue" / "Reduces high blood pressure" |

[Confidence: A | Source: 21 CFR 101.93, FDA Claims Guidance for Dietary Supplements]

**The Compliance Tripwire**: A single disease claim makes your entire product an unapproved drug, triggering FDA enforcement (warning letter → injunction → criminal prosecution).

**IonWave Positioning**: Structure/function ONLY. Never imply treatment or cure.

---

### 1.3 Required Label Elements (DSHEA Compliance)

**Mandatory Label Components**:

1. **Supplement Facts Panel**
   - List of all dietary ingredients with amounts per serving
   - % Daily Value where established (e.g., sodium, potassium, magnesium)
   - "Amount per serving" format

2. **DSHEA Disclaimer** (2026 UPDATE)
   - Required text: *"These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."*
   - **NEW**: Only required ONCE per label (not on every panel with a claim)
   - Best practice: Place on back panel in proximity to claims
   - Minimum size: 1/16 inch type (readable without magnification)

3. **Ingredient List**
   - All ingredients in descending order of predominance by weight
   - Must use common/usual name
   - Includes "other ingredients" (non-dietary components like flavoring)

4. **Manufacturer/Distributor Statement**
   - Name and address of manufacturer, packer, or distributor

5. **Net Quantity of Contents**
   - Metric + US customary units

6. **Directions for Use** (recommended but not mandatory)

[Confidence: A | Sources: 21 CFR 101.36, FDA Guidance January 2026 re: single disclaimer placement]

**IonWave Label Audit Checklist**:
- [ ] Supplement Facts panel complete with all 78 minerals listed
- [ ] DSHEA disclaimer present (once, back panel, 1/16" minimum)
- [ ] All claims on label are structure/function (no disease claims)
- [ ] No "intended to diagnose/treat/cure/prevent" language anywhere
- [ ] Ingredient list in descending order by weight
- [ ] Manufacturer/distributor address present
- [ ] Net quantity in grams + fl oz (if liquid)
- [ ] Lot number and expiration date (cGMP requirement)

### 1.4 Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP)

**21 CFR Part 111** mandates cGMPs for dietary supplements.

**Key Requirements**:
- **Facility Registration** — Manufacturing facility must register with FDA (Form 3537)
- **Quality Control** — Written procedures for identity, purity, strength, composition
- **Testing** — Incoming ingredient testing + finished product testing
- **Record Keeping** — Batch records, testing results, complaint files (1-3 years)
- **Master Manufacturing Record** — Documented SOPs for each batch
- **Complaint Handling** — Must investigate adverse event reports

**IonWave Status**:
- **Co-manufacturer** is responsible for cGMP compliance (not IonWave directly)
- IonWave must verify co-manufacturer is FDA-registered and cGMP-compliant
- Annual audit of co-manufacturer recommended

[Confidence: A | Source: 21 CFR Part 111, FDA cGMP Final Rule]

**Pre-Launch Gate**: Verify M6 Supply Chain TUP has confirmed co-manufacturer FDA registration + cGMP audit.

---

### 1.5 Adverse Event Reporting

**Mandatory Reporting** (21 CFR Part 117):
- Must report "serious adverse events" to FDA within 15 business days
- Serious = death, hospitalization, life-threatening condition, persistent disability
- Must maintain records for 6 years

**IonWave Compliance System**:
1. Customer service trained to recognize serious adverse events
2. Reporting template filed with FDA MedWatch (Form 3500A)
3. Legal counsel notified within 24 hours of serious AE
4. Non-serious AEs logged (not reported) for trend analysis

[Confidence: A | Source: 21 CFR 117, FDA Serious Adverse Event Reporting]

## Part 2: FTC Advertising & Marketing Compliance

### 2.1 FTC Substantiation Doctrine

**The Core Rule**: All advertising claims must have "a reasonable basis" BEFORE dissemination.

**"Reasonable Basis" Hierarchy** (from FTC enforcement precedent):

| Claim Type | Substantiation Required | Example |
|------------|------------------------|---------|
| **Health/Performance Benefit** | Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) | "Increases energy by 30%" |
| **Comparative Superiority** | Head-to-head study OR strong evidence | "Better absorption than pills" |
| **Testimonial** | Actual customer, typical results disclaimed | "I felt more energized within a week" |
| **General Benefit** | Competent and reliable scientific evidence | "Supports hydration" |
| **Mechanism** (non-performance) | Peer-reviewed studies on mechanism | "Ionic minerals are water-soluble" |

[Confidence: A | Source: FTC Policy Statement on Deception (1983), FTC Health Products Compliance Guide, FTC v. POM Wonderful (2016)]

**IonWave Implications**:
- **"98% bioavailable"** — Requires RCT comparing marine plasma absorption to synthetic minerals OR must be removed
- **"Sustained energy"** — Acceptable IF framed as testimonial with "results vary" disclaimer OR mechanistic claim ("minerals support cellular energy production")
- **Comparison to LMNT** — Factual comparison (3 minerals vs 78) is acceptable. Performance comparison ("works better") requires substantiation.

---

### 2.2 Deceptive Advertising Standards

**What is "Deceptive"?** (FTC 3-part test):
1. Representation, omission, or practice likely to mislead
2. Consumer acting reasonably under the circumstances
3. Material (affects consumer's decision to purchase)

**Common Violations in Supplement Industry**:

| Violation Type | Example | FTC Penalty Range |
|----------------|---------|------------------|
| **Fake testimonials** | Fictional customer reviews | $53,088+ per fake review (2026 rate) |
| **Undisclosed material connections** | Influencer paid but doesn't disclose | $10K-$50K per violation |
| **Unsubstantiated performance claims** | "Boosts HRV by 15 points" (no study) | $100K-$5M+ (varies by harm) |
| **Fake "clinical study"** | Citing non-existent research | $1M-$10M+ (fraud) |
| **Fake scarcity** | "Only 50 left" (always 50 left) | $50K-$500K |

[Confidence: A | Sources: FTC Consumer Review Enforcement Report 2025, FTC v. MOBE (2018), FTC v. Letsgetchecked (2024)]

**2026 Enforcement Climate**: FTC increased consumer review enforcement by 340% in 2025. Supplement brands are high-priority targets.

### 2.3 Consumer Review Compliance (2026 Critical Update)

**New FTC Rule on Consumer Reviews (Effective October 2024, Enforcement Ramp-Up 2025-2026)**:

**Prohibited Practices**:
1. ❌ Fake reviews (written by company, friends, contractors posing as customers)
2. ❌ Buying reviews (paying for positive reviews without disclosure)
3. ❌ Review gating (suppressing negative reviews, only soliciting from happy customers)
4. ❌ Insider reviews (employees/affiliates reviewing without disclosure)
5. ❌ Review hijacking (using reviews from different product)

**Penalties**: $53,088 per violation (2026 inflation-adjusted). A VSL with 3 fake testimonials = $159K+ fine.

**IonWave Compliance Protocol**:
- ✅ NEVER use fictional testimonials in any marketing (VSL, landing pages, ads, emails)
- ✅ POST-LAUNCH ONLY: Collect real customer testimonials with signed release forms
- ✅ Disclose incentives: If offering discount for review, must state "Review was incentivized"
- ✅ Never suppress negative reviews (if using Trustpilot/third-party, leave all reviews visible)
- ✅ Employee/affiliate reviews must disclose relationship

[Confidence: A | Source: 16 CFR Part 465 (Consumer Reviews & Testimonials Rule), FTC Enforcement Actions 2025]

**Pre-Launch Marketing Constraint**: IonWave CANNOT use customer testimonials until Month 2+ (after real customers exist). All pre-launch marketing must use structure/function claims only.

### 2.4 Influencer & Affiliate Marketing Disclosure

**FTC Endorsement Guides** (16 CFR Part 255) require "clear and conspicuous" disclosure of material connections.

**Material Connection** = anything that might affect the credibility of the endorsement:
- Payment (cash, free product, affiliate commission)
- Employment/affiliation
- Family relationship
- Any other benefit

**2026 Update**: FTC crackdown on vague disclosures. These are NO LONGER ACCEPTABLE:
- ❌ "#ad" alone (too ambiguous)
- ❌ "Thanks @brand" (doesn't state payment)
- ❌ Disclosure below "Read More" fold (not conspicuous)
- ❌ Disclosure at end of 60-second video (must be at start)

**Acceptable Disclosures**:
- ✅ "Paid partnership with @IonWave" (Instagram)
- ✅ "I'm an affiliate for this product — I earn commission on sales" (YouTube)
- ✅ "This is a sponsored post" (first sentence of blog post)
- ✅ "IonWave sent me free product to try" (unboxing video)

[Confidence: A | Source: FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR 255), FTC Influencer Guidance Update 2023]

**IonWave Affiliate/Influencer Contracts Must Include**:
- Disclosure requirement in contract
- Specific disclosure language provided
- FTC compliance training (can be 5-minute video)
- Termination clause for non-disclosure
