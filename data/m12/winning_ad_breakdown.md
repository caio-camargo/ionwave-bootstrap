## 1. Why Break Down Winners?

**Goal**: Understand WHY an ad won (not just THAT it won) to replicate success systematically.

**Benefits**:
- Extract creative formulas (hooks, structures, pacing)
- Identify IonWave-specific patterns (which angles work best?)
- Train team/creators on "what good looks like"
- Accelerate derivative production (know which elements to keep vs change)

## 2. The 7-Layer Winner Breakdown Framework

### Layer 1: Performance Data
**What**: Quantitative metrics

**Questions**:
- Final CPA? (target: <$30)
- ROAS? (target: >2.5x)
- Hook Rate? (target: >25%)
- Total spend? (cumulative)
- Lifespan? (days from launch to retirement)
- Purchase count? (≥10 for statistical significance)

**Example**:
- CPA: $24
- ROAS: 4.2:1
- Hook Rate: 32%
- Spend: $1,450
- Lifespan: 24 days
- Purchases: 60

---

### Layer 2: Strategic Positioning
**What**: Angle, audience, timing

**Questions**:
- Which angle? (Missing Mineral, Contrarian, Comparison, etc.)
- Which ICP? (Wellness Optimizers, Female Wellness, Endurance Athletes)
- What platform? (Meta, TikTok, YouTube)
- What season/timing? (launch week? holiday? summer?)

**Example**:
- Angle: Missing Mineral (curiosity gap)
- ICP: Wellness Optimizers (supplement-savvy, biohacker-lite)
- Platform: Meta (Feed + Reels)
- Timing: Month 1 launch (Feb 2026, no seasonal factor)

---

### Layer 3: Hook Analysis
**What**: First 3 seconds (or first sentence)

**Questions**:
- What type of hook? (curiosity gap, contrarian, question, result lead, etc.)
- What's the literal transcript? (word-for-word)
- What emotion does it trigger? (curiosity, fear, aspiration, frustration?)
- What's the pattern interrupt? (how does it break the scroll?)

**Example**:
- Hook Type: Curiosity Gap + Specificity
- Transcript: "Your electrolytes are missing 75 minerals. I found the one brand that has all 78."
- Emotion: Curiosity (what's missing?) + Aspiration (I want all 78)
- Pattern Interrupt: Specific number (75) creates information gap

---

### Layer 4: Body Structure
**What**: Seconds 3-27 (or middle 80% of ad)

**Questions**:
- What's the narrative arc? (problem → solution? comparison? story?)
- How many claims/proof points? (3-5 is typical)
- What's the pacing? (fast cuts every 2-3s? slow burn?)
- What visuals support the message? (product shots, text overlays, b-roll?)

**Example**:
- Arc: Problem (LMNT/Gatorade only have 3 minerals) → Solution (IonWave has 78)
- Claims: 3 (LMNT comparison, taste testimonial, cramp-free result)
- Pacing: Fast (2-3s cuts, 15s total length)
- Visuals: Creator holding LMNT packet + IonWave sachet, text overlay "78 vs 3", quick product pour shot

---

### Layer 5: Proof & Credibility
**What**: Trust-building elements

**Questions**:
- What authority signals? (founder credentials, science, testimonials?)
- What social proof? (customer count, reviews, results?)
- What specificity? (exact numbers, data points, comparisons?)
- What risk reversal? (guarantee, trial, refund policy?)

**Example**:
- Authority: None (UGC creator, not expert)
- Social Proof: Testimonial ("my cramps stopped")
- Specificity: 78 vs 3 minerals (specific comparison)
- Risk Reversal: Not mentioned (ad-level, not LP-level)

---

### Layer 6: Production Style
**What**: Format, aesthetic, technical execution

**Questions**:
- What format? (UGC, founder-led, static, testimonial?)
- What aesthetic? (raw iPhone vs polished studio?)
- What length? (15s, 30s, 60s?)
- What aspect ratio? (9:16, 1:1, 4:5?)
- Sound-on or sound-off friendly? (captions, text overlays?)

**Example**:
- Format: UGC (female creator, 30s, talking to camera)
- Aesthetic: Raw (iPhone-shot, natural lighting, kitchen background)
- Length: 15s (cut-down from 30s original footage)
- Aspect Ratio: 9:16 (vertical, optimized for Reels)
- Sound-Off: Friendly (text overlays for key claims)

---

### Layer 7: Call to Action
**What**: Final 3 seconds (or last sentence)

**Questions**:
- What's the CTA? (direct buy, link in bio, quiz, learn more?)
- How urgent? (scarcity, FOMO, time limit?)
- How frictionless? (one tap? multi-step?)
- Does it match the hook? (curiosity hook → educational LP?)

**Example**:
- CTA: "Link in bio — try IonWave"
- Urgency: None (no time limit or scarcity)
- Friction: Low (one tap to link)
- Match: Yes (curiosity hook → product LP with 78 mineral detail)

## 5. Winning Formula Extraction

### 5.1 Formula Anatomy

**Goal**: Distill winner into a replicable formula.

**Example**:

**Winner**: `IW_Meta_MissingMineral_UGC_HookA_15s_v1`

**Formula**:
1. **Hook** (0-3s): Curiosity gap + specific number → "Your [product category] is missing [X specific thing]"
2. **Comparison** (3-8s): Us vs them (specific) → "[Competitor] has [small number], [Our brand] has [large number]"
3. **Testimonial** (8-12s): Personal result → "My [problem] stopped in [X] days"
4. **Visual** (throughout): Text overlay with key stat → "[78 vs 3]"
5. **CTA** (12-15s): Low-friction → "Link in bio"

**Adaptation for Derivative**:
- Keep: Formula structure (hook → comparison → testimonial → CTA)
- Change: Hook variant ("I found the one ingredient [competitor] leaves out" instead of "Your electrolytes are missing 75 minerals")
- Test: Does new hook maintain 30%+ hook rate?

## 6. Winner Breakdown Workflow

### 6.1 When to Conduct Breakdown

**Trigger**: Ad hits "winner" criteria (CPA <$30, ≥10 conversions, 7+ days sustained).

**Timeline**: Within 24-48 hours of winner confirmation (while context is fresh).

**Owner**: Operator or creative lead.

---

### 6.2 Breakdown Process (30-45 minutes per ad)

**Step 1** (5 min): Collect performance data from Meta/TikTok/YouTube reporting
**Step 2** (10 min): Watch ad 5+ times, transcribe hook and body
**Step 3** (15 min): Fill out 7-layer breakdown template
**Step 4** (10 min): Identify key takeaways, replicable patterns, derivative ideas
**Step 5** (5 min): Log in swipe file + share with team

**Output**: Completed winner breakdown (markdown file or spreadsheet row).

---

### 6.3 Team Sharing

**Why**: Winner breakdowns are learning artifacts for entire team (operator, creators, agencies).

**How**:
- Store in `/Swipe_File/IonWave_Winners/` folder
- Add summary to `data/m13/ad_swipe_file.md`
- Share in team Slack/email: "Winner alert: [Ad name] hit $24 CPA. Breakdown attached."

**Frequency**: Immediately after each winner.

## 7. Comparative Winner Analysis

### 7.1 Winner A vs Winner B

**Use Case**: Two ads both performed well, but one slightly better. Why?

**Method**: Side-by-side breakdown comparison.

**Example**:

| Element | Winner A ($24 CPA) | Winner B ($28 CPA) |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Hook** | Curiosity gap, specific number | Contrarian claim |
| **Length** | 15s | 30s |
| **Format** | UGC | Founder-led |
| **Testimonial** | Yes (cramps stopped) | No |
| **Text Overlay** | Yes (78 vs 3) | No |

**Insight**: Winner A's specificity (78 vs 3 text overlay) + brevity (15s) + testimonial drove 15% lower CPA.

**Action**: Prioritize specificity, brevity, testimonials in next batch.

## 8. Winner Retirement Analysis

### 8.1 Post-Mortem: Why Did This Winner Fatigue?

**Trigger**: Winner retired (CPA +30%, hook rate -20%).

**Questions**:
1. **How long did it last?** (vs expected 3-5 weeks)
2. **Which fatigue indicator triggered first?** (hook rate, CPM, frequency, CPA, CTR)
3. **Did we produce derivatives in time?** (proactive at Week 2-3 or reactive at Week 4?)
4. **Did derivatives work?** (match or beat original CPA?)
5. **What would we do differently?** (produce derivatives earlier? expand audience sooner?)

**Output**: 1-paragraph retirement note in winner breakdown file.

**Example**:
"Winner retired after 24 days (vs expected 21-28 days). Hook rate declined first (-18% Week 3), followed by CPM spike (+32% Week 3). Derivatives launched Week 4 (reactive, should have been Week 2). Derivative v2 matched original CPA ($26), extended lifespan 14 days. Lesson: Produce derivatives at Week 2, not Week 4."

## 10. Breakdown Training (Onboarding)

### 10.1 For New Team Members

**Exercise**: Review 3 winner breakdowns, then break down a 4th winner yourself.

**Steps**:
1. Read Winner #1, #2, #3 breakdowns (provided)
2. Watch Winner #4 video 5+ times
3. Fill out 7-layer breakdown template (45 minutes)
4. Compare your breakdown to operator's breakdown (10 minutes)
5. Discuss: What did you miss? What patterns do you see?

**Outcome**: Team member can now conduct breakdowns independently.

**Time**: 90 minutes (one-time training).
