### ROUND 1

**CHALLENGE (Creative Director)**:
"The angle library is solid, but the ad psychology file emphasizes persuasion mechanics (Cialdini's 6) without addressing IonWave's unique constraint: marine plasma is unfamiliar to 99% of the target audience. You can't just drop 'marine plasma' in the hook and expect it to land. Where's the *education-first* creative strategy? LMNT didn't need to explain electrolytes — everyone knows the category. We're introducing a NEW concept (78 minerals from seawater). This requires a fundamentally different creative approach than the 'Missing Mineral' curiosity gap suggests. How do we bridge the familiarity gap without losing hook effectiveness?"

**RESPONSE (Media Buyer)**:
"Fair point on education, but hook rate data from other 'new category' D2C brands (e.g., AG1 introducing greens powder 2016-2018, Seed introducing probiotics 2019-2020) shows that **curiosity-first beats education-first** in cold acquisition by 2-3x CTR. Educational creative works for *warm* audiences (retargeting, email), but cold Meta/TikTok users won't sit through a 60s marine plasma explainer. The 'Missing Mineral' hook (M12 hook_library.md) tests the category (electrolytes) with a curiosity gap (what's missing?), THEN introduces marine plasma as the solution in seconds 3-8. That's the bridge. If we lead with 'marine plasma' in the hook, we lose them before they understand the benefit. Test both: education-first vs curiosity-first. My bet: curiosity wins on CPA by 30%+."

**SYNTHESIS (UGC Creator)**:
"Both of you are right, but here's the production reality: **most creators can't deliver compelling 'education-first' content**. I've filmed 500+ UGC ads. When a brief says 'explain marine plasma in 15 seconds,' creators either (a) read a script robotically (low authenticity, bombs on hook rate), or (b) oversimplify to the point of inaccuracy ('it's just ocean water!' — not helpful). Curiosity hooks ('Your electrolytes are missing 75 minerals') are EASY for creators to deliver authentically because they're conversational. Education requires expertise or charisma, which <20% of UGC creators have. If IonWave wants education-first creative, it needs **founder-led or Tier 3 production** (scripted, polished), not UGC. For Month 1-3, stick with curiosity hooks in UGC, reserve education for YouTube pre-roll or VSL (M11). That's the realistic path."

**OUTCOME**: **UPGRADED** — Gap identified: IonWave's marine plasma education constraint was under-addressed in creative_strategy.md and ad_psychology.md.

**ACTION**:
1. Add "Education Funnel Strategy" section to creative_strategy.md: Cold (curiosity hooks) → Warm (education explainers) → Hot (testimonial/social proof)
2. Update ad_psychology.md: Add "Familiarity Gap" subsection under Section 1 (Core Psychological Frameworks) with bridging strategies

### ROUND 5

"The creative_engineering.md file positions creative as 'engineering discipline' with hypothesis-driven testing, which is EXACTLY right. But there's a **disconnect between the engineering mindset and the production reality**. The creative_production_queue.md uses qualitative prioritization (P1/P2/P3 based on 'urgent/important/nice-to-have'), not quantitative. An engineering approach would prioritize by **expected value**: (Probability of Success × Potential CAC Improvement × Monthly Order Volume) - Production Cost. Example: Testing a new hook on a Tier 1 angle (40% hit rate, $8 CAC improvement, 50 orders/month, $100 production cost) has EV = (0.4 × $8 × 50) - $100 = $60. That's higher EV than testing a new angle (15% hit rate, $10 CAC improvement, same volume/cost) = (0.15 × $10 × 50) - $150 = -$75 (negative EV). The queue should be sorted by EV, not gut feel. Can we make this more rigorous?"

"I love the EV framework in theory, but it requires **hit rate and CAC improvement data that we don't have in Month 1**. The probabilistic inputs (40% hit rate for hooks, 15% for angles) come from historical data. Until Month 3-6, we're operating on industry benchmarks (M14 testing protocol: 10-20% hit rate generic), not IonWave-specific data. The P1/P2/P3 system is a **Phase 1 heuristic** that works without data. Once we have 3-6 months of IonWave testing history, THEN we can switch to EV-based prioritization. The creative_engineering.md file should note this: 'Months 1-3: Qualitative prioritization (P1/P2/P3). Months 4+: Quantitative prioritization (EV-based, requires historical hit rate data).'"

**SYNTHESIS (UGC Creator)**:

The current queue system (P1/P2/P3) solves that IF it's enforced. The EV framework is more sophisticated, but only works if: (a) IonWave has the data (Month 3+), and (b) the operator communicates priority clearly in briefs ('This is a P1 derivative of our #1 winner, deliver by Friday' vs 'This is P3 exploratory, deliver when you can'). Add priority communication to creative_brief_template.md."

**ACTION**:
