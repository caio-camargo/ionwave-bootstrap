## 1. B2B & Wholesale: When and Why

### Core Principle

> **B2B should be expansion, not escape.**
> DO NOT pursue B2B to "save" a failing D2C business. Only expand into B2B when D2C is proven.

### Readiness Checklist — All Must Be TRUE

- [ ] D2C proven (PMF achieved, unit economics work)
- [ ] Production capacity available for additional volume
- [ ] Brand has credibility (reviews, social proof)
- [ ] Margin can support wholesale pricing (see pricing section below)

### Margin Validation

| Scenario | Price to B2B | COGS | Gross Margin | Viable? |
|----------|-------------|------|-------------|---------|
| Wholesale at 50% off | $24.50 | $12 (est.) | $12.50 (51%) | ✅ YES |
| Wholesale at 50% off | $24.50 | $15 (est.) | $9.50 (39%) | ⚠️ TIGHT |
| Distributor at 60% off | $19.60 | $12 (est.) | $7.60 (39%) | ⚠️ TIGHT |
| Distributor at 60% off | $19.60 | $15 (est.) | $4.60 (23%) | ❌ TOO THIN |

**Critical**: If COGS >$15, distributor channel is not viable. Need to validate actual COGS before committing to distributor pricing.

### MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) Policy *(Dialogue Upgrade U9)*

All wholesale accounts MUST sign MAP agreement before receiving wholesale pricing.

| Policy Element | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Minimum Advertised Price | $44.10 (10% below MSRP) |
| Violation consequence | First offense: written warning. Second offense: account termination |
| Amazon listing | Must be at MSRP ($49.00) |
| D2C subscription | $39.20 (20% off MSRP) — always the best deal |
| Monitoring | Monthly price check across wholesale accounts' online storefronts |

**Pricing Hierarchy (Must Maintain)**:
1. D2C Subscription: $39.20 (best deal — drives direct relationship)
2. Amazon S&S: $41.65 (15% off)
3. Wholesale resale: $44.10+ (MAP floor)
4. MSRP: $49.00

Include MAP clause in ALL wholesale agreements. No exceptions.

### B2B Reorder Automation *(Dialogue Upgrade U3)*

Gyms and small businesses are terrible at reordering — the owner forgets, runs out, switches to whatever rep shows up. Without automated reorder, B2B accounts churn at 60-70% within 6 months.

| Program Element | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| Auto-reorder default | Set standing monthly order with auto-ship. Account only needs to cancel/modify (default = continue) |
| Minimum viable version | Automated email reminder at Day 25 of 30-day cycle with one-click reorder link |
| Auto-reorder discount | 5% off for auto-reorder accounts vs ad-hoc ordering |
| Churn monitoring | Track B2B churn monthly. Target: <20% quarterly churn rate |
| Win-back trigger | If no order in 45 days (for monthly accounts): automated outreach + re-engagement offer |

### B2B Unit Economics *(Dialogue Upgrade U14)*

| Metric | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Acquisition cost** | $200 | 3 hrs sales time × $50/hr + $50 sample cost |
| **Monthly revenue per account** | $588 | 24 units × $24.50 wholesale |
| **Monthly COGS** | $288 | 24 units × $12 (est.) |
| **Monthly gross margin** | $300 | $588 - $288 |
| **Monthly service cost** | $25 | 30 min/month × $50/hr imputed |
| **Monthly net margin** | $275 | $300 - $25 |
| **Payback period** | 0.7 months (~3 weeks) | $200 / $275 |
| **Annual LTV (80% retention)** | $2,640 | $275 × 12 × 0.8 |
| **B2B LTV:CAC** | 13.2x | $2,640 / $200 |

**Verdict**: B2B economics are excellent if retention holds. The key lever is retention — auto-reorder program (above) is critical to maintaining the 80% annual retention assumption.

## 5. Partnership & Co-Brand Framework

### Partnership Types

| Type | What It Is | Example | Value to IonWave | Priority |
|------|-----------|---------|------------------|----------|
| Affiliate | Commission on sales driven | Influencer with link | Low-risk acquisition | HIGH |
| Ambassador | Ongoing brand representative | Fitness influencer | Consistent promotion, content | HIGH |
| Content | Joint content creation | Guest on health podcast | Audience access | HIGH |
| Co-Brand | Joint product or campaign | IonWave × [Gym brand] | Credibility, reach | MEDIUM |
| Distribution | They sell our product | Gym selling to members | New channel | MEDIUM |
| Bundle | Product in their curated box | Subscription box inclusion | Discovery/trial | LOW |
| Charity | Cause partnership | 1% to ocean cleanup | Brand values alignment | LOW (but high brand fit for IonWave) |

### Ideal Partner Profile

A good IonWave partner has:
- ✅ Audience overlap with ICP (health, fitness, keto, biohacking)
- ✅ Aligned values (quality, science, transparency)
- ✅ Credibility in their space
- ✅ Not a direct competitor
- ✅ Engaged audience (engagement rate matters more than size)
- ✅ Track record of successful partnerships

### Partnership Offer Templates

| Type | Standard Offer |
|------|----------------|
| Affiliate | 15-20% commission, unique code, free product monthly |
| Ambassador | Monthly product + commission + exclusive content access |
| Co-Brand | Revenue share, joint marketing investment |
| Content | Free product + cross-promotion, no cash |
