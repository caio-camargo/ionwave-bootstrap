## 1. Taste Profile Documentation

### Why Taste Matters

Taste is the **#1 reason for churn in supplements**. The IonWave challenge: ocean mineral concentrate has an inherently mineral/salty taste. This is a feature, not a bug — but only if expectations are set correctly.

**The Rule**: Set ACCURATE expectations, then exceed them. Never oversell taste.

### Taste Profile by Flavor

| Flavor | Primary Taste | Secondary Notes | Aftertaste | Best Comparison |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|------------|-----------------|
| Citrus Lime | Tart citrus | Mild saltiness | Clean, slight mineral | Like unsweetened lime sparkling water |
| Unflavored | Salty | Mineral/ocean | Mineral | Like mild ocean water |
| [Future flavor] | TBD | TBD | TBD | TBD |

### Taste Expectation Copy — What to Say

| ✅ GOOD Copy | ❌ BAD Copy | Why |
|-------------|------------|-----|
| "Refreshing citrus taste with a clean mineral finish" | "Tastes just like regular lemonade!" | Sets wrong expectation, guarantees disappointment |
| "Subtly salty — you'll know it's working" | "You won't even taste the salt!" | Lie — they will taste salt, and then they'll feel lied to |
| "Not overly sweet like sports drinks" | "Delicious!" | Vague, sets high bar, different for everyone |
| "It tastes functional. You'll taste the minerals. That's how you know it's real." | "Best-tasting electrolyte on the market!" | Claim you can't back up, invites comparison |

**The Golden Frame**: "It tastes functional. You'll taste the minerals. That's how you know it's real."

This reframes the taste as a signal of quality rather than a flaw. Turn the weakness into the proof point.

### Taste Feedback Tracker

Track customer taste feedback to identify formula issues, improve batches, and adjust marketing copy.

| Category | What to Track | Alert Threshold | Action |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|--------|
| Too salty | Count of complaints | >15% of feedback | Reformulate or adjust copy expectations |
| Not salty enough | Count of requests | >10% | Probably fine — means expectations are set right |
| Aftertaste complaints | Specific aftertaste descriptions | >10% | Reformulate, test new masking agents |
| Sweetness level | Too sweet / not sweet enough ratio | >20% either direction | Consider offering sweetened + unsweetened variants |
| Flavor accuracy | "Doesn't taste like citrus" | >10% | Increase natural flavoring or rebrand the flavor |

## 2. Mixability & Texture Specifications

### Mixability Acceptance Standards

| Factor | Ideal Behavior | Acceptable | Problem | Fix If Problem |
|--------|---------------|------------|---------|----------------|
| Dissolve time | <30 sec with stirring | 30-60 sec | >60 sec or won't dissolve | Reformulate, finer grind |
| Clumping in pack | Free-flowing powder | Minor clumps that break easily | Hard clumps | Better moisture barrier in packaging |
| Residue in glass | None visible | Slight coating | Gritty bottom | Reformulate for solubility |
| Cold water | Dissolves fully | Needs extra stirring | Won't dissolve cold | Reformulate |
| Hot water | Dissolves instantly | Normal | Weird reaction (color change, smell) | Check heat stability |
| Foam | Minimal to none | Some initial foam (settles in 30s) | Excessive, persistent foam | Check ingredient interactions |

### QC Testing Protocol

| Test | Frequency | Method | Pass Criteria |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Dissolve time | Every batch | Stir test in 16oz room temp water | <45 sec, no visible residue |
| Clumping check | Every batch | Visual inspection + shake test | Free-flowing, no hard clumps |
| Cold water dissolve | Every batch | 16oz cold water (~40°F), stir 30 sec | Fully dissolved |
| Taste consistency | Every batch | 3-person blind taste vs reference batch | No detectable variance |
| Moisture content | Every batch | Moisture analyzer | <3% moisture |

### Usage Instructions (For Packaging & Website)

```
1. Pour 16-32oz of water into bottle or glass
2. Add one stick pack of IonWave
3. Stir or shake for 15-30 seconds
4. Enjoy immediately or refrigerate

Tips:
• Room temperature water dissolves faster
• Shaker bottle works best for on-the-go
• Stir if you see settling at bottom
• Can add to smoothies, tea, or sparkling water
```

## 4. Voice-of-Customer (VOC) Pipeline *(Dialogue Upgrade U5)*

Without systematic VOC capture, product readiness triggers are unmeasurable. This pipeline feeds the Feature Request Log and SKU expansion triggers.

### VOC Capture Sources

| Source | Method | Frequency | Output |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Post-purchase survey | Day 7 email: "What else would you like us to make?" | Every customer | Feature requests tagged to customer type |
| CS ticket tagging | Every support ticket tagged with feature-request category if applicable | Real-time | Aggregated weekly |
| Review mining | Monthly scan of site + Amazon reviews for product requests | Monthly | Top 5 requests documented |
| Social listening | Track @mentions, DMs, comments for unmet needs | Weekly | Qualitative themes |
| NPS survey | Quarterly NPS with open-ended "What would make this a 10?" | Quarterly | Strategic product direction |

### Trigger Measurement

Expansion trigger #3 ("Customer demand signal >50 requests") becomes measurable:
- **Threshold**: >50 requests across ALL channels within 60 days = demand validated
- **Tracking**: Weekly aggregation in Feature Request Log
- **Alert**: When any single request crosses 30 mentions, flag for evaluation

## 5. Product Iteration Cycle *(Dialogue Upgrade U13)*

### Quarterly Product Review

| Step | Activity | Who | Output |
|------|----------|-----|--------|
| 1 | Aggregate taste feedback tracker data | Ops Lead | Trend report: complaints, praise, categories |
| 2 | Review feature request log priorities | Product Lead | Updated priority rankings |
| 3 | Competitive product taste test | Team | Buy competitors, blind test vs current formula |
| 4 | Decision | Nilo | MAINTAIN / REFORMULATE / ADD VARIANT |

### Annual Deep Review

Full re-evaluation of formula, packaging, pricing based on 12 months of data.

| Element | Review Criteria | Action Threshold |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| Formula | Taste complaints trend, competitor formulas, new science | >15% negative taste feedback → reformulate |
| Packaging | Shelf life, clumping reports, competitor packaging | >5% packaging complaints → redesign |
| Pricing | CAC trends, competitive pricing moves, margin health | Margin <20% → price increase or COGS reduction |

### Reformulation Gate

Any formula change must pass blind taste test with existing subscribers:
- Recruit 50+ active subscribers for blind test
- Must achieve >60% "equal or prefer new formula" rating
- If <60%, do NOT ship. Iterate and re-test.
