## 1. What This Kit Does

Provides a structured framework for expanding a DTC brand beyond its initial direct-to-consumer channel into Amazon, B2B/wholesale, retail, new products, and international markets. Includes decision frameworks, readiness triggers, economic models, and operational playbooks.

## 2. Kit Components

### Instruction Document

**How to Use This Kit (14 Steps)**

1. **Validate D2C PMF first** — Do NOT expand until hero product is profitable (LTV:CAC >3x for 60+ days). Every expansion tab assumes D2C works.
2. **Map your expansion paths** — Use the Expansion Options Matrix to rank paths by readiness trigger, investment, and margin impact.
3. **Sequence, don't parallelize** — One expansion per quarter max. Operator bandwidth collapses with multiple simultaneous expansions.
4. **Run constraint scenarios** — Before committing to any expansion, run 3-4 extreme "what if" scenarios. The insights from scenarios matter more than the scenarios themselves.
5. **Build channel strategy** — Map all acquisition channels with economics, sequencing, and concentration risk rules.
6. **Set up attribution** — Day 1 attribution stack: post-purchase survey + UTM + unique codes. Don't over-engineer.
7. **Amazon: Use the 4-phase cold start** — Seed from D2C, long-tail PPC, Vine program, then broaden. Never skip the cold start.
8. **Maintain price parity** — D2C subscription must always be the best deal. Amazon at MSRP. MAP policy for all wholesale accounts.
9. **B2B: Auto-reorder is mandatory** — Default to standing monthly orders. Without automation, B2B churn is 60-70%.
10. **Model B2B economics separately** — Acquisition cost, service cost, payback, LTV:CAC for B2B are different from D2C.
11. **Retail: Capital plan before entry** — Budget $50-200K+ working capital per retail tier. Never enter national retail without $200K+ uncommitted.
12. **Product expansion: 5/7 triggers before launch** — Use the readiness trigger checklist. Below 5/7, expansion is premature.
13. **Moonshots: Capture now, execute later** — Lock the moonshot lab at <$50K MRR. It's a capture tool, not an execution tool.
14. **Review quarterly** — Quarterly product review, channel mix review, B2B retention review. Annual deep review on formula/packaging/pricing.

## 3. Adaptation Guide

### For Supplement Brands (Highest Reusability)
- Channel landscape and sequencing applies directly
- Amazon playbook is nearly universal for supplements
- B2B targets (gyms, clinics) are shared across supplement category
- Adjust: formula-specific taste/mixability documentation, regulatory claims hierarchy

### For Non-Supplement DTC Brands
- Expansion sequence applies but adjust readiness triggers (e.g., stockout rate irrelevant for software)
- Amazon playbook applies for physical products; skip for digital/software
- B2B segments will differ — replace gym/clinic with industry-appropriate segments
- Adjust: Remove taste/mixability, replace with relevant product quality metrics

### For B2B-First Brands
- Reverse the sequence — B2B is primary, D2C may be secondary or never
- B2B unit economics model is directly transferable
- Retail readiness still applies if pursuing physical retail
- Adjust: Channel strategy shifts to LinkedIn/outbound/trade shows vs Meta/TikTok

## 4. Universal Principles (Apply to Any Expansion)

1. **Expansion amplifies, it doesn't create** — If your core business isn't working, no expansion will fix it
2. **One expansion per quarter, max** — Operator bandwidth is the real bottleneck, not strategy
3. **Model the economics before committing** — Every channel has hidden costs. Model fully-loaded margin, not gross
4. **Price parity across channels** — Your own channels must always offer the best deal to the customer
5. **Automation is retention** — B2B and Amazon both require automated processes to prevent churn/drift
6. **Constraint scenarios reveal truths** — The extreme "what if" always reveals something useful about the real plan
7. **Capital before retail** — Never enter retail without excess capital for slotting, trade spend, and slow payment terms
