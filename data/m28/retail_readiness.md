## 1. The Retail Reality

> **Retail is NOT a growth hack. It's a different business model.**
> Retail amplifies success, it doesn't create it. If D2C isn't working, retail won't save you.

### Pros vs Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Massive volume potential | 40-50% margin hit (distributor + retailer) |
| Brand credibility ("As seen in Whole Foods") | Slotting fees ($5K-50K per SKU to get on shelf) |
| Customer discovery (people who don't shop online) | No customer data (retailer owns it) |
| Impulse purchase at point of need | Chargebacks, returns, compliance requirements |
| — | Long payment terms (60-90 days — cash flow strain) |
| — | One bad review/complaint on shelf = pulled |
| — | Must drive your own velocity (retail doesn't market for you) |

### Retail Progression Path

```
STAGE 1: LOCAL / INDIE (Month 9-12)
├── 5-10 local gyms, health food stores, juice bars
├── Direct relationship with owner
├── No slotting fees
├── Learn: shelf placement, velocity, customer feedback
└── Gate: Product moves without your personal push

STAGE 2: REGIONAL NATURAL (Month 12-18)
├── Erewhon (if LA-based), local natural chains
├── Small slotting fees ($0-5K)
├── Prove velocity: 2+ units/store/week minimum
└── Gate: Regional velocity data + cash reserves

STAGE 3: NATIONAL NATURAL (Month 18-24)
├── Whole Foods, Sprouts, Natural Grocers
├── Requires broker ($5-10K retainer + commission)
├── Slotting: $10-25K
├── Must drive velocity with in-store demos, digital ads
└── Gate: Strong D2C brand, proven regional velocity, $100K cash reserves

STAGE 4: MASS / CLUB (Month 24+)
├── Target, Costco, Walmart
├── Requires established broker, proven velocity data
├── Slotting: $25-100K+
├── Payment terms: 60-90 days (serious cash flow challenge)
└── Gate: $500K+ MRR total, retail-ready operations team
```

## 3. Retail Readiness Checklist

### Must-Have Before Approaching ANY Retail

- [ ] $100K+ MRR in D2C (proves demand without shelf placement)
- [ ] Strong reviews and social proof (retailers Google you)
- [ ] Retail-ready packaging:
  - [ ] UPC/EAN barcodes on all SKUs
  - [ ] Shelf-stable format verified
  - [ ] Packaging that pops on shelf (3 seconds to grab attention)
  - [ ] Nutrition facts panel compliant
  - [ ] Correct net weight declarations
- [ ] Cash reserves for slotting + slow payment (minimum $25K uncommitted)
- [ ] Production capacity for volume (can your supplier handle 5-10x current orders?)
- [ ] Broker relationship (they open doors you can't — budget $5-10K retainer)
- [ ] Pitch deck with velocity data:
  - [ ] D2C velocity (units/month)
  - [ ] Customer demographics
  - [ ] Repeat purchase rate
  - [ ] Marketing spend and plan to drive in-store traffic
- [ ] Trade spend budget (demos, promos, end-caps — $2-5K per retailer per quarter)

### Red Flags (Don't Enter Retail If...)

| Red Flag | Why It's Dangerous |
|----------|-------------------|
| D2C isn't profitable | Retail won't fix broken unit economics — it'll make them worse |
| Only one SKU | Retailers want product line, not single product. Need 2-3 SKUs minimum |
| No broker relationship | Cold-calling buyers doesn't work. Brokers have the relationships |
| Can't fund trade spend | Retail requires ongoing investment in demos, promotions, co-marketing |
| Cash runway <6 months | 60-90 day payment terms will crush cash flow |
| Can't handle returns/chargebacks | Retail has different operations. Damaged product? Your problem |

### Capital Requirements for Retail Expansion *(Dialogue Upgrade U4)*

| Retail Tier | Working Capital Needed | Components |
|-------------|----------------------|------------|
| Local/Indie (5-10 stores) | $5-15K | Inventory + samples, no slotting |
| Regional Natural (10-50 stores) | $25-50K | Inventory + slotting ($0-5K) + broker retainer |
| National Natural (50-200 stores) | $100-200K | Inventory float + slotting ($10-25K) + broker ($10-15K) + trade spend ($15-25K) |
| Mass/Club (200+ stores) | $200-500K+ | Everything above + 60-90 day payment terms on large volume |

**Fundraising trigger**: When retail velocity data proves out at local/indie tier AND D2C is at $200K+ MRR, begin discussions with investors or secure a line of credit.

**Hard rule**: Do NOT enter national retail without $200K+ in uncommitted capital.

**Payment terms financing**: Consider Purchase Order (PO) financing or invoice factoring to bridge 60-90 day payment gaps. Cost: 1-3% of invoice value. Better than cash flow crunch.

## 4. Retail Economics

### Margin Waterfall (Example: National Natural Retailer)

| Line Item | Amount | % of MSRP |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| MSRP (consumer pays) | $49.00 | 100% |
| Retailer margin (40%) | -$19.60 | — |
| Your wholesale price | $29.40 | 60% |
| Distributor cut (if applicable, 15-20%) | -$5.88 | — |
| Your net after distribution | $23.52 | 48% |
| COGS (est.) | -$12.00 | — |
| **Gross Margin** | **$11.52** | **24%** |
| Trade spend per unit (est.) | -$2.00 | — |
| **Net Margin** | **$9.52** | **19%** |

**Comparison**:
- D2C margin: ~50%+
- Amazon margin: ~26%
- Retail margin: ~19%

Retail is a **brand-building** play, not a **margin** play. You do it for legitimacy and volume, not profit.

### Volume Break-Even

| Fixed Costs (Annual) | Amount |
|---------------------|--------|
| Slotting fees (100 stores) | $10,000-25,000 |
| Broker retainer | $10,000-15,000 |
| Trade spend (demos, promos) | $15,000-25,000 |
| Packaging redesign for retail | $5,000-10,000 |
| **Total Fixed Costs** | **$40,000-75,000** |

At $9.52 net margin per unit, need **4,200-7,880 units sold in retail** just to break even on fixed costs. That's 42-79 units per store per year across 100 stores, or about 1 unit per store per week.

## 5. Working with Distributors

### Major Distributors

| Distributor | Focus | Minimum | Margin They Take | Notes |

| KeHE | Natural + conventional | Varies | 20-25% | Growing, good for mid-tier retail |
| DPI (DOT Foods) | Specialty | Lower | 15-20% | Smaller retailers, food service |

### Do You Need a Distributor?

| Scenario | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| 1-10 local stores | NO — deliver yourself, maintain relationship |
| 10-50 regional stores | MAYBE — depends on geography and logistics |
| 50+ stores / national | YES — can't self-distribute at scale |

**Rule**: Delay distributor as long as possible. Every layer between you and the shelf is margin erosion + relationship dilution.
