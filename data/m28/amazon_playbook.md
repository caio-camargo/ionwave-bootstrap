## 1. Amazon: The Strategic Decision

### Opportunity vs Risk

| Opportunity | Risk |
|-------------|------|
| 300M+ active customers | Lower margins (30-40% fees) |
| Customers already searching for electrolytes | Don't own the customer relationship |
| Prime shipping = conversion boost | Race to bottom on price |
| Capture comparison shoppers ("LMNT alternative") | Amazon can copy successful products (Basics line) |
| — | Reviews can make or break you |

### Core Principle

> **Amazon should be INCREMENTAL, not primary.**
> Don't launch on Amazon until D2C works. Amazon is a volume play with lower margin.
> Use it for customers who would never buy D2C, not to replace D2C.

### Price Parity & Anti-Cannibalization Strategy *(Dialogue Upgrade U1)*

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Amazon price ≥ D2C price | Never undercut your own website on Amazon |
| D2C subscription discount > Amazon S&S | D2C subscription = 20% off MSRP ($39.20). Amazon S&S = 15% off ($41.65). D2C always wins |
| D2C-exclusive bundles | Offer bundles on D2C that are NOT available on Amazon |
| Amazon-to-D2C bridge | Insert card in Amazon shipments: "Get 20% off your next order at ionwave.com" |
| Cannibalization monitoring | Track D2C subscriber churn rate monthly post-Amazon launch. If D2C churn spikes >3 percentage points within 60 days of Amazon launch, investigate immediately |

**Pricing Hierarchy (Must Maintain)**:
1. D2C Subscription = BEST deal ($39.20 = 20% off)
2. D2C One-time = MSRP ($49.00)
3. Amazon S&S = 15% off ($41.65)
4. Amazon One-time = MSRP ($49.00)

### When to Launch on Amazon

All must be TRUE before proceeding:

- [ ] D2C profitable and growing (at least $50K MRR)
- [ ] Strong review base on own site (can seed Amazon reviews from existing customers)
- [ ] Unit economics work at Amazon margins (~30-40% fees)
- [ ] Inventory/supply chain can handle two channels simultaneously
- [ ] Brand Registry completed (requires active trademark)
- [ ] NOT because D2C is struggling

## 2. Amazon Economics

### Fee Structure

| Item | Amount | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Selling Price | $49 | Match D2C or slightly lower |
| Referral Fee (15%) | -$7.35 | Category dependent, 15% is standard for supplements |
| FBA Fee (est.) | -$5 to -$8 | Size/weight dependent |
| Storage Fees | -$1 to -$2/mo | Monthly, higher Q4 (Oct-Dec) |
| PPC/Ads | -$5 to -$10/unit | Required at launch, decreases with organic rank |
| COGS | -$[X] | Same as D2C |
| **Net Margin** | **$[Calculate with actual COGS]** | Must be positive |

| Line Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Revenue | $47.00 |

## 3. Launch Checklist

### Pre-Launch (2-4 weeks before listing goes live)

- [ ] Amazon Seller account created (Professional plan, $39.99/mo)
- [ ] Brand Registry completed (need active trademark)
- [ ] Product photography — Amazon-specific requirements:
  - [ ] Main image: white background, product only, 85%+ of frame
  - [ ] 6+ additional images including lifestyle shots
  - [ ] Infographic images (ingredients, comparison, how-to-use)
- [ ] A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content) created
- [ ] Keyword research completed (tools: Helium 10, Jungle Scout)
- [ ] Competitive pricing analysis (LMNT, Liquid IV, DrinkLMNT on Amazon)
- [ ] FBA vs FBM decision (usually FBA for supplements)
- [ ] Inventory sent to FBA warehouses (ship 60-90 days supply)
- [ ] UPC/EAN barcodes obtained

### Launch (Week 1-2)

- [ ] Listing live with optimized title, bullets, description
- [ ] PPC campaigns set up (auto + manual campaigns)
- [ ] Launch promotions activated (coupons, Lightning Deals if eligible)
- [ ] Review generation strategy initiated (compliant — see Section 5)
- [ ] Subscribe & Save enabled (if eligible)

### Ongoing (Weekly/Monthly)

- [ ] PPC optimization — weekly bid adjustments
- [ ] Review monitoring — daily check, respond to all negative reviews
- [ ] Inventory management — reorder at 45-day supply level
- [ ] Competitor monitoring — pricing, new listings, review trends
- [ ] A+ Content refresh — quarterly at minimum
- [ ] Keyword ranking tracking — weekly

## 4. Listing Optimization Playbook

### Process

1. Identify top 3 competitors with best conversion on Amazon
2. Screenshot and document their entire listing
3. Extract: headlines, bullet points, descriptions, image strategy
4. Rewrite each element with IonWave product/brand positioning
5. A/B test variations (Amazon Experiments tool)
6. Iterate based on performance data

### Elements to Optimize

| Element | Best Practice | Who to Study | IonWave Angle |
|---------|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| Hero image | Clean, product-focused, lifestyle context | Seed, AG1 | Marine/ocean blue aesthetic, clean pack shot |
| Headline | Benefit-first, keyword-rich, <200 chars | LMNT | "78 Ocean Minerals • Complete Hydration • Zero Sugar" |
| Bullet points | Benefits > features, social proof, scannable | Ritual | Lead with mineral count, ocean source story |
| Description | Story + benefits + trust signals | Athletic Greens | Founder story, science backing, comparison to 3-mineral products |
| Social proof | Reviews, press mentions, certifications | Seed | Subscriber count, D2C review highlights |
| Price display | Subscription savings prominent | LMNT | Subscribe & Save discount visible |
| A+ Content | Brand story + comparison charts + FAQ | Liquid IV | 78 vs 3 mineral comparison, ocean sourcing infographic |

### Title Formula

```
[Brand] [Product Type] [Key Benefit] - [Key Differentiator] - [Format] - [Count]

Example:
IonWave Marine Electrolytes for Complete Hydration - 78 Ocean Minerals - Powder Stick Packs - 30 Count
```

### Bullet Point Framework (5 bullets)

1. **Primary benefit**: What it does for the customer
2. **Key differentiator**: 78 minerals vs 3 (LMNT, Liquid IV)
3. **Ingredient story**: Ocean-sourced, no artificial anything
4. **Usage**: How/when to use, serving suggestion
5. **Trust signal**: Satisfaction guarantee, subscriber count, tested/certified

## 5. Review Generation Protocol

### Target: 50 Reviews in 60 Days

Why: Social proof unlocks conversion. Below 50 reviews = low trust on Amazon. First 50 reviews are the hardest and most important.

### Compliant Methods (Amazon TOS Safe)

| Method | Timing | Expected Response Rate | Notes |
|--------|--------|----------------------|-------|
| Post-purchase email | Day 14 after delivery | 10-15% | "If you love IonWave, we'd appreciate a review" |
| SMS request | Day 21 after delivery | 5-10% | Quick text, link directly to review page |
| Package insert card | In every box | 2-5% | Physical card asking for honest review |
| Follow-up sequence | Day 30 (non-responders) | +3-5% | Second gentle ask |
| Amazon "Request a Review" button | Day 7-21 | 1-3% | Built-in Amazon tool, one-click |

### What NOT to Do (Amazon TOS Violations)

| ❌ Violation | Consequence |
|-------------|-------------|
| Offer incentives for reviews (discount, free product) | Account suspension |
| Buy fake reviews | Account termination, legal action |
| Ask for 5-star reviews specifically | TOS violation |
| Review gating (only sending happy customers to review) | TOS violation |
| Friends/family reviews | Detected and removed by Amazon |

### The Math

| Metric | Number |
|--------|--------|
| Reviews needed | 50 |
| Assume 10% review rate | — |
| Customers needed | 500 |
| Timeframe | 60 days |
| Orders per day | ~8 |
| At $49 AOV | $400/day revenue on Amazon |

**Implication**: To hit 50 reviews in 60 days, Amazon needs to be doing ~$12K/mo in revenue. This is achievable if D2C is driving awareness and Amazon captures overflow/comparison shoppers.

### Amazon Cold Start Strategy *(Dialogue Upgrade U6)*

The #1 challenge for a new Amazon listing: zero reviews, zero sales history, zero organic rank. PPC alone won't solve it.

| Phase | Timing | Strategy | Goal |
|-------|--------|----------|------|
| Phase 1: Seed from D2C | Week 1-2 | Email D2C customer list → "We're on Amazon! Leave a review" | 20-30 sales + 5-10 reviews |
| Phase 2: Long-tail PPC | Week 2-4 | Target keywords competitors ignore: "ocean mineral electrolyte", "78 mineral hydration" (NOT "electrolyte powder") | First page rank on low-competition terms |
| Phase 3: Vine Program | Week 4-8 | Enroll in Amazon Vine (official review program, ~$200/product) | 15-30 verified reviews |
| Phase 4: Broaden PPC | Week 8+ | Scale PPC to broader keywords as organic rank improves | Sustainable organic + paid traffic mix |

**Critical rule**: DO NOT run broad PPC on "electrolyte powder" until you have 50+ reviews and 3.5+ average stars. You'll burn money competing against LMNT's 50K+ reviews.
