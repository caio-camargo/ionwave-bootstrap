### Claims Hierarchy (What IonWave Can Safely Say)

| Level | What You Can Say | Example | Risk |
|-------|-----------------|---------|------|
| **GREEN — Safe** | Structure/function claims (no disease references) | "Supports hydration and mineral balance" | LOW |
| **YELLOW — Careful** | Comparative claims with evidence | "More complete mineral profile than single-electrolyte products" | MEDIUM — need substantiation file |
| **RED — Avoid** | Disease claims, cure claims, diagnosis | "Prevents muscle cramps" or "treats dehydration" | HIGH — FDA warning letter territory |

### Research Gaps (Intelligence Gaps)

1. **No head-to-head studies**: IonWave's specific formulation vs LMNT/Liquid IV — would need to commission
2. **Bioavailability claim unverified**: Ocean-source ionic minerals "more bioavailable" — need independent assay
3. **Trace mineral dose-response**: What dose of trace minerals produces measurable benefit? Literature sparse
4. **Long-term supplementation data**: Safety profile for daily ocean mineral concentrate use — no long-term studies found

## 4. Strategic Implications

### What This Landscape Tells Us

1. **Strengths are real but perishable** — mineral count and price advantage can be copied. Brand, subscription stickiness, and community are durable moats.
2. **Biggest existential risk is single supplier** — must address before scale. Everything else is manageable.
3. **Science story is the differentiator but needs better evidence** — SCI-002 and SCI-003 are the claims that separate IonWave from competitors, and they're the weakest evidence level. Invest in substantiation.
4. **Expansion should amplify proven D2C, never replace it** — every expansion tab (Amazon, B2B, retail) says the same thing: don't expand until core works. This is the central thesis of M28.
