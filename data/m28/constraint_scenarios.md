## 1. Why Constraint Scenarios?

Extreme constraints reveal hidden assumptions and force creative thinking.

**Rules**:
- You don't have to do these — but thinking through them makes your actual plan better
- Each scenario asks: "What would have to be true?" → "How could it possibly work?" → "What does this reveal about our plan?"
- The reveal is the point. The scenario is just the tool.

## 2. Constraint Scenarios

### Scenario 1: $100M Revenue in 12 Months

**What would have to be true?**

| Metric | Number | Implication |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Monthly revenue | $8.3M | 166x current target |
| Monthly orders at $50 AOV | 170,000 | Massive multi-channel |
| Daily orders | 5,700 | Full logistics operation |
| Channels required | D2C + Retail + B2B + Amazon | Can't get there with one channel |

**How could it possibly work?**
- Launch in 500+ retail stores simultaneously (requires distributor: UNFI, KeHE)
- Secure $20M+ in growth capital
- Acquire an existing electrolyte brand with distribution
- B2B: Gyms, hotels, airlines buying in bulk
- Massive influencer + PR campaign (Super Bowl level)

**What this reveals about our plan:**
1. **D2C alone can't get there** — ceiling is probably $3-5M/yr for a niche D2C electrolyte brand
2. Would need to become a fundamentally different kind of company (CPG, not DTC startup)
3. **Capital would be the main constraint**, not strategy or execution
4. Retail distribution is the unlock at this scale, but it requires years of brand building first

**Intelligence to steal**: Even if we never target $100M, this shows that building retail-ready brand equity from Day 1 (packaging, certifications, velocity data) keeps options open.

### Scenario 2: $100K MRR in 3 Months (Not 12)

**What would have to be true?**

| Metric | Number | Implication |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Launch timeline | 30 days (not 60) | Half the prep time |
| CAC from Day 1 | Under $30 | No learning curve allowed |
| Viral coefficient | >1 (each customer brings >1 new) | Word-of-mouth machine |
| Awareness event | Major PR hit | Earned media at scale |

**How could it possibly work?**
- Pre-launch waitlist of 5,000+ (convert at 10% = 500 customers Day 1)
- Coordinated influencer launch: 20 influencers posting same day
- PR story that goes viral (founder health journey, ocean science angle)
- Launch with subscription-only model (guarantees recurring from Day 1)
- Aggressive referral: give $20, get $20

**What this reveals about our plan:**
1. **We should build a pre-launch list** — this is actionable regardless of timeline
2. **Influencer strategy matters more than we thought** (validates M23)
3. **PR could be undervalued in our plan** — one viral story > $50K in ads
4. Subscription-first model is correct — validates current approach

**Intelligence to steal**: Pre-launch list building + coordinated influencer launch + PR story should be in the Phase 0 plan regardless of speed target.

### Scenario 3: $0 Marketing Budget

**What would have to be true?**

| Requirement | How |
|-------------|-----|
| Product so good it spreads organically | Taste, results, packaging all must be exceptional |
| Content strategy driving SEO traffic | Long-form blog, YouTube, social content |
| Referral program that actually works | Strong incentive + easy sharing mechanics |
| PR/earned media only | Founder story, science angle, press outreach |

**How could it possibly work?**
- Build in public (Twitter/X, YouTube) — founder's journey content
- SEO content: "best electrolytes", "LMNT alternative", "electrolyte benefits"
- Aggressive referral: give $20, get $20 (funded by saved CAC budget)
- Seed to micro-influencers for free product only (COGS only, not marketing spend)
- Community building: Reddit, Facebook groups, health forums

**What this reveals about our plan:**
1. **Our organic/content strategy is weak** — we're over-indexed on paid
2. **Referral program should be stronger** — currently an afterthought
3. **SEO is a real channel** we're ignoring — long-tail health content could be compounding asset
4. Build-in-public creates content + trust + audience simultaneously

**Intelligence to steal**: Even with marketing budget, invest in organic channels as insurance. If paid stops working (algorithm change, account ban), organic is the safety net.

### Scenario 4: 10 Hours/Week from Day 1 (Not Month 4)

**What would have to be true?**

| Requirement | How |
|-------------|-----|
| Everything automated or delegated | SOPs for every repeatable task |
| Playbooks so good anyone can execute | Bootstrap itself is this playbook |
| Systems that run without intervention | Subscription auto-renew, email flows, reporting dashboards |

**How could it possibly work?**
- Hire operator from Day 1 (equity only, no cash comp initially)
- Use AI for customer support, content generation, reporting
- Pre-build everything before launch (email flows, SOPs, dashboards)
- Only do strategy, delegate all execution
- Ruthlessly cut: anything that doesn't directly drive revenue or retention in Month 1-3

**What this reveals about our plan:**
1. **We could probably automate more than we think** — question every manual process
2. **Some things we currently plan to do aren't necessary** — what if we just didn't do them?
3. **The 10hr/week goal might be achievable earlier than Month 4** if we build systems first
4. **AI tools are underutilized** in our current plan — CS, content, analysis can be largely automated

**Intelligence to steal**: Build the 10hr/week operating model from Day 1, even if you spend more hours initially. The systems should be designed for 10hr/week operation, even if launch requires 30hr/week.

| Metric | Value | Source |

| Target | $100K MRR | M0 Trade Thesis |

### Sensitivity Analysis — What If Things Go Wrong?

| Scenario | CAC | Margin/Order | Orders Needed for $100K | Viable? |
|----------|-----|-------------|------------------------|---------|
| **Best case** | $30 | $19 | 1,754/mo (58/day) | ✅ YES |

| **Conservative** | $40 | $9 | 3,704/mo (123/day) | ⚠️ TIGHT — need LTV to save it |
| **Worst case** | $50 | -$1 | N/A | ❌ UNPROFITABLE — kill or pivot |

### Sensitivity: Conversion Rate Impact

| Conversion Rate | Traffic Needed for 68 Orders/Day | At $1 CPC | Daily Ad Spend |
|-----------------|----------------------------------|-----------|----------------|
| 4% | 1,700 visitors | $1,700 | Feasible |
| 3% | 2,267 visitors | $2,267 | Feasible |
| 2% | 3,400 visitors | $3,400 | Stretching |
| 1% | 6,800 visitors | $6,800 | Unsustainable |

### Sensitivity: Churn Impact on LTV

| Monthly Churn | Average Lifetime (months) | LTV at $49 AOV | LTV:CAC at $35 CAC |
|---------------|--------------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 5% | 20 | $980 | 28x ✅ |
| 8% | 12.5 | $612 | 17.5x ✅ |
| 12% | 8.3 | $407 | 11.6x ✅ |
| 20% | 5 | $245 | 7x ✅ |
| 30% | 3.3 | $163 | 4.7x ⚠️ |

**Key insight**: Even at pessimistic churn rates, LTV:CAC is strong. The real risk is CAC inflation, not churn. If CAC hits $50, nothing else matters.

### Kill Criteria — When to Stop

| Condition | Threshold | Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| CAC consistently too high | >$50 for 14 consecutive days | Any time | Pause paid, diagnose creative/targeting/offer |
| Contribution margin negative | Negative for 30 consecutive days | Any time | Emergency review: pricing, COGS, channel mix |
| Cash runway critical | <60 days with no path to profit | Any time | Activate emergency protocol: cut all non-essential spend |
| Conversion rate collapse | <1% site-wide for 14 days | Post-launch | LP audit, offer audit, technical audit |
| Churn spike | >25% monthly for 2 consecutive months | Post-launch | Product quality audit, customer interviews, CS review |

## 5. SEO Sequencing *(Dialogue Upgrade U8)*

SEO is a real channel but has 6-12 month payback. For a founder doing 30hr/week at launch, spending time on SEO that won't pay off for 6 months is wrong allocation.

| Phase | When | SEO Activity | Why |
|-------|------|-------------|-----|
| Pre-launch | Month -2 to 0 | **ZERO SEO** — focus on pre-launch list, influencer coordination, paid ads, email flows | Every hour pre-launch must drive Day 1 revenue |
| Phase 2 | Month 3-6 | 2 articles/month targeting comparison keywords ("LMNT alternative", "best electrolyte 2026") | Building organic foundation while paid is working |
| Phase 3 | Month 6+ | 4 articles/month if organic traffic growing >10% MoM | Scale what's working |
| Ongoing | Month 9+ | SEO audit, keyword gap analysis, content refresh cycle | Compounding returns |

## 6. Subscription Conversion Sensitivity *(Dialogue Upgrade U12)*

The portfolio reality check assumes subscription LTV, but what if subscription conversion is lower than expected?

| Subscription Rate | LTV Estimate | LTV:CAC (at $35 CAC) | Status |
|-------------------|-------------|----------------------|--------|
| 50% (optimistic) | $612 | 17.5x | ✅ STRONG |
| 40% (base case) | ~$490 | 14x | ✅ GOOD |
| 30% (conservative) | ~$370 | 10.6x | ✅ VIABLE — monitor |
| 20% (pessimistic) | ~$250 | 7.1x | ⚠️ YELLOW — investigate onboarding |
| 10% (crisis) | ~$130 | 3.7x | ❌ RED — fundamental problem |

### Subscription Conversion Improvement Levers

| If Rate Is Below 30% | Investigation | Fix |
|----------------------|---------------|-----|
| Checkout flow | Is subscription option prominent? Easy to understand? | A/B test subscription-first checkout |
| Pricing incentive | Is subscription discount compelling enough? | Test 15% vs 20% vs 25% discount |
| Trust barrier | Do customers trust auto-renewal? | Add "skip/cancel anytime" messaging prominently |
| Competitor comparison | Are competitors' subscription terms better? | Match or beat best-in-class subscription offer |
