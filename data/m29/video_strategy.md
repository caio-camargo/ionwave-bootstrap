## 1. Why Video (and Why Not Yet)

### 1.1 The Case FOR Video

- YouTube is the 2nd largest search engine — video content ranks in Google
- Video builds trust faster than text (face, voice, personality)
- Evergreen video content compounds views over time
- One video generates multiple assets: clips, social posts, thumbnails, transcripts → blog posts
- Supplement/health content performs well on YouTube (large audience demand)

### 1.2 The Case for DEFERRING Video

- Video production takes 3-5x more time per piece than blog content
- YouTube algorithm rewards consistency — sporadic posting is worse than not posting
- Blog/SEO content has better per-hour ROI for a solo founder in Year 1
- Video equipment, editing, and optimization have a learning curve
- A bad video (poor audio, lighting, pacing) can actively hurt brand perception

### 1.3 The Verdict

**Defer YouTube as a channel until Month 4-6.** Focus Phase 0-1 entirely on blog content (M16) and paid ads (M13). Video enters the mix only when:
1. Blog content cadence is established (1-2 posts/week running smoothly)
2. Paid ads have a winning creative (Milestone 3: Winner Found)
3. Either: Caio allocates dedicated video time, OR a part-time editor is hired ($200-500/month freelance)

**Exception**: Short-form video (TikTok/Reels, 30-60 seconds) can start earlier because it's low production. Talking-head clips filmed on iPhone, no editing needed. This is covered in M16's social media distribution strategy.

### 3.3 YouTube SEO Optimization

Every video should be optimized for YouTube search (YouTube is a search engine):

**Per-video checklist:**
- [ ] Title: Include primary keyword, <60 characters, curiosity-generating
- [ ] Description: 200+ words, primary keyword in first 2 lines, timestamps, links to product + blog
- [ ] Tags: 5-10 relevant tags including primary keyword and variations
- [ ] Thumbnail: Custom, high-contrast, face with expression, text overlay (3-5 words max)
- [ ] Cards + End screens: Link to related videos and product page
- [ ] Pinned comment: CTA with link
- [ ] Chapters/timestamps: Every 2-3 minutes for longer videos
- [ ] Subtitles: Upload SRT file (improves accessibility + SEO)

## 4. Shorts Strategy

YouTube Shorts (and TikTok/Reels) deserve a separate strategy because they operate on different algorithms:

### 4.1 Shorts Content Types

| Type | Example | Goal | Effort |
|------|---------|------|--------|
| **Hook clips** | First 15 seconds of a longer video | Drive to long-form | Very low (clip existing content) |
| **Quick tips** | "One thing about electrolytes most people get wrong" | Discovery | Low (1-2 min to film) |
| **Product showcase** | Mixing IonWave, taste test reaction | Brand awareness | Low |
| **Trending audio** | Ride trending sounds with relevant content | Algorithm boost | Low |
| **Science nuggets** | "Did you know ocean water has 78 minerals?" | Education | Low |

### 4.2 Shorts Best Practices

- **Hook in first 1-2 seconds** — no intro, no logo, jump straight to the interesting thing
- **Vertical format** (9:16), 15-60 seconds
- **Text overlay** — assume no sound (many viewers watch muted)
- **Post consistently** — 3-5 Shorts/week is manageable; daily is ideal if batch-filming
- **Batch-film**: Set up once, film 10-15 Shorts in one session (1-2 hours → 2-3 weeks of content)

## 5. Metrics & Targets

### 5.1 YouTube Growth Targets (Realistic for New Channel)

| Metric | Month 6 | Month 12 | Month 18 |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Subscribers** | 200-500 | 1,000-3,000 | 3,000-8,000 |
| **Views/month** | 2,000-5,000 | 10,000-30,000 | 30,000-80,000 |
| **Avg. watch time** | 3+ min | 4+ min | 5+ min |
| **CTR (impressions → clicks)** | >4% | >5% | >6% |
| **Traffic to site (GA4)** | 50-200/month | 200-800/month | 500-2,000/month |

**Danilo's targets vs. reality**:
- Danilo: 1K subs + 10K views/month by Month 6
- Reality: 200-500 subs + 2-5K views/month by Month 6 (starting at Month 4, not Month 1)

**Note**: YouTube growth is exponential, not linear. Months 1-6 will feel painfully slow. The algorithm rewards consistency over 12+ months. Most successful channels see hockey-stick growth at 50-100 videos, not before.

### 5.2 When to Invest More in Video

Escalate video investment (hire editor, upgrade equipment, increase cadence) when:
- A single video reaches 10K+ views (signal of market fit)
- YouTube drives 100+ site visits/month consistently
- Subscriber growth rate exceeds 100/month
- Revenue supports $300-500/month video production budget

[Confidence: C | Source: YouTube growth benchmarks for small brand channels. Highly variable — some channels explode early, most build slowly.]
