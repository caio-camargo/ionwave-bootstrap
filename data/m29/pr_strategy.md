## Purpose

Define IonWave's earned media strategy: how to get press coverage, podcast appearances, and brand mentions without a PR budget. This file synthesizes Danilo's future press release (strong strategic artifact), milestone press releases (4 pre-written), and media tracker (12 outlets + 5 podcasts) into an actionable PR playbook for a bootstrapped DTC supplement brand.

**The honest truth about PR at this stage**: Earned media is high-leverage but low-probability per pitch. Most early-stage DTC brands get zero press coverage in Year 1 unless they have a genuinely novel story. IonWave's marine plasma angle is novel enough to be interesting — but only if pitched correctly to the right people.

**PR will NOT**:
- Replace paid acquisition or content marketing as a growth channel
- Deliver predictable, repeatable traffic like SEO or ads
- Work through spray-and-pray press release distribution services

**PR WILL**:
- Generate high-DA backlinks that turbocharge SEO (M16 link building strategy)
- Create social proof for landing pages and ads ("As seen in...")
- Build founder credibility that compounds across all channels
- Provide content for email and social distribution

[Confidence: B | Source: DTC brand PR consensus — earned media is valuable but unpredictable. ROI is real but indirect.]

## 1. Future Press Release (Amazon "Working Backwards" Method)

### 1.1 The Technique

Write a press release as if it's 18 months from now and you've succeeded. This forces clarity on what success looks like and what must be true to get there. Amazon uses this as a planning tool — the press release is the first document written for any new product.

**Date this press release**: August 2027 (18 months from February 2026 launch target)

### 1.2 The Press Release

---

**THE NEW YORK TIMES — Business Section**
*August 2027*

**IonWave Disrupts $5B Electrolyte Market With Ocean-Sourced Formula, Hits $1.2M Annual Revenue**

*By [Reporter Name], Health & Wellness Correspondent*

In an industry dominated by synthetic formulas and sugar-laden sports drinks, a small startup has carved out a devoted following by going back to basics — or more precisely, back to the ocean.

IonWave, founded just 18 months ago, has reached $100,000 in monthly recurring revenue by selling a premium electrolyte powder containing 78 trace minerals sourced from pristine ocean water. The company has done so with no retail presence, no celebrity endorsements, and a marketing budget that would be a rounding error for competitors like Gatorade or LMNT.

"We didn't try to out-spend the big players," says Caio, who runs the company with a small team. "We focused on the science and let our customers do the talking."

The company's secret weapon: a fanatically loyal customer base with a 45% subscription rate — nearly unheard of in the supplements industry. Customer reviews consistently mention feeling "actually different" compared to other electrolyte products, which typically contain only 3-4 minerals.

"Your body is 70% water with the same mineral composition as ocean water," the company's website explains. "Why would you replenish it with something synthetic?"

The approach has resonated particularly with the keto and carnivore diet communities, where electrolyte supplementation is considered essential. Influencers in these spaces have organically promoted the product, driving word-of-mouth growth that now accounts for 30% of new customers.

Industry analysts note that IonWave represents a broader trend of premium, science-backed supplements challenging legacy brands. "The new generation of consumers wants to understand what they're putting in their bodies. They'll pay more for quality and transparency."

The company plans to expand into new product lines while maintaining its direct-to-consumer model. "We're not trying to be in every store," Caio says. "We're trying to be in every home that cares about what they consume."

### 1.4 Minimum Viable Press Pitch *(Dialogue upgrade U5)*

The future press release is the aspirational end state. But you can pitch a story well before all 8 preconditions are green. Here's what must be true before ANY media outreach:

**Must-have for any pitch** (no pitch without these):
- [ ] Product is shipping to real customers
- [ ] At least 20+ genuine customer reviews (social proof exists)
- [ ] Website is professional and complete (About page, Science page, FDA disclaimers)
- [ ] Founder has a 2-sentence pitch ready: who you are, what's different, why it matters
- [ ] COA (Certificate of Analysis) available if journalist asks about quality

**Nice-to-have for better pitches**:
- [ ] $10K+ MRR (revenue adds legitimacy)
- [ ] 30%+ subscription rate (story: "customers keep coming back")
- [ ] 1-2 podcast appearances already (social proof for journalists)
- [ ] A unique data point or customer story that's genuinely interesting

**Dream tier (for A-list outlets)**:
- [ ] $50K+ MRR
- [ ] 100+ reviews at 4.5+ stars
- [ ] Named expert (RD, MD) endorsement
- [ ] Original research or data that no one else has

[Confidence: B | Source: DTC PR practitioner consensus — journalists want proof of traction, not just a good product.]

## 2. Milestone Press Releases

### 2.1 Why Milestone Press Releases Matter

These aren't for distribution to media (most outlets won't care about a pre-revenue startup's milestones). They serve three purposes:
1. **Internal alignment** — crystallize what success looks like at each stage
2. **Investor updates** — professional milestone communication for angels/advisors
3. **Social media content** — condensed versions work as milestone announcements on LinkedIn/Twitter

### 2.2 Milestone 1: Table Set

**When**: Supply chain, credibility, and initial capital secured
**Headline**: IonWave Secures Supply Chain for Revolutionary Ocean-Sourced Electrolyte

**Key messages**:
- Partnership with marine mineral supplier secured
- Proprietary 78-mineral formula mirrors human plasma mineral composition
- Commitments from health & wellness influencers for launch support
- Shipping timeline communicated

**Distribution**: LinkedIn post (founder), email to advisor network, social media teaser

### 2.3 Milestone 2: First Blood

**When**: First real sale ships, money clears
**Headline**: IonWave Ships First Orders, Customers Report Immediate Results

**Key messages**:
- Commercial launch achieved
- Early customer feedback positive (quote with permission)
- Initial order volume and subscription rate signal product-market fit
- Available exclusively DTC

**Distribution**: Social media announcement, email to full list, update to investors/advisors

### 2.4 Milestone 3: Winner Found

**When**: 3+ ads hit >2.0 ROAS consistently
**Headline**: IonWave Cracks Growth Code, Prepares to Scale

**Key messages**:
- Scalable customer acquisition strategy identified
- Tested multiple ad variations to find what resonates
- Key metric milestones (subscription rate, NPS/CSAT)
- Growth acceleration plan

**Distribution**: LinkedIn (founder credibility), investor update, internal team milestone

### 2.5 Milestone 4: $100K MRR

**When**: North star revenue target achieved
**Headline**: IonWave Reaches $100K Monthly Revenue, Proves Premium D2C Model

**Key messages**:
- Revenue milestone achieved in [X] months
- Key metrics: subscription rate, growth rate, review count, LTV:CAC ratio
- Competing with billion-dollar brands through quality and transparency, not spend
- Next phase: product line expansion, team growth

**Distribution**: Full press push (this is the milestone worthy of real media outreach), LinkedIn, investor update, potential for industry publication coverage

## 3. Media Outreach Strategy

### 3.1 The Bootstrapped PR Approach

**Budget reality**: $0 for PR agencies. $0 for press release distribution services (PRNewswire etc. cost $500-2,000+ per release and deliver negligible value for unknown brands).

**What actually works for bootstrapped DTC brands**:

| Tactic | Cost | Effort | Expected Outcome | Timeline |
|--------|------|--------|-----------------|----------|
| **Expert quote platforms** (Qwoted, Featured.com, Connectively) | Free | 15-30 min/day | 1-3 media mentions/month | Month 1+ |
| **Podcast guesting** | Free | 2-3 hrs/week (outreach + recording) | 1-2 appearances/month | Month 2-3+ |
| **Direct journalist outreach** | Free | 1-2 hrs/week | 1-2 responses per 20 pitches | Month 3+ |
| **Press release distribution** | $500-2,000 | Low | Near-zero for unknown brands | Skip |
| **PR agency** | $3,000-10,000/month | Low (outsourced) | Premature at this stage | Phase 3+ |

### Weekly PR Time Budget *(Dialogue upgrade U1)*

**Phase 1 (Month 1-3): 2-3 hrs/week MAX**
- Expert quote platforms: 15 min/day × 5 days = 1.25 hrs
- Podcast outreach: 1 hr/week (5 pitches + follow-ups)
- Journalist outreach: 0 (not yet — build quote platform track record first)

**Phase 2 (Month 4-6): 3-4 hrs/week**
- Expert platforms: 1.25 hrs/week (continue)
- Podcast outreach + recording: 2 hrs/week
- Direct journalist pitches: 0.5-1 hr/week

**Phase 3 (Month 7-12): 4-5 hrs/week**
- Expert platforms: 1 hr/week (routine by now)
- Podcast guesting: 2 hrs/week
- Media outreach: 1-2 hrs/week

**The ROI case**: PR's primary value is not direct traffic — it's backlinks that accelerate M16 SEO. A single DA 70+ backlink from a media mention can be worth $500-2,000 in equivalent link building effort. 3 hrs/week × 4 months to get 3-5 quality backlinks = excellent ROI on time invested.

[Confidence: B | Source: DTC founder consensus — expert quote platforms and podcast guesting are the highest-ROI PR tactics for bootstrapped brands. HARO (now Connectively) was the gold standard before its 2024 transition; Qwoted and Featured.com are the current alternatives.]

### 3.2 Expert Quote Platforms

HARO (Help a Reporter Out) was the dominant platform for getting media mentions by responding to journalist queries. It transitioned to Connectively in 2024 and has since been acquired/sunset. The current landscape:

| Platform | How It Works | Cost | Best For | Response Rate |
|----------|-------------|------|----------|---------------|
| **Qwoted** | Journalists post queries, experts respond | Free (basic) | General health/wellness media | 5-15% response-to-publish rate |
| **Featured.com** | AI-matched journalist queries | Free + paid tiers | Business/startup media | 8-20% response-to-publish rate |
| **Quoted** | Curated expert matching | Free | Niche health publications | Variable |
| **ProfNet (PR Newswire)** | Journalist query service | $2,500+/year | Skip — too expensive for bootstrapped | N/A |

**Daily routine (15-30 minutes)**:
1. Check Qwoted and Featured.com for relevant queries
2. Respond to anything related to: electrolytes, hydration, supplements, wellness, ocean minerals, keto, fasting, DTC brands, bootstrapped startups
3. Keep responses concise (2-3 paragraphs max), credential yourself clearly
4. Include a headshot and one-line bio with brand mention

**Expected output**: 1-3 media mentions per month after 60-90 days of consistent responses. Each mention typically includes a backlink (DA 40-80), which directly feeds M16 SEO strategy.

[Confidence: C | Source: DTC founder reports on Qwoted/Featured.com conversion rates. HARO shutdown confirmed; replacement landscape is fragmented.]

**How to actually get covered** (not by sending press releases):
1. **Become a source first** — respond to journalist queries on expert platforms
2. **Send product samples** — to writers who cover supplements (with personalized note)
3. **Pitch a story, not a product** — "The science behind ocean mineral supplementation" not "Check out our product"
4. **Leverage milestones** — revenue milestones, customer count, unique data points
5. **Be responsive** — journalists work on tight deadlines. Reply within hours, not days.

**Realistic expectations**: For a pre-revenue brand, expect 0-2 media mentions in first 6 months through direct outreach. Expert quote platforms are more likely to produce results. The big press push happens at Milestone 4 ($100K MRR) when there's a real story to tell.

### 3.4 Podcast Target List

| Priority | Podcast | Host | Audience | Pitch Angle |
|----------|---------|------|----------|-------------|
| **1** | The Keto Savage Podcast | Robert Sikes | Keto community | IonWave's formulation for keto/fasting — highest ICP alignment |
| **2** | Fundamental Health | Paul Saladino | Carnivore/ancestral | Marine plasma science — ocean mineral sourcing story |
| **3** | Mind Pump | Sal Di Stefano | Fitness/bodybuilding | Electrolyte science, hydration for training |
| **4** | The Model Health Show | Shawn Stevenson | Health/wellness | Trace minerals, sleep + hydration connection |
| **5** | Ben Greenfield Life | Ben Greenfield | Biohacking | Marine plasma as novel biohacking ingredient |

**Podcast guesting strategy**:

1. **Start small, work up** — Don't pitch Ben Greenfield first. Start with smaller podcasts (5K-20K downloads) in the keto/fasting niche. Build a "podcast resume" with 3-5 smaller appearances before pitching A-list shows.

2. **Build relationships before pitching** — Engage with hosts on social media for 2-4 weeks. Comment on episodes. Share their content. Then DM with a pitch.

3. **Pitch template** (not the actual pitch — customize every time):
   - Subject: "[Topic that serves their audience]" — not "Can I be on your podcast?"
   - 1 sentence: Who you are + relevant credential
   - 2 sentences: What you'd talk about (topics their audience cares about)
   - 1 sentence: Why their audience specifically would benefit
   - Link to your website or a previous podcast appearance

4. **Talking points to prepare**:
   - Founder story: Why marine plasma? What's the personal connection?
   - Science: 78 minerals, isotonic composition, René Quinton research
   - Contrarian take: "Most electrolyte supplements are missing 74 minerals"
   - Practical advice: Electrolyte protocols for keto/fasting/training
   - Don't: Hard-sell the product. Be the expert, not the salesperson.

5. **Post-appearance leverage**:
   - Share episode on all social channels
   - Email to list: "I was on [podcast] — here's what we discussed"
   - Add to website "As Featured On" section
   - Request show notes include link to IonWave (backlink value)

**Realistic timeline**: First pitch → first appearance: 6-12 weeks. Expect 1 booking per 8-10 pitches to smaller podcasts, 1 per 15-20 pitches to larger ones.

## 4. PR Timeline (Phase-Gated)

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch (Month -1 to 0)
- [ ] Set up Google Alerts for "IonWave", "marine plasma electrolyte", competitor names
- [ ] Create accounts on Qwoted, Featured.com
- [ ] Draft founder bio, headshot, press kit (1-page PDF: brand story, founder bio, product photos, key stats)
- [ ] Begin responding to expert queries daily (build reputation before launch)

### Phase 1: Launch (Month 1-3)
- [ ] Distribute Milestone 1 (Table Set) via social + email
- [ ] Distribute Milestone 2 (First Blood) when first sale ships
- [ ] Continue daily expert platform responses (target: 3-5 responses/week)
- [ ] Begin podcast outreach to small/niche podcasts (target: 5 pitches/week)
- [ ] Send product samples to 5-10 relevant journalists/writers (with personalized note, not mass blast)
- **Expected output**: 1-3 expert quote mentions, 0-1 podcast bookings

### Phase 2: Building (Month 4-6)
- [ ] Continue expert platform routine
- [ ] Scale podcast outreach to mid-tier shows
- [ ] Pitch 2-3 story angles to top-priority media outlets
- [ ] Collect customer testimonials + data points for media pitches
- [ ] Distribute Milestone 3 (Winner Found) when ad performance proves out
- **Expected output**: 3-6 total mentions, 1-3 podcast appearances, 0-1 media features

### Phase 3: Scaling (Month 7-12)
- [ ] Milestone 4 ($100K MRR) — full press push if achieved
- [ ] Consider fractional PR support ($1,000-2,000/month) if revenue supports it
- [ ] Pitch to A-list media outlets and podcasts
- [ ] Compile "As Featured In" assets for website and ads
- [ ] Evaluate owned podcast launch (see podcast_strategy.md)
- **Expected output**: 6-12 total mentions, 3-6 podcast appearances, 1-3 notable media features

[Confidence: C | Source: Timeline is calibrated for bootstrapped execution. Actual PR results are highly variable — some brands get lucky early, most don't see significant earned media until they have real traction.]

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Tool | Month 6 Target | Month 12 Target |
|--------|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Media mentions | Google Alerts + Mention ($29/mo) | 5-10 | 15-30 |
| Backlinks from media | Ahrefs / GSC | 3-5 (DA 40+) | 10-20 (DA 40+) |

| Referral traffic from media | GA4 | 100-500 sessions | 500-2,000 sessions |
| "As Featured In" logos | Manual | 2-3 | 5-10 |

### Attribution Note

PR attribution is inherently messy. A podcast appearance may not drive direct traffic (listeners don't click links), but it builds brand recognition that shows up as branded search volume weeks later. Track:
1. **Direct**: UTM-tagged links in show notes and media articles
2. **Indirect**: Branded search volume trend (GSC) — spikes after media coverage
3. **Halo**: Social proof value — "As featured in [outlet]" on landing pages and ads

[Confidence: C | Source: PR measurement benchmarks for DTC brands. Attribution is genuinely difficult — these targets are directional, not precise.]
