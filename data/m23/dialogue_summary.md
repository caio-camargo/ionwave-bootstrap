## Round Log

### Round 1: Attribution Gap
- **Challenge (Skeptical Investor)**: Break-even math on hybrid deals requires 13 sales per influencer. Show the math works for micro-influencers with 10-50K followers.
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Single-post ROI is poor. The real value is the 90-day affiliate code window and compounding reach across Stories, Reels, link-in-bio.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Both miss that affiliate attribution is fragile — many customers Google the brand instead of using codes. Need to track both direct (code) and indirect (branded search lift) attribution.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U1)** — Added attribution framework to pipeline_operations.md: direct (code) + indirect (search lift, direct traffic) measurement. Decision rule for borderline ROAS partnerships.

### Round 2: FTC Compliance Package
- **Challenge (Compliance Auditor)**: Contract templates say [VOID — requires M7] but you're about to put product in 30-50 people's hands who may make prohibited health claims.
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: For gifted product, FTC requirements are lighter but still present. Need pre-seeding compliance package.
- **Synthesis (Skeptical Investor)**: Cost of one regulatory action >> cost of compliance. Need 4 components: talking points card, prohibited claims list, monitoring plan, violation response protocol. GATING requirement.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U2)** — Added Pre-Launch Compliance Package to contract_templates.md. 4-component package. NON-NEGOTIABLE gating requirement before any seeding.

### Round 3: Phase 0 vs Phase 1 Confusion
- **Challenge (Growth Engineer)**: Strategy says "Phase 1: no influencer spend" but map says "Phase 0: seed 20-30 nanos." Contradiction?
- **Response (Skeptical Investor)**: Product seeding ≠ influencer spend. Seeding = product COGS only. Phase 1 = paid UGC creators (which IS the ad creative budget). Phase 2 = paid influencer deals (incremental).
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Product seeding still creates FTC material connection. Compliance package must ship with every seeded product.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U3)** — Clarified Phase 0/1/2 distinction in influencer_creator_strategy.md. Added Unit Economics Validation Gate for Phase 2 entry (UGC ads >1.5x ROAS).

### Round 4: Perpetual UGC Rights Risk
- **Challenge (Skeptical Investor)**: Full perpetual rights for UGC — what about brand evolution, problematic creators, outdated content?
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Perpetual is industry standard for work-for-hire UGC. Risk of NOT having perpetual rights (losing a $50K/mo winning ad) > risk of perpetuity.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Keep perpetual rights but add voluntary content retirement policy: semi-annual audit, retire problematic content, archive inactive.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U4)** — Added content retirement policy to pipeline_operations.md: semi-annual UGC audit in monthly review table.

### Round 5: Tier 2 Target Validation
- **Challenge (Compliance Auditor)**: Named Tier 2 targets (DeLauer, Berry) might have current competitor deals. List could waste months of outreach.
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Add pre-outreach competitive screening: check recent sponsorships, competitor affiliate pages, exclusivity status.
- **Synthesis (Skeptical Investor)**: 15-minute screen per target before outreach. Check #ad tags, competitor affiliate pages, ambassador bios. Don't waste outreach on locked targets.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U5)** — Added pre-outreach competitive screening step to influencer_map.md with 4-point checklist.

### Round 6: Repurposing Multiplier Overstatement
- **Challenge (Growth Engineer)**: 1 video → 8-12 assets claimed, but static screenshots from video look terrible vs purpose-shot images. Multiplier overstated.
- **Response (Skeptical Investor)**: More honest: 4-6 primary derivatives (video cuts + hook swaps), 2-4 secondary (mixed quality). Still good economics.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: Additionally, every derivative needs compliance check — quotes pulled from video might contain unapproved claims.
- **Outcome**: **UPGRADED (U6)** — Revised multiplier to 6-10 (from 8-12) in creator_ugc_playbook.md. Added compliance checkpoint to repurposing checklist.

### Round 7: Budget Allocation
- **Challenge (Skeptical Investor)**: $3,500-7,000/month across creators + influencers. At what revenue level does this make sense?
- **Response (Growth Engineer)**: Phase 1 creator spend IS the ad creative budget, not separate. Phase 2 influencer spend is incremental and gated by UGC performance.
- **Synthesis (Compliance Auditor)**: UGC = ad creative investment. Influencer = incremental, gated by Unit Economics Validation.
- **Outcome**: **RESOLVED** — No content change needed. Already implicit in phasing. U3 made this explicit.

### Round 8: Ambassador Pathway (Saturation)
- **Challenge (Growth Engineer)**: No re-engagement path for top performers. Pipeline ends at "Complete."
- **Response (Compliance Auditor)**: M22 (Referral & Community) covers ambassador programs. Cross-reference exists.
- **Synthesis (Skeptical Investor)**: Agreed. No new tension. Saturation confirmed.
- **Outcome**: **RESOLVED** — M22 cross-reference handles this.

## What Would Have Been Missed

Without persona dialogue, the following risks would have remained unaddressed:
1. **Attribution undercounting** (U1) — would have killed working partnerships based on code-only ROAS
2. **FTC compliance gap** (U2) — product seeding without compliance package = regulatory exposure
3. **Phase confusion** (U3) — would have conflated product seeding with influencer cash spend, creating budget confusion
4. **Content liability accumulation** (U4) — perpetual rights without retirement policy = growing liability
5. **Wasted outreach** (U5) — approaching locked-up Tier 2 targets = months of dead effort
6. **Inflated economics** (U6) — overstated repurposing multiplier + unscreened derivatives = false confidence
