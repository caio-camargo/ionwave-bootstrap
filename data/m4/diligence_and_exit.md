## Investor Due Diligence Process

### Seed-Stage Diligence Scope (Confidence: A)

**Key Insight:** Pre-revenue seed diligence is 80% qualitative (founder, market, product) + 20% quantitative (unit economics assumptions, financial projections).

**Typical Seed Diligence Timeline:**
- Angels: 1-2 weeks (light diligence, gut-based)
- Micro-VCs: 3-6 weeks (formal process, partner meetings)
- Family Offices: 2-8 weeks (varies by decision-maker, can be fast or slow)

**Diligence Categories:**

| Category | What Investors Check | IonWave-Specific |
|----------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Financials** | Projections, unit economics, burn rate | 3-year model (M3), $30-50K raise, 65-75% margin |
| **Legal** | Entity structure, cap table, IP, contracts | LLC formation (M2), Biomarine supply agreement |
| **Product** | MVP quality, roadmap, tech/supply stack | IonWave sachets, Biomarine marine plasma, packaging |
| **Market** | TAM/SAM/SOM, competitive landscape, positioning | Electrolyte market, LMNT/Seaonic comparables |
| **Team** | Founder background, advisors, key hires | Danilo (solo founder), advisors TBD |
| **Traction** | Revenue, users, growth, retention | Pre-revenue, 7-week validation sprint (Week 0-7) |
| **Go-to-Market** | Customer acquisition strategy, channels, CAC/LTV | D2C (FB/IG ads) → Amazon → wholesale |
| **Risks** | Key risks and mitigations | CAC risk, supply chain, crowded market, solo founder |

### Diligence by Investor Type (Confidence: B)

**Angels ($5-100K):**
- **Focus:** Founder (trust, coachability, domain expertise) + Market size (big enough to be interesting)
- **Process:** 1-2 calls, review deck, maybe test product
- **Questions:** "Why you?", "Why now?", "What's the exit?"
- **IonWave Approach:** Emphasize founder story, 7-week validation rigor, clear exit path

**Micro-VCs ($100-500K):**
- **Focus:** Traction (revenue, users, growth rate) + Unit economics (CAC, LTV, margin, payback)
- **Process:** 3-5 calls, partner meetings, reference checks (customers, advisors, co-founders)
- **Questions:** "Show me traction", "What's CAC?", "How do you get to $10M revenue?"
- **IonWave Approach:** Share Week 4-6 results (units sold, CAC, repeat rate), emphasize capital efficiency

**Family Offices ($50-500K):**
- **Focus:** Mission fit (values alignment) + Downside protection (how do I get money back?)
- **Process:** 2-4 calls, may involve family members, longer decision timeline
- **Questions:** "Does this align with our health/wellness thesis?", "What if it doesn't work?", "Can I help beyond capital?"
- **IonWave Approach:** Emphasize marine plasma mission (ocean health, human performance), backup plan (kill criteria), advisory opportunities

### When to Share Data Room (Confidence: A)

**Stage 1: Initial Outreach (Cold Email / Warm Intro)**
- **Share:** Pitch deck only (link or PDF attachment)
- **Don't share:** Data room (too much too soon, overwhelms investor)

**Stage 2: First Meeting (Post-Deck Review)**
- **Share:** Pitch deck (again, in case they lost it)
- **Don't share:** Data room (wait for investor to request specific docs)

**Stage 3: Serious Interest (Post-Meeting, Investor Wants to Dig Deeper)**
- **Share:** Data room link (email: "Here's our data room with financials, legal, product specs — let me know if you need anything else")
- **Track:** Note in CRM that investor has data room access (indicates serious interest)

**Stage 4: Term Sheet Discussion (Week 6-7)**
- **Share:** SAFE docs (YC template, filled with IonWave terms)
- **Finalize:** Investor reviews, signs, wires funds

## Exit Scenarios and Valuation

### IonWave Exit Strategy (From Pitch Deck Slide 14 — Confidence: B)

**Primary Exit: PE Acquisition at 4-6x EBITDA (Year 3-5)**

**Scenario:**
- Year 3 EBITDA: $360K (from M3 Financial Model projections)
- Valuation: 4-6x EBITDA = $1.44M - $2.16M
- Year 5 EBITDA: $1.5M (extrapolated from 2.5x Y3→Y5 revenue growth at 20% net margin)
- Valuation: 4-6x EBITDA = $6M - $9M

**Rationale:**
- PE firms (private equity) buy profitable consumer brands at 4-6x EBITDA (standard multiple for $1-10M revenue D2C brands)
- PE playbook: Buy brand, scale via wholesale + international + new SKUs, sell to larger PE or strategic in 3-5 years
- IonWave fit: Profitable, high margin (65-75%), strong retention (40% repeat rate target), clean cap table

**Buyer Types:**
- Small PE firms (Wingate Partners, Encore Consumer Capital, L Catterton for smaller deals)
- Holding companies (aggregators like Thrasio, Perch for supplement brands)
- Family offices (seeking operating businesses for portfolio)

**Exit Process:**
- Approach: PE firm reaches out (if growing fast) OR Founder hires M&A advisor (if proactive)
- Timeline: 3-6 months (LOI → due diligence → definitive agreement → close)
- Founder involvement post-sale: Earnout (1-2 years) or clean exit (depends on deal structure)

**Investor Return:**
- **Example:** Invest $50K at $500K cap (10% ownership). Exit at $6M Year 3 = $600K return (12x).
- **Note:** Depends on dilution from future rounds (Series A, if raised) — SAFE holders may own <10% post-Series A

**Secondary Exit: Strategic Acquisition (Year 2-5)**

**Scenario:**
- Larger supplement brand (Thorne, Garden of Life, Goop, LMNT, AG1) acquires IonWave
- Valuation: $8-15M (2-4x revenue, depending on growth rate + strategic value)
- Rationale: Acquirer wants marine plasma category, IonWave brand, customer list, or supply chain (Biomarine relationship)

**Buyer Types:**
- **Thorne, Garden of Life, NOW Foods:** Established supplement brands seeking innovation
- **LMNT, AG1:** Direct competitors wanting to eliminate threat or add marine plasma to portfolio
- **Goop, Bulletproof:** Wellness brands expanding product lines
- **Nestlé, Unilever, Danone:** Consumer giants acquiring emerging brands (rare at <$10M revenue, but possible)

**Strategic Premium:**
- Strategic buyers pay 1.5-2x more than PE (2-4x revenue vs 4-6x EBITDA)
- Example: $1.8M Year 3 revenue x 3x = $5.4M (strategic) vs $360K EBITDA x 6x = $2.16M (PE)
- Rationale: Acquirer values strategic fit (distribution synergies, brand portfolio, customer cross-sell) more than pure financial returns

**Exit Trigger:**
- Inbound interest (acquirer reaches out)
- Founder proactive (hires M&A advisor, runs process with 3-5 potential buyers)
- Competitive threat (LMNT wants to buy before someone else does)

---

**Opportunistic Exit: Operator Buyout (Year 1-2)**

**Scenario:**
- Validation succeeds (Week 7 GREEN), but growth plateaus (can't scale past $500K-1M revenue)
- Founder offers business to operator (D2C marketer, supplement industry vet) at 1.5-3x revenue
- Valuation: $750K - $3M (depends on profitability, growth trajectory)

**Buyer Types:**
- D2C operator who wants to own business (not just advise)
- Supplement industry vet looking for acquisition target
- Existing investor who wants to take over (if founder burns out)

**Structure:**
- Seller financing (buyer pays over 2-3 years from business cash flow)
- Earnout (founder stays on 1-2 years, gets paid based on revenue/profit milestones)
- Cash upfront (rare, but possible if buyer has capital)

**IonWave Note:** This is "Plan C" (if PE and strategic exits don't materialize). Founder should target PE/strategic first, fall back to operator buyout if needed.

**Distribution Strategy: Annual Dividends (Year 2+)**

**Scenario:**
- IonWave bootstraps to profitability without Series A
- Year 2 net profit: $115K. Distribute 20-40% to investors = $23K - $46K
- Year 3 net profit: $360K. Distribute 20-40% to investors = $72K - $144K

**Investor Return (Before Exit):**
- **Example:** Invest $50K at $500K cap (10% ownership). Year 2 distribution = $2.3K - $4.6K (4.6-9.2% cash yield). Year 3 distribution = $7.2K - $14.4K (14.4-28.8% cash yield).
- **Total return by Year 3 (no exit):** $9.5K - $19K cash + $600K paper value (if $6M exit) = 13x total return (12x unrealized + 1x realized)

**Rationale:**
- Appeals to family offices and angels who want cash flow (not just paper gains)
- Reduces exit pressure (investors getting paid annually, less urgency to sell)
- Aligns incentives (founder keeps some profit, investors share in success)

**IonWave Recommendation:** Offer 20-40% distributions in pitch deck (signals investor-friendly, de-risks investment)

## Exit Preparation

### Danilo's Exit Preparation Guide (File 208 — Confidence: B)

From Danilo's Exit Preparation doc (File 208 from ops_model_v10_dump), key steps to maximize exit value:

**1. Clean Financials (Year 2-3)**
- **Action:** Use accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero), monthly reconciliation, annual audit (optional but preferred by acquirers)
- **Why:** Acquirers discount valuations for messy books (10-20% haircut if financials are unreliable)
- **IonWave:** Implement from Month 1 (don't wait until exit to fix)

**2. Revenue Quality (Recurring > One-Time)**
- **Action:** Launch subscription option (auto-delivery, 10% discount) in Year 1
- **Target:** 30-50% of revenue from subscriptions by Year 2 (predictable, high retention)
- **Why:** Recurring revenue is valued 1.5-2x more than one-time sales (predictability premium)
- **IonWave:** Offer subscription at Month 6 (once product-market fit proven)

**3. Margin Optimization (Target 70%+ Gross Margin)**
- **Action:** Negotiate COGS down (bulk ordering, supplier competition), optimize shipping (3PL vs self), reduce CAC (organic + referrals)
- **Target:** 70%+ gross margin, 20%+ net margin by Year 2
- **Why:** High-margin businesses attract premium valuations (PE wants cash flow, not just revenue)
- **IonWave:** 65-75% margin is already strong (maintain by negotiating Biomarine pricing, optimizing ads)

**4. Acquirer Identification (Build Relationships Early)**
- **Action:** Year 1-2, reach out to potential acquirers (Thorne, Garden of Life, LMNT) for "partnership" discussions (not acquisition)
- **Why:** Acquirers buy from people they know (build relationship → exit conversation is natural)
- **IonWave:** Target Thorne (supplement leader), LMNT (direct competitor), AG1 (wellness brand) for early relationship-building

**5. Customer Data and Retention (High LTV = High Value)**
- **Action:** Track LTV, cohort retention, NPS (Net Promoter Score), customer testimonials
- **Target:** 40%+ repeat rate, NPS ≥50, LTV $200+
- **Why:** Acquirers pay for customers, not just revenue (loyal customers = future cash flow)
- **IonWave:** Implement from Week 4 (first sales) — track every cohort

**6. Distribution Channel Diversification (Not Over-Reliant on One Channel)**
- **Action:** D2C (70%) + Amazon (20%) + Wholesale (10%) by Year 2
- **Why:** Single-channel risk (if FB ads die, business dies) scares acquirers
- **IonWave:** Launch Amazon Month 4, explore wholesale Month 7-12 (de-risk channel concentration)

**7. Brand and IP (Protect Trademarks, Build Brand Equity)**
- **Action:** Register "IonWave" trademark (M2), build brand recognition (PR, influencers, content)
- **Why:** Strong brand = pricing power = higher valuation (brand premium vs commoditized product)
- **IonWave:** Trademark registration (M2), PR outreach (biohacker podcasts, wellness blogs) Year 1

**8. Scalability Proof (Show Growth Trajectory, Not Just Current Revenue)**
- **Action:** Demonstrate 20-30% MoM growth (Year 1), 3-4x YoY growth (Year 1→2→3)
- **Why:** Acquirers pay for future cash flows (growth trajectory > current revenue)
- **IonWave:** If Week 7 validation succeeds, show consistent growth Month 1-6 (50 → 500 units/month = 10x growth in 6 months)
