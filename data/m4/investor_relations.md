## Investor CRM Pipeline

### 7-Stage Pipeline Framework (Confidence: A)

Based on Danilo's Investor Pipeline CRM (File 204 from ops_model_v10_dump), the fundraising pipeline has 7 stages:

| Stage | Definition | Actions | Success Metric | Avg Time |
|-------|------------|---------|----------------|----------|
| **1. Research** | Identify potential investors | Build list, research thesis/portfolio, find warm intro path | 50-100 investors listed | Ongoing |
| **2. Outreach** | Initial contact | Send warm intro request or cold email | 20-30% response rate (warm), 3-5% (cold) | Week 1-2 |
| **3. Meeting** | First call/meeting | Present deck, answer questions, gauge interest | 30-40% advance to diligence | Week 3-4 |
| **4. Diligence** | Investor researches deeper | Provide data room access, answer follow-ups, share traction | 50% advance to term sheet | Week 5-6 |
| **5. Term Sheet** | Negotiate terms | Discuss SAFE cap, discount, pro-rata rights | 60-70% convert to close | Week 6-7 |
| **6. Closed** | Investment complete | Sign docs, wire funds, add to cap table | 100% (success!) | Week 7+ |
| **7. Passed** | Investor declines | Ask for feedback, request intros, stay in touch | Learn + expand network | Any stage |

**Key Insight from Danilo's CRM:** Track 50+ investors to close 1-3 investments (conversion funnel: 50 → 10 meetings → 3 term sheets → 1-2 closes).

### Pipeline Management Best Practices (Confidence: B)

**1. Expect 50+ Investors to Close 1-3 (Conversion Reality)**
- Fundraising is a numbers game, especially for pre-revenue brands
- Don't get discouraged by passes — it's normal, not personal
- Track conversion rates to identify bottlenecks (e.g., low response rate = improve outreach, low meeting→diligence = improve deck)

**2. Parallel Processing (Not Sequential)**
- Don't wait for Investor A to pass before contacting Investor B
- Outreach to 10-20 investors in Week 1, not 1-2 per week
- Momentum matters: Multiple interested investors = negotiating leverage

**3. Weekly Follow-Ups (Persistence Without Annoyance)**
- If investor says "follow up in 2 weeks," follow up in exactly 2 weeks
- If investor ghosts after meeting, follow up weekly for 3 weeks, then move to "Passed"
- Template: "Quick update: Week 4 sales came in at 12 units, $38 CAC — ahead of target. Still interested?"

**4. Track Pass Reasons (Learn and Iterate)**
- When investor passes, ask: "What would need to be true for you to invest?"
- Common pass reasons: Pre-revenue (need traction), market concern (too crowded), team (solo founder risk), valuation (cap too high)
- If 3+ investors cite same reason, address it (e.g., add advisor to team, lower cap, gather traction before next round)

**5. Automate Where Possible (Save Time)**
- Email templates: Warm intro request, cold outreach, thank-you, weekly update
- Calendar links: Use Calendly or similar to avoid email ping-pong for scheduling
- Data room: Google Drive folder with all docs (deck, financials, legal) — send link instead of attaching files

## Investor Types and Targeting

### Angel Investors ($5-100K) — PRIMARY TARGET

**Profile:**
- High-net-worth individuals investing personal capital
- Decision criteria: Gut feel + founder + market size (intuitive, relationship-driven)
- Decision speed: 1-4 weeks (fast, informal diligence)
- Check size: $5-25K typical, $50-100K for lead angels
- Value-add: Industry connections, advisory, domain expertise, follow-on capital

**Sub-Types:**

| Type | Why They Invest | What They Want | IonWave Fit |
|------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| **D2C Operators** | "I've built/sold brands, know the playbook" | Solid unit economics, realistic projections, coachable founder | HIGH (can advise on FB ads, Amazon, scaling) |
| **Health Enthusiasts** | "I'm passionate about wellness/longevity" | Novel ingredients, category leadership, personal use case | HIGH (marine plasma is differentiated, clean label) |
| **Supplement Vets** | "I've worked in this industry 20 years" | Distribution channel access, regulatory compliance, supply chain | MEDIUM (Biomarine supply risk, retail not priority yet) |
| **Strategic Operators** | "I want to work on this, not just fund it" | Sweat equity opportunity, hands-on role, upside on effort | MEDIUM (if Danilo wants co-founder/operator) |

**Targeting Strategy:**
1. **Warm Intros (Priority #1):** Leverage Danilo's network, LinkedIn 2nd degree, advisor connections
2. **Angel Networks:** AngelList, ProductHunt Angels, FundersClub, SeedInvest (submit profile, get intros)
3. **Geography:** Brazil/LatAm (local network) + US (category leaders, larger checks, remote OK)
4. **Community:** Startup events, coworking spaces, founder Slack/Discord groups (build relationships first, pitch second)

### Micro-VCs ($100-500K) — SECONDARY TARGET

**Profile:**
- Small venture funds (<$50M AUM) investing institutional capital
- Decision criteria: Traction + unit economics + TAM (data-driven, partnership-level)
- Decision speed: 4-12 weeks (formal diligence, partner meetings)
- Check size: $100-250K typical, $500K for lead positions
- Value-add: Follow-on capital, portfolio introductions, credibility signal, board seat (sometimes)

**Sub-Types:**

| Type | Why They Invest | What They Want | IonWave Fit |
|------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Consumer/CPG Focused** | "Thesis: D2C brands, food/beverage, wellness" | $1-3M revenue (traction proof), strong margins, repeat purchase | LOW (IonWave pre-revenue, most require traction) |
| **Emerging Managers** | "First-time fund, need to deploy capital" | Concentrated bets, flexible on stage, founder-friendly terms | MEDIUM (more risk-tolerant, need to find right funds) |
| **Geographic Focus** | "LatAm founders building global brands" | Brazil/LatAm connection, US market exposure, diaspora story | MEDIUM (Danilo's Brazil roots + US market) |

**Targeting Strategy:**
1. **Research:** Use Pitchbook, Crunchbase, Signal to identify consumer/CPG micro-VCs with <$50M AUM
2. **Warm Intros:** Portfolio founders (best path), other investors, startup community connectors
3. **Cold Outreach:** 10-20% response rate if hyper-targeted (thesis fit + recent investment in similar company)
4. **Timing:** Approach after Week 6 validation (evidence-based pitch, not just promise)

**Reality Check:** Most micro-VCs won't invest pre-revenue at $30-50K raise (below minimum check size, too early). Angels are better target.

### Family Offices ($50-500K) — OPPORTUNISTIC

**Profile:**
- Wealth management for ultra-high-net-worth families ($100M+ net worth)
- Decision criteria: Mission fit + downside protection + relationship (patient, values-aligned)
- Decision speed: 2-12 weeks (varies by decision-maker access, can be fast or slow)
- Check size: $50-250K (flexible, depends on mandate)
- Value-add: Patient capital (5-10 year horizon), strategic connections, multi-stage support

**Sub-Types:**

| Type | Why They Invest | What They Want | IonWave Fit |
|------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Consumer Brand Wealth** | "Family fortune from CPG/retail, nostalgic" | Distribution access, retail relationships, operating role | MEDIUM (if family has supplement/retail connections) |
| **Health/Longevity Thesis** | "Investment mandate: wellness, human performance" | Category education, clinical research, patient capital | HIGH (marine plasma research angle, longevity positioning) |

**Targeting Strategy:**
1. **Warm Intros (Essential):** Family offices are gatekept — need intro from wealth advisor, attorney, or portfolio company
2. **Conferences:** Opal Group, Single Family Office Forum, iForum (expensive but direct access)
3. **Geography:** Miami, NYC, SF (US hubs), São Paulo (Brazil)
4. **Approach:** Long-term relationship-building (not transactional pitch)

**Reality Check:** Hardest to access, lowest priority for $30-50K raise. Pursue if warm intro arises, but don't rely on it.

## Meeting Preparation

### Pre-Meeting Research (Confidence: A)

**Investor Profile (15-20 min research):**
1. **Portfolio:** Check website/AngelList/Crunchbase for companies they've invested in
   - Identify similar companies: "You invested in LMNT — IonWave is positioned similarly"
   - Note check sizes: If they typically write $100K+ checks, $30K may be below minimum
2. **Thesis:** Read blog posts, tweets, interviews to understand what excites them
   - D2C focus? Wellness focus? Profitability focus? Underrepresented founders?
3. **Recent Activity:** Twitter/LinkedIn for recent interests, opinions, investments
   - Reference in meeting: "Saw your tweet about unit economics importance — that's why we focused on 65-75% margin"

**IonWave Prep (What to Bring):**
- Deck (screen share ready)
- Product sample (IonWave sachet, if in-person or can ship before meeting)
- Metrics cheat sheet (CAC, LTV, margin, sales — one-page reference)
- FAQ answers (see below — top 20 investor questions)

### During Meeting Structure (Confidence: A)

**Timing (30-min meeting):**
- 0-2 min: Small talk, rapport building ("How's your day going?")
- 2-5 min: Elevator pitch (problem, solution, traction, ask)
- 5-20 min: Deck walkthrough (emphasize 3-4 most important slides: problem, product, traction plan, financials)
- 20-28 min: Q&A (investor questions, objection handling)
- 28-30 min: Close with clear ask ("Raising $30K on SAFE at $250K cap — would you be interested in participating?")

**Dos:**
- **Listen > Pitch:** Let investor guide conversation, ask what they care about
- **Answer Directly:** "I don't know" > fabricating data (honesty builds trust)
- **Show Traction:** Week 3 ad CTR, Week 4 sales (if available) — evidence > promises
- **Handle Objections:** Acknowledge concern, provide evidence/logic, offer validation path
- **Take Notes:** Write down investor's concerns, questions, suggestions (shows respect, helps follow-up)

**Don'ts:**
- **Don't monologue:** Talking for 20 min straight = red flag (can't collaborate)
- **Don't get defensive:** Investor skepticism = due diligence, not personal attack
- **Don't oversell:** Overpromising → under-delivering = broken trust
- **Don't trash competitors:** "LMNT sucks" = unprofessional (instead: "LMNT is great, we're differentiated by...")

### Objection Handling (Confidence: B)

**Common Objections + Responses:**

| Objection | Investor Concern | Response |
|-----------|------------------|----------|
| **"Market is crowded"** | LMNT, Liquid IV, etc. dominate | "True, but marine plasma is novel (78 minerals vs 5-7). We're targeting biohacker/longevity segment that values completeness over brand familiarity." |
| **"You're pre-revenue"** | Too risky, no proof | "That's why we structured a 7-week validation sprint with clear success criteria (≥20 units, CAC ≤$40). If we hit metrics, risk drops. If we miss, we kill it — no throwing good money after bad." |
| **"Solo founder risk"** | What if Danilo quits/burns out? | "Fair concern. Mitigation: I have [advisors X, Y], and I'm open to operator buy-in (sweat equity + cash) if growth requires it. Also, supplement brand is operationally simple (not deep tech with 10-person team)." |
| **"Exit unclear"** | How do I get liquidity? | "Primary: PE acquisition at 4-6x EBITDA Year 3-5 = $6-9M exit. Secondary: Strategic acquisition by larger brand (Thorne, Garden of Life) = $8-15M. Also, we plan 20-40% annual distributions starting Year 2 if profitable (early liquidity)." |
| **"Valuation too high"** | $250-500K cap is steep for pre-revenue | "Open to feedback. Cap is based on [comparable raises + 7-week sprint structure + strong unit economics]. What cap would make sense to you?" (Negotiate) |
| **"CAC is high"** | $30-40 CAC on $47-59 AOV is tight | "Agreed, CAC must stay below $40 for 5x+ LTV:CAC. That's why Week 6 is economics validation gate — if CAC >$40 after optimization, we pivot or kill." |

**Objection Handling Framework:**
1. **Acknowledge:** "That's a great question" or "Fair concern"
2. **Answer:** Provide data, logic, or mitigation plan
3. **Validate:** "Does that address your concern, or should I dig deeper?"

## Investor Updates

### Monthly Update Email (Confidence: A)

**Purpose:**
- Keep investors informed (transparency builds trust)
- Show progress (momentum = confidence = follow-on investment)
- Ask for help (intros, advice, resources)

**Structure (From Danilo's Investor Update Template, File 205):**

```
Subject: IonWave Update — [Month Year] (e.g., "IonWave Update — March 2026")

Hi [Investor Name],

Quick update on IonWave progress:

**Metrics:**
- Revenue: $X (up Y% from last month)
- Units sold: X (total: Y cumulative)
- CAC: $X (down from $Y last month)
- Repeat rate: X% (up from Y%)
- Margin: X% (in line with projections)

**Wins:**
- ✅ [Milestone 1]: Launched Amazon FBA, first 15 units sold
- ✅ [Milestone 2]: Repeat customer rate hit 30% (target was 25%)
- ✅ [Milestone 3]: Featured in [blog/podcast/newsletter]

**Challenges:**
- ⚠️ [Challenge 1]: FB ad account suspended (appealed, resolved in 3 days)
- ⚠️ [Challenge 2]: Inventory lead time increased to 6 weeks (negotiating with Biomarine)

**Next Month Focus:**
- [ ] Scale ads to $3K/month (from $2K)
- [ ] Launch subscription option (increase LTV)
- [ ] Test influencer seeding (10 biohacker micro-influencers)

**Ask:**
- Anyone have contacts at [retail chain / influencer agency / supplement manufacturer]?
- Feedback on subscription pricing ($42/month for auto-delivery vs $47 one-time)?

Thanks for your support!

Best,
Danilo

P.S. [Personal touch: customer testimonial, funny anecdote, photo of product in wild]
```

**Frequency:**
- Monthly (consistent = reliable)
- Exception: Major milestones (first $10K month, profitability, acquisition offer) → send immediately

**Tone:**
- Honest (good and bad news)
- Concise (5-min read max)
- Actionable (clear asks, not vague "let me know if you can help")

**What to Include:**
- **Always:** Metrics (revenue, units, CAC, margin), wins, challenges, next focus
- **Sometimes:** Product updates, customer stories, press mentions
- **Rarely:** Personal stuff (unless relevant: "Had a baby, taking 2 weeks off")

**What to Exclude:**
- **Never:** Complaints without solutions ("This is so hard" without mitigation plan)
- **Avoid:** Overly long essays (investors get 20+ update emails/month)
- **Skip:** Irrelevant details (what you had for lunch, random musings)

### Quarterly Board Update (If Applicable — Confidence: C)

**When Needed:**
- If investor has board seat (rare for angels, common for VCs)
- If investor requests formal updates (some angels want quarterly vs monthly)

**Structure:**
- Slide deck (5-10 slides): Metrics dashboard, quarterly highlights, next quarter goals, asks
- 30-60 min call: Walk through deck, Q&A, strategic discussion

**IonWave Note:** At $30-50K raise, unlikely to have formal board. Monthly email updates sufficient.

## Relationship Management

### Post-Close Engagement (Confidence: A)

**Week 1 Post-Close:**
- Add to cap table (Carta or spreadsheet)
- Send welcome email: "Thanks for believing in IonWave. Updates monthly, feel free to reach out anytime."
- Invite to private Slack/Discord (if you have investor channel)

**Month 1-3 Post-Close:**
- Send monthly updates (as above)
- Ask for intros: "Do you know anyone at [retail chain / ad agency / influencer network]?"
- Invite to test products: "Sending you a box — would love feedback on taste/packaging"

**Month 4-6 Post-Close:**
- Share wins: "We hit break-even! Thanks for your early support."
- Offer advisory role: "Would you be open to a quarterly call to discuss growth strategy?"
- Prepare for Series A (if growth accelerates): "We're considering raising $500K Series A in Q4 — would you participate?"

**Year 1+ Post-Close:**
- Keep on monthly update list (even if they don't reply)
- Annual in-person meetup (if possible): Coffee, lunch, product demo
- Exit planning: "We're getting acquisition interest — wanted to give you heads up"

### Investor Value-Add (How to Extract Max Value)

**1. Intros (Network Access)**
- Ask specifically: "Do you know anyone at [company/role]?" (not vague "can you help with intros?")
- Make it easy: Provide forwardable blurb, clear value prop for introduction
- Follow up: After intro, update investor on outcome ("Met with X, great conversation, may lead to partnership")

**2. Advisory (Domain Expertise)**
- Schedule quarterly calls: 30-60 min strategic discussion (not operational weeds)
- Ask specific questions: "How would you approach Amazon launch?" (not "what should I do?")
- Respect time: Investors are busy, don't over-ask (monthly 1-hr call = too much, quarterly 30-min = right)

**3. Follow-On Capital (Proactive Signaling)**
- Update on growth: "We're scaling faster than expected, may need bridge round in Q3"
- Ask early: "If we raise Series A at $3M cap, would you participate?" (gauge interest before formal round)
- Offer pro-rata: If investor has pro-rata rights, honor them (relationship preservation)

**4. Credibility (Social Proof)**
- Ask for testimonial: "Can I mention you as an investor in press / on website?"
- Use for warm intros: "Sarah Chen invested in IonWave — can you intro me to [investor Z]?"
- Press mentions: "IonWave raised from [Investor Name], former founder of [Company]"

## Investor FAQ

### Top 20 Questions Investors Ask (From File 201: Investor FAQ)

Danilo's Investor FAQ document (File 201) covered common investor questions for IonWave. Below is generalized framework with IonWave-specific answers:

#### 1. What problem are you solving?

**Answer:** Existing electrolytes are incomplete (5-7 minerals like LMNT) or low-quality (sugar/artificial like Liquid IV). Athletes and biohackers want complete hydration (78 minerals) without compromise.

#### 2. Why marine plasma? What makes it different?

**Answer:** Marine plasma is ocean-isotonic (matches human blood plasma mineral ratio) with 78 minerals/trace elements vs 5-7 in typical electrolytes. Biomarine sources from clean ocean zones (Brittany, France), hypertonic concentration ensures bioavailability. Scientific backing (René Quinton research, modern clinical use).

#### 3. Who is your target customer?

**Answer:** Primary: Crossfit athletes, biohackers, longevity enthusiasts (LMNT's audience). Secondary: Health-conscious consumers seeking clean supplements. Psychographic: Values ingredient quality, reads labels, willing to pay premium ($47-59 vs $35-45 for LMNT).

#### 4. What's your competitive advantage?

**Answer:** Only marine plasma electrolyte brand with (1) 78 minerals (completeness), (2) Biomarine partnership (supply chain moat), (3) LMNT-level branding + Seaonic-level quality. Competitors pick 2 of 3, not all 3.

#### 5. How big is the market?

**Answer:** TAM: [Electrolyte/hydration market size], SAM: [Online D2C supplement buyers in US/Brazil], SOM: [Biohacker/athlete segment, IonWave's 3-5 year target]. [VOID — Exact TAM/SAM/SOM numbers not provided in context]

#### 6. What are your unit economics?

**Answer:**
- Price: $47-59 per 24-sachet box
- COGS: ~$14 (Biomarine $4-6.60, packaging, fulfillment)
- Gross margin: 65-75%
- CAC target: $30-40 (FB/IG ads)
- LTV: $200+ (4-5 purchases/year, 2-3 year retention)
- LTV:CAC: 5-6x (healthy for D2C)

#### 7. How will you acquire customers?

**Answer:** Phase 1: D2C via Shopify (FB/IG ads, influencer seeding, content marketing). Phase 2: Amazon FBA. Phase 3: Wholesale (Whole Foods, gyms). 70% D2C, 20% Amazon, 10% wholesale mix (Year 1).

#### 8. What traction do you have?

**Answer (Pre-Revenue):** Week 0 now, 7-week validation sprint starting. Week 3: Launch ads (target CTR ≥1.5%). Week 4: First sales (target ≥5 units, CAC ≤$50). Week 7: Raise/kill decision (target ≥20 units, CAC ≤$40).

**Answer (Post-Validation):** [Update with actual numbers: "Sold 25 units in Week 7, CAC $36, 2 repeat customers, 1.8% ad CTR"]

#### 9. Why are you raising $30-50K? Why not more?

**Answer:** Capital-efficient validation approach. $30-50K funds 7-week sprint (33% inventory for 2,500 units, 30% ads for 5 months, 37% working capital). If validation succeeds, we'll be profitable or raise larger round. If fails, we kill it (limited investor loss). Raising $500K pre-revenue would be irresponsible.

#### 10. What's the use of funds breakdown?

**Answer:**
- 33% Inventory: $10-16.5K for 2,500 units (5 months supply)
- 30% Ads: $9-15K for $1,800-3,000/month x 5 months
- 37% Working capital: $11-18.5K buffer (shipping, packaging, ops, surprises)
