# ROAS Decay Model

## Overview
Predictable patterns of ROAS decline driven by creative fatigue (2-4 weeks), audience saturation, and scaling-induced efficiency loss. Includes surf scaling tactics for managing decay and maintaining profitable growth.

## ROAS Decay Fundamentals [Confidence: A | Source: Performance marketing research, scaling case studies, Danilo research | Date: 2026-02]

### Why ROAS Decays

**Inevitable Truth**: All ad campaigns experience ROAS decline over time — the question is how fast and how much.

**Primary Decay Drivers**:

**1. Creative Fatigue** (2-6 weeks):
- **Definition**: Audience sees same creative repeatedly, effectiveness diminishes
- **Mechanism**: Novelty wears off → lower CTR → higher CPM (auction competitiveness drops) → higher CPA → lower ROAS
- **Timeline**:
  - **TikTok**: 2-3 weeks (fastest fatigue, algorithm favors fresh content)
  - **Meta**: 4-6 weeks (moderate fatigue, larger audience pool)
  - **YouTube**: 6-8 weeks (slowest fatigue, longer consideration cycle)

**2. Audience Saturation** (4-8 weeks):
- **Definition**: Reaching diminishing returns within target audience (most interested users already converted or exposed)
- **Mechanism**: Exhaust high-intent users → expand to lower-intent → lower conversion rate → higher CPA → lower ROAS
- **Indicator**: Frequency >3-4 (showing ads to same users multiple times per week)

**3. Scaling-Induced Decay** (continuous):
- **Definition**: As you increase spend, efficiency naturally declines (Law of Diminishing Returns)
- **Mechanism**: Spending $100/day reaches highest-intent audience → Spending $1,000/day must expand to lower-intent → Lower ROAS at higher spend
- **Expectation**: 10-30% ROAS decline when scaling 5-10x

**4. Competitive Pressure** (variable):
- **Definition**: Competitors enter market or increase spend, driving up CPMs
- **Mechanism**: More advertisers bidding for same audience → CPM increases → CPA increases → ROAS declines
- **Indicator**: CPM spike without change in frequency or creative (external factor)

**5. Seasonality** (predictable cycles):
- **Definition**: Q4 CPMs spike (holiday competition), Q1 CPMs drop (post-holiday lull)
- **Mechanism**: Increased advertiser demand during peak seasons (Q4) → auction competition → CPM inflation → ROAS decline
- **Pattern**: Q4 CPMs typically 30-60% higher than Q2

## Creative Fatigue Decay Curves [Confidence: B | Source: Creative performance tracking, industry benchmarks | Date: 2026-02]

### Meta Creative Fatigue Pattern

**Typical Lifecycle (4-6 Weeks)** — BUT budget-dependent (see below)

**Budget-Adjusted Fatigue Timeline** [Confidence: A | Source: Frequency analysis, budget/fatigue correlation studies | Date: 2026-02]:

**Root Cause**: Creative fatigue is driven by **Frequency** (how often same users see ad), not calendar time. Large budgets reach high frequency faster.

**Timeline by Daily Budget**:
- **$50/day**: 5-7 weeks to fatigue (Frequency 3-4 reached slowly)
- **$500/day**: 3-4 weeks to fatigue (Frequency 3-4 reached faster)
- **$5,000/day**: 2-3 weeks to fatigue (Frequency 3-4 reached very fast)

**Operational Implication**: Monitor **Frequency metric** (not calendar), not rigid "4-6 week" timeline.
- **Frequency <2**: Fresh, no fatigue
- **Frequency 2-3**: Early fatigue signs, prepare refresh
- **Frequency 3-4**: Moderate fatigue, refresh recommended
- **Frequency >4**: Severe fatigue, refresh immediately or reduce budget 50%

**Week 1 (Peak Performance)**:
- **Status**: New creative, algorithm exploring optimal delivery
- **CTR**: Peak (baseline + 20-40% novelty boost)
- **ROAS**: Peak (best performing period)
- **Frequency**: 1.0-1.5 (low)
- **Action**: Monitor, don't change anything

**Week 2-3 (Stable Performance)** — or Frequency 1.5-2.5:
- **Status**: Algorithm optimized, stable delivery
- **CTR**: Baseline (slight decline from Week 1, but still strong)
- **ROAS**: 90-100% of peak (minor efficiency decline acceptable)
- **Frequency**: 1.5-2.5 (moderate exposure)
- **Action**: Maintain budget, monitor Frequency

**Week 4-5 (Early Fatigue)** — or Frequency 2.5-3.5:
- **Status**: Creative fatigue beginning
- **CTR**: Decline 20-30% from peak
- **ROAS**: 70-85% of peak (noticeable efficiency loss)
- **Frequency**: 2.5-3.5 (approaching saturation)
- **Action**: Prepare creative refresh, consider budget reduction to extend lifespan

**Week 6+ (Advanced Fatigue)** — or Frequency >4:
- **Status**: Severe creative fatigue
- **CTR**: Decline >40% from peak
- **ROAS**: <70% of peak (no longer efficient)
- **Frequency**: >4 (audience oversaturated)
- **Action**: Pause or reduce budget 50%, deploy new creative immediately

**Frequency-First Monitoring** (Recommended):
- **Check Frequency daily** (Meta Ads Manager → Frequency column)
- **Ignore calendar weeks** (they're a proxy, not the cause)
- **Refresh creative when Frequency >3** (proactive, not reactive)
- **Budget Adjustment**: If Frequency rising fast (>0.5/week increase), reduce budget 20% to slow accumulation

**Example**:
```
Week 1: CTR 2.0%, ROAS 4.0x (Peak)
Week 2: CTR 1.8%, ROAS 3.8x (Stable, 95% of peak)
Week 3: CTR 1.7%, ROAS 3.6x (Stable, 90% of peak)
Week 4: CTR 1.5%, ROAS 3.2x (Early Fatigue, 80% of peak)
Week 5: CTR 1.2%, ROAS 2.6x (Early Fatigue, 65% of peak) → Action needed
Week 6: CTR 0.9%, ROAS 2.0x (Advanced Fatigue, 50% of peak) → Pause/refresh
```

### TikTok Creative Fatigue Pattern

**Typical Lifecycle (2-4 Weeks)** — Faster than Meta

**Week 1 (Peak Performance)**:
- **Status**: New creative, TikTok algorithm heavily favors fresh content
- **CTR**: Peak (baseline + 30-50% novelty boost, higher than Meta)
- **ROAS**: Peak
- **Action**: Scale aggressively if performing (TikTok rewards winners fast)

**Week 2 (Rapid Decline)**:
- **Status**: Algorithm begins deprioritizing (favors newer content)
- **CTR**: Decline 30-40% from Week 1 (steep drop)
- **ROAS**: 70-80% of peak
- **Action**: Prepare creative refresh (Week 3-4 likely fatigue)

**Week 3-4 (Advanced Fatigue)**:
- **Status**: Severe fatigue, algorithm heavily deprioritized
- **CTR**: Decline >50% from peak
- **ROAS**: <60% of peak
- **Action**: Deploy new creative immediately, pause fatigued creative

**TikTok-Specific**:
- **Creative Velocity Critical**: TikTok requires 2x creative refresh rate vs Meta (2 weeks vs 4 weeks)
- **Algorithm Favors New**: Fresh creatives get distribution boost (incentive to refresh frequently)

### YouTube Creative Fatigue Pattern

**Typical Lifecycle (6-8 Weeks)** — Slower than Meta/TikTok

**Week 1-2 (Peak Performance)**:
- **Status**: New creative, algorithm learning optimal targeting
- **View Rate**: Peak
- **CPA**: Lowest (most efficient period)
- **Action**: Scale if performance strong

**Week 3-5 (Stable Performance)**:
- **Status**: Optimized delivery, slower fatigue than social platforms
- **View Rate**: 90-95% of peak (minor decline)
- **CPA**: 100-110% of baseline (acceptable)
- **Action**: Maintain budget, monitor

**Week 6-8 (Gradual Fatigue)**:
- **Status**: Creative fatigue developing
- **View Rate**: Decline 20-30% from peak
- **CPA**: 120-130% of baseline
- **Action**: Prepare creative refresh (not as urgent as TikTok/Meta)

**Week 9+ (Advanced Fatigue)**:
- **Status**: Significant fatigue
- **View Rate**: Decline >30% from peak
- **CPA**: >140% of baseline
- **Action**: Deploy new creative

**YouTube-Specific**:
- **Longer Lifespan**: YouTube creative lasts 1.5-2x longer than Meta (less frequent exposure, longer consideration)
- **Slower Refresh Cadence**: Can run same creative 6-8 weeks vs 4-6 (Meta) or 2-3 (TikTok)

## Audience Saturation Decay Curves [Confidence: B | Source: Scaling case studies, audience analysis | Date: 2026-02]

### Saturation Model

**Concept**: As you scale, you exhaust high-intent audience and expand to lower-intent, reducing efficiency.

**Audience Segmentation by Intent**:
- **Tier 1 (High Intent)**: 10-20% of target audience → ROAS 4-6x → Reached at $100-500/day
- **Tier 2 (Medium Intent)**: 30-40% of target audience → ROAS 2.5-4x → Reached at $500-2,000/day
- **Tier 3 (Low Intent)**: 50-60% of target audience → ROAS 1.5-2.5x → Reached at $2,000-10,000/day

**Saturation Timeline**:

**Phase 1: High-Intent Saturation (First 30 Days)**:
- **Spend Level**: $50-500/day
- **Audience**: Most interested users (remarketing, high-match LAL, strong interest signals)
- **ROAS**: Peak performance (3-5x typical)
- **Frequency**: 1-2 (low saturation)

**Phase 2: Medium-Intent Expansion (Days 31-90)**:
- **Spend Level**: $500-2,000/day
- **Audience**: Moderately interested users (broader LAL, expanded interests)
- **ROAS**: 70-85% of peak (efficiency decline as reach expands)
- **Frequency**: 2-3 (moderate saturation)

**Phase 3: Low-Intent Saturation (Day 91+)**:
- **Spend Level**: $2,000-10,000+/day
- **Audience**: Low-intent users (broad targeting, lower match quality)
- **ROAS**: 50-70% of peak (significant efficiency decline)
- **Frequency**: 3-4+ (high saturation, reaching same users repeatedly)

**Example**:
```
Month 1: Spend $100/day, ROAS 4.5x (High-Intent)
Month 2: Spend $500/day, ROAS 3.6x (80% of peak, Medium-Intent expansion)
Month 3: Spend $1,500/day, ROAS 2.9x (64% of peak, Low-Intent expansion)
Month 4: Spend $3,000/day, ROAS 2.4x (53% of peak, Saturation evident)
```

**Mitigation Strategies**:
1. **Audience Expansion**: Add new LAL seeds, expand geo, test new demographics
2. **Creative Refresh**: New creative can re-engage saturated audience
3. **Platform Diversification**: Expand to new platform (Meta → TikTok → YouTube) to reach different audience pools
4. **Offer/Angle Variation**: Test new value propositions to appeal to lower-intent users

## Scaling-Induced ROAS Decay [Confidence: A | Source: Scaling frameworks, 20% Rule research, Danilo analysis | Date: 2026-02]

### The Law of Diminishing Returns

**Principle**: Doubling spend does NOT double conversions (efficiency declines as you scale).

**Expected ROAS Decline by Scale**:

**Spend: $100/day → $500/day (5x increase)**:
- **Expected ROAS Decline**: 10-15%
- **Example**: 4.0x ROAS → 3.4-3.6x ROAS
- **Reason**: Still within high-intent audience, minor decay

**Spend: $500/day → $2,500/day (5x increase)**:
- **Expected ROAS Decline**: 20-30%
- **Example**: 3.5x ROAS → 2.5-2.8x ROAS
- **Reason**: Expanding into medium-intent audience, noticeable decay

**Spend: $2,500/day → $10,000/day (4x increase)**:
- **Expected ROAS Decline**: 30-40%
- **Example**: 2.8x ROAS → 1.7-2.0x ROAS
- **Reason**: Reaching low-intent audience, CPM inflation, saturation

**Total: $100/day → $10,000/day (100x increase)**:
- **Expected ROAS Decline**: 50-60%
- **Example**: 4.0x ROAS → 1.6-2.0x ROAS
- **Reason**: Compounding decay factors (creative fatigue, audience saturation, CPM inflation)

**Key Insight**: 50% ROAS decline to achieve 100x scale is NORMAL and ACCEPTABLE (absolute profit still increases).

**Profitability Check**:
```
Scenario A (Small Scale): $100/day, 4.0x ROAS
  Revenue: $400/day
  Profit (40% margin): $160/day profit - $100/day spend = $60/day net profit

Scenario B (Large Scale): $10,000/day, 2.0x ROAS (50% decline)
  Revenue: $20,000/day
  Profit (40% margin): $8,000/day profit - $10,000/day spend = -$2,000/day net loss

Wait — Issue! At 2.0x ROAS with 40% margin, break-even is 2.5x ROAS.
Need to maintain >2.5x ROAS OR improve margin to stay profitable at scale.
```

**Corrected Profitability Example**:
```
Scenario B (Adjusted): $10,000/day, 2.5x ROAS (38% decline from 4.0x)
  Revenue: $25,000/day
  Profit (40% margin): $10,000/day profit - $10,000/day spend = $0 break-even

Scenario C (Profitable Scale): $5,000/day, 3.0x ROAS (25% decline from 4.0x)
  Revenue: $15,000/day
  Profit (40% margin): $6,000/day profit - $5,000/day spend = $1,000/day net profit
  ($1,000/day × 30 = $30,000/month net profit vs $60/day × 30 = $1,800/month at small scale)
```

**Lesson**: Scaling with ROAS decay is profitable IF you stay above break-even ROAS (determined by contribution margin from channel_profitability_matrix.md).

## Surf Scaling Methodology [Confidence: B | Source: Danilo research, performance marketing tactics | Date: 2026-02]

### Concept

**Traditional Scaling**: Rigid 20% budget increases every 3-4 days, regardless of performance fluctuations.

**Surf Scaling**: Dynamic budget adjustments based on real-time performance — increase aggressively during high-performance periods, reduce during low-performance periods.

**Metaphor**: Surfer catches wave (high ROAS day) and rides it (increase budget), pulls back when wave crashes (low ROAS day, reduce budget).

### Execution Framework

**Daily Performance Classification**:

**Hot (ROAS >120% of Target)**:
- **Example**: Target 3.0x ROAS, yesterday achieved 3.6x+
- **Action**: Increase budget 20-30% same day (ride the wave)
- **Rationale**: Performance above target, algorithm optimized, capture volume while hot

**Warm (ROAS 90-120% of Target)**:
- **Example**: Target 3.0x ROAS, yesterday achieved 2.7-3.6x
- **Action**: Maintain budget (stable performance)
- **Rationale**: Performance acceptable, no change needed

**Cool (ROAS 70-90% of Target)**:
- **Example**: Target 3.0x ROAS, yesterday achieved 2.1-2.7x
- **Action**: Reduce budget 10-20% (caution, investigate cause)
- **Rationale**: Performance below target but not critical, reduce exposure until diagnosed

**Cold (ROAS <70% of Target)**:
- **Example**: Target 3.0x ROAS, yesterday achieved <2.1x
- **Action**: Reduce budget 30-50% or pause (troubleshoot immediately)
- **Rationale**: Performance significantly below target, likely creative fatigue, audience saturation, or external issue

### Example Surf Scaling Sequence

```
Day 1 (Monday): Campaign $500/day, ROAS 3.8x (Hot, target 3.0x)
  → Action: Increase to $650/day (+30%)

Day 2 (Tuesday): Campaign $650/day, ROAS 3.5x (Hot)
  → Action: Increase to $845/day (+30%)

Day 3 (Wednesday): Campaign $845/day, ROAS 2.9x (Warm)
  → Action: Maintain $845/day

Day 4 (Thursday): Campaign $845/day, ROAS 2.3x (Cool)
  → Action: Reduce to $675/day (-20%), investigate (creative fatigue? pixel issue?)

Day 5 (Friday): Campaign $675/day, ROAS 3.2x (Hot, after creative refresh deployed Thursday PM)
  → Action: Increase to $810/day (+20%)

Day 6 (Saturday): Campaign $810/day, ROAS 1.9x (Cold, weekend performance drop typical)
  → Action: Reduce to $405/day (-50%), monitor over weekend

Day 7 (Sunday): Campaign $405/day, ROAS 2.1x (Cool, weekend continues)
  → Action: Maintain $405/day, plan Monday increase
```

**Result**: Campaign budget flexes 50-180% based on performance (maximum volume during hot periods, minimum risk during cold periods).

### When to Use Surf Scaling

**Best Fit**:
- **Experienced Media Buyers**: Requires daily active management and quick decision-making
- **High-Volume Accounts**: 50+ conversions/day enables daily decision confidence
- **Creative-Sensitive Products**: Performance tightly coupled to creative freshness (fashion, trend-driven, seasonal)
- **Volatile Markets**: High variance in day-to-day performance (deal sites, flash sales, news-driven products)

**Poor Fit**:
- **Beginner Media Buyers**: Risk of over-reacting to daily noise (need experience to distinguish signal vs noise)
- **Low-Volume Accounts**: <10 conversions/day creates high daily variance (not statistically significant)
- **Stable Products**: Performance highly consistent day-to-day (20% Rule sufficient, surf scaling adds unnecessary complexity)

### Surf Scaling Risks

**1. Over-Reaction to Noise**:
- **Risk**: Daily volatility can trigger unnecessary changes (one bad day doesn't mean campaign failing)
- **Mitigation**: Use 2-day rolling average instead of single-day performance (smooths variance)

**2. Learning Phase Resets**:
- **Risk**: Frequent >20% budget changes can trigger learning phase resets (Meta/TikTok)
- **Mitigation**: Reserve surf scaling for campaigns out of learning phase (50+ conversions/week achieved)

**3. Operator Fatigue**:
- **Risk**: Daily optimization requires sustained attention (burnout risk)
- **Mitigation**: Use automated rules (increase/decrease budget based on ROAS thresholds) or limit surf scaling to top 3-5 campaigns only

## ROAS Decay Mitigation Strategies [Confidence: A | Source: Optimization frameworks, scaling best practices | Date: 2026-02]

### Strategy 1: Creative Refresh Cadence

**Objective**: Replace fatigued creative before severe performance decline.

**Cadence by Platform**:
- **TikTok**: Every 2-3 weeks (new creative or derivative)
- **Meta**: Every 4-6 weeks (new creative or derivative)
- **YouTube**: Every 6-8 weeks (new creative)

**Derivatives vs New Concepts**:
- **Derivatives** (80% of refreshes): Same concept, new hook/talent/visual (fast, cheap, 70-80% of original performance)
- **New Concepts** (20% of refreshes): Entirely new ad concept (slow, expensive, potential for breakthrough 100-200% performance)

**Execution**:
- Deploy new creative BEFORE fatigue hits <70% of peak (proactive, not reactive)
- Example: Week 4 of Meta ad (early fatigue), deploy derivative to replace before Week 6 (advanced fatigue)

### Strategy 2: Audience Expansion

**Objective**: Reach new audience pools to avoid saturation.

**Expansion Tactics**:
1. **Lookalike Expansion**: LAL 1% → LAL 3% → LAL 5% (broader seed matching)
2. **Geographic Expansion**: State → National → International (new audience pools entirely)
3. **Demographic Expansion**: 25-34 → 35-44 age group, Female → Male audience
4. **Interest Expansion**: Niche interests → Broader interests → Broad targeting

**Execution**:
- Launch horizontal expansion tests (from scaling_playbook.md) when frequency >3 or CPM +25%
- Allocate 20-30% of total budget to expansion tests (maintain portfolio of scaling + expanding campaigns)

### Strategy 3: Budget Cycling (Pause and Restart)

**Objective**: Reset audience fatigue by pausing campaign, allowing "cooldown," then restarting.

**Method**:
1. **Pause Campaign**: When ROAS <70% of peak and creative refresh not ready
2. **Cooldown Period**: 7-14 days (audience "forgets" creative)
3. **Restart Campaign**: Same creative, same audience, budget 50% of original (test if performance rebounds)

**Expected Outcome**:
- **30-50% ROAS Recovery**: Not full reset, but partial recovery (CTR improves, frequency resets)
- **Use Case**: Buy time until new creative ready (temporary fix, not long-term solution)

**Risk**: Loss of learning phase status (campaign re-enters learning when restarted)

### Strategy 4: Platform Diversification

**Objective**: Expand to new platforms to access different audience pools and reset saturation.

**Execution**:
- **Meta Saturated**: Expand to TikTok or YouTube (same creative, different platform)
- **Single-Platform Risk**: Over-reliance on one platform (algorithm change, ad account ban, CPM spike = business risk)
- **Diversification Target** (from channel_diversification.md): <50% revenue from single platform by Month 12

**Expected Outcome**:
- **Lower ROAS on New Platform**: New platform typically 70-85% of mature platform ROAS (learning curve)
- **Portfolio Effect**: Blended ROAS across platforms may decline slightly, but risk reduced and total volume increased

### Strategy 5: Offer/Angle Variation

**Objective**: Re-engage saturated audience with new value proposition.

**Tactics**:
1. **Discount/Promo**: Original offer $50 → New offer $50 with 20% off (price incentive)
2. **Bundle**: Original offer single product → New offer product bundle (value perception)
3. **Angle Shift**: Original angle "lose weight" → New angle "boost confidence" (same product, different emotional hook)

**Execution**:
- Test new offer/angle when creative refreshes exhausted (offer change higher risk than creative change)
- Allocate 10-20% of budget to offer tests (most budget remains on proven offer)

## ROAS Decay Forecasting [Confidence: C | Source: Modeling frameworks, limited case study data | Date: 2026-02]

### Simple Decay Model

**Assumption**: ROAS decays predictably based on time and scale.

**Formula**:
```
Expected ROAS = Peak ROAS × (1 - Creative Fatigue Factor) × (1 - Scaling Decay Factor)
```

**Example**:
```
Peak ROAS: 4.0x (Week 1)
Weeks Elapsed: 5 (Meta)
Spend Increase: 5x ($100/day → $500/day)

Creative Fatigue Factor: 0.20 (20% decline at Week 5 for Meta, based on curve)
Scaling Decay Factor: 0.12 (12% decline for 5x spend increase)

Expected ROAS = 4.0 × (1 - 0.20) × (1 - 0.12) = 4.0 × 0.80 × 0.88 = 2.82x

Actual ROAS at Week 5, $500/day: 2.9x (close to forecast)
```

**Use Case**:
- Forecasting performance for budget planning (expected ROAS at Month 3 with 10x scale)
- Setting realistic expectations (avoid surprise when ROAS declines from 4.0x to 2.5x)

**Limitations**:
- Simplified model (doesn't account for competitive pressure, seasonality, creative refresh timing)
- High variance (actual ROAS may be ±30% from forecast)
