# Hook Testing Matrix

## Overview
Systematic framework for high-velocity creative testing with emphasis on hook variations (first 3-5 seconds), kill criteria integration from M14, and platform-specific creative fatigue timelines.

## Cold Start Protocol [Confidence: A | Source: Launch phase creative strategy, TWP expert dialogue | Date: 2026-02]

### If You Have No Winners Yet (Days 1-21)

**Problem**: The hook testing matrix assumes you have a winning ad to iterate from. But on Day 1, you have no winners—what hooks do you test first?

**Solution**: Reference `first_100_ads.md` Month 1 creative testing matrix for bootstrap diversification:

**Initial 5-Hook Diversification** (Month 1, Days 1-14):
1. **UGC Testimonial** (talking head, customer result)
2. **Problem/Solution** (text overlay, problem → product → result)
3. **Before/After** (visual transformation)
4. **Educational** (how-to, value-first)
5. **Social Proof** (review compilation, star ratings)

**Goal**: Test diversified hook categories (Problem Callout, Result Transformation, Educational, Social Proof) to identify which resonates with your audience.

**After First Winner Validated** (≥10 purchases, ROAS ≥target, Days 14-21):
- **Apply this document's hook variation methodology** (test 5-10 derivatives of winner)
- **Continue new concept testing** (20-30% of creative production) while iterating winners (70-80%)

**Transition Point**: Once you have 1-2 validated winners, shift from bootstrap diversification (first_100_ads.md) to systematic iteration (this document).

## Hook Fundamentals [Confidence: A | Source: Creative testing frameworks, Danilo research, platform best practices | Date: 2026-02]

### What is a Hook?

**Definition**: The first 3-5 seconds of an ad that determines whether user stops scrolling and watches.

**Why It Matters**:
- **3-Second Decision**: Users decide to watch or scroll within 3 seconds
- **80% of Performance**: Hook quality drives 80% of ad performance (body/CTA contribute 20%)
- **Thumb-Stop Power**: Great hook = high CTR, poor hook = wasted impressions

**Hook Performance Indicators**:
- **3-Second Video View Rate**: % of impressions that watch ≥3 seconds (>40% good, <25% poor)
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate)**: % of impressions that click (>1.5% good for Meta, >1.0% TikTok)
- **Hook Hold Rate**: % of viewers who watch past hook to body (>60% good)

### Hook Categories [Confidence: A | Source: Creative frameworks, winning ad analysis | Date: 2026-02]

**1. Pattern Interrupt**:
- **Concept**: Visual or verbal element that breaks scroll pattern (unexpected, unusual, contrasting)
- **Examples**:
  - "STOP SCROLLING" text overlay
  - Unexpected sound (glass breaking, loud noise)
  - Visual contrast (bright color against dark background)
  - Fast motion or camera movement
- **Best For**: Cold audiences, attention-grabbing, top-of-funnel
- **Risk**: Can feel "clickbaity" if body doesn't deliver on promise

**2. Problem/Pain Callout**:
- **Concept**: State specific problem target audience experiences (creates immediate relevance)
- **Examples**:
  - "Tired of acne that won't go away?"
  - "Struggling to lose weight after 40?"
  - "Hate spending hours editing videos?"
- **Best For**: Problem-aware audiences, direct response, high intent
- **Risk**: Can be repetitive if overused in market

**3. Result/Transformation**:
- **Concept**: Show end result immediately (before/after, testimonial, outcome)
- **Examples**:
  - "I lost 30 pounds in 90 days"
  - Before/after split screen
  - "This tool cut my workload in half"
- **Best For**: Solution-aware audiences, proof-driven buyers
- **Risk**: Can lack context if result isn't immediately believable

**4. Curiosity/Question**:
- **Concept**: Ask question or create curiosity gap (makes user want to know answer)
- **Examples**:
  - "Why do 10,000 marketers use this tool?"
  - "The #1 mistake killing your conversion rate"
  - "What I wish I knew before starting my business"
- **Best For**: Cold audiences, story-based content, educational positioning
- **Risk**: Can feel manipulative if payoff is weak

**5. Relatable Scenario**:
- **Concept**: Depict specific scenario target audience relates to (situational identification)
- **Examples**:
  - "You've tried every diet but nothing sticks"
  - "It's 2 AM and you're still editing your presentation"
  - "Your competitors are outranking you on Google"
- **Best For**: Warm audiences, storytelling, emotional connection
- **Risk**: Must be highly specific to audience (generic scenarios don't work)

**6. Social Proof/Authority**:
- **Concept**: Lead with credibility (testimonial, expert, stats, badges)
- **Examples**:
  - "10,000+ 5-star reviews"
  - "As seen on Forbes, TechCrunch, WSJ"
  - "I'm a dermatologist and here's what I recommend"
- **Best For**: High-trust required (health, finance, B2B), skeptical audiences
- **Risk**: Can feel boastful if not backed by strong proof

**7. How-To/Educational**:
- **Concept**: Promise to teach something valuable (educational value proposition)
- **Examples**:
  - "3 ways to double your conversion rate"
  - "How to rank #1 on Google in 30 days"
  - "The secret to perfect skin (it's not what you think)"
- **Best For**: Educational content, thought leadership, top-of-funnel nurturing
- **Risk**: User may consume content without clicking (watch but don't convert)

## Hook Testing Matrix [Confidence: A | Source: Creative testing frameworks, M14 alignment | Date: 2026-02]

### Testing Framework

**Goal**: Test 5-10 hook variations per winning ad concept to identify highest-performing hook.

**Structure**:
```
Ad Concept A (Body + CTA consistent)
  ├── Hook 1: Pattern Interrupt
  ├── Hook 2: Problem Callout
  ├── Hook 3: Result/Transformation
  ├── Hook 4: Curiosity Question
  └── Hook 5: Social Proof
```

**Variables Held Constant**:
- **Body**: Same middle section (product demo, explanation, benefits)
- **CTA**: Same call-to-action (offer, urgency, button)
- **Format**: Same format (UGC video, testimonial, product demo)

**Variables Tested**:
- **Hook Only**: First 3-5 seconds (verbal script + visual)

**Example**:
```
Ad Concept: Skincare product (acne solution)
Body (consistent): 15 seconds of product demo + ingredients explanation
CTA (consistent): "Try it risk-free for 30 days"

Hook Variations:
  Hook A: "STOP! If you have acne, watch this" (Pattern Interrupt)
  Hook B: "Tired of acne that won't go away no matter what you try?" (Problem Callout)
  Hook C: "My skin cleared up in 2 weeks using this" (Result/Transformation)
  Hook D: "Why do dermatologists recommend this ingredient?" (Curiosity)
  Hook E: "10,000+ 5-star reviews from acne sufferers" (Social Proof)
```

### Testing Protocol [Confidence: A | Source: M14 Creative Testing Protocol, Danilo research | Date: 2026-02]

**Phase 1: Initial Launch (Days 1-7)**:

**Setup**:
- Launch 5 hook variations simultaneously (same campaign, different ads)
- Budget: $50-100/day per platform (split across 5 ads = $10-20/day per hook)
- Audience: Same audience for all hooks (Broad or proven LAL)
- Goal: Identify top 2 hooks by Day 7

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- **Primary**: CTR (highest CTR wins)
- **Secondary**: 3-second video view rate, ROAS (if sufficient conversion volume)
- **Minimum Viable Data**: 10,000+ impressions per hook (ensures statistical significance)

**Day 7 Decision**:
- **Keep Top 2 Hooks**: Highest CTR + best ROAS (if ≥5 conversions)
- **Kill Bottom 3 Hooks**: Lowest CTR or underperforming ROAS
- **Budget Reallocation**: Shift budget from killed hooks to top 2

**Phase 2: Validation (Days 8-14)**:

**Setup**:
- Run top 2 hooks with increased budget ($100-200/day per hook)
- Monitor: ROAS, CPA, conversion volume
- Goal: Validate which hook scales with efficiency

**Evaluation Criteria** (from M14):
- **Minimum Viable Data**: 10 purchases per hook by Day 14
- **Target ROAS**: Meets or exceeds target (e.g., 3.0x)
- **Consistency**: ROAS stable for 3+ consecutive days

**Day 14 Decision**:
- **Winner**: Hook with highest ROAS + ≥10 purchases → Scale
- **Runner-Up**: If 2nd hook within 80% of winner ROAS, keep both (portfolio approach)
- **Kill Losers**: Hooks that don't meet M14 criteria

**Phase 3: Scaling (Day 15+)**:

**Setup**:
- Scale winner(s) using 20% Rule from scaling_framework.md
- Monitor creative fatigue (CTR decline, frequency, CPM)
- Prepare derivatives (same hook, minor variations)

### Kill Criteria by Testing Phase [Confidence: A | Source: M14 Creative Testing Protocol | Date: 2026-02]

**Early Kill (Days 1-7)**:
- **CTR <50% of best performer** after 10,000+ impressions
  - Example: Best hook CTR = 2.0%, this hook CTR = 0.9% → Kill
- **3-Second View Rate <25%** (audience not stopping to watch)
- **CPM >150% of average** (auction not competitive for this creative)

**Mid-Stage Kill (Days 8-14)**:
- **<10 purchases by Day 14** (M14 minimum viable data not reached)
- **ROAS <50% of target** with ≥5 conversions (statistically unlikely to improve)
  - Example: Target 3.0x ROAS, achieving 1.2x with 7 conversions → Kill

**Late-Stage Kill (Day 15+, Fatigue Detection)**:
- **CTR decline >30%** from peak (creative fatigue)
- **Frequency >4** (audience oversaturation)
- **ROAS decline >25%** from baseline with no recovery after 5 days
- **CPM increase >40%** (audience saturation driving up costs)

## Platform-Specific Hook Strategies [Confidence: A | Source: Platform best practices, creative research | Date: 2026-02]

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Hook Strategy

**Creative Fatigue Timeline**: 4-6 weeks

**Hook Best Practices**:
- **First Frame Matters**: Static first frame must be compelling (thumbnail for Reels)
- **Captions Recommended**: 85% of Facebook videos watched without sound
- **Hook Length**: 3-5 seconds (can be slightly longer than TikTok)
- **Format Preferences**:
  - **Feed**: Problem callout or social proof hooks perform well
  - **Stories/Reels**: Pattern interrupt or relatable scenario (vertical format, full-screen)

**Testing Velocity**:
- **Test Frequency**: 2-4 new hooks per month per winning ad concept
- **Creative Lifespan**: Expect 4-6 weeks before refresh needed (CTR decline signal)

**Top-Performing Hook Types** (Meta-Specific):
1. **UGC Testimonial Hook**: Real person speaking to camera ("I used to struggle with...")
2. **Before/After Hook**: Visual proof immediately (split-screen or transition)
3. **Text Overlay Hook**: Bold text on first frame ("Are you making this mistake?")

### TikTok Hook Strategy

**Creative Fatigue Timeline**: 2-4 weeks (faster than Meta)

**Hook Best Practices**:
- **Native Feel Critical**: Ads that look like organic TikToks perform 3x better
- **Sound On**: TikTok users 90%+ watch with sound (verbal hook critical)
- **Hook Length**: 1-3 seconds (faster scroll pace than Meta)
- **Vertical Only**: 9:16 aspect ratio mandatory

**Testing Velocity**:
- **Test Frequency**: 4-6 new hooks per month (higher refresh rate than Meta)
- **Creative Lifespan**: 2-4 weeks typical (creative fatigues faster due to algorithm)

**Top-Performing Hook Types** (TikTok-Specific):
1. **Trend-Jacking Hook**: Leverage trending sounds, formats, memes
2. **Talking Head Hook**: Creator speaking directly to camera (authentic, native feel)
3. **Fast-Cut Hook**: Rapid visual cuts in first 2 seconds (matches TikTok pacing)

**TikTok-Specific Considerations**:
- **Trend Sensitivity**: Hooks leveraging current trends outperform evergreen by 2x
- **Creator Partnerships**: TikTok creators as talent (native feel) outperform actors
- **Sound/Music**: Trending sounds can boost distribution (algorithm favors trending audio)

### YouTube Hook Strategy

**Creative Fatigue Timeline**: 6-8 weeks (longer consideration, less frequent exposure)

**Hook Best Practices**:
- **5-Second Non-Skip Window**: Users can't skip for 5 seconds (In-Stream ads)
- **Hook Length**: 5-8 seconds (longer attention span than social)
- **High Production Value**: YouTube audiences expect higher quality (less tolerance for raw UGC)
- **Sound Critical**: 95%+ watch with sound (YouTube is lean-in, not lean-back)

**Testing Velocity**:
- **Test Frequency**: 1-2 new hooks per month (longer lifespan than Meta/TikTok)
- **Creative Lifespan**: 6-8 weeks typical

**Top-Performing Hook Types** (YouTube-Specific):
1. **Story Hook**: Begin narrative immediately ("Let me tell you about the time...")
2. **Curiosity Hook**: Question that promises answer later ("Why do experts recommend...")
3. **Educational Hook**: Value proposition upfront ("In this video, you'll learn 3 ways to...")

**YouTube-Specific Considerations**:
- **Companion Banner**: 5-second hook must work with clickable companion banner below video
- **Context Matters**: Ad appears before video user chose (hook must acknowledge interruption gracefully)

## Hook Variation Techniques [Confidence: B | Source: Creative production frameworks, winning ad analysis | Date: 2026-02]

### Derivative Hook Creation (Fast Iteration)

**Concept**: Create 5-10 hook variations from single winning ad without full re-shoot.

**Technique 1: Text Overlay Variations**:
- **Original Hook**: "Tired of acne?"
- **Variations**:
  - "Struggling with acne?"
  - "Acne won't go away?"
  - "Tried everything for your acne?"
  - "Acne ruining your confidence?"
- **Production Time**: <1 hour (text overlay changes only)

**Technique 2: Cut-Down/Extend**:
- **Original Hook**: 5-second hook
- **Variations**:
  - 3-second cut-down (faster pace)
  - 7-second extended (more context)
- **Production Time**: 1-2 hours (editing only)

**Technique 3: Talent Swap**:
- **Original Hook**: Female creator, age 25
- **Variations**:
  - Male creator, age 25 (same script)
  - Female creator, age 35 (different demo)
  - Female creator, age 25, different ethnicity (representation)
- **Production Time**: 2-4 hours per variation (re-shoot with different talent)

**Technique 4: Visual Variations**:
- **Original Hook**: Indoor setting, bright lighting
- **Variations**:
  - Outdoor setting (different background)
  - Dark/moody lighting (different feel)
  - Close-up vs wide shot (framing change)
- **Production Time**: 2-4 hours per variation (re-shoot in different setting)

**Technique 5: Verbal Variations**:
- **Original Hook**: "Are you tired of acne?"
- **Variations**:
  - "If you have acne, watch this"
  - "Acne sufferers, this is for you"
  - "10 years of acne cleared in 2 weeks"
- **Production Time**: 1-2 hours (re-record audio, same visuals)

### Hook Testing Prioritization [Confidence: B | Source: Creative testing ROI analysis | Date: 2026-02]

**Highest ROI (Test First)**:
1. **Text Overlay Variations**: Fastest (1 hour), lowest cost ($0-50), moderate impact (10-30% CTR variance)
2. **Verbal Variations**: Fast (1-2 hours), low cost ($50-200), high impact (20-50% CTR variance)
3. **Cut-Down/Extend**: Fast (1-2 hours), low cost ($50-200), moderate impact (10-30% CTR variance)

**Medium ROI (Test Second)**:
4. **Talent Swap**: Moderate speed (2-4 hours), moderate cost ($200-500), high impact (30-100% CTR variance)
5. **Visual Variations**: Moderate speed (2-4 hours), moderate cost ($200-500), moderate impact (20-40% CTR variance)

**Lowest ROI (Test Last)**:
6. **Full Concept Re-Shoot**: Slow (1-2 days), high cost ($1,000-5,000), highest impact (50-200% CTR variance)

**Recommendation**:
- **Start with Derivatives**: Test 5-10 text/verbal/cut-down variations (fast, cheap)
- **Scale to Talent Swap**: If derivatives exhaust, test different talent (moderate cost)
- **Reserve Budget for New Concepts**: Full re-shoots for breakthrough creative (high cost, high upside)

## Hook Testing Tracker Template [Confidence: B | Source: Operational framework | Date: 2026-02]

**Spreadsheet Columns**:
- **Ad Name**: Hook identifier (e.g., "Hook_ProblemCallout_v1")
- **Hook Category**: Pattern Interrupt, Problem Callout, Result, etc.
- **Launch Date**: Date ad launched
- **Platform**: Meta, TikTok, YouTube
- **Impressions**: Total impressions to date
- **CTR**: Click-through rate (%)
- **3-Sec View Rate**: % of impressions watching ≥3 seconds
- **Conversions**: Total conversions
- **ROAS**: Return on ad spend
- **Status**: Testing, Winner, Killed, Fatigued
- **Notes**: Insights, reasons for kill/scale decisions
