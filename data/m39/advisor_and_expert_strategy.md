## 1. The Four Trust Dimensions (Verification Framework)

### 1.1 Strategic Trust: "Is the big picture right?"

**Scope**: Thesis (M0), market analysis (M1), business model (M2), strategic frameworks (M38), exit scenarios.

**Key Questions**:
- Is the thesis defensible against sophisticated scrutiny?
- Is market timing right? (too early = education burden; too late = competitive)
- Is TAM real or fantasy? (bottoms-up validation)
- Can this be venture-scale? (path to $100M+ exit)
- Is business model capital-efficient? (D2C with <$2M raise is possible)

**Verifier Profiles**:

**Profile 1: Exited Founder**
- Built D2C consumer brand $0→$50M+ revenue
- Raised institutional capital OR bootstrapped profitably
- Exited via acquisition, PE rollup, or still operating at scale
- **Value**: Pattern-matching (has seen what works/fails), credibility (done it before), network (can open doors)

**Profile 2: VC Partner**
- Consumer-focused fund (Forerunner, Silas, Lerer Hippeau, etc.)
- Invests at Seed/Series A ($1-5M checks)
- Portfolio includes D2C supplement or wellness brands
- **Value**: Market perspective (sees 100s of pitches), diligence rigor (knows what metrics matter), fundraising guidance (what VCs actually want)

**Profile 3: PE Operator**
- Works at consumer rollup (Branded, Foundry, Monogram, etc.)
- Evaluates acquisition targets (knows what makes brands valuable)
- Post-acquisition value creation (operational improvements)
- **Value**: Exit perspective (what makes brand acquirable), operational benchmarks (margin expectations, EBITDA targets), valuation frameworks

**IonWave Target**: 2-3 strategic advisors (1 founder, 1 VC, 1 PE operator).

**Current Status**: [VOID — requires network mapping and outreach]

### 1.2 Domain Trust: "Is the technical execution right?"

**Scope**: Product formulation (M5), manufacturing (M6), supply chain (M8), regulatory/compliance (M9), quality/testing (M7).

**Key Questions**:
- Is formulation safe, effective, and manufacturable at target COGS?
- Are supplement claims legally defensible? (structure/function vs disease claims)
- Is supply chain resilient to disruptions? (single-source risk, lead times)
- Are quality testing protocols sufficient? (heavy metals, microbial, potency)
- Are there hidden regulatory landmines? (FDA warning letters, FTC enforcement)

**Verifier Profiles**:

**Profile 1: Supplement Formulator**
- 10+ years formulating dietary supplements
- Experience with capsules, powders, gummies (multiple formats)
- Works with contract manufacturers OR independent consultant
- **Value**: Formulation feasibility (can we make this?), COGS reality check (pricing assumptions), ingredient sourcing (supplier reliability)

**Profile 2: Regulatory Attorney**
- Specializes in FDA supplement regulation (DSHEA, FDCA)
- Experience with structure/function claims vs disease claims
- Defends against FDA warning letters, FTC enforcement actions
- **Value**: Claims defensibility (what can we legally say?), labeling compliance (Supplement Facts panel), risk mitigation (avoid FDA scrutiny)

**Profile 3: Supply Chain Expert**
- Managed D2C supply chain at scale ($10M+ revenue)
- Experience with 3PLs, inventory management, freight optimization
- Worked with supplement manufacturers (GMP compliance)
- **Value**: Supplier selection (how to vet manufacturers), inventory strategy (how much to hold?), logistics optimization (reduce shipping cost)

**IonWave Target**: 1 formulator (fractional or consultant), 1 regulatory attorney (retained), 1 supply chain advisor.

**Current Status**: [VOID — requires procurement via network + cold outreach]

### 1.3 Execution Trust: "Is the operational plan realistic?"

**Scope**: Unit economics (M4), marketing channels (M15-M21), pricing strategy (M10), operations (M26-M28), team/hiring (M31-M37).

**Key Questions**:
- Are unit economics assumptions realistic? (CAC, LTV, margins)
- Is CAC achievable in practice? (Facebook $45 CAC vs $75 reality)
- Is marketing channel mix optimal? (how much FB vs Google vs influencer?)
- Is pricing strategy defensible? (cost-plus vs value-based, competitive positioning)
- Is org structure right for stage? (lean early, scale hiring milestones)

**Verifier Profiles**:

**Profile 1: D2C Operator**
- Scaled D2C brand $0→$10M+ revenue (preferably supplement/wellness)
- Owns P&L OR was COO/VP Operations
- Managed team of 10+ (hiring, culture, execution velocity)
- **Value**: Unit economics reality check (CAC inflation, LTV compression), operational playbook (what to build when), hiring guidance (who to hire first)

**Profile 2: Growth Marketer**
- Scaled Facebook/Instagram ads $10K→$500K/month
- Experience with creative testing, audience segmentation, funnel optimization
- Managed 7-figure ad budgets with ROAS >3x
- **Value**: CAC optimization (creative strategies, audience tactics), channel expansion (when to add new channels), measurement (attribution, incrementality)

**Profile 3: Retention/Email Expert**
- Built email/SMS programs driving 30%+ of revenue
- Experience with Klaviyo, Attentive, subscription optimization
- Managed retention for D2C brand with >50% repeat rate
- **Value**: LTV expansion (how to increase repeat purchase), retention tactics (email flows, SMS campaigns, subscription), churn reduction

**IonWave Target**: 1-2 D2C operators (generalists who scaled brands), 1 growth marketer (if paid acquisition is primary channel), 1 retention expert (if LTV expansion is critical).

**Current Status**: [VOID — requires targeted outreach to D2C community]

### 1.4 Vibes Trust: "Does this feel right?"

**Scope**: Team dynamics, founder-market fit, culture, decision-making velocity, intuitive pattern-matching.

**Key Questions**:
- Does this team have "it"? (energy, conviction, adaptability)
- Is there hidden dysfunction? (co-founder conflict, misaligned incentives)
- Are they moving fast enough? (bias to action vs analysis paralysis)
- Does founder have unfair advantage? (domain expertise, network, insight)
- Is there product-founder fit? (genuine passion vs opportunistic)

**Verifier Profile**:

**The Vibes Checker**
- Operated 10+ Trades (breadth of pattern-matching)
- Strong intuition (can sense dysfunction, spot red flags)
- Not afraid to give hard feedback (will say "this doesn't feel right")
- **Value**: Pattern-matching (has seen success/failure modes), intuition (gut check on team/strategy), hard truths (challenges consensus)

**IonWave Status**: Nilo currently fulfills Vibes Trust role (validates Strategic + Domain + Execution + Vibes across all TUPs).

**Constraint**: Vibes Trust is hardest to procure externally (requires deep relationship, high trust, willingness to be brutally honest). Typically co-founder, close mentor, or long-time collaborator.

## 3. Social Capital Mapping

### 3.1 Network Mapping Protocol

**Purpose**: Identify warm intro paths to target advisors (warm intros convert 5-10x higher than cold outreach).

**Step 1: List Current Network**
- [VOID — requires actual network inventory: Danilo/Nilo connections]
- Categories:
  - Previous co-founders, colleagues, employees
  - Investors (current, previous, angels who passed)
  - Advisors (formal, informal)
  - Industry contacts (conferences, Slack groups, Twitter mutuals)
  - Friends/family in relevant industries

**Step 2: Map 2nd-Degree Connections**
- Use LinkedIn "2nd-degree connections" search
- Filter by: Company (target firms), Title (target roles), Industry (supplements, D2C, consumer)
- Example: Search "Forerunner Ventures" → see who in network is connected to Forerunner partners

**Step 3: Prioritize Warm Paths**
- Rank connections by strength (Close friend >Colleague >Acquaintance >Mutual follows)
- Rank connectors by relevance (Industry insider >Adjacent >Unrelated)
- Identify top 10 warm intro paths to each target advisor category

**Step 4: Request Intros**
- Script: "I'm building a D2C supplement brand and looking for an advisor with [specific expertise]. I saw you're connected to [target]. Would you be comfortable making an intro? Happy to share more context."
- Make it easy: Provide forwardable intro blurb (2-3 sentences about brand, what you're looking for)
- Follow up: Thank connector, close loop on outcome

### 3.2 Cold Outreach Protocol (When No Warm Path Exists)

**Channel Selection**:
- **LinkedIn**: Best for professional outreach (VCs, operators, consultants)
- **Twitter/X DM**: Good for D2C community (many operators very responsive)
- **Email**: If you have email (via AngelList, Crunchbase, website)
- **Slack/Discord**: D2C communities (DTC Collective, Baubble, etc.)

**Message Template** (LinkedIn/Email):

```
Subject: Quick question about [specific topic they know]

Hi [Name],

I'm [your name], building [1-sentence brand description]. I came across your work at [company/achievement] and your experience with [specific relevant thing] is exactly what we need as we [specific challenge].

Would you be open to a 20-minute call to get your take on [specific question]? Happy to compensate for your time or explore an advisory relationship if there's a fit.

Either way, thanks for [specific thing they built/wrote/did].

Best,
[Your name]
[Link to brand or deck]
```

**Key Principles**:
- Specificity (show you've done research, not spray-and-pray)
- Respect time (20 minutes, not "pick your brain")
- Clear ask (what do you want from them?)
- Reciprocity (offer compensation, show value to them)
- Short (5 sentences max)

**Conversion Rates** (estimated):
- Warm intro: 50-80% response rate, 30-50% convert to call, 10-20% convert to advisor
- Cold LinkedIn: 20-40% response rate, 10-20% convert to call, 2-5% convert to advisor
- Cold email: 10-30% response rate, 5-15% convert to call, 1-3% convert to advisor
- Twitter DM: 30-60% response rate (if small following), 15-30% convert to call, 5-10% convert to advisor

**Volume Strategy**: To land 5 advisors across 4 trust dimensions (20 total relationships), need:
- Warm intro path: 30-50 intro requests → 15-25 calls → 20 relationships
- Cold outreach path: 100-200 outreach messages → 20-40 calls → 20 relationships

## 4. Engagement Protocols

### 4.1 Initial Advisor Call (60-90 Minutes)

**Pre-Work** (before call):
- Send context pack (1-pager on brand, thesis, key questions you need answered)
- Review their background (LinkedIn, blog posts, portfolio)
- Prepare 10-15 specific questions (not generic "what do you think?" but "should we prioritize subscription or one-time purchase initially?")

**Call Structure**:

**Phase 1: Context Setting** (10 min)
- Quick intro: Who you are, what you're building, why them
- Current state: Where are you? (traction, stage, team)
- What you need: Specific help you're seeking (strategic guidance, domain expertise, etc.)

**Phase 2: Deep Dive** (40-60 min)
- Ask prepared questions (go deep on 5-7 topics vs shallow on 20)
- Listen for: What are they skeptical about? What lights them up? What patterns do they see?
- Take notes (or record with permission)

**Phase 3: Advisor Fit Exploration** (10-20 min)
- If call went well: "This has been incredibly helpful. Would you be open to an ongoing advisory relationship?"
- Explain what advisory means: Quarterly calls, ad-hoc questions via email, small equity comp
- If they're interested: "Let me send you a formal proposal with equity details, time commitment expectations."
- If they're not: "Totally understand. Could I follow up in [3 months] as we hit [milestone]?"

**Post-Call**:
- Send thank-you note within 24 hours
- If advisor fit: Send formal proposal within 48 hours
- If not: Add to network, stay in touch (share updates quarterly)

### 4.2 Advisor Agreement Structure

**Standard Advisory Agreement Components**:

**Time Commitment**:
- 4 quarterly calls per year (60 min each)
- Ad-hoc email/Slack questions (respond within 48 hours best effort)
- Optional: Annual in-person meeting (if geography allows)
- **Total**: ~20-30 hours per year

**Equity Compensation**:
- **Strategic Advisor** (high-value, ongoing): 0.25-0.5% equity
- **Domain Expert** (specialized, frequent): 0.1-0.25% equity
- **Execution Advisor** (tactical, periodic): 0.05-0.15% equity
- **Vesting**: 4-year vest (monthly or quarterly), 1-year cliff
- **Accelerate on exit**: Single-trigger acceleration (advisor doesn't lose equity if Trade sells)

**Cash Compensation** (alternative or supplement to equity):
- **Consulting rate**: $150-300/hr (for experts who prefer cash over equity)
- **Retainer**: $1-3K/month (for ongoing high-touch advisory)
- **Product**: Free product for life (low-cost, high-value for supplement brand)

**Termination**:
- Either party can terminate with 30 days notice
- Vested equity remains (unvested equity forfeited)
- No cause required (this is advisory, not employment)

**Confidentiality**:
- Standard NDA (advisor sees financials, strategy, customer data)
- No moonlighting for direct competitors during term + 1 year after

**Example Proposal Email**:

```
Subject: Advisory Proposal — [Brand Name]

Hi [Advisor Name],

Thanks again for the call last week. Your insights on [specific thing discussed] were exactly what we needed, and I'd love to formalize an ongoing advisory relationship.

Here's what I'm proposing:

**Role**: Strategic Advisor (focus on [specific domain])
**Time Commitment**: 4 quarterly calls/year (~60 min each) + ad-hoc email questions
**Compensation**: 0.25% equity (4-year vest, 1-year cliff, single-trigger acceleration)
**Start Date**: [Date]

I'll send over the formal advisory agreement via DocuSign. Let me know if you have questions or want to discuss details.

Excited to work together!

Best,
[Your name]
```

### 4.3 Ongoing Engagement Rhythm

**Quarterly Advisor Calls** (schedule in advance):
- Send pre-read 48 hours before call (1-pager: progress since last call, key metrics, open questions)
- Call agenda: 10 min recap, 40 min Q&A, 10 min next steps
- Post-call: Send notes + action items within 24 hours

**Ad-Hoc Questions** (email/Slack):
- Use sparingly (don't abuse access)
- Be specific ("Should we hire ops lead before growth marketer?" not "What should we do?")
- Respect response time (48 hours is reasonable, not instant)

**Investor Updates** (if advisor is investor or potential future investor):
- Add to monthly or quarterly update distribution
- Keep them informed (even if not asking for anything)
- Transparency builds trust (share bad news, not just wins)

**Annual Review** (each year):
- Assess: Is advisor still valuable? Are we utilizing them?
- If yes: Continue (vest next year)
- If no: Mutual conversation about sunsetting relationship (no hard feelings)

## 5. Advisor Portfolio Strategy

### 5.1 Optimal Advisor Count

**Too Few Advisors** (<3): Missing critical perspectives, single-point-of-failure for expertise.

**Too Many Advisors** (>10): Coordination overhead, dilution, conflicting advice.

**IonWave Target**: 5-8 advisors across four trust dimensions.

**Allocation**:
- **Strategic Trust**: 2-3 advisors (exited founder, VC, PE operator)
- **Domain Trust**: 2 advisors (formulator, regulatory attorney) + 1 consultant (supply chain)
- **Execution Trust**: 2-3 advisors (D2C operator, growth marketer, retention expert)
- **Vibes Trust**: 1 (Nilo, internal)

**Total Equity Budget**: 1.0-1.5% (spread across 5-8 advisors)

### 5.2 Sequencing: Who to Recruit First?

**Phase 1: Foundation** (Pre-Product, Months 0-3)
- Priority: Strategic Trust (1 exited founder OR 1 VC)
- Why: Need thesis validation, market size confirmation before building
- Secondary: Domain Trust (1 formulator)
- Why: Need formulation feasibility before committing to product direction

**Phase 2: Build** (Product Development, Months 3-9)
- Priority: Domain Trust (complete with regulatory attorney, supply chain consultant)
- Why: Need to build compliant, manufacturable product
- Secondary: Execution Trust (1 D2C operator)
- Why: Need to design unit economics model, plan go-to-market

**Phase 3: Scale** (Launch, Months 9-18)
- Priority: Execution Trust (complete with growth marketer, retention expert)
- Why: Need to scale customer acquisition and retention
- Secondary: Strategic Trust (add VC + PE operator if not already aboard)
- Why: Prepare for fundraise or M&A conversations

**Logic**: Sequence mirrors TUP dependencies (can't optimize marketing before product exists, can't scale before proving unit economics).

### 5.3 Advisor Performance Review

**Annual Assessment** (for each advisor):

**Metric 1: Engagement** (did they show up?)
- Completed 4 quarterly calls? (yes/no)
- Response rate to ad-hoc questions? (>80% = good)
- Proactive value-add? (sent intros, articles, ideas without being asked)

**Metric 2: Impact** (did their advice matter?)
- Number of decisions influenced by advisor input (count specific instances)
- Quality of advice (did it work? did we use it?)
- Unique perspective (did they see things we missed?)

**Metric 3: Value Perception** (do we want to keep them?)
- Founder gut check: Is this relationship worth the equity? (yes/no/maybe)
- Alternative: Could we get same value elsewhere? (if yes → reconsider)
- Future value: Will they be even more valuable as we scale? (if yes → definitely keep)

**Decision Rule**:
- High engagement + High impact → Definitely keep (vest next year)
- Low engagement + High impact → Have conversation (are they too busy? reduce commitment?)
- High engagement + Low impact → Have conversation (is expertise mismatch? pivot role?)
- Low engagement + Low impact → Mutual exit (part ways amicably, freeze vesting)

**Honesty**: Advisors appreciate direct feedback. If relationship isn't working, better to end early than drag out.

## 6. Domain-Specific Procurement Tactics

### 6.1 Finding Formulators

**Method 1: Contract Manufacturer Referrals**
- Contact 5-10 supplement manufacturers (request quote for small batch)
- Ask: "Do you have an in-house formulator, or do you recommend independent consultants?"
- Manufacturers often know best independent formulators (they work with multiple brands)

**Method 2: Industry Conferences**
- SupplySide West (Las Vegas, annual) — largest supplement ingredient/formulation event
- Attend formulation workshops, talk to speakers, collect business cards
- Exhibitor list = directory of formulators, ingredient suppliers

**Method 3: LinkedIn + NutraIngredients**
- Search: "supplement formulator" OR "nutraceutical formulator"
- Read NutraIngredients articles, note author bios (many are formulators/consultants)

**Method 4: Reverse Engineering**
- Find brands you admire (formulation quality, transparency)
- Research: Who formulated their products? (sometimes disclosed on About page, blog posts)
- Cold outreach: "I saw you formulated [Brand X]. Would you be open to consulting on a new supplement brand?"

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Years of experience (10+ preferred)
- Format expertise (capsules, powders, gummies)
- Manufacturer relationships (can they recommend reliable partners?)
- Regulatory knowledge (understands FDA, structure/function claims)
- Communication style (can they explain technical details simply?)

### 6.2 Finding Regulatory Attorneys

**Method 1: Law Firm Directories**
- Amin Talati Wasserman (leading supplement regulatory firm)
- Kleinfeld, Kaplan & Becker
- Greenspoon Marder
- Hyman, Phelps & McNamara (FDA boutique)

**Method 2: Bar Association Referrals**
- American Bar Association (ABA) — Health Law Section
- Search: "FDA regulatory attorney" + "dietary supplements"

**Method 3: Reverse Engineering**
- Find brands with aggressive but compliant marketing (Momentous, Athletic Greens)
- Research: Who are their attorneys? (sometimes disclosed in FDA correspondence, warning letter responses)

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- FDA supplement experience (not just pharma or medical devices)
- Defended warning letters (shows they can handle enforcement)
- Claims review experience (can review marketing copy, landing pages)
- Retainer structure (prefer retainer vs hourly for ongoing relationship)

### 6.3 Finding D2C Operators

**Method 1: LinkedIn Reverse Search**

- Look up their operators: COO, VP Operations, GM
