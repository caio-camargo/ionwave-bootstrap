## 3. FTC Compliance for Supplement Landing Pages (2026)

### Core Requirements
1. **Truthful advertising**: All ads must be truthful and not misleading (FTC Act Sections 5 and 12)
2. **Substantiation required BEFORE disseminating ads**: Adequate substantiation for all objective claims
3. **Scientific evidence standard**: Health benefit claims require "competent and reliable scientific evidence" -- typically randomized, controlled human clinical trials (RCTs)

### Updated Health Products Compliance Guidance (Dec 2022)
- Replaced the 1998 "Dietary Supplements: An Advertising Guide for Industry"
- Now applies to ALL health-related claims, not just dietary supplements
- Quality of RCTs matters more than quantity
- Replication in independently-conducted studies adds evidentiary weight

### Testimonial & Endorsement Rules (Revised July 2023)
- **Material connections MUST be disclosed**: payments, free products, employment, family ties, affiliate relationships
- **Disclosures must be "clear and conspicuous" and "unavoidable"**
- On social media, disclosures cannot require consumer action to see them
- Video ads: disclosures must be in the video itself (visual and/or audible matching the claim medium)
- **Before/after testimonials**: Must either be substantiated as representative of typical results OR include clear disclosure of generally expected results
- **No fake reviews**: Consumer Reviews and Testimonials Rule prohibits fabricated testimonials
- Incentivized reviews are allowed but incentive must be disclosed; cannot require specific sentiment
- **Health product advertisers** held to higher monitoring standard for endorser oversight
- Intermediaries (agencies, PR firms) can be liable for deceptive endorsements they knew or should have known were deceptive

### 2026 Enforcement Priorities
- **Influencer/social media marketing**: Highest enforcement risk for wellness and supplement brands
- **Consumer Review Rule**: Civil penalties up to $53,088 per violation (increasing with inflation)
- **FTC-FDA coordination**: Both agencies reviewing social media and influencer content
- **"Made in USA" claims**: Aggressive policing continues
- **Functional/physiologic claims**: Plaintiffs shifting focus to these areas in consumer fraud litigation

### Recent Enforcement Actions (2025-2026)
- NextMed (Dec 2025): Final order for deceptive advertising of GLP-1 weight-loss programs
- Warning letters to 10 companies for Consumer Review Rule violations (Dec 2025)
- FTC signaling reduced tolerance for gray-area compliance strategies

### Required Disclosures Checklist for Supplement Landing Pages
- [ ] FDA disclaimer: "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA..."
- [ ] Typical results disclosure for any testimonials showing exceptional results
- [ ] Material connection disclosures for all endorsers/influencers
- [ ] "Individual results may vary" (while helpful, does NOT satisfy FTC requirements alone)
- [ ] Clear identification of advertising content (vs editorial)
- [ ] Substantiation documentation on file for every claim made

## 4. DSHEA (Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act) Requirements

### Claims Taxonomy

| Claim Type | Allowed? | Example | Requirement |
|------------|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Structure/Function** | YES | "Supports digestive health" | Substantiation + FDA notification + disclaimer |
| **Nutrient Deficiency** | YES | "Prevents scurvy when caused by Vitamin C deficiency" | Must also say how common the disease is in US |
| **General Well-being** | YES | "Promotes a feeling of well-being" | Same as structure/function |
| **Disease/Drug Claims** | NO | "Cures diabetes" / "Treats arthritis" | Only allowed for FDA-approved drugs |
| **Implied Disease Claims** | NO | "Lowers cholesterol" (implies heart disease prevention) | FDA uses 10 criteria to distinguish |

### Structure/Function Claim Requirements
1. Manufacturer must have substantiation that the claim is "truthful and not misleading"
2. Must submit notification to FDA within 30 days of first marketing with the claim text
3. Must include the DSHEA disclaimer prominently displayed and in boldface type
4. Must NOT imply the product diagnoses, treats, cures, or prevents any disease

### DSHEA Disclaimer (Mandatory)
> "This statement has not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

### Disclaimer Placement Rules (21 CFR 101.93)
- Must be placed adjacent to the structure/function claim OR linked with a symbol (e.g., asterisk)
- Traditionally required on each panel where a structure/function claim appears
- **December 2025 UPDATE**: FDA announced enforcement discretion -- will no longer enforce the "each panel" requirement
- FDA is considering formally amending the regulation to remove the multi-panel requirement
- Disclaimer still MUST appear on product labeling and be properly linked to each claim

### FDA vs FTC Jurisdiction
- **FDA**: Claims on product labeling (physical label, package inserts)
- **FTC**: Claims in advertising and direct marketing (digital ads, landing pages, emails, social media)
- **Internet sales**: Subject to BOTH FDA and FTC regulations simultaneously

## 8. A/B Testing Statistical Significance for E-commerce

### Sample Size & Duration Guidelines

| Factor | Guideline |
|--------|-----------|
| Statistical significance threshold | 95% (industry standard) |
| Minimum Detectable Effect (MDE) | 2-5% for most tests; 1-3% for mature programs |
| Sample per variation (example) | ~30,244 visitors for 10% MDE |
| Ideal test duration | 2-6 weeks |
| Minimum variations | 2 (A vs B) for low-traffic sites |

### Sample Size Formula
- **Total visitors needed** = Sample size per variation x Number of variations
- **Days to run** = Total visitors needed / Average daily visitors
- Use MDE of 2-5% as starting point if no historical data

### Common Mistakes
1. **Peeking** -- checking results before reaching significance; inflates false positive rate
2. **Stopping too early** -- ending test as soon as p-value hits 0.05 without planned sample size
3. **Running too many variations** -- dilutes traffic, extends time to significance
4. **Ignoring seasonality** -- holiday traffic, promotions, external events confound results
5. **Confusing statistical significance with business value** -- a statistically significant 0.1% lift may not matter
6. **Testing too long** -- beyond 6 weeks, confounding factors accumulate

### Modern Stats Engines (2026)
Both VWO and Optimizely have moved beyond simple frequentist p-value testing:

**VWO SmartStats (Bayesian)**
- Results declared at 95% probability threshold
- ROPE (Region of Practical Equivalence) for asymmetric power
- Real-time reporting with built-in bias correction
- Accounts for sequential testing errors and Bonferroni errors

**Optimizely Stats Engine (Sequential)**
- Two-tailed sequential likelihood ratio test with false discovery rate controls
- Can declare winner/loser up to 2x faster if observed effect exceeds MDE
- Named Leader in 2026 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Personalization Engines

## 9. Video Sales Letter (VSL) Best Practices for Supplements (2026)

### Optimal Length
- **General VSL**: 10-15 minutes for optimal engagement
- **Supplements (mid-price $40-80)**: 10-20 minutes
- **High-ticket supplements ($100+)**: 20-30 minutes
- **Simple/low-price products**: 3-6 minutes (can outsell long-form by 300%)
- **Rule**: As short as possible while delivering the full message

### VSL Structure (PAS Framework)

**Phase 1: Hook / Pattern Interrupt (0:00-0:30)**
- Curious question, unbelievable statistic, or story teaser
- Must snap viewer out of distraction immediately
- Example: "What if the bloating you've been dealing with isn't actually about the food you eat?"

**Phase 2: Problem Identification (0:30-3:00)**
- Define the problem the audience faces
- Make it urgent and relatable
- Use bullet list of pain points

**Phase 3: Problem Agitation (3:00-5:00)**
- Explain why it's worse than they think
- Address consequences of inaction
- Create emotional resonance

**Phase 4: Solution Introduction (5:00-8:00)**
- Present product as THE solution
- Explain mechanism of action
- For supplements: ingredient science, formulation rationale

**Phase 5: Credibility & Social Proof (8:00-12:00)**
- Doctor/expert endorsement
- Clinical studies or ingredient research
- Customer testimonials (with FTC-compliant disclosures)
- Before/after results (with typical results disclosure)

**Phase 6: Offer & Urgency (12:00-15:00)**
- Price anchoring and value stacking
- Limited-time offer or bonus incentives
- Risk reversal (money-back guarantee)

**Phase 7: Call to Action (15:00+)**
- Clear, specific next step
- Repeat key benefits
- Final urgency push

### Compliance Considerations for Supplement VSLs
- All claims must have substantiation on file
- Testimonials must include typical results disclosures
- Doctor endorsements must disclose material connections
- Structure/function claims only (no disease claims)
- DSHEA disclaimer must appear (can be on the landing page below the video)
- FTC monitors video advertising with same scrutiny as written ads

### Do VSLs Still Work for Supplements in 2026?
YES -- Emma Relief / Konscious Keto is proof of concept with their 30-minute VSL driving $4M+/yr in revenue and Inc. 5000 ranking. However:
- Attention spans require tighter hooks and faster pacing than pre-2020 VSLs
- Mobile optimization is critical (70%+ traffic is mobile)
- Compliance scrutiny is higher than ever
- Best results when VSL is part of a multi-funnel strategy (not standalone)

### Funnel Characteristics

- Doctor-endorsed VSL as primary conversion mechanism
- Heavy paid social (Facebook/Instagram) as primary acquisition channel
- TV advertising for scale (Tatari)
- Multi-channel retention (SMS + email + loyalty + subscriptions)
- Aggressive upsell/downsell sequences

### Red Flags / Caution Points
- F rating with BBB (24 complaints in past 12 months)
- Customer complaints about billing discrepancies, aggressive upsells, refund delays
- Address listed may be virtual office
- Website registered in Puerto Rico
- Dr. Gina Sam's presentation criticized for purposely raising viewer anxiety

### What to Learn from This Funnel
**DO**:
- Use a doctor/expert authority figure in your VSL
- Build a multi-channel tech stack (SMS + email + affiliates + paid social)

- Invest in subscription infrastructure early (Recharge)
- Test heavily on Facebook/Instagram (259+ creatives = significant testing volume)
- Consider TV advertising once unit economics support it (Tatari)

**DON'T**:
- Use anxiety-manipulation tactics that generate BBB complaints
- Implement aggressive upsells that create billing confusion
- Make refunds difficult (generates chargebacks and reputation damage)
- Sacrifice long-term brand trust for short-term revenue
