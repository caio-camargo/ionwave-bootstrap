## Advisory Board vs. Board of Directors

| Dimension | Board of Directors | Advisory Board |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| Legal authority | Fiduciary duties; binding decisions | No legal authority; advisory only |
| Liability | Personal liability (mitigated by D&O/indemnification) | No personal liability |
| Compensation | Equity + cash (at scale) | Equity only (typically) |
| Meetings | Formal, minutes required | Informal, no minutes required |
| Removal | Shareholder vote required | CEO discretion |
| Commitment | Significant (monthly meetings, fiduciary obligations) | Light (quarterly calls, occasional intros) |

**Key Point**: Advisors have no fiduciary duty, no voting power, and no governance authority. They are paid (in equity) for expertise, introductions, and credibility. [Confidence: B | Source: Standard startup advisory practice | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Advisor Compensation Framework

**Standard Advisory Equity Grants:**

| Engagement Level | Equity | Vesting | Expected Commitment |
|-----------------|--------|---------|---------------------|
| Standard Advisor | 0.25% | 2yr monthly, 6mo cliff | Quarterly calls + email access |
| Major Advisor | 0.50% | 2yr monthly, 6mo cliff | Monthly calls + active intros |
| Strategic Advisor | 0.75-1.0% | 2yr monthly, 6mo cliff | Weekly availability + deep involvement |

[Confidence: B | Source: FAST Agreement (Founder/Advisor Standard Template) by Founder Institute, Carta benchmarks | Verified: 2026-02-11]

**Budget Guard**: Total advisory equity should not exceed 2-3% of total cap table. At pre-seed, this means 4-6 advisors max at 0.25-0.50% each. [Confidence: C | Source: Standard startup cap table management | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Advisor Agreement Template

Every advisor signs a formal Advisor Agreement including:

1. **Term**: 24 months (renewable)
2. **Compensation**: NSO (Non-Qualified Stock Option) grant, vesting per schedule above
3. **Confidentiality**: NDA provisions covering all company information
4. **IP Assignment**: Any work product created for the company belongs to the company
5. **Conflicts**: Disclosure of any competing relationships
6. **Termination**: Either party can terminate with 30 days notice; unvested equity forfeited
7. **No Fiduciary Duty**: Explicitly states advisor has no fiduciary obligations

**Recommended Template**: FAST Agreement (Founder Institute) — free, widely used, investor-recognized. [Confidence: B | Source: Founder Institute FAST Agreement | Verified: 2026-02-11]

## Advisor Selection Criteria

### Scoring Framework (Per Candidate)

| Criterion | Weight | 5 (Excellent) | 3 (Good) | 1 (Weak) |
|-----------|--------|---------------|----------|----------|
| Domain expertise relevance | 30% | Exact domain match | Adjacent domain | General business |
| Network quality | 25% | Warm intros to 5+ targets | 2-3 intros | Name recognition only |
| Availability/responsiveness | 20% | Responds in <24hrs, proactive | Responds in 48hrs | Sporadic |
| Track record | 15% | Built/exited company in domain | Senior executive in domain | Consultant/academic |
| Complement to existing team | 10% | Fills critical gap | Adds perspective | Overlaps existing |

**Minimum Score to Offer Advisory Agreement**: 3.5/5.0 weighted average

### Red Flags in Potential Advisors

- Requests >1% equity for standard advisory role
- Wants a "co-founder" title without full-time commitment
- Cannot articulate specific ways they will help
- Has 10+ active advisory roles (spread too thin)
- Competing relationship with a direct competitor
- Asks for cash compensation at pre-seed stage
- Wants board observer rights without investment

## Operating Cadence

### Quarterly Advisory Board Meeting (Recommended)

| Element | Duration | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Company update | 15 min | KPIs, milestones, key challenges (CEO presents) |
| Strategic topic | 25 min | Pre-selected topic for advisor input |
| Open discussion | 15 min | Advisor questions, suggestions, connections to offer |
| Follow-ups | 5 min | Specific asks for each advisor |
| **Total** | **60 min** | |

### Between Meetings

- Advisors available for ad-hoc email/Slack questions
- CEO sends monthly 1-page update (can be same as investor update)
- Specific asks communicated directly (introductions, review requests)
- Track advisor engagement — inactive advisors should be released

### Annual Review

- Evaluate each advisor's contribution annually
- Renew or sunset advisory relationships based on actual value delivered
- Rotate in new advisors as company needs evolve (e.g., Phase 2 needs differ from Phase 1)
