## Purpose

This OpKit provides the complete legal and entity foundation for launching a D2C consumer brand. It covers entity formation, governance, equity planning, IP protection, consumer-facing legal documents, insurance, banking, and litigation preparedness. Designed for pre-launch to seed-stage companies.

## Instructions: 12-Step Entity & Legal Setup

### Step 1: Strategic Entity Decision (Day 1)
Decide your entity structure based on fundraising strategy:
- **VC track**: Delaware C-Corp (standard, recommended for most)
- **Bootstrapping**: Home state LLC (simpler, cheaper)
- **Web3/DAO**: Delaware C-Corp + planned future Wyoming DAO LLC subsidiary
- **Multi-brand**: Plan holding company architecture but start with standalone entity

### Step 2: Form the Entity (Day 1-3)
- File Certificate of Incorporation with Delaware Division of Corporations
- Obtain EIN from IRS (free, immediate)
- Appoint registered agent in Delaware
- File BOI Report with FinCEN (within 90 days)

### Step 3: Organizational Actions (Day 3-5)
- Hold organizational board meeting (adopt bylaws, elect officers, authorize shares)
- Issue founder stock with Restricted Stock Purchase Agreements
- FILE 83(b) ELECTIONS WITHIN 30 DAYS (no exceptions, no extensions)
- Adopt Employee Stock Option Plan (10-15% pool)

### Step 4: Cap Table Setup (Week 1-2)
- Create clean cap table with authorized shares, founder grants, and ESOP pool
- Model dilution scenarios through Series A
- Select cap table software (Pulley free tier or spreadsheet at minimum)
- Document all equity grants with board consent

### Step 5: IP Protection (Week 2-3)
- Execute IP Assignment Agreements from all founders/contractors
- Conduct trademark search (USPTO TESS + comprehensive)
- File trademark application in relevant classes
- Document trade secrets with NDA framework
- Secure domain names and social media handles

### Step 6: Insurance Setup (Week 3-4)
- General liability ($1M minimum)
- Product liability ($2-5M for supplement/consumable companies)
- Cyber liability (for D2C/e-commerce)
- D&O insurance (before accepting investment)
- Work with industry-specialist broker

### Step 7: Banking (Week 1-2)
- Open business bank account (Mercury for VC track, Relay for bootstrap)
- Set up account structure (Operating + Revenue + Tax Reserve)
- Connect to Shopify/e-commerce platform
- Connect to accounting software

### Step 8: Consumer-Facing Legal Documents (Week 3-4)
- Terms of Service (including subscription/auto-renewal disclosures)
- Privacy Policy (GDPR + CCPA compliant)
- Cookie Policy with consent management platform
- Product-specific disclaimers (FDA/DSHEA for supplements)
- Return/refund policy

### Step 9: Governance Framework (Week 2-4)
- Establish board meeting cadence (quarterly minimum)
- Create board consent resolution templates
- Document decision-making authority matrix
- Set up corporate records storage

### Step 10: Equity Strategy (Pre-First-Investment)
- Decide funding instrument (SAFE recommended at pre-seed)
- Set valuation cap range based on market benchmarks
- Model SAFE stacking dilution if multiple investors
- Document MFN and pro rata provisions

### Step 11: Litigation Preparedness (Pre-Launch)
- Build FTC advertising substantiation file
- Complete product safety testing (Prop 65 for supplements)
- Implement click-to-cancel for subscriptions
- Identify and establish relationships with counsel
- Create incident response protocol

### Step 12: Advisory Board (Month 2-3)
- Identify 3-5 target advisors across key domains
- Use FAST Agreement template for advisory agreements
- Set up quarterly advisory board meeting cadence
- Budget 2-3% of equity for advisory grants

## Grading Rubric: Entity & Legal Quality Assessment

### A-Grade (Excellent — 9-10/10)
- All documents attorney-reviewed and customized
- Active trademark registration (not just filed)
- Insurance in force with appropriate limits
- Cap table software active with all SAFEs modeled
- Privacy compliance verified by counsel
- FTC substantiation file complete and organized
- Board meeting minutes current

### B-Grade (Good — 7-8/10)
- Entity formed with proper governance documents
- All legal documents drafted but pending attorney review
- Trademark filed (awaiting registration)
- Insurance quotes obtained, policies being finalized
- Cap table modeled in software
- Privacy framework implemented with CMP
- FTC compliance framework in place

### C-Grade (Adequate — 5-6/10)
- Entity formed correctly
- Template-based legal documents (not customized)
- Trademark search done but not filed
- Insurance researched but not purchased
- Cap table in spreadsheet only
- Basic privacy policy posted
- Some FTC compliance gaps

### D-Grade (Insufficient — 3-4/10)
- Entity formed but missing governance documents
- No attorney review on any document
- No trademark protection
- No insurance
- Cap table not documented
- Privacy policy copied from template without customization
- No FTC compliance effort

### F-Grade (Failing — 1-2/10)
- Wrong entity type or no entity formed
- No legal documents
- IP not assigned to entity
- 83(b) elections missed
- No insurance, no banking, no compliance

## Scaffold: Entity & Legal Foundation Template

### For Any New D2C Brand, Create:

1. **Entity Decision Memo** (1-2 pages)
   - Entity type and state selection rationale
   - Holding company considerations
   - Fundraising strategy alignment

2. **Formation Checklist** (1 page)
   - Checklist of all formation steps with status tracking
   - Cost estimates and timeline

3. **Cap Table Model** (spreadsheet or software)
   - Authorized shares, founder grants, ESOP pool
   - Dilution scenarios through Series A

4. **IP Protection Plan** (1-2 pages)
   - Asset inventory
   - Trademark filing plan
   - Trade secret protection measures

5. **Legal Document Set**
   - Terms of Service
   - Privacy Policy
   - Cookie Policy
   - Product-specific disclaimers

6. **Insurance Plan** (1 page)
   - Tiered insurance checklist (launch-critical → growth)
   - Budget estimates by phase

7. **Litigation Preparedness Plan** (1-2 pages)
   - Top risk scenarios for your industry
   - Emergency contact list
   - Prevention measures

## Adaptation Guide

### For Non-Supplement D2C Brands
- Remove FDA/DSHEA-specific sections from TOS and disclaimers
- Adjust insurance checklist (product liability still needed but supplement-specialist broker not required)
- Remove Prop 65 considerations (unless product contains chemicals on the list)
- Privacy and cookie compliance framework is fully reusable

### For Bootstrapped (Non-VC) Brands
- Home state LLC instead of Delaware C-Corp
- Skip SAFE/equity strategy sections
- Simpler governance (no board, just operating agreement)
- Skip D&O insurance until revenue justifies it
- Cap table replaced by membership interest tracking

### For International D2C Brands
- Add jurisdiction-specific entity formation requirements
- Expand privacy compliance beyond US + EU
- Add international trademark strategy (Madrid Protocol)
- Consider international holding company tax structures

### For Multi-Brand Portfolios
- Implement holding company structure from formation
- IP ownership at parent level with licensing framework
- Separate cap tables per entity or consolidated at parent
- Cross-brand shared services framework

## Universal Principles

1. **Form before you fund**: Entity must exist before accepting any money
2. **83(b) is non-negotiable**: Missing the 30-day deadline is the costliest mistake in startup law
3. **Vesting protects everyone**: Including founders from each other
4. **Attorney review is not optional**: Templates start you; attorneys finish you
5. **Privacy is a moat, not a cost**: Proactive compliance builds consumer trust
6. **Insurance before revenue**: Product liability coverage must precede first sale
7. **Cap table is a living document**: Update after every equity event, use software from first external investment
8. **Plan for litigation, not just prevention**: Every supplement company will face a legal challenge eventually
