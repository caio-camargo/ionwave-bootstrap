## Terms of Service Framework

### Section 1: Agreement to Terms

**Required Elements:**
- Clear statement that using the website constitutes agreement to these terms
- Minimum age requirement (18+ for supplement purchases; some states restricting supplement sales to minors) [Confidence: B | Source: Skadden 2026 regulatory outlook — growing patchwork of state age restrictions | Verified: 2026-02-11]
- Last updated date (prominently displayed)
- Method for notifying users of material changes
- Browsewrap vs. clickwrap considerations (clickwrap at checkout is more enforceable)

### Section 2: Products and Services

**Required Elements:**
- Description of products offered (dietary supplements)
- **DSHEA Disclaimer** (MANDATORY): "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease." [Confidence: A | Source: DSHEA Section 403(r)(6), FDA labeling requirements | Verified: 2026-02-11]
- Accuracy of product descriptions (reservation of right to correct errors)
- Product availability limitations
- Right to limit quantities
- Pricing terms (prices subject to change; error correction)

### Section 3: Subscription Terms

**Critical for Auto-Renewal Compliance:**
- **FTC Negative Option Rule (2024)**: Must provide clear and conspicuous disclosure of material terms BEFORE obtaining billing information [Confidence: A | Source: 16 CFR Part 425, FTC Negative Option Rule effective 2024 | Verified: 2026-02-11]
- **Required disclosures:**
  - That the subscription will auto-renew
  - Frequency of charges
  - Amount of charges
  - How to cancel (must be at least as easy as sign-up — "click-to-cancel")
  - Cancellation deadline relative to next charge date
- **State-specific requirements**: California (ARL, SB-313), New York, and others have additional auto-renewal disclosure requirements
- **Click-to-cancel**: FTC rule requires cancellation mechanism as easy as sign-up. No phone-only cancellation. Online enrollment = online cancellation option required. [Confidence: A | Source: FTC Negative Option Rule, 2024 amendments | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Section 4: Orders and Payment

**Required Elements:**
- Order acceptance/rejection rights
- Payment methods accepted
- Sales tax collection and remittance
- Currency (USD)
- Billing errors and dispute resolution
- Authorization for recurring charges (subscription)

### Section 5: Shipping and Delivery

**Required Elements:**
- Shipping methods and estimated delivery times
- Shipping costs and free shipping thresholds
- Risk of loss (transfers to buyer upon delivery to carrier — UCC standard)
- International shipping limitations (if applicable)
- FTC Mail Order Rule: Must ship within 30 days of order (or stated timeframe) or offer cancellation [Confidence: A | Source: 16 CFR Part 435, FTC Mail, Internet, or Telephone Order Merchandise Rule | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Section 6: Returns and Refunds

**Required Elements:**
- Return window (30-day standard for supplements; some brands offer 60-90 day satisfaction guarantee)
- Condition requirements (opened vs. unopened)
- Refund method (original payment method)
- Return shipping responsibility
- Exclusions (if any)
- **State requirements**: Some states mandate minimum return periods or conspicuous posting of return policy

### Section 7: Intellectual Property

**Required Elements:**
- Ownership of all website content, trademarks, copyrights
- Limited license to use website for personal, non-commercial purposes
- Prohibition on copying, distributing, or modifying content
- User-generated content license (if applicable — reviews, social media)

### Section 8: User Conduct

**Required Elements:**
- Prohibited activities (fraud, scraping, reverse engineering, harassment)
- Account security responsibilities
- Right to terminate accounts for violations
- Compliance with applicable laws

### Section 9: Health and Safety Disclaimers (Supplement-Specific)

**CRITICAL for supplement companies:**
- **Not medical advice**: Products and content are not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment
- **Consult healthcare provider**: Users should consult their physician before using any dietary supplement
- **Allergen warnings**: Disclose common allergens (if applicable)
- **Pregnancy/nursing**: Standard warning to consult physician
- **Drug interactions**: Standard warning about potential interactions
- **FTC substantiation**: All product claims must be substantiated per FTC standards
- **Prop 65 warning** (California): If applicable, include Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act notice [Confidence: B | Source: CA OEHHA Prop 65 requirements, FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Section 10: Limitation of Liability

**Required Elements:**
- Cap on damages (typically limited to amount paid for products)
- Exclusion of consequential, incidental, and punitive damages
- Disclaimer of warranties (as permitted by law)
- Some states (e.g., New Jersey, Connecticut) limit enforceability of liability limitations

### Section 11: Dispute Resolution

**Options:**
- **Arbitration clause** (with class action waiver): Common in D2C, but increasingly challenged
- **Forum selection**: Delaware courts (matches incorporation state)
- **Governing law**: Delaware law
- **Small claims exception**: Allow small claims court as alternative to arbitration
- **Note**: California and some states restrict enforceability of arbitration clauses and class action waivers in certain consumer contexts

### Section 12: Privacy

- Reference to separate Privacy Policy (Tab 10)
- Statement that privacy practices are governed by Privacy Policy

### Section 13: Modifications

- Right to modify terms at any time
- Notification method (email for material changes; posting for minor changes)
- Continued use constitutes acceptance of modified terms

### Section 14: Contact Information

- Company legal name and address
- Customer service email and phone
- Legal/compliance contact (for legal notices)

## TOS Review Triggers (When to Update)

- New product launches (update product descriptions, allergen warnings)
- Subscription model changes (pricing, frequency options)
- New state regulations (auto-renewal, privacy, age restrictions)
- Expansion to new channels (Amazon, wholesale — may need separate terms)
- Material business changes (new entity structure, merger)
- FTC or FDA enforcement actions in the supplement industry
