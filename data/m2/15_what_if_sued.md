## Litigation Risk Profile for D2C Supplement Brands

### Why Supplement Companies Get Sued

D2C supplement companies face a higher litigation risk than many other consumer product categories due to:

1. **FDA does not pre-approve supplements** — product safety responsibility is entirely on the company [Confidence: A | Source: FDA.gov | Verified: 2026-02-11]
2. **Health claims attract scrutiny** — FTC aggressively enforces against unsubstantiated health claims [Confidence: A | Source: FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance | Verified: 2026-02-11]
3. **Class action attorneys target supplements** — Prop 65, false advertising, and consumer protection statutes enable fee-shifting that incentivizes plaintiff attorneys
4. **Contamination/adverse reactions** — even with good manufacturing practices, risks exist
5. **Subscription/auto-renewal disputes** — consumer protection agencies increasingly targeting subscription businesses

## Top 8 Litigation Scenarios for IonWave

### Scenario 1: Product Liability (Adverse Reaction)

**Risk Level**: HIGH
**Trigger**: Customer claims supplement caused adverse health effect (allergic reaction, illness, drug interaction)

**Immediate Response Protocol:**
1. Preserve all evidence (batch records, CoA, manufacturing records, customer communications)
2. Notify insurance carrier IMMEDIATELY (late reporting can void coverage)
3. Do NOT admit fault or make public statements
4. Document the adverse event using FDA MedWatch process
5. If serious adverse event: Mandatory FDA reporting within 15 business days (DSHEA Section 761) [Confidence: A | Source: 21 USC 379aa-1, FDA adverse event reporting requirements | Verified: 2026-02-11]
6. Engage product liability defense attorney
7. Initiate internal investigation (was batch CoA clean? Any manufacturing deviations?)

**Defense Strategies:**
- CoA documentation showing product met specifications at time of manufacture
- Third-party cGMP audit trail
- Warning labels and disclaimers properly displayed
- Customer health history may reveal pre-existing conditions or drug interactions

**Financial Exposure**: $50K-$500K per individual claim (defense costs + potential settlement). Class action: $500K-$5M+
**Insurance Coverage**: Product liability policy (see Tab 12 — $2-5M recommended)

### Scenario 2: FTC Enforcement (Unsubstantiated Health Claims)

**Risk Level**: HIGH
**Trigger**: FTC investigates marketing claims that lack adequate scientific substantiation

**Common Triggers for Supplement Companies:**
- Claims about disease treatment/prevention (crossing structure/function boundary)
- Testimonials implying atypical results without disclosure
- Influencer content making health claims without proper disclosures [Confidence: A | Source: FTC-FDA coordination on social media, 2026 enforcement priorities | Verified: 2026-02-11]
- "Made in USA" claims without meeting "all or virtually all" standard

**Immediate Response Protocol:**
1. Do NOT destroy or alter any marketing materials (evidence preservation)
2. Engage FTC defense attorney (specialized — not general business counsel)
3. Compile substantiation file for every claim made
4. Identify and preserve all advertising, social media, influencer content
5. Cooperate with investigation while protecting rights

**Financial Exposure**: $10K-$50K (consent decree/compliance costs) to $500K-$2M+ (civil penalties). FTC can also require refunds to all affected consumers.
**Prevention**: IonWave M7 Regulatory workshop already flagged 7 VSL violations with $420K-$2M exposure — P0 corrections identified. [Confidence: B | Source: M7 Regulatory workshop findings | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Scenario 3: California Prop 65 Lawsuit

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM-HIGH (if selling to CA customers without proper warnings)
**Trigger**: Product contains listed substance (e.g., lead, cadmium, arsenic) above Safe Harbor levels without Prop 65 warning

**Key Facts:**
- Private plaintiff attorneys can file Prop 65 claims (no government involvement needed)
- Bounty hunter incentive: Plaintiff attorneys collect fees + penalties
- Settlement range: $30K-$100K+ for supplement companies
- Heavy metals naturally present in many marine/mineral supplements

**Prevention (CRITICAL for Marine Plasma Product):**
1. Test every batch for Prop 65 listed substances (include in CoA requirements per M6)
2. If any substance exceeds Safe Harbor levels: Add Prop 65 warning to label and website
3. If testing shows compliance: Maintain records as defense
4. Consider proactive Prop 65 warning as low-cost risk mitigation (many supplement companies add the warning preemptively)

[Confidence: B | Source: CA OEHHA Prop 65 requirements, supplement industry Prop 65 litigation patterns | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Scenario 4: Subscription/Auto-Renewal Dispute

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM
**Trigger**: Customer or state AG claims auto-renewal was not adequately disclosed or cancellation was too difficult

**Key Regulations:**
- FTC Negative Option Rule (2024): Click-to-cancel required [Confidence: A | Source: 16 CFR Part 425 | Verified: 2026-02-11]
- California ARL (SB-313): Clear disclosure + easy cancellation
- Multiple states have auto-renewal specific statutes

**Prevention:**
- Implement click-to-cancel (online enrollment = online cancellation)
- Clear disclosure before collecting billing information
- Confirmation email for every subscription charge
- Easy-to-find cancellation mechanism (account dashboard + customer service)
- See Tab 9 TOS subscription terms for full compliance framework

### Scenario 5: IP Infringement (Trademark)

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM
**Trigger**: Another company claims IonWave trademark infringes on their existing mark, or IonWave discovers infringement of its marks

**If IonWave Is Accused:**
1. Do NOT ignore cease and desist letters
2. Engage trademark attorney to assess validity of claim
3. Search for prior art / prior use
4. Negotiate resolution (coexistence agreement, name modification, licensing)
5. If necessary, defend in TTAB proceedings or federal court

**If IonWave Discovers Infringement:**
1. Document infringement (screenshots, purchases, evidence)
2. Engage trademark attorney for cease and desist
3. Escalate to TTAB opposition or federal litigation if necessary
4. Monitor marketplace regularly (set up Google Alerts, trademark watching services)

**Financial Exposure**: $10K-$100K+ (defense or prosecution of trademark claims)

### Scenario 6: Employee/Contractor Dispute

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM
**Trigger**: Misclassification claim, discrimination, wrongful termination, wage/hour violation

**Key Risk Factor for IonWave**: M1 Labor Chain workshop flagged contractor misclassification as critical risk (one of 5 critical findings). [Confidence: B | Source: M1 workshop findings | Verified: 2026-02-11]

**Prevention:**
- Follow M1 classification guidelines strictly
- Document all employment/contractor relationships properly
- Implement proper onboarding and offboarding protocols
- EPLI insurance (see Tab 12)

### Scenario 7: Data Breach / Privacy Violation

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM
**Trigger**: Customer data breach, or violation of CCPA/GDPR data handling requirements

**Financial Exposure**: $50K-$500K+ (notification costs, regulatory fines, litigation)
**Prevention**: See Tabs 10-11 for privacy and cookie compliance. Cyber liability insurance (Tab 12).

### Scenario 8: Co-Founder Dispute

**Risk Level**: MEDIUM (if multiple founders)
**Trigger**: Disagreement over equity, direction, contributions, or departure terms

**Prevention (CRITICAL):**
- Vesting on all founder stock (4yr/1yr cliff)
- Written founder agreement before any work begins
- IP assignment from all founders
- Clear role definitions
- Shotgun/buyout provisions for irreconcilable disputes
- Up to 60% of founder disputes stem from equity distribution [Confidence: B | Source: Eqvista, Harvard Business School founder dispute research | Verified: 2026-02-11]
