**Meeting Cadence**: Quarterly (even for single-person board — creates documented decision trail for investor due diligence) [Confidence: C | Source: Startup governance best practice, Clerky guidance | Verified: 2026-02-11]

**Documentation Per Meeting**:
- Unanimous written consent (in lieu of meeting) is acceptable for routine matters
- For significant decisions (fundraising, pivots, IP deals), hold actual meeting with minutes
- Store all board records in a secure, organized location (corporate records folder)

## Board Member Selection Criteria

### Independent Director Criteria

The independent director is the most strategically important board appointment. They break ties and set governance tone.

**IonWave-Specific Ideal Profile:**

| Criterion | Weight | Why |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| D2C/CPG operating experience | 30% | Understands supplement/consumer brand execution |
| Fundraising network | 20% | Introduces to Series A+ investors |
| No conflict with investors | 15% | True independence — not affiliated with any investor |
| Supplement/wellness industry knowledge | 15% | Regulatory awareness (FDA/FTC) |
| Board experience at 2+ startups | 10% | Knows governance mechanics |
| Diversity of perspective | 10% | Avoid echo chamber |

**Where to Find Independent Directors:**
- Angel investor networks (not investing in this round)
- Industry conferences (Natural Products Expo West, SupplySide)
- Founder peer networks
- Board matching services (Advisory Cloud, The Board List)

### Investor Director Criteria

Investor board seats are typically assigned by the lead investor of each round. When choosing lead investors, evaluate their board member:
- Operating experience (have they built companies?)
- Responsiveness (will they actually help between meetings?)
- Portfolio conflicts (do they have competing investments?)
- Follow-on capacity (can they support the next round?)

## Board Meeting Operations

### Standard Meeting Agenda (Phase 2+)

| Section | Time | Content |
|---------|------|---------|
| Call to Order | 2 min | Quorum check, conflict disclosures |
| Approve Prior Minutes | 3 min | Approve minutes from last meeting |
| CEO Report | 15 min | KPIs, milestones, key decisions, challenges |
| Financial Review | 10 min | P&L, cash position, burn rate, runway |
| Strategic Discussion | 20 min | One or two strategic topics requiring board input |
| Action Items | 5 min | Decisions to ratify, follow-up assignments |
| Executive Session | 5 min | Board-only (CEO may step out if needed) |
| **Total** | **~60 min** | |

### Board Reporting Package (Sent 3-5 Days Before Meeting)

1. **Dashboard Summary**: Key metrics (MRR, CAC, LTV, churn, NPS)
2. **Financial Statements**: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, burn rate, runway
3. **Operating Update**: Product, marketing, team, partnerships
4. **Key Decisions Needed**: 1-3 items requiring board vote or input
5. **Fundraising Update** (if applicable): Pipeline, term sheets, timeline

### Meeting Cadence by Phase

| Phase | Frequency | Format | Duration |
|-------|-----------|--------|----------|
| Phase 1 (Pre-seed) | Quarterly | Written consent or video call | 30 min |
| Phase 2 (Seed) | Monthly | Video call | 60 min |
| Phase 3 (Series A) | Monthly | Video call + quarterly in-person | 90 min |

## Compensation

### Phase 1 (Pre-seed): No Board Compensation
- Founders serve as directors without additional compensation
- Standard for bootstrapped/pre-seed stage [Confidence: B | Source: Standard startup practice | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Phase 2 (Seed): Equity Only for Independent
- Investor directors: No compensation (their fund's return is their compensation)
- Independent directors: 0.25-0.5% equity grant, vesting over 2-3 years [Confidence: C | Source: Startup board compensation benchmarks | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Phase 3 (Series A+): Cash + Equity
- Independent directors: $10-25K annual retainer + equity refresh
- Committee chairs: +$5-10K annual retainer
- Investor directors: Still no cash compensation [Confidence: C | Source: Series A+ governance standards | Verified: 2026-02-11]

## Director Protections

### Indemnification
- **Bylaws provision**: Include broad indemnification of directors and officers [Confidence: A | Source: DGCL Section 145 | Verified: 2026-02-11]
- **Indemnification agreements**: Separate agreement for each director (provides individual enforceability beyond bylaw protection)
- **D&O Insurance**: Required before seating independent directors (see Tab 12)

### Exculpation
- **Charter provision**: Eliminate personal liability for breach of duty of care (permitted under DGCL Section 102(b)(7))
- Does NOT eliminate liability for: duty of loyalty violations, bad faith, intentional misconduct, unlawful dividends
- Standard in virtually all Delaware corporate charters
