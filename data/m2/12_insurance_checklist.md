## Supplement-Specific Insurance Considerations

### Why Supplement Companies Face Higher Insurance Costs

1. **FDA does not pre-approve supplements**: Unlike drugs, supplements do not undergo FDA approval before sale. The company bears full responsibility for product safety. [Confidence: A | Source: FDA.gov — Information for Industry | Verified: 2026-02-11]
2. **Contamination risk**: Industry data shows that roughly 25% of dietary supplements contain substances not listed on the label. [Confidence: B | Source: CFC dietary supplement insurance guide | Verified: 2026-02-11]
3. **Claims frequency**: Legal and investigation costs comprise over 70% of supplement insurance claims. [Confidence: B | Source: products-liability-insurance.com | Verified: 2026-02-11]
4. **Limited carrier market**: Many insurance carriers will not write product insurance for dietary supplements. Work with a supplement-specialist broker. [Confidence: B | Source: Admiral Insurance, Castle Rock Agency | Verified: 2026-02-11]

### Factors Affecting Premium

| Factor | Lower Premium | Higher Premium |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Manufacturing | Third-party cGMP certified | Self-manufactured or unaudited |
| Ingredients | Well-studied, established safety | Novel ingredients, limited safety data |
| Claims | Structure/function only | Any disease-related language |
| Revenue | <$500K annual | >$1M annual |
| History | Clean claims history | Prior claims or recalls |
| CoA documentation | Batch-tested, documented | Limited or no testing |
| International sourcing | Domestic only | International (esp. China) |

### Carrier Selection for Supplement Companies

**Specialist carriers and brokers:**

| Provider | Specialty | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Insurance Canopy | Small supplement businesses | Online quotes, competitive for <$1M revenue |
| Veracity Insurance | Vitamins and supplements | Industry specialist |
| Chubb | Mid-market+ supplement companies | Premium carrier, comprehensive coverage |
| CFC Underwriting | Dietary supplement program | Specialized program for supplements |
| Navion Insurance Associates | Dietary supplement liability | Deep industry expertise |

**Recommendation**: Start with Insurance Canopy or Veracity for competitive pre-launch quotes. Move to Chubb or CFC as revenue scales beyond $500K. [Confidence: C | Source: Carrier comparison research | Verified: 2026-02-11]

## Risk Mitigation (Reduce Premiums Over Time)

| Action | Premium Impact | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Maintain cGMP-certified manufacturer | -10-20% | Verify and document (see M6) |
| Batch-level CoA testing | -5-15% | Standard practice from Day 1 |
| No disease claims in marketing | Prevents coverage denial | FTC/FDA compliance (see M7) |
| Document adverse event protocol | Required by carriers | FDA MedWatch reporting process |
| Clean claims history | Compound savings over time | Rigorous quality control |
| Prop 65 compliance | Prevents CA claims | CoA testing for heavy metals |

## Annual Insurance Review Process

1. **90 days before renewal**: Review coverage limits vs. actual revenue, headcount, product lines
2. **60 days before renewal**: Get competitive quotes from 2-3 brokers
3. **30 days before renewal**: Select carrier, negotiate terms
4. **On renewal**: Confirm all certificates of insurance are current and distributed to partners/vendors who require them
5. **Ongoing**: Report any incidents or potential claims promptly (late reporting can void coverage)
