## 5-Dimension Quality Rubric

### Dimension 1: Evidence Coverage (4/5)

**Assessment**: All 15 content tabs are filled with substantive content. No tab is a placeholder. Every tab contains actionable checklists, comparison tables, or decision frameworks. Research draws from 2026-current sources across entity formation, privacy law, supplement regulation, insurance, and cap table management.

**What's Strong**:
- Entity formation (Tabs 2, 4) grounded in well-documented Delaware C-Corp patterns
- Privacy/cookie compliance (Tabs 10-11) reflects January 2026 regulatory changes
- Insurance (Tab 12) includes supplement-specific carriers and cost ranges
- Cap table (Tab 3) includes Carta/Pitchbook 2026 benchmark data
- Litigation scenarios (Tab 15) are specific to supplement D2C

**What's Weak**:
- DAO content (Tabs 2, 5) relies on emerging frameworks with limited case law
- Some cost estimates are wide ranges (insurance, legal budgets)
- No IonWave-specific data (e.g., actual trademark search, actual insurance quotes)

**Grade**: 4/5

### Dimension 2: Confidence Honesty (4/5)

**Assessment**: Every substantive claim has an inline confidence grade with source attribution and verification date. The grading follows the project's source quality hierarchy consistently. D-grade items are explicitly documented with upgrade paths. Health data processing, DAO governance, and cost estimates are honestly graded C-D where evidence is thin.

**What's Strong**:
- A-grade items limited to well-sourced regulatory requirements (FDA, IRS, DSHEA)
- B-grade items have specific source citations (Carta data, USPTO, formation services)
- D-grade items explicitly noted with upgrade paths
- No grade inflation detected during dialogue review

**What's Weak**:
- Some C-grade items could arguably be B (well-sourced but not independently verified)
- Cost estimates could use more specific ranges

**Grade**: 4/5

### Dimension 3: Upgrade Path Quality (4/5)

**Assessment**: Every tab ends with an Intelligence Gaps table that specifies: (1) what the gap is, (2) its impact level, and (3) a concrete upgrade path. Gaps range from "engage attorney" to "run trademark search" to "obtain insurance quotes" — all actionable.

**What's Strong**:
- 50+ intelligence gaps documented across 15 tabs
- Each gap has an explicit, actionable upgrade path
- Impact levels appropriately prioritized (CRITICAL for equity split, attorney review)
- Upgrade costs estimated where possible

**What's Weak**:
- Some upgrade paths are generic ("engage attorney") rather than specific ("engage attorney from [firm type] for [specific deliverable]")
- Timeline estimates for upgrades could be more specific

**Grade**: 4/5

### Dimension 4: Actionability (5/5)

**Assessment**: The content is designed for immediate use. Tab 1 (Legal Checklist) provides a complete sequenced action plan. Tab 4 (Entity Formation Guide) is a step-by-step execution guide. Tab 12 (Insurance) has a tiered priority system. Tab 13 (Bank Account) includes specific platform recommendations with comparison tables. All tabs include implementation checklists.

**What's Strong**:
- Sequenced priority framework (P0/P1/P2/P3) across multiple tabs
- Cost estimates attached to action items
- Decision frameworks (not just information) for key choices
- Cross-references between tabs prevent siloed execution
- IonWave-specific recommendations (not just generic guidance)

**What's Weak**:
- Very minor: Some decision frameworks require founder input that cannot be pre-determined (equity split, holding co structure)

**Grade**: 5/5

### Dimension 5: OpKit Reusability (3.5/5)

**Assessment**: The OpKit (OK-M2-001) is designed for any D2C supplement brand pursuing VC fundraising. The entity formation, cap table, insurance, and compliance frameworks generalize well. However, the DAO-compatible architecture is IonWave-specific and may not apply to most supplement brands. Privacy and cookie compliance frameworks are fully reusable.

**What's Strong**:
- Entity formation guide works for any supplement D2C startup
- Cap table framework generalizes to any VC-track consumer brand
- Insurance checklist applies to any supplement company
- Privacy/cookie compliance framework is jurisdiction-neutral

**What's Weak**:
- DAO architecture is IonWave-specific (niche applicability)
- Holding company phasing assumes multi-brand vision (not universal)
- Some content overly specific to IonWave (marine plasma, Studio 4)

**Grade**: 3.5/5
