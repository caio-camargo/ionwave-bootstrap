## When to Hire: Solo → Team Transition

### Phase 1: $0-$25K MRR — Operator Only
**Team Size**: 1 person (founder or operator)
**Scope**: Everything. Supplier coordination, ads, landing pages, email, fulfillment, investor relations.

**Don't hire yet if**:
- Revenue <$25K MRR
- Founder time <60 hrs/week consistently
- No repeatable playbook documented
- Unclear what tasks to delegate

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo M31 tab 642 "Internal Team" + industry standard | Date: 2026-02-16]

**IonWave Year 1 Reality**:
Pre-launch to Month 6, operator-only model. First hire likely Month 3-4 if hitting $15-25K MRR trajectory.
[Confidence: D | Reasoning: Pre-revenue estimate | Upgrade Path: Month 1-2 actual revenue data]

---

### Phase 2: $25K-$50K MRR — Add Creative PM
**Team Size**: Operator + Creative PM (part-time 20-30 hrs/week)
**Trigger**: Ad creative volume exceeds 40 hrs/month, or founder creative burnout

**What Creative PM Owns**:
- Ad creation (static, video, UGC)
- Video editing and design support
- Creative testing coordination
- On-call for creative sprints

**Compensation**: $25-40/hr or per-project
[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tabs 640-641, aligned with Upwork 2026 rates $39/hr avg | Date: 2026-02-16]

---

### Phase 3: $50K-$100K MRR — Add Ops PM
**Team Size**: Operator + Creative PM + Ops PM (part-time 20-30 hrs/week)
**Trigger**: Customer service >15 hrs/week, or 3PL complexity

**What Ops PM Owns**:
- Customer service (Tier 1-2 escalations)
- Inventory management and 3PL coordination
- Data entry, research, admin tasks
- Influencer outreach execution

**Compensation**: $10-20/hr (VA tier) or $25-40/hr (experienced ops)
[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo labor_strategy tab 643 + 2026 contractor market data]

---

### Phase 4: $100K+ MRR — Add Growth PM
**Team Size**: Operator + 3 PMs (Creative, Ops, Growth), max 4 people total
**Trigger**: New channel expansion (e.g., Amazon, B2B, retail pilots)

**What Growth PM Owns**:
- New channel testing and launch
- Partnership development
- Expansion market research
- Growth experiments

**Compensation**: $25-40/hr + performance bonus
[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tab 641 "Team1"]

**THE CONSTRAINT: 3-4 PMs MAXIMUM**
If you need more than 4 people, the system is too complex. Simplify before scaling headcount.
[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 641 "Team Structure" — explicit constraint]

## Labor Philosophy: Hire PMs Who Think, Not VAs Who Execute

**Core Principle**: We hire thinkers, not task executors.

**PM vs VA Distinction**:
| Dimension | PM (Product Manager) | VA (Virtual Assistant) |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Scope** | Owns outcomes | Executes tasks |
| **Decision-making** | Autonomous within domain | Requires instruction |
| **Compensation** | $25-40/hr + bonus/equity | $10-20/hr |
| **Hours** | 20-30/week (focused) | Variable (task-based) |
| **Example** | "Own ad creative strategy, test 5 angles, optimize to <$35 CAC" | "Edit these 10 videos per brief" |

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 643 "Labor Strategy" — "We hire PMs who can think, not VAs who just execute"]

**When to hire VA vs PM**:
- **VA**: Repetitive, well-defined tasks (data entry, basic CS, influencer outreach)
- **PM**: Strategic ownership (ad optimization, creative strategy, ops systems design)

[Confidence: B | Reasoning: Logical framework from Danilo's philosophy, validated by 2026 D2C hiring trends showing skills-based hiring prioritized over traditional retail experience | Source: accio.com/business/ecommerce-hiring-trends-2026]

## Operator vs Employee vs Contractor

### Operator (Equity-Based, Full Ownership)
**Definition**: Operates the business end-to-end as if they own it. Studio provides oversight, not execution.

**Scope**: Everything (supplier, ads, LP, email, fulfillment, investors)
**Hours**: 50-60/week
**Compensation**: Equity-based (10-25% typical for operator), below-market cash or none early
**Reports To**: Studio/Founder
**When to Hire**: Pre-launch or Month 1 if founder isn't operating full-time

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 640 "IONWAVE TEAM STRUCTURE" + tab 645 compensation framework]

**Operator vs CEO**:
- Operator = execution-focused, part of portfolio, equity-heavy comp
- CEO = strategy + execution, full-time single venture, can raise capital independently, hire/fire authority

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tab 659 "CEO JOB SPEC" — distinction well-defined]

### Full-Time Employee (Equity + Cash)
**When**: Post-PMF (>$1M ARR typically), need dedicated strategic roles

**Compensation**:
- Cash: At-market salary
- Equity: 0.5-2% typical
- Vesting: 4-year with 1-year cliff (industry standard)

[Confidence: B | Source: Carta benchmarks + Danilo tab 645 | Date: 2026-02-16]

**2026 Market Context**:
58% of UK tech startups offer equity to all employees (up 16% YoY). However, boards/VCs focus on dilution amid tighter funding.
[Confidence: A | Source: Ravio 2026 Compensation Trends report via ravio.com | Date: 2026-02-16]

**Hybrid Creative Collaboration Model** (remote-first with quality):
85% of workers prioritize remote flexibility, but creative work benefits from in-person collaboration. **Solution**: Remote 90% of time, co-location for 3-5 day creative sprints every 6-8 weeks.

**How it works**:
- **Remote by default**: Creative PM works from home, async communication (Slack, Loom)
- **Creative sprints**: Every 6-8 weeks, Creative PM + Operator co-locate for 3-5 days intensive work (brainstorm 20+ ad angles, shoot UGC, design LP variants)
- **Cost**: 4-6 sprints/year × $500-1K travel = $2-6K/year (worth it for quality + talent pool access)

[Confidence: B | Source: Dialogue Round 6 upgrade + FlexJobs 55% prefer hybrid | Date: 2026-02-16]

## PM Competence Framework

**What is PM Competence?**
Deep understanding of product, market, and customer such that you can make good decisions without asking.

### Signs of PM INCOMPETENCE:
- Can't explain product in detail without looking it up
- Can't name competitors and their positioning
- Can't describe ideal customer's daily life
- Guessing at pricing rationale
- Surprised by customer objections

### Signs of PM COMPETENCE:
- Can debate product decisions from first principles
- Knows competitor strengths/weaknesses cold
- Can predict customer reactions
- Pricing tied to clear value logic
- Customer objections anticipated and scripted

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 669 "PM COMPETENCE FRAMEWORK" — 59-row detailed assessment]

---

### Competence Assessment: 18-Question Scorecard

#### PRODUCT (6 questions):
1. What are the exact ingredients? (Competent: lists all with mg amounts | Incompetent: vaguely knows "electrolytes")
2. Why ocean-sourced vs synthetic? (Competent: 78 minerals, natural ratios, plasma match | Incompetent: "It's more natural")
3. What health claims can we make? (Competent: knows FDA structure/function limits | Incompetent: not sure/makes stuff up)
4. How is it manufactured? (Competent: knows supplier process | Incompetent: no idea)
5. What does it taste like? (Competent: has tasted, can describe | Incompetent: hasn't tried it)
6. Why this price point? (Competent: unit economics + positioning logic | Incompetent: competitor-based guess)

#### MARKET (6 questions):
1. Who are top 5 competitors? (Competent: names, positioning, pricing, weaknesses | Incompetent: "LMNT and... um...")
2. What's LMNT's weakness? (Competent: synthetic only, no trace minerals | Incompetent: "They're expensive?")
3. Market size? (Competent: TAM/SAM/SOM with sources | Incompetent: "$X billion" no source)
4. Why now? (Competent: specific trends with evidence | Incompetent: "Health is trending")
5. Who else tried and failed? (Competent: names failures and why | Incompetent: don't know)
6. What are substitutes? (Competent: coconut water, sports drinks, salt tabs | Incompetent: "Just other electrolytes")

#### CUSTOMER (6 questions):
1. Describe ideal customer's day (Competent: detailed routine with pain points | Incompetent: "Active health-conscious person")
2. Top 3 objections to buying? (Competent: price, taste, "I have LMNT" | Incompetent: not sure)
3. What triggers purchase? (Competent: specific moments/contexts | Incompetent: "When they need electrolytes")
4. What words do THEY use? (Competent: quotes from interviews/reviews | Incompetent: benefits language - our words)
5. What would disappoint them? (Competent: specific unmet expectations | Incompetent: "If it didn't work")
6. Why would they churn? (Competent: specific reasons with data | Incompetent: "If they find something better")

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 669 — complete framework with competent vs incompetent examples]

---

### Scoring Rubric

**Total possible**: 90 points (18 questions × 5 points each)

**Score Bands**:
- **75-90**: PM Competent — Ready to execute
- **60-74**: PM Developing — Gaps to fill before scaling
- **45-59**: PM Deficient — Major knowledge gaps, pause and learn
- **<45**: PM Incompetent — Not ready to run this product

**RULE: Don't spend money on ads until PM Score > 60**
You're burning cash if you don't understand what you're selling.

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 669 — explicit scoring bands and ad spend rule]

### How to Build Competence (10-Step Protocol)

1. **USE THE PRODUCT** for 2+ weeks
2. **READ** every tab in the ops model
3. **DO** 10+ customer interviews
4. **STUDY** 5 competitors deeply (buy their products)
5. **READ** all reviews (yours and competitors')
6. **WRITE** ad copy yourself (forces understanding)
7. **ANSWER** customer support tickets (reveals gaps)
8. **VISIT** the supplier/manufacturer
9. **TALK** to industry experts
10. **TEACH** someone else about the product (reveals gaps)

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 669 — complete competence-building protocol]

**IonWave Application**:
Operator must complete this protocol Week 1-2. PM Competence Score should be >75 by Week 3 before first ad dollar spent.
[Confidence: D | Reasoning: Pre-launch assumption | Upgrade Path: Operator hired, competence scored]

## Intelligence Gaps

### CRITICAL Gaps (require validation before scaling):
1. **First hire timing for IonWave** — $15K? $25K? Founder burnout trigger?
   **Impact**: Premature hire = cash burn. Late hire = founder burnout, missed growth.
   **Validation**: Track founder hrs/week Month 1-3. If >60 hrs sustained + revenue >$15K MRR, start recruiting.

2. **Operator equity split** — 10%? 15%? 20%? Negotiation range?
   **Impact**: Too low = can't attract A-players. Too high = founder dilution regret.
   **Validation**: Survey 3-5 D2C operators, ask "What equity % would you require for pre-launch D2C brand with $30-50K studio backing?"

3. **Remote vs in-person for creative PM** — Does creative work require co-location?
   **Impact**: Geographic restriction limits talent pool vs collaboration quality.
   **Validation**: Test both. Hire remote first (larger pool). If creative output <8/10 quality after Month 1, reassess.

### MEDIUM Gaps (useful but not blocking):
4. **Contractor marketplace best fit** — Upwork vs Contra vs Freelancer.com vs Twitter/X DMs?
   **Impact**: Sourcing efficiency.
   **Validation**: Post same role on 3 platforms, track applicant quality and response rate.

5. **Onboarding time to autonomy** — Is 8-11 hrs realistic for Minimum Viable Onboarding?
   **Impact**: Founder time investment.
   **Validation**: Time actual onboarding, compare to M19 CRM "8-11 hour Day 1 setup" benchmark.

### LOW Gaps (nice to know):
6. **Venture Partner ROI** — Do deal-maker roles pencil at pre-PMF stage?
   **Impact**: Likely Phase 3+ role (post-$100K MRR), not Year 1-2 relevant.
   **Validation**: Defer until $50K+ MRR, revisit with real partnership opportunities.
