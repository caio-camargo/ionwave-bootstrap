## Compensation Philosophy

**Core Beliefs**:
- **Pay fairly** — Not lowest possible, not highest possible. Market-informed, context-adjusted.
- **Equity for value creators** — Those who build long-term value get equity. Task executors get cash.
- **Transparency** — People know how comp decisions are made (frameworks, not arbitrary).
- **Performance matters** — High performers earn more over time (bonuses, equity acceleration, promotions).

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 645 "Compensation Philosophy"]

## Compensation Framework by Role Type

| Role Type | Cash | Equity | Notes |
|-----------|------|--------|-------|

## Equity Allocation Benchmarks (2026 Data)

### Typical Startup Equity Pool:
- **Total employee pool**: 13-20% of total equity
- **First hire median**: ~1% of company
- **Recommended baseline**: 0.75% for mid-level technical first hire

**Seniority Multipliers**:
- Junior: 1x
- Mid-level: 5x
- Senior: 10x

[Confidence: A | Source: Carta benchmarks 2026 via Mucker Capital + Ravio 2026 report | Date: 2026-02-16]

**Example**: If first hire (mid-level operator) gets 0.75%, then:
- Junior PM: 0.15% (0.75 ÷ 5)
- Senior Head of Ops: 7.5% (0.75 × 10)

**Reality Check**: These benchmarks are for traditional startups. D2C operator model skews higher (10-25%) due to:
- Equity-heavy comp (low/no cash)
- Full P&L ownership
- Studio 4 model (operators run multiple ventures)

[Confidence: B | Reasoning: D2C operator model differs from typical startup employee | Source: Danilo tab 645 + industry norms]

- **44% opt for equal splits** (50/50)

### Studio 4 + Operator Model (IonWave):
| Party | Equity Range | Typical | Rationale |
|-------|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **Studio 4** | 50-70% | 60-65% | Capital provider, studio infrastructure, oversight |
| **Operator** | 10-25% | 15-20% | Execution owner, sweat equity, upside participation |
| **Future hires** | 5-15% | 10% | Employee pool (reserved for key hires) |
| **Advisors** | 1-3% | 2% | Strategic advisors, industry experts |

[Confidence: C | Source: Danilo tab 645 + Studio 4 model inference | Note: Specific Studio 4/Operator split TBD, requires negotiation]

**IonWave Operator Negotiation**:
Start at 15% (midpoint), adjust based on:
- **+5%** if operator has deep D2C track record (scaled brand to $100K+ MRR)
- **+3%** if operator invests cash ($5-10K)
- **-3%** if operator is junior (first operator role)
- **-5%** if operator wants above-market cash comp

**Dilution Waterfall Example** (critical for cap table planning):
| Party | Equity % | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Studio 4 | 65% | Capital provider, infrastructure, oversight |
| Operator | 20% | Execution owner (upper range of 10-25%) |
| **Subtotal Allocated** | **85%** | Studio + operator |
| **Remaining** | **15%** | For future hires, advisors, reserve |
| ↳ Employee pool | 10% | Reserved for Creative PM, Ops PM, Head of Ops, etc. |
| ↳ Advisors | 3% | Strategic advisors (0.25-1% each, ~3-5 advisors) |
| ↳ Reserve | 2% | Buffer for unexpected key hires |

**Implication**: If you grant operator 20%, you have only 10% left for ALL future employees. At $100K+ MRR with 3-4 PMs, that 10% must cover: Creative PM (0.5-1%), Ops PM (0.5-1%), Growth PM (0.5-1%), Head of Ops (1-2%) = 3-5% total. Feasible but tight. **Don't over-grant to operator** without understanding downstream constraints.

## Vesting Schedules (Industry Standard)

### Standard 4-Year Vest with 1-Year Cliff:
- **Year 1**: 0% (cliff — must stay 1 year to get any equity)
- **Year 1 + 1 day**: 25% vests
- **Months 13-48**: Monthly vesting (1/48th per month of remaining 75%)

**Example**: Operator with 15% equity, 4-year vest:
- Month 0-11: 0%
- Month 12: 3.75% (25% of 15%)
- Month 13: 3.75% + 0.234% = 3.984%
- Month 48: 15% fully vested

[Confidence: A | Source: Industry standard, Carta data, Danilo tab 645]

**Early Exercise Option**:
Some operators negotiate right to buy unvested shares at strike price ($0.001 for pre-revenue). Starts capital gains clock earlier.

[Confidence: B | Source: Startup equity compensation guides | Note: Requires 83(b) election filing within 30 days]

## Cash vs Equity Trade-Offs

### Operator Cash Compensation Scenarios:

| Scenario | Cash | Equity | When to Use |
|----------|------|--------|------------|
| **Max Equity** | $0/year | 20-25% | Operator has runway (savings/spouse income), max upside |
| **Balanced** | $30-50K/year | 15-18% | Operator needs some cash, willing to defer upside |
| **Cash-Heavy** | $60-80K/year | 10-12% | Operator prioritizes stability, less equity risk |

[Confidence: C | Source: Danilo tab 645 framework + market norms | Note: IonWave-specific ranges TBD]

**Rule of Thumb**: Every $10K cash = ~1.5-2% equity reduction (rough equivalent at $300-500K pre-money valuation).

**Example Negotiation**:
- Operator wants $40K cash instead of $0
- $40K ÷ $10K = 4 units
- 4 units × 1.75% avg = 7% equity reduction
- If starting at 20%, cash-adjusted offer = 13% equity + $40K cash

[Confidence: D | Reasoning: Valuation-dependent math, pre-money valuation ($300-500K) is assumption | Upgrade: Formalize Studio 4 valuation policy]

## Market Data Sources (2026)

### Compensation Benchmarks:
1. **Levels.fyi** — Tech roles, salary + equity data (mostly Bay Area/NYC, adjust -20-30% for remote/D2C)
2. **Glassdoor** — General salary data, self-reported (treat as directional, not precise)
3. **LinkedIn Salary** — Emerging tool, less data than Glassdoor
4. **Pave** — Startup-specific comp data (subscription required, $$$)
5. **Carta** — Equity benchmarks, vesting schedules (free reports published annually)
6. **Ask founders at similar stage** — Most accurate for D2C/similar model (informal network survey)

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 645 + market sources verified 2026-02-16]

**IonWave Strategy**:
Use Carta for equity benchmarks (free), ask D2C founder network for cash comp (informal), ignore Levels.fyi (tech-skewed).
