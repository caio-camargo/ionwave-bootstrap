## Operator Sourcing Channels

### 1. Upwork (Primary for contractors/PMs)
**How**: Search "D2C", "ecommerce", "Shopify operators"
**Pros**: Large pool (18M freelancers), $39/hr avg rate, vetted portfolios
**Cons**: Variable quality, requires screening rigor

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 656 + Upwork 2026 stats (affinco.com) | Date: 2026-02-16]

### 2. Twitter/X (Best for operators with ownership mindset)
**How**: DTC community search, "looking for opportunity", direct outreach
**Pros**: High-signal candidates (self-marketing = ownership), cultural fit easier to assess
**Cons**: Smaller pool, time-intensive networking

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tab 656 + D2C Twitter trends 2026]

### 3. LinkedIn (Traditional, lower signal for D2C)
**How**: Search "previous brand operators", filter by D2C/Shopify experience
**Pros**: Professional profiles, easy to verify background
**Cons**: Resume-optimized (not execution-optimized), less ownership signal

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tab 656]

### 4. Communities (Trends, EcomCrew, D2C Slack/Discord)
**How**: **Community engagement playbook** (3-step, 2-4 week process)
1. **Lurk 2-4 weeks** — Observe who contributes high-quality insights, who asks smart questions
2. **Contribute value** — Answer questions, share tactical insights, build credibility (not selling)
3. **DM high-signal contributors** — Personalized outreach referencing their contributions, not cold-pitch

**Pros**: Warm intros, pre-vetted by community reputation, higher response rate than cold outreach
**Cons**: Time-intensive (2-4 weeks), competitive (everyone recruiting from same pool)

**Specific Communities**:
- Trends.vc community (D2C founders, operators)
- Private D2C Slack/Discord groups (ask for invites from D2C founders)
- EcomCrew forums (more established brands, good for senior hires)

[Confidence: B | Source: Danilo tab 656 + Dialogue Round 5 upgrade]

### 5. Referrals (Highest signal, lowest volume)
**How**: Ask other founders "Who would you hire if you could clone someone?"
**Pros**: Trusted vetting, cultural fit pre-screened
**Cons**: Network-limited, slow

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 656 + hiring best practices]

**IonWave Sourcing Strategy**:
**Pipeline-Building Timeline** (always-be-recruiting):
- **Pre-launch**: Post operator role (hire Month 1-2, but start sourcing 4-6 weeks early)
- **Month 1-2**: Post Creative PM role (hire Month 3-4, build pipeline early)
- **Month 3**: Post Ops PM role (hire Month 5-6)

Why start 4-6 weeks early? Best candidates have multiple offers in 2026's competitive market (4.5% unemployment). Early pipeline = select from pre-vetted pool when ready to hire, not scrambling.

**Month 2-3 Operator Hire Execution**:
Start with referrals (ask 5 D2C founders), parallel-post Upwork + Twitter. Target 10-15 applicants for 3-5 screening interviews. Compress to 2-4 week hire timeline by pre-building pipeline.

[Confidence: C | Source: Dialogue Round 2 upgrade | Reasoning: Market-validated timeline strategy]

## Interview Process: 3-Stage Funnel

### Stage 1: Screening Interview (30 min, phone/video)
**Purpose**: Filter for D2C experience, results orientation, learning velocity, motivation

**Questions**:
1. **Background**: Walk me through your D2C experience
2. **Results**: Best result you achieved? What drove it?
3. **Process**: How do you approach launching a new product?
4. **Learning**: Tell me about something you taught yourself
5. **Motivation**: Why this opportunity vs starting your own brand?

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 652 "Screening Interview"]

**Pass Criteria**:
- Can articulate specific D2C results with numbers
- Demonstrates ownership mindset ("I figured it out" vs "I was told to...")
- Learning velocity: self-taught skills in last 6-12 months
- Clear motivation beyond just cash

**IonWave Screening** (Month 2-3):
Run 3-5 screening interviews. Advance 1-2 to focused interview.
[Confidence: D | Upgrade: Actual interview funnel data]

---

### Stage 2: Focused Interview (60-90 min, deep dive)
**Purpose**: Assess domain competence (media buying, creative, ops, analytics)

**Questions by Domain**:
- **Media Buying**: Show me a campaign you ran. Walk me through optimization.
- **Creative**: How do you approach testing? What makes a winning ad?
- **Ops**: How do you manage inventory? What systems do you use?
- **Analytics**: What metrics do you track daily? How do you make decisions?

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 653 "Focused Interview"]

**IonWave Application**:
Focus on media buying + creative (core Year 1 competencies). Ops/analytics secondary.
Give candidate IonWave M0 thesis, ask: "If you were launching this, what would you test first and why?"

**PM Competence Assessment Integration**:
Weave PM Competence Framework (M31 tab 669, 18 questions) conversationally into this interview. Don't administer as formal 2-hour test. Ask 10-12 critical questions naturally:
- Product: "What are the exact ingredients? Why ocean-sourced vs synthetic?"
- Market: "Who are top 5 competitors? What's LMNT's weakness?"
- Customer: "Describe ideal customer's day. Top 3 objections to buying?"

If candidate can't answer 12+ of 18 competently, they lack PM Competence (score <60). Don't hire.

[Confidence: B | Source: Dialogue Round 1 upgrade | Reasoning: Conversational embedding reduces time barrier while maintaining rigor]

---

### Stage 3: Reference Check (15-20 min per reference, 2-3 references)
**Questions**:
1. How would you describe their working style?
2. What were their biggest strengths?
3. Where did they struggle or need support?
4. Would you work with them again? Why or why not?
5. How did they handle ambiguity and pressure?

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 654 "Reference Interview"]

**Red Flags**:
- Hesitation on "Would you work with them again?"
- "Needed a lot of direction" (conflicts with autonomy requirement)
- "Struggled under pressure" (D2C is high-pressure)

## Firing / Offboarding Guide (When Letting Someone Go)

### BEFORE FIRING - Checklist
- ☐ Is this decision final? (Have you tried to make it work?)
- ☐ Documented performance issues? (Written warnings if applicable)
- ☐ Legal review? (Especially for employees, discrimination risk)
- ☐ Severance prepared? (If offering)
- ☐ Final paycheck ready? (Required immediately in some states)
- ☐ Access revocation plan? (Systems, email, Slack, etc.)
- ☐ Equipment return plan? (Laptop, etc.)
- ☐ Communication plan? (What to tell team)

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 646 "Firing Offboarding Guide" — 33 rows]

### THE CONVERSATION

**DO**:
- Be direct and clear ("We're letting you go" not "we're restructuring")
- Be brief (5-10 minutes, not a long discussion)
- Be compassionate but firm
- Have a witness present
- Explain next steps (severance, final pay, benefits)

**DON'T**:
- Apologize excessively
- Negotiate or debate
- Make promises you can't keep
- Discuss other employees
- **Do it on Friday** (gives them weekend to stew with no network/recruiter access — do Monday-Thursday for immediate action)
- **Do it right before major holidays** (Thanksgiving week, Christmas week — same reasoning, limited support network)

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 646]

### IMMEDIATE OFFBOARDING (Day of firing):
- ☐ Revoke email access
- ☐ Revoke Slack/Teams access
- ☐ Revoke all tool access (Shopify, ad accounts, etc.)
- ☐ Revoke code repository access
- ☐ Change shared passwords
- ☐ Remove from company credit cards
- ☐ Collect equipment
- ☐ Remove from bank accounts (if applicable)
- ☐ Update cap table (if equity holder)

[Confidence: A | Source: Danilo tab 646]
