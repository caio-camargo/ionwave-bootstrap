## VSL Portfolio Strategy

**Don't bet on one VSL. Build a portfolio.**

VSLs fatigue after 4-6 weeks. You need a pipeline of new angles constantly testing.

### Month-by-Month Portfolio Build

| Month | Activity | Expected Outcome | Cumulative Winners | Monthly Revenue Enabled |
|-------|----------|------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Month 1** | Test 3-5 angles ($6,000-10,000 investment) | 1 winner | 1 | $5,000-15,000 |
| **Month 2** | Scale M1 winner, test 3 new angles ($6,000) | 1 more winner | 2 | $15,000-30,000 |
| **Month 3** | Scale M1+M2 winners, test 3 new angles ($6,000) | 1 more winner | 3 | $30,000-100,000 |

**Total investment by Month 3**: $7,500-15,000 (production + testing)
**Expected monthly revenue enabled**: $30,000-100,000 (assumes 3 winners scaling)

**Key insight**: By Month 3, you should have 3 winning VSLs, each on its own scaling curve. One fatigues → others carry revenue. Continuous testing = continuous winners.

[Confidence: B | Source: Derived from ClickBank portfolio strategies, IonWave-specific data unavailable until post-launch | Date: 2026-02-09]

## VSL Fatigue Timeline

| Week | CPA Trend | CTR Trend | Action |
|------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Week 1-2** | Stable or improving | Stable | Scale aggressively (20% budget increase every 48 hrs) |
| **Week 3-4** | Stable | Slight decline (10-15%) | Monitor frequency, prepare new hook variants |
| **Week 5-6** | Increasing 10-20% | Declining 20%+ | Launch new hook on same angle OR retire VSL, launch new angle |
| **Week 7+** | Increasing 30%+ | Declining 30%+ | Kill VSL, archive learnings, move to next angle |

**Average winning VSL lifespan**: 4-6 weeks at peak performance.

**Fatigue signals**:
- CPA rises 20%+ from peak
- CTR declines 30%+
- Frequency > 2.5 (same person seeing ad multiple times)

**Mitigation**: Launch new hook variant on winning angle every 2-3 weeks (extends lifespan to 8-10 weeks).

[Confidence: A | Source: Direct response creative fatigue benchmarks (Agora, ClickBank) | Date: 2026-02-09]

## Break-Even Analysis

**Question**: At what CPA does IonWave break even on first purchase?

**Assumptions**:
- AOV: $47 (1-month subscription)
- COGS: $14 (30% of AOV per M3 Financial Model)
- Gross margin: $33 (70% of AOV)
- Contribution margin after ad spend: $33 - CPA

**Break-even CPA**: $33 (at 70% gross margin scenario)

**IonWave scenarios**:
- **67% margin** (Bootstrap scenario): Break-even CPA = $31.49
- **40% margin** (Danilo scenario): Break-even CPA = $18.80

**Current target CPA ($30)**: Profitable at 67% margin, UNPROFITABLE at 40% margin on first purchase.

**Why this matters**: If margin is 40% (Danilo), we MUST have high subscription rates (3.7x LTV) to hit profitability. If margin is 67% (Bootstrap), we're profitable on first purchase.

**REC-001 BLOCKER**: VSL economics depend on resolving margin dispute. At 40% margin, we need 80%+ subscription rate. At 67% margin, 50% is fine.

[Confidence: A | Source: M3 Financial Model data, M25 Financial Ops margin analysis | Date: 2026-02-09]
