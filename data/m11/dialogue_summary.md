## Key Themes

### 1. FTC Compliance Is Existential (3 critical upgrades)
- Fictional testimonials flagged in graded script
- Mechanism (98% bioavailability) requires RCT substantiation OR pivot to structure/function claims
- Guarantee language must match actual refund capability
- Paid testimonials need material connection disclosure
- Quarterly compliance audits mandatory

**Takeaway**: Legal review is not optional. One FTC violation costs $53K+ per instance.

### 2. Testing Hierarchy Needs Precision (4 upgrades)
- Angle vs hook distinction clarified (ANG-01-05 are hooks on ONE angle)
- Kill criteria need minimum purchase thresholds (10+) to avoid small-sample variance
- CPA creep tolerance thresholds added (< 15% green, 15-30% yellow, > 30% red)
- Hook rate benchmarks don't apply to category-creating products

**Takeaway**: Statistical rigor prevents false kills and false winners.

### 3. Portfolio Strategy Over Hero Creative (3 upgrades)
- Winner rate adjusted to industry standard (10-20%, not 20-40%)
- Portfolio health dashboard added (track meta-patterns across all VSLs)
- Kill-and-pivot protocol added (when all angles fail, pause and meta-analyze)

**Takeaway**: VSL production is a portfolio game. One winner pays for 5-10 killed angles.

### 4. Production Efficiency Matters (3 upgrades)
- B-roll + VO validated over founder video for testing phase (cheaper, safer, faster to iterate)
- Creative derivatives (cut-downs) extend VSL lifespan without full rebuild
- Platform allocation adjusted to match IonWave demo (28-45 skews older = more YouTube, less TikTok)

**Takeaway**: Speed and cost efficiency in testing phase compound over time.

### 5. Meta-Learning Accelerates Improvement (2 upgrades)
- Portfolio health dashboard tracks winner rate, avg CPA, best hook types
- Kill-and-pivot protocol forces meta-analysis when assumptions break

**Takeaway**: M11 content should get smarter over time (Month 4 VSLs should perform better than Month 1).
