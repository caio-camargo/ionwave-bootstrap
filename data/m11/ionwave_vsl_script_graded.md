### SOLUTION INTRO [1:30-2:00]

**[VISUAL: Ocean depths, beautiful underwater footage]**

✅ **Historical credibility** — René Quinton is real
**Grade**: A | Verifiable historical figure

⚠️ **CRITICAL OVERSIMPLIFICATION** — This claim requires heavy context:
- Quinton's work was in 1897 (pre-modern chemistry)
- "98% identical" refers to mineral composition, not chemical structure
- Modern science has nuanced this finding

**Grade**: D | **FTC RISK — Cannot make "98% identical" claim without RCT substantiation**
**Correction**: "René Quinton observed similarities between blood plasma and seawater mineral composition"

> Not similar. Nearly identical. Same minerals. Same ratios. Same ionic form.

⚠️ **Unsubstantiated** — "Same ratios" is debatable
**Grade**: D | Soften to "similar mineral profile"

✅ **Accurate** — Quinton did advocate for marine plasma therapy
**Grade**: B | Historical fact

> French hospitals used it for decades. Olympic athletes swear by it. Biohackers guard it like a secret.

⚠️ **Vague social proof** — Needs specifics or soften
**Grade**: C | "French hospitals" is accurate (Quinton Plasma was used medically), but "Olympic athletes" is unsubstantiated
