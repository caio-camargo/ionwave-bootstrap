## Core Thesis

**A VSL is not a marketing task. It's a Trade.**

The Video Sales Letter is the single highest-leverage asset in a D2C business. It converts cold traffic into customers. Everything else — ads, email, retention — amplifies or follows the VSL. If the VSL doesn't convert, nothing else matters.

[Confidence: A | Source: Agora divisions generated $25M+ from two VSLs | Date: 2026-02-09]
[Confidence: A | Source: Vidalytics hosted 20,000+ VSLs selling $2B+ in products | Date: 2026-02-09]

Most D2C brands treat VSLs as creative guesswork. **We treat them as engineering.**

## Why VSL-as-Trade?

A VSL has all the structural properties of a Trade:

| Property | VSL Equivalent | Business Trade Equivalent |
|----------|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Thesis** | The hook/angle — falsifiable, killable | Business thesis — why this company should exist |
| **Financial Model** | What CAC does it produce at what spend level | ODD-2 Financial Model |
| **Contracts** | Creator agreements, licensing, usage rights | ODD-4 Contracts |
| **Implementation** | Script → Production → Testing → Scaling | Execution phases with gates |
| **Kill Criteria** | If CPA > $X after $Y spend, kill this angle | Business kill criteria (cash, unit economics) |
| **Post-Mortem** | What worked, what didn't, what transfers to next VSL | Trade retrospective |

Treating it as a Trade means it gets the same rigor as the business itself.

[Confidence: A | Source: Internal framework validated by Danilo's M11 structure | Date: 2026-02-09]

## VSL Trade Assemblages

Every VSL Trade contains these components:

### 1. Thesis (The Hook)
- Core angle
- Target avatar
- Primary claim
- Emotional driver
- Mechanism (why it works)

**Kill criterion**: If hook rate < 20% after $50 spend, angle is wrong.

### 2. Script (The Blueprint)
Full VSL script structure:
- Hook (0:00-0:05) — stop the scroll
- Problem (0:05-0:30) — make them feel the pain
- Agitation (0:30-1:00) — make the pain urgent
- Mechanism (1:00-2:00) — explain WHY your solution works
- Proof (2:00-3:00) — make them believe
- Offer (3:00-3:30) — make it a no-brainer
- CTA (3:30-4:00) — tell them exactly what to do
- Objection Loop (4:00-5:00, optional) — handle remaining doubt

See `script_architecture.md` for detailed structure.

### 3. Financial Model
- Expected CPA at budget tiers
- Breakeven ROAS
- LTV:CAC threshold
- Scaling curves
- Portfolio ROI (Month 1-3 projections)

See `financial_model.md` for cost structures and ROI scenarios.

### 4. Contracts
- Creator agreements (UGC, voiceover)
- Licensing (music, stock footage)
- Usage terms (platform rights, duration)

**Critical for health/supplement**: FTC compliance package mandatory before production. See `production_spec.md` compliance section.

### 5. Production Spec
- Shot list
- B-roll requirements
- Graphics/animations
- Audio specs
- Format/length variants (Meta, TikTok, YouTube)

See `production_spec.md`.

### 6. Testing Protocol
- Variables to test (angle → hook → body → offer → CTA hierarchy)
- Sample sizes (min 3,000-5,000 impressions per variant)
- Statistical thresholds (95% confidence target)
- Winner criteria (CPA, hook rate, hold rate, CTR, ROAS)
- Kill rules (hard kills vs soft kills)

See `testing_protocol.md` for complete statistical framework.

### 7. Creative Variants
- Hook variants (5+ per round)
- CTA variants
- Length variants (3-min, 5-min, 10-min)
- Format variants (IG/TikTok/YouTube/Meta)

### 8. Post-Mortem Template
- What converted (retention curves, engagement breakpoints)
- What dropped off (fall-off points in script)
- Qualitative feedback (comments, DMs, survey responses)
- Lessons for next VSL

See `iteration_system.md` for post-mortem framework.

## VSL Lifecycle (TUP Phases)

| Phase | Duration | Activities | Gate Criteria |
|-------|----------|------------|---------------|
| **Imagination** | 1 week | Research angles, study competitors, identify hooks, draft thesis | Thesis documented + 3 angle options |
| **Script Development** | 1 week | Write full script, internal review, revise, lock | Script passes stranger test — someone could produce from script alone |
| **Pre-Production** | 3-5 days | Creator casting, location/setup, shot list, contracts signed | All production requirements locked |
| **Production** | 1-3 days | Film/record, capture B-roll, voiceover, graphics | Raw footage complete |
| **Post-Production** | 3-5 days | Edit, add graphics, create variants, format for platforms | Master + 3 variants ready |
| **Testing** | 2-4 weeks | Deploy ads, collect data, test hooks/CTAs/lengths, statistical analysis | Winner identified OR all killed |
| **Scaling** | Ongoing | Increase spend on winner, monitor fatigue, plan next VSL | CPA stable at scale for 2+ weeks |
| **Post-Mortem** | 1 day | Document learnings, update angle library, feed into next Trade | Post-mortem complete |

**Total cycle**: 4-6 weeks from imagination to scaled winner (or kill).

## Key Principles

### 1. Portfolio Over Hero Creative

Don't bet on one VSL. VSLs fatigue after 4-6 weeks. You need a pipeline.

**Portfolio strategy**:
- **Month 1**: Test 3-5 angles → Expect 1 winner
- **Month 2**: Scale winner, test 3 new angles → Expect 1 more winner
- **Month 3**: 2 winners scaling, 3 new in test → Expect 1 more winner

**By Month 3**: 3 winning VSLs, each on its own scaling curve.

**Investment**: $7,500-15,000 in production + testing
**Expected monthly revenue enabled**: $30,000-100,000

[Confidence: B | Source: Derived from M11 financial model + ClickBank benchmarks | Date: 2026-02-09]

### 2. Test Angle, Not Execution

Testing hierarchy (highest to lowest impact):
1. **ANGLE** (which story/mechanism) — 10x impact
2. **HOOK** (first 3-5 seconds) — 3-5x impact
3. **BODY** (proof/mechanism section) — 2x impact
4. **OFFER** (pricing/bonuses) — 1.5x impact
5. **CTA** (button text) — 1.1x impact

Never test lower-level variables before higher-level winners are found.

[Confidence: A | Source: Direct response testing best practices (Agora, ClickBank) | Date: 2026-02-09]

### 3. Data Over Gut

**Hard kill criteria** (stop immediately):
- CPA > 3x target after $150 spend
- Hook rate < 15% after 2,000 impressions
- Zero purchases after $250 spend

**Never kill based on**:
- Single-day data (variance too high)
- "Gut feeling" (data only)
- Before minimum impressions reached (false signal)

### 4. Compliance Is Existential

For health/supplement VSLs:
- **FTC requires RCTs** (randomized controlled trials) to substantiate health claims
- Video content is FULLY in scope for FTC enforcement (not just written ads)
- Influencer/UGC content must meet same substantiation standards
- Material connections (payments, free product) must be disclosed clearly

**Critical**: Pre-clear all health claims with legal before production. One FTC violation = $53,088+ fine per instance.

[Confidence: A | Source: FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance (Dec 2022) | Date: 2026-02-09]
