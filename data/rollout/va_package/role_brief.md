## Your Job

You have one job: **find an MBA intern type** to lead the next phase of this project.

You are not executing the business. You are not making strategic decisions. You are not evaluating whether the business model is sound. Your job is to identify, reach out to, screen, and recommend a candidate who can do the next thing in the chain: find a VP-level deals person.

---

## What an "MBA Intern Type" Means

We're not necessarily looking for someone currently enrolled in an MBA program — though that's one strong profile. We're looking for someone with this combination:

**Must have:**
- Can understand a business opportunity from written materials (not just a pitch deck — a structured operating manual)
- Can do structured, professional outreach to people more senior than them
- Can evaluate candidates against a defined rubric (not just "gut feel")
- Can manage a pipeline over 4-12 weeks without hand-holding
- Reliable — delivers what they promise, when they promise it

**Nice to have:**
- MBA student or recent MBA graduate
- Business development or consulting background
- Startup experience (even informal)
- Comfortable working with AI tools (they'll work with Claude daily)

**Not required:**
- Industry expertise (marine supplements, D2C, ecommerce)
- Executive experience
- Large professional network (though it helps)

The MBA intern's job is to find a VP-level deals person. They need enough sophistication to pitch credibly to senior people, but they don't need to be senior themselves.

## What You Receive

1. **This role brief** — what you're reading now
2. **Sourcing playbook** — where to find MBA intern candidates, what channels to use, outreach volume targets
3. **Screening criteria** — the rubric you'll use to evaluate candidates
4. **Outreach templates** — message templates you can adapt for different channels
5. **Trade Pitch One-Pager** — the document you use to explain the opportunity to candidates
6. **Bilateral contract** — what you deliver, what you're paid, on what schedule
7. **Access to Claude** — your AI co-pilot for the entire process

## What You Deliver

| # | Deliverable | When | Format |
|---|-------------|------|--------|
| 1 | **Brief Acknowledgment** | 48 hours after receiving this package | Written confirmation you've read everything + 3 questions |
| 2 | **Sourcing Plan** | Week 1 | Channels, volume targets, timeline |
| 3 | **Weekly Pipeline Report** | Every Friday | Table: candidate name, source, status, notes |
| 4 | **Candidate Shortlist** | By Week 4 (hard deadline: Week 6) | 3+ candidates scored on all rubric dimensions |
| 5 | **Final Recommendation** | 1 week after shortlist | Top candidate with rationale + evidence |

Full specifications for each deliverable are in the deliverable registry (`compliance/deliverable_registry.md`). Claude will remind you when each one is due.

---

## Timeline

- **Week 1**: Read the brief, submit your sourcing plan, begin outreach
- **Weeks 2-3**: Active sourcing and screening. Weekly pipeline reports.
- **Week 4**: First shortlist due (3+ screened candidates). If not ready, explain what's needed.
- **Week 5**: Final recommendation. One candidate, with your reasoning.
- **Week 6**: Hard deadline for shortlist if not met by Week 4.

Total engagement: **4-6 weeks**.

## How Claude Helps You

Claude (the AI you're working with right now) is not just a chatbot — it's your project assistant for this engagement. Claude:

- **Knows what's due and when.** It will remind you of upcoming deadlines.
- **Helps you draft outreach.** Give it context about a candidate and it will help you write personalized messages.
- **Helps you evaluate candidates.** Discuss candidate profiles with Claude and it will help you apply the screening rubric consistently.
- **Helps you write reports.** Tell Claude what happened this week and it will help you format the pipeline report.
- **Tracks your progress.** Claude maintains a running view of where you are in the process.

You are expected to do your sourcing and screening work **through** Claude as much as possible. This isn't surveillance — it means Claude has the context to help you better, and it means the project has a record of what happened.

- **Technical details.** You don't need to understand marine plasma electrolytes or D2C marketing. You need to understand what kind of person we're looking for (the screening criteria tell you) and how to find them.
