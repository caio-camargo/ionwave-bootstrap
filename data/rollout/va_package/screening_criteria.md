## How to Use This Rubric

You will evaluate every candidate who passes initial interest against this rubric. The rubric has **6 dimensions**, each scored 1-5. A candidate needs a **weighted score of 3.5+ (out of 5)** to make the shortlist.

You don't need to be an expert to apply this rubric. Each dimension has **specific observable indicators** — things you can see in a conversation, a writing sample, or a brief interview. You're checking for evidence, not making judgment calls about business strategy.

Claude can help you apply the rubric: describe what you observed and Claude will help you map it to scores.

## Screening Process

### Stage 1: Initial Screen (15-20 min conversation or async exchange)

**Purpose**: Filter out obviously wrong fits before investing time in full evaluation.

**Quick-check questions:**
1. "What interests you about this opportunity?" — Looking for: specificity, not generic enthusiasm
2. "Describe a time you managed a multi-week project with deliverables." — Looking for: concrete example with dates, actions, outcomes
3. "How would you approach reaching out to someone significantly more senior than you?" — Looking for: thoughtfulness about pitch, not just "I'd email them"

**Pass to Stage 2 if**: They give concrete, specific answers to all three. Not generic, not vague, not "I'm a hard worker."

**Reject if**: Can't articulate a concrete project they've managed, or the idea of outreach to senior people makes them visibly uncomfortable.

### Stage 2: Full Evaluation (30-45 min conversation + writing sample)

Apply the full rubric below. This is the evaluation that determines shortlist placement.

## Evaluation Rubric

### Dimension 1: Communication Quality (Weight: 25%)

**What you're assessing**: Can this person write and speak clearly enough to pitch to a VP-level person?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Writing is crisp, professional, and persuasive. Speaks with confidence and clarity. Can adjust tone for the audience. |
| **4** | Writing is clear and professional. Speech is organized. Minor rough edges but overall strong. |
| **3** | Adequate communication. Gets the point across but lacks polish. Would need coaching before pitching VP candidates. |
| **2** | Communication is disorganized or unclear. Frequent filler, vague language, or poor writing structure. |
| **1** | Cannot communicate effectively in writing or speech. Immediate disqualification. |

**How to test**: Ask for a writing sample — "Write a 3-paragraph message to a VP-level person explaining this opportunity." Review their outreach templates response from the screening conversation.

### Dimension 2: Project Management Ability (Weight: 20%)

**What you're assessing**: Can they manage a pipeline over 4-12 weeks without someone checking on them daily?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Has managed multi-week projects with clear deliverables. Uses tracking systems (spreadsheets, project tools). Proactively flags blockers. |
| **4** | Has project experience. Can describe their tracking method. Delivers on commitments. |
| **3** | Some project experience but less structured. May need check-ins to stay on track. |
| **2** | Limited project management evidence. Mostly reactive rather than proactive. |
| **1** | No evidence of managing independent projects. Needs constant direction. |

**How to test**: "Walk me through how you'd organize a 10-week outreach campaign. What tools would you use? How would you track progress?"

### Dimension 3: Business Comprehension (Weight: 20%)

**What you're assessing**: Can they understand the Trade well enough to pitch it credibly?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | After reading the Trade Pitch One-Pager, can explain the opportunity in their own words with nuance. Asks smart questions about the model. |
| **4** | Understands the core proposition. Can articulate the value. Questions show engagement. |
| **3** | Grasps the basics but misses nuance. May oversimplify or misrepresent parts of the model. Coachable. |
| **2** | Struggles to explain the opportunity after reading materials. Confused by the model. |
| **1** | Cannot understand or articulate the business model even with materials provided. |

**How to test**: Give them the Trade Pitch One-Pager. After they read it (give 10-15 min), ask: "Explain this opportunity to me as if I'm a VP-level person considering joining." Judge clarity and accuracy.

### Dimension 4: Outreach Sophistication (Weight: 15%)

**What you're assessing**: Can they approach senior professionals without being awkward, pushy, or too timid?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Has experience pitching or selling to senior people. Understands personalization, timing, and follow-up cadence. Comfortable with rejection. |
| **4** | Has done professional outreach (recruiting, sales, business development). Can write a personalized message. |
| **3** | Limited outreach experience but understands the principles. Would improve with templates and coaching. |
| **2** | Outreach feels generic or uncomfortable. Relies on mass messaging or avoids it. |
| **1** | No outreach experience. Unable to draft a credible message to a senior professional. |

**How to test**: "Draft a LinkedIn message to a VP of Sales at a D2C brand, inviting them to learn about this opportunity." Evaluate personalization, tone, and call to action.

### Dimension 5: Reliability & Follow-Through (Weight: 10%)

**What you're assessing**: Will they actually do the work, on time, without chasing?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | References or examples demonstrate consistent delivery. Shows up on time. Meets deadlines without reminders. |
| **4** | Generally reliable. One or two minor lapses but overall dependable. |
| **3** | Somewhat reliable but may need reminders. History of inconsistent follow-through. |
| **2** | Poor reliability indicators. Missed the screening call time, late to respond, vague about commitments. |
| **1** | Demonstrably unreliable. Red flags in the screening process itself. |

**How to test**: Observe their behavior during the screening process. Did they respond when they said they would? Did they show up on time? Did they prepare the writing sample when asked?

### Dimension 6: Motivation & Fit (Weight: 10%)

**What you're assessing**: Are they genuinely interested, and for the right reasons?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Clearly excited about the opportunity. Understands the equity model. Asks questions that show they've thought about the commitment. |
| **4** | Interested and engaged. Understands what's being asked. |
| **3** | Interested but generic motivation. Could be applying to many things. |
| **2** | Lukewarm interest. Seems to be going through the motions. |
| **1** | Not genuinely interested. Motivated only by resume line or misunderstands the commitment. |

**How to test**: "Why this project and not something else?" and "What concerns do you have about this role?" — genuine candidates have real questions.

### Decision Thresholds

| Score Range | Decision |
|-------------|----------|
| **4.0 - 5.0** | Strong candidate. Shortlist. |
| **3.5 - 3.9** | Viable candidate. Shortlist if pipeline is thin. |
| **3.0 - 3.4** | Borderline. Keep in pipeline but do not shortlist unless no better options. |
| **Below 3.0** | Reject. Not a fit for this role. |

**Shortlist requirement**: Minimum 3 candidates scoring 3.5+ before submitting your shortlist deliverable.

---

## Automatic Disqualifiers

These override the rubric score — if any are present, reject regardless of other dimensions:

1. **Cannot write a coherent paragraph.** This person will be pitching VP candidates in writing. If their writing is poor, nothing else matters.
2. **No concrete example of any project they've managed.** Even a class project counts. Zero evidence = too high a risk.
3. **Demands cash compensation and won't consider equity.** The MBA intern role is equity-compensated. If this is a dealbreaker, they're not the right fit.
4. **Missed the screening call/conversation without explanation.** If they can't show up reliably during the courtship phase, they won't during the work.
