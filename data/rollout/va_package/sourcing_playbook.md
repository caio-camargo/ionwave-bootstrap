## Overview

You're looking for an "MBA intern type" — someone sophisticated enough to pitch a VP-level person, organized enough to manage a 4-12 week recruiting pipeline, and sharp enough to evaluate candidates against a rubric. They don't need to be in an MBA program, but that's the caliber.

This playbook tells you where to find them, how to approach them, and what volume to target.

---

## 1. Sourcing Channels (Ranked by Expected Signal Quality)

### Tier 1: Highest Signal

**A. MBA Programs — Career Services & Student Groups**

MBA programs produce exactly the profile we want. Target second-year students or recent graduates looking for project-based experience.

- **How to access**: Contact career services offices directly. Many maintain job boards or project boards for student opportunities. Also reach out to student-led clubs: entrepreneurship clubs, consulting clubs, venture capital clubs.
- **Target schools**: Don't limit to top-10. Strong candidates come from mid-tier MBA programs where students are hungrier for differentiated experience. Any AACSB-accredited program is a reasonable target.
- **Pitch angle**: "Structured project-based experience with a real venture. AI-augmented workflow. Equity compensation. 4-12 week commitment."
- **Volume target**: Contact 10-15 programs. Expect 1-3 interested candidates per responsive program.
- **Timeline**: 1-2 weeks to get responses from career services. Candidates available quickly.

**B. LinkedIn — Targeted Search**

Search for profiles that match the candidate description.

- **Search terms**: "MBA candidate", "MBA 2026", "business development associate", "management consultant", "venture fellow", "startup fellow"
- **Filters**: 1-5 years experience, MBA education (current or recent), located in [target geography or remote]
- **How to approach**: Personalized connection request → brief pitch message. Reference something specific from their profile.
- **Volume target**: Send 30-50 outreach messages over Weeks 1-3. Expect 10-15% response rate = 3-7 conversations.
- **Cost**: Free tier works for initial outreach. LinkedIn Recruiter ($100/mo) if you need InMail at scale.

### Tier 2: Good Signal

**C. Startup/Venture Communities**

People in startup communities often have the exact combination of hustle + business understanding we need.

- **Platforms**: AngelList/Wellfound, Y Combinator's Work at a Startup, Indie Hackers community, startup Slack/Discord channels
- **Who to look for**: People who've done venture fellowships, startup internships, accelerator programs, or who are actively seeking project-based startup work
- **Pitch angle**: "Structured deal-sourcing project for a de-risked D2C venture. AI co-pilot. Equity compensation."
- **Volume target**: Post in 5-10 communities. Expect 5-15 inbound applications.

**D. Freelance Platforms (Upwork, Toptal)**

Higher volume, more variable quality. Useful as a supplement.

- **Search terms**: "business development", "executive recruiting", "startup consulting", "deal sourcing"
- **What to look for**: Freelancers with demonstrated ability to run outreach campaigns, manage pipelines, and write professional communications
- **Volume target**: Post one well-crafted job listing. Screen from applicant pool (expect 10-20 applications).
- **Cost**: Upwork takes a service fee (typically 5-20% on top of contractor rate).

### Tier 3: Supplementary

**E. Personal/Warm Network**

If you have contacts in business, education, or startup environments:
- Ask: "Do you know anyone MBA-caliber who'd be interested in a structured project-based role with equity?"
- Even 2-3 warm referrals can be higher-quality than 50 cold outreach messages.

**F. University Business Schools — Direct Outreach**

Beyond career services, some professors run practicum courses or capstone projects where students need real-world projects.
- Contact entrepreneurship or strategy professors directly
- Position IonWave as a capstone project opportunity

## 2. Outreach Volume Targets

| Week | Activity | Volume |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Week 1** | Set up channels, draft outreach, begin sending | 20+ initial outreach messages across channels |
| **Week 2** | Active outreach + follow-up on Week 1 | 20+ new outreach + follow-up on non-responders |
| **Week 3** | Screening conversations with responders | 5-10 screening conversations |
| **Week 4** | Focused interviews with top candidates | 3-5 focused evaluations → shortlist |

**Minimum weekly outreach**: 15 new contacts per week for the first 3 weeks. If you're below this, your pipeline will be too thin.

**Pipeline funnel math**:
- 60+ outreach messages → ~10 responses (15-20% rate) → ~5 screening conversations → ~3 shortlist candidates → 1 recommendation

This is approximate. Quality of your outreach and targeting affects conversion at every step.

## 4. Tracking Your Pipeline

Maintain a pipeline tracker. Claude can help you format this, but the minimum information per candidate:

| Field | Required |
|-------|----------|
| Candidate name | Yes |
| Source channel | Yes |
| Date of first contact | Yes |
| Response status | Yes (No response / Responded / Interested / Declined) |
| Screening status | Yes (Not yet screened / Screened / Passed / Failed) |
| Notes | Yes (brief — what stood out or raised concerns) |

Your **Weekly Pipeline Report** (due every Friday) is this tracker in table form. Claude will prompt you for it.

## 5. Red Flags During Sourcing

Watch for these patterns in your pipeline:

| Red Flag | What It Might Mean | Action |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| Very high response rate (>40%) | Your pitch may be too vague or over-promising | Review outreach for accuracy |
| Very low response rate (<10%) after 30+ messages | Targeting or messaging is off | Adjust channels or message. Ask Claude for help revising. |
| All candidates from one channel | Pipeline concentration risk | Diversify to at least 2 channels |
| Candidates who can't articulate why they're interested | May be applying to everything | Probe motivation in screening |
| Candidates asking about cash compensation first | Misaligned with equity model | Clarify compensation early, don't waste time on mismatches |

---

## 6. When to Escalate

- **Week 3 with <5 responses**: Your sourcing strategy may need adjustment. Flag this in your weekly report. Claude will help you diagnose.
- **Week 4 with no shortlist-ready candidates**: This is the soft deadline. If you don't have 3 candidates who pass initial screening, escalate the situation — explain what's happening, what you've tried, and what you think needs to change.
- **Week 6 with no shortlist**: Hard deadline. If the pipeline hasn't produced viable candidates by Week 6, the engagement may need to be restructured.
