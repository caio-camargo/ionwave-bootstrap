## Material Hierarchy

Not every candidate sees everything at once. Disclosure is staged to match their engagement level.

### Stage 1: Initial Interest (First Contact → Initial Conversation)

**What to share**: Trade Pitch One-Pager only.

| Material | Location | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Trade Pitch One-Pager** | `data/rollout/trade_pitch_onepager.md` | 1-page overview: what IonWave is, the market, the economics, the team structure, the timeline |

**Why only this**: VP-level people don't read lengthy packages before a first conversation. The One-Pager is designed to communicate the opportunity in 5 minutes. If they're interested after reading it, they'll take the call.

**How to share**: Send as a PDF or clean markdown attachment with your outreach message or immediately after they express interest.

### Stage 2: Engaged Interest (After Initial Conversation → Before Deep Evaluation)

**What to share**: Selected TUPs from the full Trade.

| Material | Location | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **M0: Trade Thesis** | `data/m0_trade_thesis/` | The strategic foundation — why this market, this product, this approach |
| **M4: Fundraising** | `data/m4/` | Capital Formation playbook — investor targeting, pipeline methodology, pitch framework |
| **M1: Operator System** | `data/m1/operator_system.md` | How the Operator role works — compensation, onboarding, certification |
| **Chain Specification** | `data/rollout/chain_specification.md` | How the cascade works — where the VP fits, what happens before and after them |

**Why these**: These 4 documents give the VP candidate enough depth to evaluate the opportunity seriously without drowning them in 41 TUPs. They cover: the business case (M0), their specific function (M4 + chain spec), and the role they'll recruit for (M1).

**How to share**: "Here are 4 key documents from the Trade that cover the business thesis, the fundraising playbook, the operator role you'd recruit for, and the overall team structure. I'd suggest reading them before our next conversation." Give them 1 week to review.

### Stage 3: Serious Evaluation (After Deep Evaluation → Shortlist/Negotiation)

**What to share**: Full Trade access.

| Material | Location | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Full imagination passet** | `data/` (all 41 TUPs) | Complete operating manual across all domains |
| **VP Brief** | `data/rollout/vp_brief/` | Their specific role brief, integration requirements, bilateral contract |

**Why now**: At this stage, the candidate is seriously considering the role. Full Trade access lets them do their own due diligence. It also starts the integration process — the more they read, the more prepared they are for the integration work artifact (their first deliverable as VP).

**How to share**: Provide repository access or a curated document package.

## Your Pitch: What to Say (Not Just What to Show)

### The Core Pitch (2-3 minutes)

Adapt this to your own voice — don't read it verbatim:

> IonWave is a premium marine plasma electrolyte brand. The electrolyte market is validated — LMNT does $66M+ in annual revenue. The gap is at the premium end: ocean-sourced minerals, where the #1 complaint is taste. Nobody's solved the taste problem with premium ingredients.
>
> What makes IonWave different isn't the product — it's the system. The founder built a complete operating manual: 41 structured business models covering every function, from formulation to paid advertising to financial operations. Each one is quality-scored and designed for execution by someone who hasn't met the founder. The entire system is AI-augmented — the operator works with Claude AI as a daily co-pilot.
>
> The venture has built-in kill criteria. If COGS exceeds $0.60, if CAC exceeds $75 for 14 days, if landing page conversion is below 2% — the business pauses or kills. No sunk-cost trap.
>
> We're looking for a VP of Deals to lead two things: Capital Formation — raising a seed round and building an advisor network — and Operator recruitment — finding the person who runs the business day-to-day. Compensation is meaningful equity, in the 5-10% range.

### Handling Common Questions

| Question | How to Answer |
|----------|--------------|
| "Where's the founder?" | "The founder designed the system. The model is founder-independent by design — the right people execute based on the quality of the playbook, not personal relationships with the founder. There's a compliance system (Claude AI) monitoring execution." |
| "Why equity and not cash?" | "The venture is pre-revenue. Cash goes to execution (product, marketing, operations). The equity offer is significant because the founding team is small and the playbook is complete — you're not joining a chaos-stage startup, you're executing a structured system." |
| "What if the business doesn't work?" | "Kill criteria are built in. The first 8 weeks validate or kill the core hypotheses. If the numbers don't work, the business stops before significant capital is burned. Your equity risk is bounded by these gates." |
| "Can I talk to the founder?" | "The founder is intentionally out of the execution chain. [Check with Caio on whether/when a founder conversation is available.] I can share the full Trade so you can evaluate the system directly." |
| "How much equity exactly?" | "The range is 5-10%. The exact terms are part of the negotiation. I can share the VP bilateral contract framework so you can see the full structure." [Note: DEC-CHAIN-002 — exact amount is TBD. Be transparent about this.] |
| "41 business models — that sounds comprehensive. Is it tested?" | "Honest answer: it's comprehensive but unexecuted. This is a first-edition system. The documentation quality is high — each model is self-graded with confidence levels — but execution will be the real test. That's why the kill criteria exist: the system is designed to validate quickly." |

## Pitch Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- **Lead with the Trade's quality.** The 41-TUP system is genuinely unusual. Most startups have a pitch deck. This has a complete operating manual.
- **Be honest about what's unproven.** Pre-revenue. First-edition system. Untested execution. Candidates respect honesty more than hype.
- **Emphasize the de-risking.** Kill criteria, phase gates, bounded commitment — these reduce the VP's equity risk.
- **Let the materials do the heavy lifting.** Your job is to get them to read the materials. The Trade itself is the best pitch.

### Don't:
- **Oversell.** Don't promise $2M EBITDA as a certainty. It's a hypothesis with defined validation steps.
- **Hide the cascade model.** Be transparent that you (MBA intern) found them through a VA, and the VP will find an Operator. Some candidates will find this unusual — that's fine. The ones who understand systems thinking will appreciate it.
- **Pitch without understanding the Trade.** If a VP candidate asks you a question about the business and you can't answer, your credibility drops. Spend Week 1 absorbing the materials.
- **Rush the process.** VP-level candidates take time to evaluate. Give them space to read, think, and come back with questions.
