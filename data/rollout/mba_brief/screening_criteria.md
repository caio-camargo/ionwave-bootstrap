## How to Use This Rubric

Evaluating a VP-level candidate is harder than evaluating an MBA intern. You're assessing someone who is likely more experienced than you in deal-making and fundraising. This rubric gives you **structured, observable criteria** to anchor your evaluation.

You are not expected to perfectly judge a VP candidate's quality. You ARE expected to:
1. Apply this rubric honestly
2. Document specific evidence for each score
3. Flag any dimension where you feel uncertain
4. Let the rubric structure your evaluation rather than relying on impression

Claude can help: walk through a candidate conversation with Claude and it will help you map observations to rubric scores.

## Evaluation Rubric

### Dimension 1: Deal-Closing Track Record (Weight: 25%)

**What you're assessing**: Has this person actually closed deals, raised money, or landed partnerships — not just "worked on" them?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Multiple concrete deals they personally closed. Can name amounts, counterparties, timelines. Clear pattern of closing, not just initiating. |
| **4** | 1-2 significant deals they led. Clear on their specific contribution vs. team effort. |
| **3** | Has deal experience but in supporting role. "I helped with the fundraise" vs. "I closed the round." |
| **2** | Claims deal experience but vague on specifics. Can't name numbers or timelines. |
| **1** | No deal-closing evidence. Purely advisory or strategic role with no closing function. |

**How to test**: "Walk me through two or three deals you personally closed. What was the context, what was your role, and what was the outcome?" Press for specifics — amounts, timelines, how they overcame obstacles.

### Dimension 2: Trade Comprehension (Weight: 20%)

**What you're assessing**: Can they understand the IonWave Trade well enough to pitch it to investors and evaluate operator candidates?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | After reviewing Trade materials, can articulate the value proposition, unit economics, risk framework, and competitive positioning in their own words. Asks nuanced questions. Identifies strengths AND weaknesses. |
| **4** | Good comprehension of the core model. Can pitch it credibly. May miss some nuance but gets the substance. |
| **3** | Understands the basics but oversimplifies. Might pitch the opportunity but couldn't defend it under investor scrutiny. |
| **2** | Surface-level understanding. Reads the One-Pager but can't go deeper. |
| **1** | Cannot comprehend or articulate the business model even with full materials provided. |

**How to test**: Give them the Trade Pitch One-Pager and 2-3 key TUPs (M0 Trade Thesis, M4 Fundraising, M1 Operator System). After a week of review, ask: "Pitch me IonWave as if I'm an angel investor considering a $25K check. Then tell me the three biggest risks."

### Dimension 3: Network & Reach (Weight: 20%)

**What you're assessing**: Can they reach investors and operator candidates — either through existing network or demonstrated ability to build connections quickly?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Has an active, relevant network. Can name 10+ people they'd reach out to for Capital Formation or operator recruiting. Previous experience building investor/talent pipelines. |
| **4** | Has some relevant connections. Can identify 5-10 specific people. Has done network-building before in related domains. |
| **3** | Limited existing network but has demonstrated ability to build relationships from scratch. Has a credible plan for how they'd build the pipeline. |
| **2** | No relevant network and vague about how they'd build one. "I'd use LinkedIn" without specifics. |
| **1** | No network and no evidence of relationship-building capability. |

**How to test**: "If you started this role next week, who are the first 10 people you'd reach out to for Capital Formation? Why them?" And: "Tell me about a time you built a professional network from scratch."

### Dimension 4: Execution Discipline (Weight: 15%)

**What you're assessing**: Will they actually deliver structured output on schedule, or will they "network" without producing tangible progress?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Track record of managing pipelines with structured output. Can describe their personal system for tracking commitments. References or examples of consistent, on-time delivery. |
| **4** | Generally disciplined. Has managed projects with deliverables. Minor gaps but overall reliable. |
| **3** | Some structure but inconsistent. May need accountability mechanisms to stay on track. |
| **2** | Little evidence of structured execution. Works in bursts. Deliverables are afterthoughts. |
| **1** | No evidence of disciplined execution. Pure relationship/networking orientation without output accountability. |

**How to test**: "In your most recent deal-focused role, how did you track your pipeline? What was your weekly rhythm?" And observe: do they show up on time for calls? Do they follow through on commitments made during the screening process?

### Dimension 5: Capital Formation Strategy (Weight: 10%)

**What you're assessing**: Do they have a coherent approach to seed-stage fundraising for a D2C venture?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Has specific, actionable ideas for IonWave fundraising. Understands seed dynamics — SAFEs, angel check sizes, D2C investor landscape. Has done this before. |
| **4** | Has general fundraising knowledge and a reasonable approach. May not have seed-specific experience but understands the mechanics. |
| **3** | Understands fundraising conceptually. Has a basic plan but hasn't done it. Would need to learn the specifics. |
| **2** | Vague understanding. "I'd reach out to investors" without strategy. |
| **1** | No understanding of fundraising mechanics. |

**How to test**: "What's your approach to raising a $30-50K seed round on a SAFE for a pre-revenue D2C brand? Who would you target and why?"

### Dimension 6: Motivation & Commitment (Weight: 10%)

**What you're assessing**: Are they genuinely committed to this specific opportunity, and will they sustain effort over months?

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Clearly excited about IonWave specifically (not just any opportunity). Asks deep questions. Willing to commit 10+ hours/week. Understands and accepts equity compensation. |
| **4** | Interested and engaged. Understands the commitment. Has realistic expectations. |
| **3** | Interested but noncommittal. "Let me think about it" without urgency. Might be weighing many options. |
| **2** | Lukewarm. Primarily motivated by equity upside without genuine interest in the venture. |
| **1** | Not genuinely committed. Would likely deprioritize IonWave when something shinier appears. |

**How to test**: "What concerns do you have about this role?" (Genuine candidates have real concerns.) And: "What would you deprioritize in your current commitments to make time for this?"

### Decision Thresholds

| Score Range | Decision |
|-------------|----------|
| **4.0 - 5.0** | Strong candidate. Shortlist immediately. |
| **3.5 - 3.9** | Viable candidate. Shortlist. |
| **3.0 - 3.4** | Borderline. Flag concerns. Include only if pipeline is thin. |
| **Below 3.0** | Reject. Not a fit for this role. |

**Shortlist requirement**: Minimum 2 candidates scoring 3.5+ before submitting your shortlist deliverable.

## Automatic Disqualifiers

| Condition | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cannot name a single deal they personally closed** | The role is fundamentally about closing. No evidence = too high a risk. |
| **Refuses equity compensation entirely** | The compensation structure is equity-based. If this is a dealbreaker, they're not aligned. |
| **Cannot commit 10+ hours/week** | Capital Formation and operator recruiting require sustained effort. |
| **Wants a passive/advisory role** | This is an active execution role with deliverables and deadlines. |
| **Cannot comprehend the Trade after adequate review time** | If they can't understand the business after a week with the materials, they can't pitch it. |

## A Note on Evaluating "Up"

You're evaluating someone who is likely more experienced and senior than you. This is the nature of the cascade — and it's the hardest structural challenge in the model.

**What helps:**
- The rubric gives you objective anchors. You don't need to be a VP to check whether someone can name specific deals they've closed.
- The Trade Comprehension test is concrete. Either they can articulate the business model or they can't.
- Claude has the full Trade context and can help you assess whether a candidate's statements about the business are accurate.
- If you're genuinely uncertain about a dimension, **say so** in your report. "I scored this 3 but I'm not confident — here's what I observed, and I think a more experienced evaluator should weigh in." Honest uncertainty is more valuable than false confidence.

**What doesn't help:**
- Don't be intimidated into inflating scores because someone is senior or impressive.
- Don't skip the rubric because "they're clearly good." Apply it consistently.
- Don't ignore red flags because everything else is strong. Document them.
