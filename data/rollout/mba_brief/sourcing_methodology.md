## Overview

Finding a VP-level deals person is fundamentally different from finding an MBA intern. VP candidates:
- Don't respond to job postings (they get recruited, not applied to)
- Evaluate the opportunity as much as you evaluate them
- Need to be convinced by the quality of the Trade, not just the pitch
- Have options — your outreach competes with everything else they're considering

This methodology is designed for that reality.

---

## 1. The Funnel

Your pipeline math is different from a VA sourcing an intern:

```
Research & targeting:  50+ potential candidates identified
    ↓
Outreach:              30-40 personalized messages sent
    ↓
Responses:             8-12 engaged (20-30% response rate for personalized outreach)
    ↓
Screening calls:       5-8 initial conversations
    ↓
Deep evaluation:       3-4 rubric-scored evaluations
    ↓
Shortlist:             2-3 candidates presented
    ↓
Recommendation:        1 top candidate
```

This funnel takes **8-12 weeks** to run through. Start outreach in Week 2 and maintain volume through Week 8 minimum.

## 2. Research Phase (Week 1-2)

Before you send a single message, do research.

### Build a Target List

Create a spreadsheet with 50+ names. For each person:

| Field | What to capture |
|-------|----------------|
| Name | Full name |
| Current role | What they do now |
| Relevant experience | Fundraising, BD, D2C, CPG — what's relevant |
| Why they might be interested | Career transition? Looking for upside? Industry passion? |
| How to reach them | LinkedIn, email, warm intro path |
| Priority | A (strong fit), B (possible fit), C (stretch) |

### Where to Research

- **LinkedIn**: Search "VP Business Development", "VP Sales", "Head of Partnerships" + filter by D2C, CPG, consumer health, supplements
- **AngelList/Wellfound**: Browse investor profiles in consumer/health categories
- **Crunchbase**: Look at D2C companies that raised seed rounds — who was involved in those raises?
- **Twitter/X**: Follow D2C founder conversations. Who's actively discussing fundraising, brand building, deal-making?
- **Industry events/conferences**: Natural Expo, D2C conferences — speaker lists and attendee networks

### Warm Intro Mapping

For your top 15-20 targets, try to find a warm introduction path:
- Do you share any LinkedIn connections?
- Are they active in communities you can access?
- Can Caio or anyone in the project network make an introduction?

Warm intros convert 3-5x higher than cold outreach.

## 3. Outreach Approach

### Principles

1. **Personalize or don't bother.** VP-level people delete generic messages. Reference something specific about their background.
2. **Lead with the Trade's quality.** The One-Pager is your hook. "41 business models, quality-scored" is differentiated. Use it.
3. **Be direct about what you're asking.** Don't be cagey. "We're looking for a VP of Deals with equity compensation in a structured D2C venture."
4. **Respect their time.** Ask for 15 minutes, not an hour. If they're interested, they'll give you more.
5. **Follow up once.** If no response after 5-7 days, one follow-up. Then move on. Don't pester VP-level people.

### Message Structure

Every outreach message should follow this structure:

```
1. HOOK (1-2 sentences): Why you're reaching out to THEM specifically
2. CONTEXT (2-3 sentences): What IonWave is and what makes it different
3. ASK (1-2 sentences): What you're looking for and what's in it for them
4. NEXT STEP (1 sentence): Clear, low-commitment call to action
```

### Example LinkedIn Message

> Hi [Name] — your work at [Company] building [specific thing] caught my attention. I'm sourcing for a VP-level role in a premium D2C health venture.
>
> What's unusual: the venture has a complete operating manual — 41 structured business models, quality-scored, with AI-augmented execution. It's not a pitch deck; it's a full system designed for execution.
>
> We're looking for someone who can lead Capital Formation (seed fundraising) and recruit an operating partner. Compensation is meaningful equity (5-10%).
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call to see if there's a fit? Happy to share the project brief beforehand.

### Example Email (Warm Intro)

> Subject: Introduction — VP role in D2C health venture

> Hi [Name],
>
> [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out. I'm helping build the leadership team for IonWave — a premium marine plasma electrolyte brand with a uniquely comprehensive foundation.
>
> What makes this different: the venture is backed by 41 documented business models covering every operational domain, from formulation to financial operations. Each is quality-scored and designed for AI-augmented execution. The founder built the system; we're finding the people to execute it.
>
> The VP role involves two parallel tracks: Capital Formation (seed fundraising, advisor network) and Operator recruitment. Compensation is significant equity.
>
> [Mutual connection] thought your background in [specific experience] might make this interesting. Would you have 15 minutes this week or next to discuss?
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

## 4. Screening Process

### Initial Conversation (15-20 min)

**Purpose**: Mutual fit check. You're evaluating them AND they're evaluating the opportunity.

**Your agenda**:
1. Brief intro — who you are, your role in the cascade (be transparent)
2. Quick pitch — the One-Pager version of IonWave (2-3 minutes)
3. Their reaction — let them ask questions. Their questions tell you a lot.
4. Their background — "Tell me about your most relevant deal-closing experience"
5. Logistics — availability, compensation expectations, timeline fit

**What to listen for**:
- Do they ask smart questions about the business model? (Good sign)
- Do they focus only on "what do I get"? (Neutral — normal for senior people, but watch for pure transactional mindset)
- Can they articulate relevant experience concretely? (Must-have)
- Do they seem energized or going through the motions? (Important signal)

**After the call**: Share the Trade Pitch One-Pager (if not shared already). If they're interested in going deeper, share access to the full Trade.

### Deep Evaluation (30-60 min + Trade review)

For candidates who pass the initial conversation:

1. **Trade Comprehension Test**: After they've had time to review the Trade materials (give 1 week), ask them to explain the opportunity in their own words. Can they articulate the value proposition, the unit economics, and the risk framework?

2. **Deal History Deep-Dive**: Ask them to walk you through 2-3 specific deals they've closed. What was the context? What was their specific role? What made it work? What almost went wrong?

3. **Capital Formation Strategy**: "If you were leading fundraising for IonWave, how would you approach it? Who would you target? What's your first 30 days?" — You're not looking for the right answer; you're looking for structured thinking.

4. **Rubric Scoring**: Apply the full screening criteria rubric (see screening_criteria.md).

## 5. Pipeline Management

### Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Monday | Review pipeline. Prioritize outreach for the week. |
| Tuesday-Thursday | Outreach, follow-ups, screening conversations |
| Friday | Update pipeline tracker. Submit biweekly report (on report weeks). |

### Pipeline Tracker Fields

| Field | Required |
|-------|----------|
| Candidate name | Yes |
| Source | Yes |
| Date of first contact | Yes |
| Intro type (cold/warm) | Yes |
| Response status | Yes |
| Screening status | Yes (Not contacted / Contacted / Responded / Screened / Evaluated / Shortlisted / Rejected) |
| Trade materials shared? | Yes (None / One-Pager / Full Trade) |
| Notes | Yes |

### Volume Targets

| Period | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Weeks 2-3 | 15+ new outreach messages |
| Weeks 4-5 | 10+ new outreach + follow-ups. First screening conversations. |
| Weeks 6-8 | 5+ new outreach. Focus shifts to deep evaluation of top candidates. |
| Weeks 9-12 | Shortlist refinement. Focused conversations. Negotiation. |

## 6. Common Challenges

| Challenge | What to Do |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Low response rate (<15%)** | Review your messaging. Are you personalizing? Is the hook compelling? Ask Claude to review your outreach. |
| **Candidates interested but won't commit time** | Clarify the 10+ hours/week expectation early. If they can't commit, they're not a fit regardless of how good they are. |
| **All candidates want cash compensation** | Be transparent about equity. Some retainer may be negotiable. If nobody will take equity, flag this — it may indicate the equity offer needs adjustment (DEC-CHAIN-002). |
| **Candidates are interested but want to talk to the founder** | This is reasonable at VP level. Coordinate with Caio to determine if/when a founder conversation is appropriate. |
| **You can't reach senior enough people** | Warm intros matter more at this level. Invest in intro mapping. Also consider whether your own credibility/positioning in outreach is strong enough. Ask Claude for help. |
