## Your Job

You have one primary job: **find a VP-level deals/fundraising person** for the IonWave venture.

This is not a general internship. It's a focused, deliverable-driven engagement where you're responsible for identifying, pitching, evaluating, and recommending a VP candidate who will lead Capital Formation (fundraising) and recruit the Operator who runs the business.

---

## Context: Where You Fit

You are the second node in a recruiting cascade:

```
Danilo (Founder) → VA → [YOU] → VP → Operator
```

The VA found you. Your job is to find the VP. The VP will then find the Operator and lead fundraising. The Operator will execute the business.

You don't execute the business. You don't fundraise. You find the person who does.

---

## What You're Pitching

IonWave is a premium marine plasma electrolyte brand — a health supplement business designed for direct-to-consumer online sales.

What makes this different from a typical startup pitch:
1. **Complete operating manual.** 41 structured business models (TUPs) covering every operational domain, from product formulation to paid advertising to financial operations. Each is quality-scored and confidence-graded.
2. **AI-augmented execution.** The entire system is designed for AI legibility — the operator works alongside Claude daily.
3. **De-risked validation.** Kill criteria are defined upfront. The business has clear "go/no-go" gates.
4. **Founder-independent.** The system is designed to work without the founder in the execution chain.

You have the **Trade Pitch One-Pager** for quick conversations, and you have access to the **full Trade** (imagination passet) for deep context. Read both.

## Timeline

- **Week 1**: Absorb the brief and the Trade. Submit onboarding acknowledgment.
- **Week 2**: Submit VP sourcing plan. Begin outreach.
- **Weeks 3-8**: Active outreach and screening. Biweekly reports.
- **Week 8**: First shortlist due (2-3 screened VP candidates).
- **Weeks 9-12**: Focused conversations with top candidates. Negotiation.
- **Week 12**: Hard deadline for shortlist if not met by Week 8.
- **Weeks 10-14**: Final recommendation and handoff.

Total engagement: **8-14 weeks**. This is the hardest and slowest link in the chain.

## What You Don't Need to Worry About

- **Running the business.** That's the Operator's job.
- **Fundraising.** That's the VP's job.
- **Product formulation, supply chain, marketing.** All documented in the Trade. You should understand them enough to pitch credibly, but you won't execute any of them.
- **Managing the VA.** The VA's job is done. They found you. You don't manage them.
- **Finding the Operator.** The VP will do that.

---

## What You Should Worry About

- **Understanding the Trade well enough to pitch it.** If you can't explain IonWave to a VP candidate compellingly, the pitch fails. Spend Week 1 absorbing.
- **Outreach quality over quantity.** VP-level people get pitched constantly. Your outreach must be personalized, professional, and specific about why this opportunity is different.
- **Managing your pipeline actively.** 4-12 weeks goes fast. If you're not outreaching consistently and following up, you'll reach Week 12 with an empty funnel.
