## The VP Role

The VP has two parallel functions:

1. **Capital Formation** — Raise seed funding, build an advisor network, create investor pipeline
2. **Find the Operator** — Recruit the person who will execute the business day-to-day

This is a deals person. They need credibility with investors and senior operational talent. They need to be able to pitch the Trade and close people.

---

## Profile: Who This Person Is

### The Frame

Think: **someone who makes deals happen**. They've either raised money, closed partnerships, recruited executives, or sold high-value professional services. They're comfortable in rooms with senior people. They can pitch, negotiate, and follow through.

They are NOT:
- A passive advisor who gives opinions but doesn't execute
- A consultant who writes reports but doesn't close deals
- A first-time professional who's "excited about fundraising"

### Must-Have Characteristics

| Characteristic | Why It Matters | Observable Evidence |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Credibility with senior people** | They'll pitch investors and recruit an operator. If they can't be taken seriously in those rooms, nothing happens. | Track record of working with or selling to executives. Previous fundraising, BD, or executive recruiting experience. |
| **Deal-closing ability** | Capital Formation requires getting people from "interested" to "committed." This is a closing function, not a relationship function. | Can point to deals they personally closed (not just supported). Revenue they generated, funds they raised, or partnerships they landed. |
| **Business model comprehension** | They need to understand the Trade well enough to pitch it — not just the One-Pager, but the underlying logic. | Can read the Trade and articulate the value proposition, unit economics, and risk framework in their own words. |
| **Network or network-building ability** | They need to reach investors and operator candidates. Either existing network or demonstrated ability to build one quickly. | Previous roles that required outbound relationship building. Warm intro access to relevant communities. |
| **Execution discipline** | This role has deliverables and deadlines. A VP who networks but doesn't deliver structured output will stall the chain. | Track record of meeting commitments, managing pipelines, reporting progress. |

### Nice-to-Have

| Characteristic | Why It Helps |
|---------------|-------------|
| **D2C / CPG / supplement industry experience** | Understands the market, knows the players, has relevant network |
| **Startup fundraising experience** | Has done seed rounds before, knows the mechanics |
| **Comfort with AI-augmented workflows** | Will work with Claude daily — previous AI tool experience reduces friction |
| **Entrepreneurial experience** | Has built or run something — understands operator challenges |

### Disqualifiers

| Trait | Why It Disqualifies |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Cannot articulate how they've closed deals** | This role is fundamentally about closing — interest without follow-through wastes 3-6 months |
| **Wants a passive/advisory role** | This is an active execution role with deliverables. If they want to "advise" rather than work, wrong fit. |
| **Demands large cash compensation pre-revenue** | This is an equity role. Some retainer may be negotiable, but if they need significant cash upfront, the incentive structure doesn't work. |
| **Cannot commit 10+ hours/week** | Capital Formation and operator recruiting require sustained effort. A few hours here and there won't close anything. |

## Where to Find VP Candidates

### High-Signal Sources

| Source | Who You'll Find | How to Access |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Angel investor networks** | People who invest in D2C/CPG and might want an operating role | AngelList, local angel groups, alumni networks |
| **D2C founder communities** | Former founders who've raised money and might want a deals role in a new venture | Twitter/X D2C community, EcomCrew, industry Slack/Discord |
| **Management consulting alumni** | Post-consulting professionals looking for operating partner roles | McKinsey/BCG/Bain alumni networks, LinkedIn |
| **Corporate development professionals** | People who do M&A or partnerships at CPG companies and want startup exposure | LinkedIn targeted search, industry events |
| **Venture capital associates/principals** | VC-adjacent people looking for operating roles | VC firm websites, LinkedIn, warm intros through investors |
| **Executive recruiting firms** | Recruiters who place VP/C-level in D2C/CPG may know candidates in transition | Direct outreach to recruiting firms specializing in consumer brands |

### Lower-Signal Sources (Supplementary)

| Source | Who You'll Find | Caveats |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **LinkedIn cold outreach** | Broad pool, high noise | Personalize aggressively. Generic messages to VP-level people get ignored. |
| **Job boards (AngelList, Work at a Startup)** | Active job seekers | May find people between roles, but also attracts less committed candidates |
| **MBA alumni networks** | Career-changers into venture | May lack deal-closing experience |

## Compensation Framework for the VP

The VP is compensated primarily with equity. This is the pitch you're making:

| Component | Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Equity** | 5-10% [DEC-CHAIN-002 — exact amount TBD] | Meaningful stake in a structured, de-risked venture |
| **Retainer** | Possible, amount TBD | May be necessary for candidates who need some cash flow |
| **Upside** | Aligned with venture success | If IonWave hits its targets ($2M EBITDA at 24 months), 5-10% equity is substantial |

**How to pitch the economics**: The Trade documents a business with 60% gross margins, a $30K MRR Year 1 target, and a $2M EBITDA 24-month potential. At 5-10% equity, a successful IonWave generates $100-200K+ in annual equity value. This is not a moonshot — it's a de-risked bet with a complete playbook.

**How to handle "but it's pre-revenue"**: Acknowledge it honestly. The Trade is comprehensive but unexecuted. The kill criteria mean the venture won't bleed money — it will either validate or stop. The equity is a bet on execution, with better information than most seed-stage ventures.

## Integration Requirement

The VP, once hired, must complete an **integration work artifact** before proceeding with their functions. This is a condensed Trade narrative in their own words — a forcing function to verify they've absorbed the Trade deeply enough to pitch investors and recruit operators.

You should mention this during your pitch: the VP role includes a structured onboarding that ensures deep comprehension of the venture. This is a feature, not a bug — it means the VP enters Capital Formation with genuine understanding, not surface-level talking points.
