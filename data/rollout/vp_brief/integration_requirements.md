## Why This Exists

The VP role requires pitching IonWave to investors and evaluating operator candidates. Both require genuine comprehension of the Trade — not surface familiarity with the One-Pager, but deep enough understanding to answer hard questions, defend the model under scrutiny, and assess whether an operator candidate truly grasps the system.

The Integration Work Artifact is the proof of that comprehension. It is a **quality gate** — you cannot proceed with Capital Formation or Operator recruiting until this deliverable passes.

---

## The Assignment

Write a **condensed Trade narrative** (2,000-3,000 words) that covers three models:

### Model 1: The Business Model

Answer in your own words (not copy-paste from TUPs):

- What is IonWave's value proposition and who is the target customer?
- What are the unit economics — COGS, price point, margin, and why do they work?
- What differentiates IonWave from competitors (LMNT, Seaonic, others)?
- What are the kill criteria — when does the business stop vs. continue?
- What is the 90-day validation plan?

**What we're checking**: Do you understand the business well enough to pitch it to an investor who will ask hard questions?

### Model 2: The Execution Model

Answer in your own words:

- How is the Trade structured? (41 TUPs — what does this mean practically?)
- How does the operator work with Claude (AI co-pilot)?
- What does the operator do in the first 90 days? (Phase gates, milestones)
- What is the compensation structure and why is it designed the way it is?
- What are the operator kill criteria?

**What we're checking**: Do you understand the system well enough to evaluate whether an operator candidate can execute it?

### Model 3: The Capital Model

Answer in your own words:

- How much capital does IonWave need and what is it used for?
- What type of investors should be targeted and why?
- What is the fundraising instrument (SAFE, etc.) and terms?
- What's the investor pitch — why should someone write a check?
- What risks does an investor face and how are they mitigated?

**What we're checking**: Do you understand the funding needs well enough to lead Capital Formation?

---

## Format Requirements

- **Length**: 2,000-3,000 words total across all three models
- **Voice**: Your own words. This should sound like YOU explaining IonWave to someone, not like a document you reformatted.
- **Structure**: Clear headings for each model. Concise, direct prose. No filler.
- **Honest gaps**: If something is unclear to you after reading the Trade, say so. "I don't fully understand X and would want to discuss it" is a valid and valuable statement.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Dimension | What We're Checking | Pass Criteria |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Accuracy** | Does the narrative correctly represent the Trade? | No material misrepresentations. Numbers, positioning, and strategy match the TUPs. |
| **Comprehension depth** | Does it go beyond the One-Pager? | References specific TUP content (not just surface talking points). Shows understanding of trade-offs and nuances. |
| **Own voice** | Is this genuinely rewritten, not reformatted? | Reads as a person explaining, not a document summarizing. Has opinions and observations. |
| **Critical thinking** | Does the VP identify strengths AND weaknesses? | Acknowledges risks, flags uncertainties, identifies what's unproven. |
| **Investor-ready** | Could this narrative serve as the basis for an investor pitch? | Clear enough to explain IonWave to a skeptical angel in 10 minutes. |

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Pass** | Demonstrates sufficient comprehension to proceed. May have minor gaps but fundamentally understands the Trade. |
| **Pass with notes** | Mostly good but has specific gaps that need addressing. VP proceeds but receives targeted questions to resolve gaps. |
| **Revise** | Significant misunderstandings or insufficient depth. VP rewrites with specific feedback. |
| **Fail** | Fundamental lack of comprehension. Reconsider VP placement. |

## Why This Matters

This is the strongest anti-degradation mechanism in the chain. At every earlier stage, there's a risk that the Trade's signal degrades — each person understands less than the person who briefed them. The integration artifact reverses this: it forces you to prove you've absorbed the Trade before you represent it to investors and operator candidates.

If you skip or phone in this step, everything downstream suffers:
- Investors get a weaker pitch
- Operator candidates get a shallower picture of the venture
- The compliance agent can't evaluate whether you're representing the Trade accurately

Take this seriously. The 2 weeks of absorption will make every subsequent conversation more effective.
