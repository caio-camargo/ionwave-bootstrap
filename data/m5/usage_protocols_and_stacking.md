## Executive Summary

This document translates IonWave's formulation science into practical customer guidance:
1. **Dosing protocols by situation** (keto, fasting, workout, hot weather, travel, hangover, general wellness)
2. **Supplement stacking** (what works, what to avoid, timing optimization)
3. **Drug interactions** (blood pressure meds, diuretics, antibiotics)
4. **Safety contraindications** (kidney disease, heart conditions, pregnancy)
5. **Troubleshooting** (GI upset, headaches, taste issues)

**Core Principle:** IonWave is designed for **flexible, context-specific dosing** (1-3 servings daily) based on individual sodium needs, activity level, and dietary approach.

## 1. Dosing Protocols by Situation

### 1.1 General Wellness (Baseline Support)

**Target User:** Healthy adult with no specific metabolic stressor (not keto, not fasting, moderate activity)

**Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 1 serving (1 stick pack) daily
- **Timing:** Morning (upon waking) or pre-workout
- **Dilution:** 16-20 oz water (isotonic for comfort)
- **Rationale:** Provides baseline electrolyte support (1,000mg Na = 43% DV), mild insurance against dietary deficiency

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Maintains hydration throughout day
- Supports consistent energy (electrolyte balance = stable cellular function)
- Prevents subclinical deficiency (especially Mg, which 50-60% of US population lacks)

**Notes:**
- General wellness users may find 1,000mg sodium unnecessary if consuming processed foods (already 3-5g Na daily)
- IonWave shines most in low-sodium diets (whole foods, home-cooked)

---

### 1.2 Ketogenic Diet (Fat-Adapted State)

**Target User:** Individual on ketogenic diet (<50g carbs daily), whether for weight loss, metabolic health, or therapeutic reasons

**Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 2-3 servings daily (2,000-3,000mg sodium)
- **Timing:**
  - Serving 1: Morning (upon waking, before coffee)
  - Serving 2: Mid-afternoon (combat energy dip)
  - Serving 3 (optional): Evening (if active, or during keto adaptation phase)
- **Dilution:** 16-20 oz water per serving
- **Rationale:** Ketogenic diets suppress insulin → reduced aldosterone → increased renal sodium excretion (kidneys dump 2-3g Na in first week, ongoing losses 1-2g/day)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Prevents "keto flu":** Headaches, fatigue, muscle cramps, brain fog (90% of symptoms = electrolyte deficiency, not ketone adaptation)
- **Improves energy:** Sodium maintains blood volume → stable blood pressure → better brain perfusion
- **Reduces muscle cramps:** Magnesium + potassium support muscle relaxation/contraction

**Keto Adaptation Phase (Weeks 1-4):**
- **Higher dose:** 3 servings daily (3,000mg Na) during transition
- **Symptom-driven:** If headache/fatigue persists, increase to 4 servings (4,000mg Na) temporarily
- **Taper:** Once fat-adapted (Week 4+), reduce to 2 servings daily (maintenance)

**Evidence Base:**
- Volek & Phinney (2011): "The Art and Science of Low Carbohydrate Living" recommends 3-5g sodium daily for keto
- Westman et al. (2007): Low-carb diets increase sodium excretion by 1-2g/day vs. high-carb

---

### 1.3 Intermittent Fasting (Extended Fasting)

**Target User:** Individual practicing intermittent fasting (16:8, 20:4, OMAD, or multi-day fasts)

**Protocol (16:8 or 20:4 Daily Fasting):**
- **Dose:** 1-2 servings daily
- **Timing:**
  - Serving 1: Mid-fast (e.g., 10am if fasting until 2pm) — breaks no fast (zero calories)
  - Serving 2 (optional): Post-workout (if training fasted)
- **Dilution:** 16-20 oz water (sip over 30-60 min, not chugged)
- **Rationale:** Fasting = no food sodium → increased reliance on supplemental electrolytes; prevents postural hypotension (lightheadedness upon standing)

**Protocol (Multi-Day Fasting, 48-72+ Hours):**
- **Dose:** 2-3 servings daily (every 6-8 hours)
- **Timing:** Evenly spaced (e.g., 8am, 2pm, 8pm)
- **Dilution:** 20-24 oz water per serving (larger volume for extended fasting)
- **Rationale:** No dietary sodium intake → 100% reliance on supplementation; prevents severe electrolyte depletion (risk of cardiac arrhythmia at 72+ hours if neglected)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Prevents fasting-induced fatigue:** Sodium maintains blood volume and pressure
- **Reduces hunger:** Electrolyte balance stabilizes blood sugar (via adrenal function)
- **Supports autophagy:** Proper electrolytes = cellular processes function optimally (fasting benefits maximized)

**Safety Note:**
- Extended fasting (>72 hours) should be medically supervised
- Refeeding after multi-day fasts requires electrolyte monitoring (refeeding syndrome risk—phosphate, magnesium shifts)

### 1.4 Endurance Exercise (Marathon, Cycling, Triathlon)

**Target User:** Athlete engaging in prolonged exercise (>60 minutes, high sweat rate)

**Protocol:**
- **Pre-workout:** 1 serving, 30-60 min before exercise
- **During workout:** 1 serving per hour of activity (for sessions >90 min)
- **Post-workout:** 1 serving immediately after (within 30 min)
- **Dilution:** 16-24 oz water per serving (isotonic for rapid absorption)
- **Rationale:** Sweat loss = 500-1,000mg sodium per hour (varies by individual); IonWave replaces at 1:1 ratio

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Prevents exercise-associated hyponatremia (EAH):** Drinking plain water during endurance exercise dilutes blood sodium → confusion, nausea, seizures (documented in marathons)
- **Maintains performance:** Electrolyte balance = sustained muscle contraction, nerve signaling
- **Reduces cramping:** Magnesium + sodium + potassium prevent electrolyte-induced cramps (note: not all cramps are electrolyte-related—some are neuromuscular fatigue)

**Hot Weather Modification:**
- Increase to 1.5 servings per hour if exercising in >85°F (sweat rate doubles)
- Monitor urine color (pale yellow = adequate hydration; dark amber = dehydrated)

**Evidence Base:**
- American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM): 300-600mg sodium per hour for endurance athletes
- IonWave provides 1,000mg → suitable for high sweat rates (2+ liters/hour)

---

### 1.5 High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT, CrossFit)

**Target User:** Athlete engaging in intense, short-duration workouts (30-60 min, high sweat volume)

**Protocol:**
- **Pre-workout:** 1 serving, 15-30 min before (fuel electrolyte reserves)
- **Post-workout:** 1 serving immediately after (repletion)
- **Dilution:** 12-16 oz water (slightly more concentrated for rapid uptake)
- **Rationale:** HIIT = brief but intense sweat loss (500-700mg sodium in 45 min session); pre-loading + post-loading prevents deficit

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Supports high power output:** Sodium + potassium = muscle contraction efficiency
- **Reduces recovery time:** Electrolyte repletion + hydration = faster clearance of metabolic waste
- **Prevents post-workout fatigue:** "Hitting the wall" often = electrolyte depletion + glycogen (IonWave addresses electrolyte component)

---

### 1.6 Hot Weather / High Sweat Environments

**Target User:** Individual in hot climate (>85°F), outdoor labor, or sauna/hot yoga enthusiast

**Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 2-3 servings daily (adjust based on sweat volume)
- **Timing:**
  - Serving 1: Morning (pre-load before heat exposure)
  - Serving 2: Mid-day (during peak heat)
  - Serving 3: Evening (if continuing heat exposure, or post-sauna)
- **Dilution:** 20-24 oz water per serving (higher volume for heat)
- **Rationale:** Heat stress = increased sweat rate (can reach 1-2 liters/hour) → sodium loss up to 2,000mg/hour in extreme cases

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Prevents heat exhaustion:** Maintains blood volume (sodium = water retention)
- **Reduces heat cramps:** Magnesium + electrolytes prevent muscle cramping in calves, hamstrings
- **Supports thermoregulation:** Adequate hydration + electrolytes = effective sweating (body's cooling mechanism)

**Sauna-Specific Protocol:**
- 1 serving before sauna (30 min prior)
- 1 serving after sauna (immediate rehydration)
- Volume: 24-32 oz water total (sauna dehydration = 1-2 lbs water loss in 20-30 min session)

---

### 1.7 Travel (Air Travel, Time Zone Changes)

**Target User:** Frequent traveler (business, vacation) experiencing dehydration from air travel

**Protocol:**
- **Pre-flight:** 1 serving, 1-2 hours before departure
- **In-flight:** 1 serving per 4-6 hours of flight (for long-haul)
- **Post-arrival:** 1 serving upon landing (combat jet lag)
- **Dilution:** 16-20 oz water per serving
- **Rationale:** Airplane cabin humidity = 10-20% (desert-level dryness) → passive water loss via respiration; altitude cabin pressure = mild diuresis (increased urination)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Reduces jet lag:** Proper hydration + electrolytes = better circadian adaptation
- **Prevents in-flight fatigue:** Dehydration = brain fog, headaches (common traveler complaint)
- **Supports immune function:** Air travel = immune stress (dry mucous membranes, recirculated air); hydration maintains mucosal barrier

**Travel-Specific Tips:**
- Avoid alcohol in-flight (diuretic effect compounds dehydration)
- Pair IonWave with 8-12 oz water per hour of flight (total fluid intake)

---

### 1.8 Hangover Recovery

**Target User:** Individual recovering from alcohol consumption (dehydration, electrolyte depletion)

**Protocol:**
- **Before bed (post-drinking):** 1 serving (preventive measure)
- **Upon waking:** 1-2 servings (rehydration)
- **Dilution:** 16-20 oz water per serving
- **Rationale:** Alcohol = diuretic (suppresses ADH, anti-diuretic hormone) → increased urination → sodium, potassium, magnesium loss; acetaldehyde (alcohol metabolite) = inflammatory, worsened by dehydration

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Reduces hangover severity:** Headache, fatigue, nausea (partially electrolyte/dehydration-mediated)
- **Faster recovery:** Rehydration + electrolytes = improved liver function (metabolizing remaining alcohol)
- **Limits "hangxiety":** Magnesium supports GABA function (calming neurotransmitter, depleted by alcohol)

**Realistic Expectations:**
- IonWave addresses dehydration/electrolyte component (~40-50% of hangover symptoms)
- Does NOT address: Acetaldehyde toxicity, inflammation, sleep disruption (other interventions needed: NAC, vitamin C, quality sleep)

## 2. Supplement Stacking Guide

### 2.1 Synergistic Combinations (Safe & Effective)

**IonWave + Magnesium Glycinate (Evening Dose)**
- **Rationale:** IonWave provides 60mg Mg (chloride/sulfate form); glycinate form adds 200-400mg for total 260-460mg daily (meets RDA)
- **Benefit:** IonWave = daytime energy, glycinate = nighttime relaxation (glycine = calming amino acid)
- **Timing:** IonWave AM/PM, magnesium glycinate 1-2 hours before bed
- **Safety:** Total Mg <500mg (below UL of 350mg from supplements—slight excess, but glycinate gentle on GI)

**IonWave + Creatine Monohydrate**
- **Rationale:** Creatine draws water into muscle cells (cell volumization) → increased fluid needs
- **Benefit:** IonWave ensures adequate hydration + electrolytes to support creatine's intracellular water retention
- **Timing:** IonWave pre/post-workout, creatine 5g daily (anytime)
- **Safety:** No interactions; synergistic for performance

**IonWave + Protein Powder (Post-Workout)**
- **Rationale:** Protein synthesis requires hydration + electrolytes (cellular machinery = water-dependent)
- **Benefit:** Combined recovery (amino acids + electrolytes + fluids)
- **Timing:** Mix IonWave + protein shake post-workout (or separate by 15-30 min)
- **Safety:** No interactions

**IonWave + Vitamin D + Omega-3s (Daily Wellness Stack)**
- **Rationale:** Vitamin D supports calcium absorption (synergizes with IonWave's trace minerals); omega-3s reduce inflammation (complements hydration)
- **Benefit:** Holistic wellness (electrolytes + bone health + cardiovascular health)
- **Timing:** IonWave AM, Vitamin D + fish oil with meals (fat-soluble absorption)
- **Safety:** No interactions

**IonWave + Electrolyte-Rich Foods (Whole-Food Synergy)**
- **Examples:** Avocado (potassium), spinach (magnesium), bone broth (sodium)
- **Benefit:** IonWave supplements dietary intake (not replaces); whole foods provide cofactors (vitamins, phytonutrients)
- **Strategy:** Use IonWave to fill gaps (e.g., keto diet low in K-rich fruits → IonWave + avocado daily)

---

### 2.2 Caution Combinations (Timing or Dose Adjustments Needed)

**IonWave + High-Dose Potassium Supplements**
- **Concern:** IonWave = 200mg K; additional K supplement = risk of exceeding safe intake (>3,500mg can cause hyperkalemia in sensitive individuals)
- **Guideline:** If taking separate K supplement (e.g., potassium citrate 500mg), limit IonWave to 1 serving daily + monitor for symptoms (tingling, irregular heartbeat)
- **Contraindication:** Do not combine if on potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone, amiloride)

**IonWave + Pre-Workout Supplements (Stimulant-Based)**
- **Concern:** Many pre-workouts contain electrolytes (sodium, potassium) → double-dosing risk
- **Guideline:** Check pre-workout label; if contains >500mg Na, reduce IonWave to ½ serving (or time separately—IonWave 1 hour before, pre-workout 15 min before)
- **Safety:** Total sodium <5,000mg daily (IonWave 1,000mg + pre-workout 500mg + diet 2-3,000mg = safe)

**IonWave + Diuretic Medications (Prescription)**
- **Types:** Furosemide (Lasix), hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ)
- **Concern:** Diuretics increase electrolyte excretion (K, Mg) → IonWave may be beneficial, but dose adjustment needed
- **Guideline:** Consult physician; may require blood work (serum K, Mg) to optimize IonWave dose (1-2 servings likely helpful, but individual variation)

**IonWave + Antibiotics (Quinolones, Tetracyclines)**
- **Concern:** Magnesium binds to these antibiotics → reduced absorption → treatment failure
- **Guideline:** Separate by ≥2 hours (e.g., antibiotic at 8am, IonWave at 10am or later)
- **Affected antibiotics:** Ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin, doxycycline, tetracycline

---

### 2.3 Avoid Combinations (Contraindicated)

**IonWave + Potassium-Sparing Diuretics**
- **Medications:** Spironolactone (Aldactone), amiloride, triamterene
- **Risk:** Hyperkalemia (elevated blood potassium) → cardiac arrhythmia, muscle weakness, potentially fatal
- **Mechanism:** These drugs prevent K excretion + IonWave adds 200mg K → accumulation
- **Recommendation:** **DO NOT USE** without physician approval + blood monitoring

**IonWave + ACE Inhibitors / ARBs (Blood Pressure Meds)**
- **Medications:** Lisinopril, enalapril, losartan, valsartan
- **Risk:** These drugs increase potassium retention → IonWave's 200mg K = hyperkalemia risk
- **Recommendation:** **Consult physician before use**; may require reduced IonWave dose or blood K monitoring

**IonWave + Severe Kidney Disease (CKD Stage 4-5)**
- **Concern:** Kidneys cannot excrete excess K, Mg → dangerous accumulation
- **Recommendation:** **CONTRAINDICATED** unless under nephrologist supervision

## 4. Safety Contraindications

### 4.1 Medical Conditions

**ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS (Do Not Use Without MD Approval):**
1. **Chronic Kidney Disease (Stage 3-5):** Impaired K, Mg excretion → accumulation risk
2. **Addison's Disease:** Adrenal insufficiency → already elevated K (aldosterone deficiency)
3. **Hyperkalemia (diagnosed):** Elevated blood K → IonWave worsens
4. **Severe Heart Failure (NYHA Class III-IV):** Sodium restriction often prescribed → 1,000mg Na contraindicated

**RELATIVE CONTRAINDICATIONS (Consult Physician First):**
1. **Hypertension (uncontrolled):** High sodium intake may worsen; however, context matters (if low-carb diet, IonWave may be appropriate)
2. **Kidney Stones (calcium oxalate):** High sodium increases urinary calcium → stone risk (drink adequate water to mitigate)
3. **Pregnancy/Lactation:** Generally safe, but consult OB-GYN for personalized guidance
4. **Children (<18 years):** No established pediatric dosing; not recommended without pediatrician approval

---

### 4.2 Special Populations

**Pregnancy (Trimesters 1-3):**
- **Electrolyte needs increase** (blood volume expansion, fetal development)
- **IonWave context:** 1-2 servings likely safe (Na 1,000-2,000mg within pregnancy guidelines)
- **Recommendation:** Consult OB-GYN; monitor blood pressure (some pregnant women sodium-sensitive)

**Breastfeeding:**
- **Electrolyte needs elevated** (milk production, fluid loss)
- **IonWave context:** Generally safe (electrolytes pass into milk but at physiological levels)
- **Recommendation:** Consult physician if concerns; IonWave may help prevent postpartum depletion

**Elderly (65+ years):**
- **Considerations:** Decreased kidney function (common), polypharmacy (multiple meds)
- **IonWave context:** 1 serving daily likely safe if no CKD; higher doses require MD approval
- **Recommendation:** Check medication list for interactions (ACE inhibitors, diuretics common in elderly)

**Athletes (Competitive, Drug-Tested):**
- **IonWave context:** NSF Certified for Sport (planned) → banned substance-free
- **Recommendation:** Wait for NSF certification before use if subject to WADA/USADA testing

## 5. Troubleshooting Common Issues

### 5.1 Gastrointestinal Upset

**Symptom:** Nausea, diarrhea, stomach cramps within 30-60 min of consuming IonWave

**Possible Causes:**
1. **Too concentrated (hypertonic):** Dissolved in <12 oz water → osmotic diarrhea (like magnesium citrate laxative)
2. **Too fast consumption:** Chugging → rapid osmotic shift → GI distress
3. **Sensitive stomach:** Magnesium sulfate = laxative effect in some individuals

**Solutions:**
- **Increase dilution:** Use 20-24 oz water (isotonic solution gentler on GI)
- **Sip slowly:** Consume over 20-30 min (not 5 min gulp)
- **Take with food:** Small snack (e.g., handful of nuts) buffers GI (though fasting users may not prefer this)
- **Reduce dose:** Start with ½ serving, gradually increase to full serving over 1-2 weeks (GI adaptation)

---

### 5.2 Headaches

**Symptom:** Headache after starting IonWave (paradoxical, since it's supposed to prevent them)

**Possible Causes:**
1. **Overhydration (hyponatremia):** Drinking excessive water with IonWave → dilutes blood sodium further
2. **Magnesium sensitivity:** Some individuals experience headaches from Mg supplementation (rare, but documented)
3. **Detox reaction:** Unlikely, but some report transient headaches when correcting chronic dehydration

**Solutions:**
- **Check water intake:** Aim for urine pale yellow (not clear); clear = overhydration
- **Reduce dose:** Try ½ serving for 3-5 days, then increase
- **Switch timing:** Take IonWave with food (slows absorption, may reduce headache trigger)
- **If persists >1 week:** Discontinue and consult physician (rare idiosyncratic reaction)

---

### 5.3 Muscle Cramps (Persistent)

**Symptom:** Muscle cramps continue despite using IonWave

**Possible Causes:**
1. **Non-electrolyte cramps:** Neuromuscular fatigue, dehydration (need more water, not just electrolytes)
2. **Insufficient magnesium:** 60mg Mg = 14% DV (may need additional Mg glycinate 200-400mg)
3. **Calcium deficiency:** IonWave's Ca content = trace (may need separate Ca supplement if diet low)
4. **Overtraining:** Cramps = muscle damage signal (rest needed)

**Solutions:**
- **Increase water intake:** 0.5-1 oz per lb body weight daily (e.g., 150 lb person = 75-150 oz water)
- **Add magnesium glycinate:** 200-400mg before bed (targets muscle relaxation)
- **Stretch & foam roll:** Mechanical interventions (not all cramps are electrolyte-mediated)
- **Reduce training volume:** If chronic cramping, consider overtraining syndrome

---

### 5.4 Taste Issues

**Symptom:** "Too salty," "bitter aftertaste," "can't finish the drink"

**Possible Causes:**
1. **High salt sensitivity:** 1,000mg Na noticeable to those accustomed to low-sodium diets
2. **Stevia aftertaste:** Genetic variation (TAS2R receptors) → some people taste stevia as very bitter
3. **Ocean mineral taste:** Trace minerals (Mg sulfate, trace metals) = slight metallic/mineral notes

**Solutions:**
- **Increase dilution:** 24-32 oz water (dilutes saltiness)
- **Add flavor enhancers:**
  - Lemon/lime wedge (fresh citrus masks salt)
  - Cucumber slices (spa water effect)
  - Mint leaves (cooling, distracts from mineral taste)
- **Mix with other beverages:**
  - Coffee (IonWave unflavored in morning coffee = "electrolyte coffee")
  - Smoothies (blend with fruit, masks taste entirely)
  - Bone broth (savory context = salt tastes appropriate)
- **Try unflavored SKU (if available):** Flavor yourself with preferences

---

### 5.5 No Perceived Benefit

**Symptom:** "I don't feel any different after taking IonWave"

**Possible Causes:**
1. **Not electrolyte-deficient:** If diet already adequate in Na/K/Mg (e.g., eating 3,000mg Na daily from food), IonWave = redundant
2. **Incorrect use case:** General wellness user may not notice benefit (vs. keto/fasting user who feels dramatic improvement)
3. **Insufficient dose:** 1 serving may be too low for high-sweat individual
4. **Other limiting factors:** Fatigue from poor sleep, stress, nutrient deficiencies beyond electrolytes (e.g., iron, B12)

**Solutions:**
- **Reassess use case:** IonWave designed for keto, fasting, exercise, heat stress (if none apply, benefit minimal)
- **Increase dose:** Try 2 servings daily for 1 week (especially if exercising or keto)
- **Track metrics:** Subjective (energy, cramps, focus) + objective (workout performance, fasting duration tolerance)
- **Address other factors:** Sleep 7-9 hours, manage stress, ensure adequate protein/calories

## 6. Edge Cases & Advanced Protocols

### 6.1 Carnivore Diet

**Context:** All-meat diet (high protein, high fat, zero carbs, naturally high in sodium from meat)

**IonWave Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 1 serving daily (or less)
- **Rationale:** Carnivore diet naturally provides 2-4g sodium from meat (especially if salted); IonWave = supplemental insurance, not primary source
- **Note:** Carnivore dieters may benefit more from potassium (meat low in K vs. vegetables)

---

### 6.2 Vegan/Plant-Based Athletes

**Context:** Plant-based diet (naturally lower in sodium, higher in potassium from fruits/vegetables)

**IonWave Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 1-2 servings daily (especially around workouts)
- **Rationale:** Plant-based diets = low Na (unless heavily salted) → IonWave fills gap; K already adequate from produce
- **Note:** Vegan athletes may prefer algae-based omega-3s + B12 alongside IonWave (common deficiencies)

---

### 6.3 Shift Workers / Sleep-Deprived

**Context:** Irregular sleep schedule, night shifts, chronic sleep deprivation

**IonWave Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 1-2 servings daily (timed around wake/sleep transitions)
- **Timing:** Upon waking (whenever that is—6pm for night shift workers), mid-shift (combat fatigue)
- **Rationale:** Sleep deprivation = impaired adrenal function → suboptimal electrolyte regulation; IonWave provides exogenous support
- **Note:** IonWave does NOT replace sleep (foundational); use as harm reduction during unavoidable sleep loss

---

### 6.4 High-Altitude Environments

**Context:** Living/traveling above 8,000 ft (altitude = increased urination, dehydration risk)

**IonWave Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 2-3 servings daily during acclimatization (first 3-7 days at altitude)
- **Rationale:** Altitude diuresis = increased fluid/electrolyte loss; IonWave prevents altitude sickness symptoms (headache, fatigue overlap with dehydration)
- **Note:** Pair with >1 gallon water daily (altitude = dry air → insensible water loss via respiration)
