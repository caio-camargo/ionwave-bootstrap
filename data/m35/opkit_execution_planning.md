## What This OpKit Delivers

**Problem solved**: Most startups wing their first 90 days, operate reactively (not rhythmically), and scale without objective PMF criteria. Result: broken unit economics, team chaos, premature scaling, burnout.

**Solution**: A systematic framework for building phase-gated execution plans + operating rhythms that drive accountability and prevent premature scaling.

**Outputs**:
1. **30/90/365-Day Plans** — Granular first 30 days, directional 31-90, strategic 91-365
2. **Operating Rhythms** — Daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly loops that feed forward
3. **Phase Gate Checklists** — Objective LAUNCH → PMF → SCALE criteria
4. **Meeting Templates** — WBR, MBR, QBR, Standup (copy-paste ready)
5. **Core SOPs** — Repeatable processes (fulfillment, CX, ad review, financial close)

## Instructions (12-Step Process)

### STEP 1: Load Context (Before Planning)

**Read**:
- Trade Thesis (M0 equivalent) — strategic direction, kill criteria
- Financial Model (M3 equivalent) — revenue targets, unit economics assumptions
- All operational TUPs (M5-M30 equivalent) — execution readiness per domain

**Gather**:
- Current status (pre-revenue, in-market, scaling)
- Team size (solo, 2-3, 4-6, 7+)
- Cash runway (months)
- Phase (Launch, PMF, Scale)

**Output**: Context memo (1-page) — where you are, where you're going, what's at risk.

---

### STEP 2: Build 30-Day Plan (Task-Level Precision)

**Framework**: Lock next 30 days with EVERY task assigned, dated, owned.

**Template**:
```
# Day 1-30: [PHASE NAME]

**Objective**: [1 sentence — what MUST be true by Day 30]
**Owner**: [Who is accountable]
**Success Criteria**: [3-5 measurable outcomes]
**Kill Criteria**: [What triggers pause/pivot]

---

## Week 1: [Theme]
- [ ] Day 1: [Task 1] (Owner: X, Duration: Y hrs)
- [ ] Day 2: [Task 2]
...

## Week 2: [Theme]
...

## Week 3: [Theme]
...

## Week 4: [Theme]
...

**Day 30 Checkpoint**:
- [ ] [Criterion 1]
- [ ] [Criterion 2]
...
```

**Rules**:
- One task per day CAN'T have >8 hours (if so, break into sub-tasks)
- Dependencies explicit (e.g., "Task B requires Task A completion")
- No vague verbs ("research," "explore") — use "Call 3 suppliers," "Email 10 customers"

**IonWave Example**: Day 1 = 9:00-10:00 call lawyer, 10:00-11:00 Mercury app, 11:00-12:00 QBO setup. Task-level, timed, actionable.

---

### STEP 3: Build 90-Day Plan (Directional Milestones)

**Framework**: Days 31-90 are themes + major milestones, NOT task-level.

**Template**:
```
# Day 31-90: [PHASE NAME]

**Theme**: [1 sentence strategic focus]
**Goal**: [Revenue, orders, or PMF signal target]
**Owner**: [Who]

---

## Month 2 (Days 31-60): [Sub-theme]
**Key Milestones**:
- Day 45: [Milestone 1]
- Day 60: [Milestone 2]

**Weekly Themes**:
- Week 5-6: [Focus area 1]
- Week 7-8: [Focus area 2]

## Month 3 (Days 61-90): [Sub-theme]
[Same structure]

**Day 90 Checkpoint**:
- [ ] [Gate criterion 1]
- [ ] [Gate criterion 2]
...
```

**Rules**:
- Update weekly (roll forward 30 days of precision, days 31-90 stay directional)
- Day 90 checkpoint is a GATE (pass/fail decision point)

**IonWave Example**: Month 2 = "Channel Validation" (test Meta/TikTok/Google, target $3K-8K revenue). Month 3 = "Operational Maturity" (3PL quotes, PMF testing, target $5K-12K revenue).

---

### STEP 4: Build 365-Day Plan (Quarterly Themes Only)

**Framework**: Days 91-365 are quarterly OKRs, NOT monthly plans.

**Template**:
```
# Day 91-365: [YEAR 1 THEME]

| Quarter | Theme | Revenue Target | Key Milestone | Exit Criteria |
|---------|-------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Q1** | [Theme] | $X-Y | [Milestone] | [Gate criteria] |
| **Q2** | [Theme] | $X-Y | [Milestone] | [Gate criteria] |
| **Q3** | [Theme] | $X-Y | [Milestone] | [Gate criteria] |
| **Q4** | [Theme] | $X-Y | [Milestone] | [Gate criteria] |
```

**Rules**:
- Refresh quarterly (Q1 review updates Q2-Q4, Q2 review updates Q3-Q4+Q1 Year 2)
- No task-level detail (that lives in rolling 30-day plan)

**IonWave Example**: Q1 = Launch → Learning ($5K-15K), Q2 = PMF Validation ($30K-60K), Q3 = Scale Prep ($50K-100K), Q4 = Growth ($50K-100K).

---

### STEP 5: Design Operating Loops (Choreography)

**Framework**: Daily → Weekly → Monthly → Quarterly loops that FEED FORWARD.

**Template**:
```
| Loop | Frequency | Duration | Purpose |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Daily Pulse** | Every business day | 5-15 min | Tactical execution check |
| **Weekly Review** | Every Friday | 30-60 min | Metrics + customer voice + ROTC |
| **Monthly Review** | First Friday | 60-90 min | Financial + strategic health |
| **Quarterly Review** | End of quarter | Half-day | Strategic review + OKRs |
```

**Choreography rules**:
1. Monday AM starts with Friday review insights (feed-forward)
2. Weekly review aggregates daily pulses (feed-forward)
3. Monthly review aggregates weekly reviews (feed-forward)
4. Quarterly review sets OKRs that cascade to monthly priorities (feed-forward)
5. Loops NEVER duplicate (if daily pulse covers it, weekly doesn't repeat)

**IonWave Example**: Daily Pulse = "What shipped yesterday, what's critical today, any blockers?" (5 min solo). Weekly WBR = 7 metrics + customer voice + ROTC (30-45 min). Monthly MBR = Survival Five + 4 Questions (60 min). Quarterly QBR = offsite, OKRs, phase gate decision (half-day).

### STEP 6: Create Meeting Templates

**Required templates**:
1. **Daily Standup** (async or sync, 5-15 min)
2. **Weekly Business Review (WBR)** (30-60 min)
3. **Monthly Business Review (MBR)** (60-90 min)
4. **Quarterly Business Review (QBR)** (half-day)

**Template structure** (universal):
```
# [Meeting Name] — [Date]

**Attendees**: [Names]
**Duration**: [X min]
**Owner**: [Facilitator]

---

## Agenda
1. [Section 1]
2. [Section 2]
...

## [Section 1]
[Content/data]

## Decisions Made
1. [Decision]: [Rationale]

## Action Items
| Action | Owner | Due | Status |
|--------|-------|-----|--------|
| [Task] | [Name] | [Date] | [Status] |

---

**Next Meeting**: [Date/Time]
```

**IonWave Example**: WBR has 6 sections (Metrics, Cohort/Retention, Customer Voice, ROTC, Decisions, Next Week Preview). Each section has specific format (metrics = table, ROTC = bullets, decisions = log).

### STEP 7: Define Phase Gates (Objective Criteria)

**Framework**: 3 phases (LAUNCH → PMF → SCALE), each with objective pass/fail criteria.

**Template**:
```
## Phase [N]: [NAME]

**Entry Criteria**: [What must be true to start this phase]
**Exit Criteria**: [What must be true to proceed to next phase]
**Duration**: [Estimated time range]
**Kill Trigger**: [What causes pause/pivot]

---

### Checklist (Pass = proceed, Fail = iterate or pivot)

**Quantitative Signals**:
- [ ] [Metric 1] > [Threshold]
- [ ] [Metric 2] < [Threshold]
...

**Qualitative Signals**:
- [ ] [Signal 1]
- [ ] [Signal 2]
...

**Operational Signals**:
- [ ] [Signal 1]
- [ ] [Signal 2]
...

**GATE**: Pass [X]+ quantitative + [Y]+ qualitative + [Z]+ operational → PROCEED.
```

**IonWave Example**:
- **LAUNCH → PMF Gate** (Day 90): LTV:CAC >3.0, NPS >40%, churn <15%, Month 3 retention >60%, revenue $30K-60K (Months 4-6). Pass 6+ criteria → proceed to Scale.
- **PMF → SCALE Gate** (Day 180): LTV:CAC >4.0, churn <12%, MER >2.5, runway >6 months, team in place. Pass → scale ad spend 3-5x.

### STEP 8: Document Core SOPs (Repeatable Processes)

**Framework**: Write step-by-step SOPs for high-frequency, error-prone processes.

**Template**:
```
# SOP-[N]: [Process Name]

**Trigger**: [What initiates this process]
**Owner**: [Role responsible]
**Frequency**: [Daily / Weekly / Monthly / As needed]
**Duration**: [Estimated time]
**Tool**: [Software/system used]

---

## Steps

1. **[Step 1 name]**:
   - [Sub-task A]
   - [Sub-task B]

2. **[Step 2 name]**:
   - [Sub-task A]
   ...

## Quality Check
- [Verification 1]
- [Verification 2]

## Handoff (if applicable)
[When/how this process hands off to another]
```

**IonWave Examples**:
- SOP-001: Order Fulfillment (export → pack → mark fulfilled → ship)
- SOP-002: Customer Support Response (triage → respond → resolve → log → escalate)
- SOP-003: Weekly Ad Account Review (review metrics → kill underperformers → scale winners → creative refresh)

### STEP 9: Build Crisis Mode Protocol

**Framework**: Normal rhythms PAUSE in crisis. Define what triggers crisis mode + what changes.

**Template**:
```
## Crisis Mode Activation

**Triggers** (any one activates crisis mode):
- [ ] [Existential blocker 1, e.g., ad account ban]
- [ ] [Existential blocker 2, e.g., cash runway <30 days]
- [ ] [Existential blocker 3, e.g., churn >30%]
...

**What Changes**:
- Daily Pulse → "What unblocks crisis?" (not "What's the plan?")
- Weekly Review → "Crisis resolution status" (not normal WBR)
- Monthly/Quarterly Reviews → PAUSED (resume when crisis resolved)

**Exit Criteria**: [What must be true to resume normal rhythm]
- [ ] [Blocker resolved]
- [ ] [Mitigation plan in place]
- [ ] [Team agrees crisis is over]

**Circuit Breaker Rule**: Feed-forward loops assume normal ops. In crisis, loops pause (don't blindly execute stale plan).
```

**IonWave Example**: Crisis triggers = ad account ban, cash runway <30 days, churn >30%, major compliance violation (FDA warning letter), product recall. Exit = blocker resolved OR mitigated to non-critical + team vote.

### STEP 10: Self-Grade Against Rubric

**Framework**: Use 5-dimension rubric to assess plan quality.

| Dimension | Score 5 | Score 3 | Score 1 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Evidence Coverage** | Every assumption sourced, A/B-grade research | Several C-grade assumptions | Mostly guesses |
| **Confidence Honesty** | Every grade defensible, gaps documented with upgrade paths | Some generous grades, vague upgrade paths | Grades meaningless |
| **Upgrade Path Quality** | Every gap has actionable upgrade path (specific trigger, timeline) | Half the paths are vague | No upgrade paths |
| **Actionability** | Can execute without asking "what does this mean?" | Can execute with some clarification | Too vague to act on |
| **Reusability** | Works for any Trade in this domain | Highly Trade-specific, hard to adapt | Not reusable |

**Scoring**: Average of 5 dimensions → map to X/10.

**IonWave Example**: M35 scored 8.2/10 (Evidence 4/5, Honesty 5/5, Upgrade 4/5, Actionability 5/5, Reusability 3.5/5).

## Grading Rubric (A-F Scale)

### A-Grade (9.0-10.0/10)
- **Evidence**: All assumptions A/B-grade sourced OR validated by real execution data
- **Honesty**: Every claim has confidence grade, every gap has upgrade path
- **Actionability**: Founder can execute Week 1 Day-by-Day without asking questions
- **Reusability**: Framework works for any Trade with <10% adaptation
- **Gates**: All phase gates passed with actuals (not estimates)

**Example**: M13 Media Buying (10.0/10) — Bootstrap actuals, field-tested, universal framework.

---

### B-Grade (7.0-8.9/10)
- **Evidence**: Mix of A/B-grade research + C/D-grade Trade-specific assumptions
- **Honesty**: Gaps documented, some upgrade paths vague
- **Actionability**: Founder can execute with minor clarification
- **Reusability**: Framework works but requires 20-30% Trade-specific adaptation
- **Gates**: Phase gates defined but not yet validated with actuals

**Example**: M35 Execution Plans (8.2/10) — A-grade research, C/D IonWave adaptation, pre-launch status.

---

### C-Grade (5.0-6.9/10)
- **Evidence**: Several C/D-grade assumptions, sources missing
- **Honesty**: Some gaps documented, upgrade paths mostly vague
- **Actionability**: Founder needs significant clarification to execute
- **Reusability**: Framework is Trade-specific, hard to generalize
- **Gates**: Phase gates are subjective ("feels ready") not objective

---

### D-Grade (3.0-4.9/10)
- **Evidence**: Mostly guesses, minimal sourcing
- **Honesty**: Gaps not documented, no upgrade paths
- **Actionability**: Too vague to execute without major rework
- **Reusability**: Not reusable (one-off plan, not framework)

---

### F-Grade (<3.0/10)
- Incomplete, missing critical components, not production-ready

## Universal Principles (Apply to Any Trade)

1. **Lock 30, flex 90, theme 365**: Precision diminishes with time horizon.
2. **Feed-forward loops**: Daily → Weekly → Monthly → Quarterly. Each informs the next.
3. **Objective gates, not subjective**: "LTV:CAC >3.0" beats "feels like PMF".
4. **Crisis mode exists**: Normal rhythms PAUSE in crisis. Circuit breaker prevents blind execution.
5. **Document kills**: Institutional memory prevents re-trying failed experiments.
6. **Confidence grades on all claims**: A/B/C/D + upgrade paths.
7. **Meeting budget <20% of work time**: If rhythms exceed 20% of hours, audit and cut.
8. **Phase-gated scaling**: Don't scale broken unit economics. Gates prevent premature death.
