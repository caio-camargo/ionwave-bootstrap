## Current State: D2C Execution Planning (2026)

**Consensus view**:
- **30/60/90 framework dominant**: First 30 days locked with task-level precision, 31-90 directional, beyond 90 strategic themes only. [Confidence: A | Source: medium.com/@dcirl/building-your-2026-strategy | Date: 2025-12]
- **Velocity > perfection**: In 2026's AI era, speed + direction beats analysis paralysis. Lock the next 30 days, stay flexible beyond. [Confidence: A | Source: Medium D2C strategy | Date: 2025-12]
- **3-month trial period**: D2C brands take 30 days to set up, 30 days to test, 30 days to see first scale signs. [Confidence: B | Source: mocaup.in/d2c-digital-marketing-agency-in-2026 | Date: 2026]

**Standard practices**:
- Pre-launch checklists (13-20 steps covering legal, product, technical, creative)
- Weekly metric reviews (revenue, CAC, ROAS, cohort retention)
- Monthly financial reviews (P&L, cash, runway)
- Quarterly strategic pivots (OKRs, hiring, market expansion)

**Key metrics tracked**:
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) — benchmark $30-60 for supplements
- LTV:CAC ratio — target >3.0 for PMF, >4.0 for scale
- MER (Merchandising Efficiency Ratio) — revenue / ad spend, target >2.0
- Cohort retention — Month 1 >70%, Month 3 >60%
- NPS "very disappointed" threshold — 40%+ for PMF

## Best in Class: Who Executes Exceptionally Well?

### Amazon: Weekly Business Review (WBR)

**What they do**:
- Executive team meets every Wednesday for 60-90 minutes
- Review 400-500 metrics (current state of all businesses)
- Rooted in Six Sigma DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control)
- 2x2 matrix framework for data synthesis
- Four sections: Business Update, Risks/Observations/Trends/Challenges (ROTC), Metrics (current/target/trend), Customer Highlights/Lowlights

**Why it works**:
- Predictable cadence (never skipped, even if CEO absent)
- Data-driven (no anecdotes without metrics)
- Accountability (owners assigned to every metric)
- Pattern detection (weekly frequency catches trends early)

**Limitations**:
- Requires mature data infrastructure (automated dashboards)
- Time-intensive (60-90 min with 10+ executives = expensive)
- 400-500 metrics overwhelming for small team

[Confidence: A | Source: commoncog.com/the-amazon-weekly-business-review, paulmduvall.com/mastering-weekly-business-reviews | Date: 2026]

---

### Mutiny: Quarterly Offsite Operating Rhythm

**What they do**:
- Quarterly in-person offsite (anchors operating rhythm)
- Offsite focused on OKRs + projects for next quarter (not brainstorming)
- Translate quarterly goals into weekly goals for execution discipline
- Mid-quarter check-ins to adjust

**Why it works**:
- In-person offsites build alignment faster than Zoom
- OKR-driven (every quarter has 3-5 clear objectives)
- Weekly translation prevents "set and forget" syndrome

**IonWave adaptation**:
- Solo founder can't do "offsite" with self → coffee shop or park (change of environment)
- Quarterly = Day 90, 180, 270, 365 reviews (aligns with phase gates)

[Confidence: B | Source: cascade.app/blog/operating-cadence-and-rhythm | Date: 2026]

### HashiCorp: Scorecard-Driven Rhythms

**What they do**:
- Annual, quarterly, weekly rhythms
- Scorecards define objectives at each level
- Scorecard reviews are rituals (not optional meetings)
- Cascading structure: annual → quarterly → weekly

**Why it works**:
- Scorecards make priorities explicit (no "everything is important")
- Ritual cadence builds muscle memory (team knows rhythm)
- Cascading prevents misalignment (weekly work ties to quarterly goals)

**IonWave adaptation**:
- Survival Five scorecard (M25) is monthly ritual
- WBR 7-metric MVD is weekly ritual
- OKRs are quarterly ritual

[Confidence: B | Source: practicahq.com/skill/operating-cadence | Date: 2026]

### First Round Capital Portfolio: PMF Levels Framework

**What they do**:
- Define 4 levels of PMF: Nascent → Developing → Strong → Extreme
- Each level has objective criteria (NPS, LTV:CAC, retention, growth rate)
- Founders use levels to gate decisions (don't scale at Nascent, do scale at Strong)

**Why it works**:
- Prevents premature scaling (kills startups)
- Objective criteria reduce founder bias ("I think we have PMF" → "Do we pass the 6 quantitative gates?")
- Levels inform hiring, fundraising, channel spend

**Criteria** (Strong PMF):
- 40%+ users "very disappointed" without product (NPS threshold)
- LTV:CAC >3.0
- Retention curves plateauing (not cliff-ing)
- Growth predictable (not erratic)

[Confidence: A | Source: firstround.com/levels | Date: 2026]

## Where the Industry is Wrong

### Myth 1: "Ship fast, iterate later" means skip planning

**Why it's wrong**:
- Velocity = speed + **direction**. Without direction (plan), speed is just thrashing.
- "Ship fast" means lock the next 30 days with precision, not wing it.
- Iteration requires baseline (you can't iterate if you don't know what you're testing).

**What's right**:
- Lock 30 days with task-level granularity.
- Days 31-90 directional (themes, milestones).
- Beyond 90 strategic only (quarterly goals).

**IonWave stance**: Day 1-30 plan is non-negotiable. Day 31-90 is flex. Day 91+ is hypothesis.

---

### Myth 2: "Meetings are waste" (async-only bias)

**Why it's wrong**:
- Async works for status updates. Fails for brainstorming, conflict resolution, complex problems.
- High-bandwidth communication (sync) needed for strategic decisions.
- Amazon WBR is synchronous for a reason (pattern detection requires discussion).

**What's right**:
- Default to async for low-bandwidth (status, FYIs, decisions that can wait 24 hours).
- Reserve sync for high-bandwidth (strategy, conflict, urgent blockers).
- Small teams (<3 people) can be more async. Teams 4+ need sync rituals.

**IonWave stance**: Solo founder (Month 1-3) = mostly async (self-reflection). Team (Month 4+) = daily standup + weekly WBR sync.

---

### Myth 3: "Phase gates are waterfall thinking" (agile purists)

**Why it's wrong**:
- Agile ≠ no gates. Agile = frequent validation checkpoints.
- Phase gates prevent scaling broken unit economics (common D2C failure mode).
- Gates are go/no-go decision points, not "must complete all tasks" checkboxes.

**What's right**:
- Launch → PMF gate: Don't scale ads if CAC >$100 sustained.
- PMF → Scale gate: Don't hire team if LTV:CAC <3.0.
- Gates allow iteration: If you fail PMF gate, you iterate 30-60 days, not proceed to scale.

**IonWave stance**: Day 90 gate (Launch → PMF) and Day 180 gate (PMF → Scale) are hard stops. No gate pass = no proceed.

---

### Myth 4: "Solo founders can skip operating rhythms"

**Why it's wrong**:
- Solo founders need rhythms MORE than teams (no external accountability).
- Without rhythms, solo founders drift (work reactively, not strategically).
- Daily pulse (5 min) + weekly WBR (30 min) + monthly MBR (60 min) = 6 hours/month = 3.5% of work time = trivial overhead for massive alignment gain.

**What's right**:
- Solo founders use lighter rhythms (shorter duration, less formal).
- Rhythms scale with team (5 min solo standup → 15 min team standup).
- Skipping rhythms acceptable ONLY in crisis mode (then resume).

**IonWave stance**: Solo founder uses full rhythm stack (daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly), just compressed duration.

## Our Contrarian Take

### Take 1: "Choreography" is the underrated meta-skill

**Conventional wisdom**: Focus on execution. Do the work. Ship product. Talk to customers.

**Our take**: **Choreography** (sequencing loops so they feed each other) is what separates organized founders from chaotic founders.

**Why we're right**:
- Daily pulse → weekly WBR → monthly MBR → quarterly QBR is a **feed-forward system**. Each loop informs the next.
- Without choreography, loops collide (e.g., Daily standup repeats WBR topics = waste) or gap (e.g., WBR doesn't inform MBR = misalignment).
- Choreography is cheap to design (1-2 hours upfront) but expensive to live without (compounding misalignment costs).

**Evidence**:
- Amazon's WBR works because it feeds into monthly/quarterly reviews (not standalone).
- Mutiny's quarterly offsite works because it translates to weekly goals (not "set and forget").

**IonWave application**:
- Monday AM starts with Friday WBR insights (feed-forward).
- Monthly MBR aggregates weekly WBRs (feed-forward).
- Quarterly QBR sets OKRs that cascade to monthly priorities (feed-forward).

---

### Take 2: "Phase gates should be stricter for D2C than SaaS"

**Conventional wisdom**: B2B SaaS uses gates (Sales-Led Growth, Product-Led Growth gates). D2C is more fluid (just scale what works).

**Our take**: D2C **needs stricter gates than SaaS** because unit economics decay faster at scale (creative fatigue, CAC inflation, churn acceleration).

**Why we're right**:
- SaaS scales linearly (more customers = more revenue, marginal cost near zero).
- D2C scales sub-linearly (more ad spend = diminishing ROAS due to audience saturation, creative fatigue).
- Scaling D2C with LTV:CAC <3.0 = burning cash faster than you realize.

**Evidence**:
- First Round PMF levels framework exists because startups scale too early.
- D2C brands with <3.0 LTV:CAC who scale aggressively often implode within 12-18 months (burn cash, can't fundraise, wind down).

**IonWave application**:
- Day 90 gate: If LTV:CAC <3.0, do NOT proceed to Month 4-6 scale. Iterate.
- Day 180 gate: If LTV:CAC <4.0, do NOT scale to $10K+/month ad spend. Fix unit economics first.

---

### Take 3: "Operating loops = insurance against founder blindness"

**Conventional wisdom**: Good founders have intuition. They "just know" when to pivot or scale.

**Our take**: **All founders are blind to some patterns.** Operating loops force pattern detection even when founder intuition fails.

**Why we're right**:
- Founder intuition is vulnerable to confirmation bias (seeing what you want to see).
- Weekly WBR forces confrontation with data (e.g., "CAC has crept up 5% weekly for 4 weeks" → can't be ignored).
- Monthly MBR forces "What didn't work?" question → surfaces uncomfortable truths.

**Evidence**:
- Amazon's WBR exists because even Jeff Bezos + executives need systematic pattern detection.
- Founders who skip reviews often miss inflection points (e.g., churn accelerating, CAC spiking) until crisis.

**IonWave application**:
- WBR ROTC section (Risks, Observations, Trends, Challenges) is mandatory, even if uncomfortable.
- Monthly MBR "What didn't work?" forces honesty (ego-deflating but necessary).

## Implications for IonWave

### What we do differently (from industry standard):

1. **Choreography-first design**: We explicitly designed loop sequencing (Mon starts with Fri WBR, WBR feeds MBR, MBR feeds QBR). Most startups add loops ad-hoc (collision risk).

2. **Stricter gates than peers**: Most D2C brands scale at "Developing PMF" (30-39% NPS). We require "Strong PMF" (40%+ NPS, LTV:CAC >3.0, churn <15%) before Month 7+ scale.

3. **Solo founder rhythm discipline**: Most solo founders skip rhythms ("too small for meetings"). We use full rhythm stack (daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly), just compressed.

4. **Phase-gated meeting cadence**: Month 1-3 solo (lightweight), Month 4-9 small team (medium), Month 10+ scaling team (full). Meeting budget scales with team size and revenue.

5. **Feed-forward loops**: Every loop outputs to the next (daily → weekly → monthly → quarterly). No loop is standalone.

### What we steal (from best-in-class):

1. **Amazon WBR structure**: ROTC (Risks/Observations/Trends/Challenges) format, customer highlights/lowlights, metric-driven.

2. **First Round PMF levels**: Objective criteria for gating decisions (40% NPS, LTV:CAC >3.0, retention >60%).

3. **Mutiny quarterly offsite**: Change of environment for strategic review (coffee shop, not office).

4. **HashiCorp scorecard rituals**: Survival Five (M25), MVD 7 metrics (M30), OKRs (quarterly) are all scorecard-driven.

### What we avoid (anti-patterns):

1. **Meeting sprawl**: No status update meetings. No meetings without agendas. No recurring meetings that lost purpose.

2. **Premature scaling**: No Month 2 hiring if Month 1 CAC >$80. No Month 7 scale if Day 180 gate failed.

3. **Data debt**: No manual metric assembly (automate dashboards). No arguing about data accuracy in meetings (fix pipelines async).

4. **Async-only dogma**: Yes to async for status. No to async for brainstorming or conflict resolution.

5. **"Just execute" without plan**: No winging it. Day 1-30 locked, Day 31-90 directional, Day 91+ strategic.

## 2026-Specific Trends

### AI-Powered Automation Collapsing Overhead

**Trend**: Manual weekly metric assembly (2-4 hours) → AI-generated dashboards (15 min review). [Confidence: A | Source: rowzero.com/blog/weekly-business-review | Date: 2026]

**Implication for IonWave**:
- Month 1-3: Manual (Google Sheets, Shopify exports) = 2-3 hours/week.
- Month 4+: Automate (Geckoboard, Databox, Triple Whale) = 30 min/week.
- Savings: 1.5-2.5 hours/week = 6-10 hours/month = 1.5-2.5% of founder time.

### Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) for D2C Discovery

**Trend**: 83% of product discovery now happens through AI-powered channels (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Brands that master AEO see 3.2x higher conversion from AI-referred traffic. [Confidence: A | Source: radianmarketing.com/d2c-ecommerce-seo-strategy-2026 | Date: 2026]

**Implication for IonWave**:
- SEO isn't dead, it's AI-mediated. Content must answer questions clearly (not keyword-stuffed).
- Product pages need "What is marine plasma?" "How is it different from LMNT?" "Is it safe?" — FAQ-style, AI-parseable.
- M16 Content & SEO already addresses this (YMYL/E-E-A-T, pillar content).

### Consent Mode v2 & Privacy-First Tracking

**Trend**: GDPR Consent Mode v2 mandatory (March 2024). GA4 behavioral modeling + server-side tracking are new baseline. [Confidence: A | Source: M9 research | Date: 2026]

**Implication for IonWave**:
- M9 Week 1 Setup Sequence includes Consent Mode v2 from Day 1 (not bolted on later).
- Server-side tracking (CAPI, sGTM) prevents 15-25% attribution loss from client-side blocking.
